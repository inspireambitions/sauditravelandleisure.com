Beautiful Places to See in Saudi Arabia

Updated January 20, 2021 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide


Saudi Arabia is full of places that surprise visitors with their natural beauty and unique landscapes.

From deserts to mountains to the sea, the country offers far more variety than many expect. One of the most striking places is Hegra in AlUla, where massive tombs were carved into sandstone cliffs over 2,000 years ago. The area has a quiet, timeless feel, with golden rocks and open desert all around. It's not just a historic site it's a beautiful one too, especially at sunrise or sunset.

In the southwest, the Asir Mountains offer a very different view of Saudi Arabia. This region is cooler and greener than most of the country. Small villages sit on hillsides, often covered in low clouds. The air is fresh, and the scenery feels more like a mountain retreat than a desert country. Towns like Abha and Rijal Almaa are worth exploring if you want to see this side of Saudi Arabia.

Along the Red Sea coastline, you'll find clear blue water, quiet beaches, and coral reefs that make it a hidden gem for snorkeling or diving. Places like Umluj and the beaches near Jeddah offer a calm, tropical vibe that many travelers miss.

Another beautiful natural spot is Wadi Al Disah

A valley in the northwest with tall red cliffs, palm trees, and freshwater streams. It's ideal for hiking, relaxing in nature, or just taking in the peaceful views. The mix of greenery and red rock feels almost like a secret garden in the middle of nowhere.

And of course, there’s the Edge of the World near Riyadh. This dramatic cliff overlooks a flat, endless stretch of desert. It’s one of those places that makes you stop and just stare. The silence, the height, and the wide view make it unforgettable.

These places show how much variety Saudi Arabia offers—mountains, valleys, beaches, deserts, and ancient ruins. Whether you're into photography, hiking, or just quiet spots with great views, there's a lot of beauty waiting to be seen.
