Who Can Visit Dubai? The Basics

Visa Types Explained: Which One Applies To You?

Visa on Arrival: What It Covers and Who Gets It

Visa on arrival is the simplest path for many travelers. It is typically stamped into your passport upon arrival at Dubai International (DXB) or other UAE entry points. Common features:

  • Standard duration: 30 days for many nationalities; certain passports are granted a 90-day multiple-entry stamp.
  • Purpose: tourism, family visiting, short business visits.
  • Extension: In many cases a 30-day visa can be extended once through GDRFA procedures, but extensions and rules can differ depending on nationality.

If your passport is eligible, you can board your flight without prior visa arrangements. But keep in mind the UAE occasionally adjusts policies; always verify before departure.

Pre-Arranged Visas: When They芒聙聶re Required

Some nationalities must obtain a visa before travel. Typical routes to arrange a pre-approved visa include applying through the airline you fly with (for example, using Manage Your Booking with an airline like Emirates), via a UAE-based sponsor (hotel or host resident), or through a visa processing agency such as VFS Global.

Pre-arranged visas come in short-term options (48-hour, 96-hour transit permits) and longer tourist visas (30-day single or multiple-entry, 60-day variants). Requirements often include a passport copy, a passport-style photo, confirmed return ticket, and hotel reservation or host tenancy agreement.

Step-by-Step: How to Apply for a Dubai Visa

If You Qualify for Visa on Arrival

If your passport qualifies for visa on arrival, your process is straightforward:

  • Book your flight and ensure your passport has at least six months芒聙聶 validity.
  • Print your return or onward ticket and hotel booking confirmation.
  • On arrival, follow immigration signs for visitors and present your passport; the officer will determine the visa stamp and duration.

Carry copies of your travel documentation and a business card or contact details for your hotel. Small details like having a printed confirmation save time if immigration requests proof.

If You Need a Pre-Arranged Visa

For nationals who must apply in advance, follow this standard workflow:

  1. Choose the right visa type for your stay (transit, 30-day tourist, 60-day long-term tourist, single vs multiple entry).
  2. Collect required documents: passport copy, passport photo, confirmed airline ticket, hotel reservation or host details, and any additional documents requested for your country.
  3. Apply through the approved channel: airline booking portal, online visa center recommended by your carrier, or a sponsored UAE ticketing office.
  4. Pay the visa fee and save the confirmation. If the visa is electronic, print the entry permit and carry it on arrival.

Our readers often find airline-managed visas convenient; if traveling with an airline that offers this service, you can usually apply via your booking reference. Applying Through a Sponsor in the UAE

If you have family or a friend in the UAE, they can act as your sponsor and apply for a visa on your behalf. That requires the sponsor to visit an immigration office or ticketing office with your details and pay the applicable fees. Sponsors must typically guarantee visitor compliance and sometimes deposit refundable sums for certain visa categories.

Using an Agency: Pros and Cons

Visa agencies simplify paperwork and handle logistics, which is helpful when you cannot apply directly or need additional document certification. The trade-offs are additional fees and less direct control. Always verify the agency芒聙聶s credentials and request tracked application receipts.

Common Reasons for Denial

  • Visas from prior visits not officially cancelled.
  • Missing or inconsistent travel documents (no return ticket, hotel booking mismatch).
  • Presence of certain criminal or civil legal cases preventing exit/entry.
  • Traveling with restricted items or unprescribed medications requiring permits.
  • Entry on diplomatic/official passports without proper prior visas (specific rules apply).

Practical steps protect you from being refused:

  • Secure printed copies of all confirmations: visa approval, hotel booking, return ticket.
  • If you have used a visa previously, verify it was formally cancelled if required by UAE rules.
  • Check banned or restricted items before packing; controlled medications often require formal permits.
  • If you have legal or financial disputes in any Gulf country, resolve these in advance芒聙聰exit bans are strictly enforced.

Arrival at Dubai Airports: Immigration and Screening

What Happens at Immigration

Dubai conducts identity verification and often random additional screening. Expect to present your passport and printed visa (if pre-arranged), and sometimes proof of accommodation or onward travel. Officials may perform visual checks or ask for supporting documents. Arriving passengers occasionally are directed to secondary screening for document verification.

If you were issued an electronic visa, carry a printed copy to present on request. The immigration officer will confirm the visa stamp duration and may ask routine questions about your plans and accommodation. Simple, clear answers and ready access to your documentation help clear this process quickly.

Customs, Baggage and Restricted Items

Customs in the UAE strictly controls items like certain medicines, narcotics, e-cigarettes, CBD products, religious proselytizing materials, and some electronics. Prescription medicines often require an import permit from the UAE health authorities. Carry prescriptions and, for controlled substances, secure formal approvals before travel.

Declare cash amounts above the legal threshold if required. Always follow customs guidance and check the updated list of prohibited items before you pack.

Health, Safety and Local Laws

Safety Reality Check

Dubai is generally safe for tourists, with low violent crime levels and strong public security. The city is heavily policed and monitored, which contributes to safety but also means local laws are enforced strictly. Be mindful that behavior acceptable in many Western cities芒聙聰public drunkenness, overt displays of affection, certain types of speech芒聙聰can be illegal in the UAE.

Terrorism threats and regional tensions mean travelers should monitor local news and register with their embassy if advised. The UAE enforces exit controls in cases involving legal disputes, outstanding debts, or criminal investigations. Travelers should be aware their ability to leave the country can be halted until issues are resolved.

Dress, Behavior and Religious Considerations

Dubai is cosmopolitan, but conservative behavior is expected in public spaces. Dressing modestly in heritage areas, religious sites, and government buildings is advisable. Swimwear is fine at beaches and private hotel pools, but avoid wearing it in shopping malls and city streets.

Public displays of affection such as kissing are discouraged. Drinking alcohol is allowed in licensed venues, but public drunkenness can lead to arrest. Respect local customs and use discretion with social media content芒聙聰public online behavior can have real-world consequences.

Women and LGBTQ Travelers

Women can travel safely in Dubai, but it is smart to adopt low-profile behaviors at night and in unfamiliar contexts: favor groups for evening outings, avoid isolated areas, and be cautious about accepting drinks from strangers. The legal environment for LGBTQ people is restrictive; same-sex relationships and expressions can be subject to penalties. Absolute discretion is necessary.

Money, Communication and Transport

Currency and Payment Methods

The UAE dirham (AED) is the local currency and is pegged to the US dollar (approx. AED 3.67 = USD 1). Credit and debit cards are widely accepted, and contactless payments are common. Still, carry some cash for small purchases and local taxis.

ATMs are plentiful, but follow bank-safety habits: use well-lit, secure locations and hide your PIN. Avoid carrying large cash sums and split cards/cash across your belongings.

Mobile and Internet

Prepaid SIM services are available upon arrival and are easy to obtain from providers like Du and Etisalat. Travel plans often include generous data allowances. Many major tourist venues and hotels provide reliable Wi芒聙聭Fi.

Avoiding Common Scams and Safety Traps

Dubai is generally safe, but petty crime and common scams exist. Romance and online scams rely on emotional manipulation; never send money to people you meet online without independent verification. Gold scams, taxi overcharging (insist on the meter), and ATM skimming are occasional issues. Use hotel-recommended services and licensed operators for tours and excursions.

When dealing with street vendors or private sellers (gold, electronics), request receipts and avoid large cash transfers without authentication. For online bookings, use reputable portals and confirm bookings directly with hotels. If a deal seems too good to be true, it often is; protect your personal data and bank details.
