Can I Get a Visa on Arrival in Muscat

Updated October 24, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Muscat has become a magnet for travelers in the Gulf — whether for short beach escapes, cultural discovery, or business travel — and many visitors arrive with a practical question: can I get a visa on arrival in Muscat? Understanding how Oman's entry rules work will save you time at the airport and reduce the risk of unexpected problems at immigration.

Short answer: Yes — but eligibility depends on your nationality, residency status, and the type of visit you plan. Oman offers visa-free entry to citizens of many countries, visa-on-arrival for specific nationalities, and a robust eVisa system for most others. Sponsored visas and work permits follow a different process and require pre-arranged paperwork.

This article explains exactly who can get a visa on arrival in Muscat, when it is allowed, how to prepare before travel, what happens at Muscat International Airport, and sensible alternatives if you are not eligible. I will also share the practical checklist and the common pitfalls to avoid so you can move from curiosity to a confident, well-planned visit. As the KSA Travel Insider & Cultural Guide at Saudi Travel & Leisure, my goal is to give you the actionable blueprint that turns visa questions into smooth arrival experiences.

Eligibility Overview: Who Can Get a Visa on Arrival in Muscat?

Categories of entry

Oman structures entry into a few practical categories: visa-exempt nationals, nationals eligible for visa on arrival, eVisa applicants, and those who require a sponsor (employment, family reunion, or certain business categories). Citizens of Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) states travel visa-free, while many other nationalities either receive automatic entry for a short period or can secure a visa on arrival at Muscat’s international checkpoints.

Visa-exempt nationals and short stays

Oman currently allows citizens of more than 100 countries to enter without a visa for short stays (commonly up to 14 days). These arrangements often require a passport with at least six months’ validity, a confirmed onward or return ticket, and proof of accommodation or sufficient funds. If you belong to this group, you will not need a visa on arrival — you simply present your documents at immigration.

Nationals eligible for visa on arrival

Some nationalities that are not fully visa-exempt can obtain a tourist visa on arrival at airports and designated land borders. The practical effect is the same as an eVisa but processed at the point of entry. If your nationality falls into this group, you should still verify current rules before travel because eligibility can change.

eVisa users and unsponsored tourist visas

For many travelers the Royal Oman Police (ROP) eVisa system is the recommended route: apply online for a Tourist Visit Visa (Unsponsored) and receive authorization before travel. This is often faster and removes uncertainty on arrival. Citizens of some countries are required to use the eVisa system rather than relying on visa-on-arrival.

Sponsored visas (employment, family, business)

If you are traveling for work, joining family who reside in Oman, or your visit involves specific contractual business activities, a sponsor in Oman must arrange the appropriate visa. The sponsor is responsible for depositing the visa with the Visa Deposit counter at Muscat International Airport at least 24 hours prior to your arrival (more detail in later sections).

The Laws and Agencies Behind Oman’s Entry Rules

Who manages visas in Oman?

The Royal Oman Police (ROP) operates the central eVisa portal and manages immigration procedures at ports of entry. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs provides overarching policy and diplomatic coordination, while border control and airport immigration implement screening and stamping on arrival.

Why policies change and how to stay current

Oman periodically updates visa categories, fees, and lists of eligible countries. Seasonal events, diplomatic changes, and public health considerations can also prompt short-term adjustments. For reliable, up-to-date guidance, consult official ROP channels and the country’s foreign ministry before booking travel.

The Practical Differences Between Visa-On-Arrival, eVisa, and Sponsored Entry

Visa on arrival — convenience, limitations, and risks

Visa on arrival is convenient because it allows eligible visitors to secure entry at the airport without prior paperwork. However, it carries three practical drawbacks: first, you must confirm eligibility ahead of time; second, arrivals can face queues and processing delays; third, failure to meet supporting requirements (passport validity, return ticket, proof of funds) can result in denied entry. If you rely on visa on arrival, prepare all supporting documents and arrive early.

eVisa — predictability and peace of mind

Applying through the ROP eVisa system removes much of the uncertainty. You submit your passport details, travel itinerary, and payment online and receive an approval letter or visa that you present at immigration. For those unsure about on-arrival eligibility, the eVisa is the safer choice.

Sponsored visas — mandatory for work and some business activities

If you’re traveling for employment, to join family, or for certain categories of business, your Omani sponsor must obtain the visa in advance and deposit it at the Muscat Airport Visa Deposit counter. Sponsored visas typically cannot be processed on arrival; the sponsor handles the formalities and ensures the visa is available when you land.

How to Get a Visa on Arrival in Muscat — Step-by-Step Blueprint

Below is a focused step-by-step checklist you can follow before you travel and immediately after landing. Use it as an operational blueprint to avoid surprises.

  1. Check eligibility: Confirm whether your nationality is visa-exempt, eligible for visa on arrival, or must use the eVisa system through the Royal Oman Police website.
  2. Validate your passport: Ensure at least six months’ validity remaining from your planned entry date.
  3. Book travel and accommodation: Have a confirmed onward/return ticket and a hotel reservation or contact details for where you will stay.
  4. Prepare proof of funds and documentation: Carry printed or digital copies of bank statements or credit cards if requested, and any invitation or sponsor paperwork if applicable.
  5. If sponsored, confirm deposit: Ensure your sponsor deposited your visa at the Muscat Airport Visa Deposit counter at least 24 hours before arrival and verify the deposit time and receipt with them.
  6. Arrive with the right payment method: Be prepared to pay visa fees in the approved currency or by card if required; check the current fee on the ROP site.
  7. On arrival at Muscat International Airport: Follow signs to immigration, present your passport and supporting documents, and pay any fees; if a visa deposit applies, go to the Visa Deposit counter located in the arrival hall before proceeding to immigration.
  8. Keep copies of your entry stamp and visa: These are needed for hotel check-in, local SIM registration, and potentially when exiting Oman.

(That checklist is the single list used in this article. The rest of the guidance will be prose-based to maintain clarity and depth.)

Sponsored Visas: The Deposit Procedure at Muscat International Airport

How sponsoring works and where to deposit the visa

When an Omani sponsor obtains a visa on your behalf — commonly for employment or family reunion — the sponsor must deposit the stamped visa with the Visa Deposit office in Muscat Airport’s arrival hall. The deposit must be completed at least 24 hours before the passenger arrives.

The Visa Deposit counter is located in the arrival meet-and-greet area, next to the Baggage Enquiry counter near the airport canteen. Knowing this exact location speeds up arrival for sponsored passengers because immigration officers will look for the deposited visa.

Visa Deposit counter opening hours

The Visa Deposit office maintains specific hours:

  • Sunday–Thursday: 9 AM–1 PM and 2 PM–5 PM  
  • Saturday: 8 AM–1 PM and 2 PM–4 PM

Sponsors should arrange the deposit within these hours and obtain a receipt or confirmation for the traveler to present if needed.

What the sponsor needs to provide

Sponsors should bring the original visa approval and the traveler’s passport details. They must ensure the deposit is properly logged with the airport authorities; any lapse can lead to arrival delays or denial of entry.

Arrival Procedures at Muscat International Airport

Step through arrival efficiently

Upon landing, follow signs for Arrival / Immigration. If you’re sponsored and your sponsor has deposited your visa, note the Visa Deposit desk’s location and allow time for verification. Have your passport, eVisa approval (if applicable), return ticket, and hotel booking ready.

Immigration officers will examine your passport, any printed or electronic visa authorization, and supporting documents. They may ask about the purpose of your visit and request proof of sufficient funds. Be calm, concise, and present documents readily.

Baggage, customs, and health screening

After immigration you collect luggage and pass through customs. Customs in Oman enforces restrictions on items such as pornography and firearms; there are limitations on bringing alcohol unless purchased through duty-free. If you carry prescription medications, verify that they are permitted in Oman and carry a doctor’s prescription.

During public health events, travelers may encounter additional screening or documentation requirements (for example, vaccination status or health forms). Confirm current requirements before you travel.

Visa Fees, Duration, and Extensions

Typical tourist visa lengths and fees

Tourist visit visas commonly range from 10 days to 30 days for unsponsored tourist categories; some eVisas can be issued for longer stays depending on nationality. Fees vary by visa type and nationality and are set by the Royal Oman Police. To avoid surprises, check the ROP eVisa portal for current fee schedules before travel.

Extensions and overstaying

If you wish to extend a tourist stay, you must apply through the proper channels before your visa expires. Overstaying carries fines and potential legal consequences; traffic and immigration records are linked and can complicate departure. If circumstances prevent timely departure (medical emergency, for example), contact local authorities and your embassy for assistance.

Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

Mistake: assuming visa-on-arrival applies to everyone

Do not assume visa-on-arrival applies to your passport. Check the ROP lists and confirm whether your nationality must apply in advance. Relying on incorrect assumptions is the leading cause of denied boarding or entry.

Mistake: inadequate passport validity

An expired or insufficiently valid passport (less than six months) will typically lead to refusal. Renew your passport well before travel if necessary.

Mistake: missing sponsored visa deposit

If your visit is sponsored, verify that the sponsor deposited the visa with the airport office. A failure to deposit in time can result in significant delays at the gate or denial of entry.

Mistake: unclear travel purpose

Immigration officers may ask brief questions about your visit. If your purpose is a business meeting, print the invitation; if tourism, have your hotel reservation and itinerary. Clear, professional documentation speeds processing.

Mistake: carrying restricted items or banned media

Oman enforces conservative public decency and content laws. Avoid carrying prohibited materials and verify medication legality ahead of travel.

Alternatives If You Are Not Eligible for Visa On Arrival

Apply for an eVisa

If visa on arrival does not apply to you, the ROP eVisa system is usually the fastest and most reliable alternative. Applying online gives you peace of mind, reduces processing time at the airport, and provides a confirmation you can present on arrival.

Use a sponsor in Oman

For employment or family visits, arrange for an Omani sponsor to apply on your behalf. The sponsor arranges deposit and ensures paperwork is in order prior to arrival.

Contact your nearest embassy or consulate

If you have unusual circumstances — complex travel reasons, legal issues, or medical needs — contact the Omani embassy or consulate in your region for tailored advice.

Special Cases and Requirements

GCC nationals

Citizens of GCC countries (Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates) do not require a visa to enter Oman. They typically cross with national ID or passport and enjoy facilitated entry.

Residency in major countries

Travelers who are residents of certain countries (United States, Canada, UK, Schengen area, Japan, Australia) sometimes gain simplified access or shorter granted-stay privileges, even if they are not nationals. Verify whether residency confers visa-on-arrival rights before travel.

Health and screening requirements

For work or residence visas, Oman requires certain medical checks; for tourists, health requirements are less intrusive but can change with global health situations. For example, travelers from yellow fever areas may need a certificate of vaccination. For long-term visas, authorities may require specific medical testing, including HIV/AIDS testing in certain cases; consult official channels to confirm your obligations.

Dual nationals and Omani laws

Oman does not recognize dual nationality in the same way some countries do; if you hold Omani citizenship, entering with another passport may cause complications. If you have dual nationality with Oman, consult your local embassy.

Travel Logistics After You Arrive in Muscat

Getting from the airport to the city

Taxis at Muscat International Airport are readily available and are the most direct option for first-time visitors. Pre-booked airport transfers are also a preferred option for visitors who want a seamless arrival. Car rental counters operate in the arrival area if you prefer independent mobility — ensure your international driving permit and home-country license are acceptable.

Accommodation and SIM cards

Present your passport and entry documents when checking into hotels. Purchasing a local SIM card at the airport or in the city requires passport registration; keeping photocopies of your passport and visa makes the process quicker.

Respectful behavior and local customs

Oman is a conservative and hospitable country. Dress modestly in public spaces — especially in smaller towns and religious sites. Public displays of affection are frowned upon. Alcohol consumption is allowed in designated licensed venues and private settings for non-Muslims, but public intoxication is illegal.

Safety, police, and emergency numbers

Oman is generally considered safe. Emergency numbers and the location of your embassy are useful information to store pre-departure. If you encounter legal issues, contact your embassy for assistance; the U.S. Embassy and other diplomatic missions maintain consular helplines for their citizens.

Regional Travel Considerations: Flights, Land Borders, and Connections

Flying via Dubai or Abu Dhabi

Many travelers transit through Dubai or Abu Dhabi when heading to Muscat. If you plan flights via these hubs, verify transit visa requirements for those countries and connection times. If you want to use a regional itinerary that includes time in the UAE, check inbound visa rules in both the UAE and Oman. For practical guidance on regional connections and transit planning, consult regional travel resources to compare options and schedules.

Overland travel and border crossings

Oman shares land borders with the UAE and Saudi Arabia as well as Yemen (where entry is highly discouraged). Cross-border formalities differ by crossing point; some land borders issue visas on arrival, while others require prior clearance. If you plan to drive across borders, ensure that your vehicle documentation and insurance meet cross-border requirements.

Comparing entry rules in neighbouring Gulf states

Entry rules vary across the Gulf. If your trip includes multiple Gulf states, check the distinct visa regimes for each country so you can plan the right mix of visas and residency documentation.

How to Prepare a Smooth Application Before You Travel

Verify your passport and supporting documents

Confirm passport validity of six months or more and compile digital and printed copies of your flight itinerary, hotel reservations, proof of funds, and any invitation letters or sponsor letters. Save eVisa confirmation pages and emails in offline form (screenshot or PDF) to present at immigration if mobile networks are unreliable.

Confirm payment options for visa fees

Some arrival visas allow payment by card, others require cash in specific currencies. Check the ROP payment options in advance and bring a backup payment method.

Notify your sponsor or point of contact

If you have a sponsor or host in Oman, confirm that their deposit and paperwork are completed and that they can be reached on arrival. Keep the sponsor’s contact number handy and confirm the visa deposit reference or receipt number.

Travel insurance and medical arrangements

Purchase travel medical insurance that covers your planned stay and activities. In case of medical emergency, hospitals and clinics will require identification and insurance details; a travel plan that includes medical evacuation cover is sensible for remote excursions.

Legal and Safety Considerations to Keep in Mind

Criminal records and entry denial

Some countries maintain strict policies about past criminal convictions. Oman’s immigration authorities can deny entry in specific cases. If you have a criminal record or pending legal issues, consult the Omani embassy before traveling to understand potential restrictions.

Prescription medications and controlled substances

Check Oman’s list of permitted medications. Some commonly prescribed drugs are regulated and may require prior permission or a prescription in Arabic. Carry a doctor’s letter, pharmacy labels, and prescriptions to avoid confiscation and legal issues.

Photography and cultural sensitivity

Avoid photographing certain government buildings, airports, and military installations. If in doubt, ask permission before photographing people, especially in rural areas. Respect local customs at religious sites and marketplaces.

Practical Scenarios and Which Option to Choose

Short tourist stay, eligible for visa-on-arrival or visa-free

If your nationality is on the visa-exempt or visa-on-arrival list and your stay is brief, you can plan to use airport processing. Still, have printed confirmations and funds proof for a smoother experience.

Longer stays or multiple-entry needs

For stays longer than standard tourist stamps or for multiple entries, secure an eVisa or a longer-duration visa via the ROP. Pre-arranged permissions reduce the need for in-country extensions.

Work, family reunion, or contractual business

Arrange sponsorship and documentation in advance. Employers or family sponsors handle deposits and will guide you through the airport deposit procedure.

Conclusion

Understanding whether you can get a visa on arrival in Muscat is an essential step in planning your trip to Oman. Eligibility depends on nationality, residency status, and the purpose of travel; for many travelers the ROP eVisa is the safer option, while visa-on-arrival remains a convenient route for eligible passports. Sponsored visas carry their own deposit procedures and schedules that sponsors must follow carefully to prevent arrival delays. Prepare your passport, tickets, accommodation details, and proof of funds, and verify the latest rules on the Royal Oman Police eVisa portal before you travel.

FAQ

Can I get a visa on arrival in Muscat if I hold a passport from the United States or the United Kingdom?

Yes — citizens of many Western countries, including the United States and the United Kingdom, can obtain short tourist visas either on arrival or through the Royal Oman Police eVisa system. For certainty and to avoid delays, many travelers from these countries choose the eVisa before departure.

What documents will immigration ask for when I arrive in Muscat?

Be ready with a passport valid for at least six months, a confirmed return or onward ticket, proof of hotel reservation or accommodation, and evidence of sufficient funds. If you are traveling under a sponsored visa, carry sponsor contact details and confirmation that the sponsor deposited the visa at the airport.

If I’m traveling for work, can I get a visa on arrival?

No. Work visas and many business categories require a sponsor in Oman who obtains the appropriate visa in advance and deposits it at the Visa Deposit counter at Muscat International Airport. Verify that your sponsor completes this process 24 hours before your arrival.

What happens if my passport is lost or stolen while I’m in Oman?

Report the loss immediately to the Royal Oman Police and to your embassy or consulate. Omani authorities require entry verification for departure, and replacing a passport can take time. Keep photocopies of your passport and visa on a separate device or with your accommodation to speed the recovery process.
