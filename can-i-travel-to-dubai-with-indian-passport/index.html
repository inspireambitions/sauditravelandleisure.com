Conditional Visa on Arrival: Whatâs New and Who Qualifies

The current landscape

The UAE has established facilities for granting visas on arrival to Indian nationals under specific conditions. Over recent updates, eligibility has expanded to include Indian passport holders who also hold valid visas or residence permits from certain countries. The policy is designed to facilitate short leisure and business visits without a lengthy prearranged visa process.

Typical eligibility scenarios

You are likely eligible for the visa on arrival if one of the following applies to you:

  • You hold a valid US visa or US green card (minimum six months validity).
  • You hold a valid residence permit or long-term visa from the UK or certain EU countries.
  • You hold a resident permit issued by Australia, Canada, Japan, New Zealand, the Republic of Korea (South Korea), or Singapore (recent expansions).
  • You have a valid multiple-entry Schengen-type residency card from eligible EU countries.

Note: Rules and qualifying lists can be updated. Always confirm before travel.

Visa details and costs

A common visa-on-arrival offering for eligible Indian nationals is a single-entry short-duration visa (examples have included 14-day stay options) with a fee attached (historically around USD 63 for certain 14-day single-entry permits). Exact fees and permitted stay length can vary by issuance date and the specific program in force when you travel.

How to use this route

When you arrive in Dubai, proceed to immigration with your passport and supporting documents proving your qualifying visa/residence permit (physical card or digital proof is best). Immigration officers may ask for a printed copy of the visa or residence permit; given recent requirements for visual screening at Dubai International, carry a hard copy of your qualifying documents and a printed copy of any visa confirmation.

If granted at the counter, your passport will be stamped with the entry permit. If preapproval was needed via an airline or online system before arrival, have the confirmation ready. Always check the exact criteria and fee structure ahead of departure.

Prearranged Tourist and Visit Visas: Options, Costs, and Process

The most universal option

If you are not eligible for visa on arrival, a prearranged tourist visa (also called an e-visa) is the standard route. These are available as single- or multiple-entry and in durations commonly covering 30 or 60 days. They are issued in advance of travel and remain the most flexible option for longer stays.

Types and typical timelines

Tourist visa categories often include:

  • Short-term single entry (30 days)
  • Short-term multiple entry (30 days)
  • Long-term single entry (60 days)
  • Long-term multiple entry (60 days)

Processing times can range from a few working days to a week depending on the service provider. Fees vary by type; for example, 30-day single-entry visas commonly have a lower fee than 60-day or multiple-entry versions. Some visas are extendable once in the UAE (extensions incur additional fees and paperwork).

How to apply

There are three common ways to apply for a prearranged tourist visa:

  • Apply online through the UAE immigration portal or through the airline managing your booking (if eligible).
  • Apply via an approved visa processing center such as VFS Global or a travel agency authorized to submit applications.
  • Have a UAE-based sponsor (hotel, host, or relative) apply on your behalf through authorized channels.

Required documents routinely include a passport copy, passport-sized photo, confirmed hotel booking or tenancy contract, proof of return ticket, proof of funds, and the visa fee payment. Some nationalities require additional documentation; check specific requirements for Indian travelers.

Practical tips for a smooth application

  • Submit at least 7â10 working days before travel whenever possible.
  • Ensure your passport has six monthsâ validity beyond the intended stay.
  • Match your hotel booking dates and flight itinerary with the visa dates exactly to prevent processing delays.
  • Keep electronic and printed copies of the visa confirmation and the passport bio page with you while traveling.

Short Transit Visas: For Stopovers Only

48-hour and 96-hour transit visas

If you are merely stopping over in Dubai on the way to another destination and your layover qualifies, short transit visas of 48 or 96 hours are sometimes available. These are typically non-extendable and are designed for passengers who leave the airport during a brief gap in their itinerary.

How to get one

Transit visas are issued through airlines or via airline ticketing offices. The applicant usually needs an onward confirmed ticket and must meet passport validity requirements. Processing timelines are short, but you should apply well in advance of travel when possible.

Arrival at Dubai: Immigration, Screening, and Practical Arrival Tips

The arrival sequence

After disembarking, youâll pass through immigration. Dubai International Airport has introduced additional visual screening steps for random visitors; if selected, youâll be asked to provide a printed copy of your visa or visa number confirmation page. Have documentation ready to avoid delays or charges for printing.

Baggage reclaim and customs come next. Declare any items as required and be mindful of restricted items and duty-free allowances. Finally, local transport options (taxis, ride-hailing, metro) will get you to your accommodation.

What immigration officers check

Officers will typically verify:

  • Passport validity and visa status.
  • Purpose of visit, accommodation details, and return/onward ticket.
  • Supporting documents for visa-on-arrival or preapproved e-visas (residence cards, foreign visas, sponsor letters if applicable).
  • That the traveler is not inadmissible for security, health, or other legal reasons.

Be clear, concise, and honest when answering questions.

Common arrival-related pitfalls and how to avoid them

  • Not carrying a printed confirmation when required. Solution: Always print your visa confirmation and qualifying documents.
  • Mismatched travel dates between visa and ticket. Solution: Match bookings with application details.
  • Insufficient funds or unclear travel purpose. Solution: Carry bank statements, prepaid hotel confirmations, or proof of prepaid tours.
  • Overstaying your visa. Solution: Track your permitted days and apply for extensions through official channels before expiry.

Extensions, Overstays, and Fines

Extending a visit

Certain tourist visas can be extended once while in the UAE. Extension processes are handled through immigration offices or service centers; they require passport presentation, payment of extension fees, and sometimes an updated ticket or stay proof. Extensions are not guaranteed; apply early if you need more time.

Penalties for overstaying

Overstay fines are assessed per day and increase with the duration of overstay. Immigration authorities can impose daily penalties and may require clearance or settlement before allowing departure. Overstaying can also complicate future visa applications. If you risk overstaying, contact immigration immediately to explore legal options.

Common Reasons for Entry Refusal and How to Prevent Them

Typical causes and preventive measures

  • Invalid or insufficient documentation. Prevent by double-checking passport validity, visa confirmation, and ticket details.
  • Mismatched or suspicious travel purpose. Prevent by carrying clear proof of accommodation and itinerary.
  • Criminal records or adverse watchlist entries. Prevent by ensuring you have no outstanding legal matters and checking any country-specific restrictions before traveling.
  • Health or vaccination issues when emergency public health measures are in effect. Prevent by keeping updated on current health advisories.
  • Prior immigration violations. Prevent by resolving previous overstays or fines before attempting a new entry.

If you are denied entry, remain calm, request the reason in writing if possible, and follow the instruction provided by immigration. In many cases, consular or legal help may be necessary.

FAQ

Do Indian passport holders need a visa to enter Dubai?

Most Indian passport holders require a visa unless they qualify for a conditional visa-on-arrival. Eligibility for on-arrival visas depends on holding valid visas or residence permits from qualifying third countries. How long does the visa-on-arrival stay allow Indian visitors to stay?

The stay permitted under visa-on-arrival schemes varies by program; recent offerings include short stays such as 14 days for eligible Indian nationals. Always verify the specific allowance at the time you travel.

Can I extend my Dubai tourist visa if I want to stay longer?

Some tourist visas are extendable once, subject to immigration approval and payment of extension fees. Check the visa terms carefully and apply for extensions through the official channels before your permit expires.
