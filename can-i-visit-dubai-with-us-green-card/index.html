Understanding Visa Basics: Who Decides Entry

Who controls entry to Dubai?

Immigration and visa policy for Dubai is set by UAE federal authorities and implemented at points of entry, including Dubai International Airport. Airlines and consulates act as intermediaries; they will check that you have the correct visa or entry permit before boarding. The passport you hold is the primary determinant of entry rules. Residency documents â such as a U.S. green card, U.S. visa, or EU residence permit â can change the options available to you, but they do not automatically grant visa-free travel.

Difference between U.S. green card holders and U.S. citizens

A common confusion arises because both groups live in the U.S. permanently. A U.S. citizen travels under a U.S. passport and benefits from visa-free entry to the UAE. A U.S. lawful permanent resident (green card holder) may still hold any other passport, and entry rules depend on that passport. The green card is a residency document, not a substitute for visa-free status.

Categories of entry options

There are several practical entry routes to Dubai that travelers commonly use:

  • Visa on arrival: Available for holders of certain passports and, for some nationalities, available if you also hold a valid U.S. visa or green card.
  • Pre-arranged eVisa or consular visa: Applied for online or via airline/consulate before travel.
  • Sponsor visa: Arranged by a UAE resident, company, or hotel on your behalf.
  • Transit permits: Short-duration gates for passengers stopping over in Dubai for a short period.

How a U.S. Green Card Affects Different Nationalities

When a green card helps

For several nationalities, holding a valid U.S. green card or U.S. visa creates a special pathway. The UAE has specific arrangements that allow nationals of some countries to receive short visit permits on arrival if they present a valid U.S. visa or green card. For example, Indian passport holders with a valid U.S. visa or green card can now obtain a 14-day visa on arrival under certain conditions. Similar facilitation exists for a number of other nationalities, but the exact terms â length of stay, fees, and whether an extension is allowed â differ.

Practical rule: passport first, green card second

Always start by checking the UAE entry rules for your passport nationality. Only after determining whether your passport is eligible for immediate entry or visa facilitation should you layer in the potential benefits of your U.S. green card.

Step-By-Step: Confirming Your Eligibility and Applying

How to Apply: Routes and Where to Go

Visa on Arrival

Visa on arrival is the easiest route when available. Citizens of many countries receive a 30- or 90-day visa on arrival using their passport alone. If your nationality is not on the visa-on-arrival list but the UAE has a "visa on arrival with U.S. visa/green card" rule for your passport, the process will be similar: present your passport and U.S. green card at immigration and receive the stamp or entry permit.

Key operational points:

  • Passport validity: Most authorities require passport validity of at least six months.
  • Valid residency proof: Carry your physical U.S. green card (not a photocopy) or a valid, unexpired U.S. visa subject to the specific nationality rules.
  • Fees: Many visa-on-arrival options are free or low-cost, but check the current fee schedule.
  • Extensions: Some short-stay visa-ons can be extended once; others cannot. Confirm on arrival.

Pre-Arranged eVisa or Consular Visa

If you must pre-arrange a visa, you have several choices. Emirates and other carriers allow visa processing if your flights are booked with them. Alternatively, you can apply through VFS Global or directly through UAE immigration portals. For tourist visas, youâll select single-entry or multiple-entry and a 30- or 60-day validity depending on your needs.

Common application requirements include:

  • Digital passport copy and passport photo
  • Proof of return/onward travel
  • Confirmed hotel booking
  • Payment of visa fee
  • Occasionally, proof of financial means

Visa By Sponsor

If you have family, friends, a host, or an employer in the UAE, they can sponsor your visit. Sponsors usually submit your passport details and supporting documentation to a UAE immigration office, and once approved you will be sent an entry permit. This route is common for non-visa-exempt nationalities.

Transit Permits

Transit permits (48- or 96-hour) are available for passengers passing through Dubai who need to leave the airport briefly or have a long stopover. Airlines often assist with these if you have the relevant flights booked.

What To Expect At Immigration In Dubai

Arrival screening procedure

Dubai routinely carries out random visual and document checks. When you approach immigration:

  • Present your passport and U.S. green card if applicable.
  • Expect a short interview: the officer may ask the purpose of your visit, where you will stay, and the length of your trip.
  • At times, additional screening or verification may be required; keep printed confirmation of your visa or sponsor approval to hand.

Secondary inspection

If you are flagged for secondary inspection, remain calm. Secondary checks are administrative and typically resolve quickly once you present the supporting documents described above. If thereâs a problem, airlines and consulates can often help resolve pending authorizations.

Overstays and fines

Overstaying your permitted time accrues daily fines under UAE law. If your plans change, request an extension before your exit date at the immigration office; not all visas are extendable.

Common Problems and How To Solve Them

Problem: Airline refuses boarding because of missing pre-approval

Airlines are responsible for verifying visa status before permitting boarding. If you are denied boarding, ask the airline to explain which document they require (eVisa, sponsor confirmation, or visa-on-arrival waiver). If you have a sponsor, request a faxed or emailed copy of the visa prior to departure. Keep contact numbers for your hotel, sponsor, and consulate accessible.

Problem: Your green card is expired or near expiry

A green card must be valid for the travel path that requires it. If it is expired or near expiry, apply for renewal sooner rather than later; many immigration checks require validity of at least six months. If renewal is not possible before travel, use a different legal residency proof where applicable (e.g., valid U.S. visa) and contact the UAE embassy to confirm acceptance.

Problem: Difference between eVisa and visa on arrival entitlements

If you qualify for both, eVisa gives you certainty before travel. Visa on arrival is convenient but can be denied at the discretion of the border officer. If your schedule is fixed and you want certainty, obtain a pre-arranged visa.

Problem: Overstay or lost passport

If you overstay, report to immigration and resolve fines before exit. For lost passports, contact your consulate immediately and obtain emergency travel documents; sponsors and airlines typically require consular confirmation before travel.

Troubleshooting Real-Life Scenarios: Examples and Solutions

Scenario: You hold a non-exempt passport, and the airline says you need a visa

Solution: Ask the airline to specify which visa type and route they require. If you qualify for a sponsor application, request the sponsor to send the entry permit email or fax before departure. If you prefer certainty and have time, apply for an eVisa.

Scenario: Your green card was issued recently and is stamped pending administrative updates

Solution: Immigration can be strict about validity. If there is any ambiguity in your green card status, obtain official documentation from USCIS or your attorney proving validity, or travel under a different valid visa if possible.

Scenario: You want to extend your stay beyond the visa-on-arrival limit

Solution: Check immediately with UAE immigration counters for extension eligibility. Some visas can be extended once for a fee; others cannot. If an extension is not permitted, consider departing and re-entering with a pre-arranged multiple-entry visa as long as rules permit.
