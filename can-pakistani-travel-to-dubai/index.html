Can Pakistani Travel to Dubai

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Dubai remains one of the region’s busiest hubs for Pakistani travellers—whether for business, family visits, medical care, or a holiday. With evolving visa rules, frequent airline visa services, and a new diplomatic agreement between the UAE and Pakistan that affects only official and diplomatic passports, it’s understandable that many prospective travellers ask the straightforward question: can Pakistani travel to Dubai?

Short answer: Yes. Pakistani citizens can travel to Dubai, but ordinary passport holders must have a valid UAE visa or entry permit before boarding. Diplomatic and official passport holders benefit from a recent exemption agreement, but this does not apply to standard travel documents. Ordinary travellers should arrange a visit, transit, or tourist visa through an approved pathway (airline, sponsor, travel agency, or the UAE embassy) and meet standard entry requirements such as passport validity and supporting documents.

This article explains exactly how Pakistani travellers can get to Dubai, step by step. You’ll find clear explanations of visa types and routes, a practical application checklist you can follow tonight, guidance for arrival and entry, the common mistakes that delay or deny travel, and the best planning frameworks used by Saudi Travel & Leisure to make international travel predictable and stress-free. Whether you’re booking flight-and-hotel together or seeking to use an airline visa service, this is the operational blueprint for a smooth trip to Dubai.

Why the Rules Matter Now

Recent policy changes and what they mean

Diplomatic activity between the UAE and Pakistan has increased recently, resulting in an agreement that removes visa requirements for holders of diplomatic and official passports. That is an important development for state travelers and government delegations, but it does not change the position for ordinary Pakistani passport holders. For most travellers the practical reality remains: you must secure a visa or entry permit before you travel to Dubai unless you fall into a specific visa-on-arrival category (which Pakistanis generally do not).

The UAE’s visa architecture has also evolved to offer multiple fast-tracked entry options through airlines, online platforms, and sponsors within the Emirates. Major carriers like Emirates and Etihad operate integrated visa services for passengers who meet their conditions. On the ground, Dubai Airports’ immigration processes include random visual screening and may require printed visa documents at the counter, so planning ahead saves time and reduces risk of denial.

The most common misconceptions

Many travellers assume that because the UAE is a major international destination with liberal policies for some nationalities, the same applies to all passports. Others believe a hotel booking is always sufficient—when in many cases the visa application will ask for additional evidence of funds, sponsor details, or return tickets. Another persistent myth is that a GCC residence makes visa processes identical; only certain GCC residency rules apply, and eligibility varies by nationality and type of residence permit.

Understanding the exact entry conditions for Pakistani citizens and the practical routes to obtain a visa prevents last-minute surprises at the airport and avoids expensive rebooking or visa refusals.

Who Needs a Visa and Who Doesn’t

Diplomatic and official passports

Under the latest UAE–Pakistan agreement, holders of diplomatic and official passports can travel between the two countries without a prearranged visa for official business. This exemption is targeted and administrative—designed to facilitate official delegations. If you hold an official or diplomatic passport, confirm the practical implementation with the issuing authority before travel, because operational details and documentation expectations are often handled through ministries and embassies.

Ordinary passport holders

Ordinary Pakistani passport holders must obtain an appropriate visa or entry permit before arrival. There is no general visa-on-arrival for Pakistani nationals at Dubai’s airports. The most commonly used options for ordinary travellers are:

  • Prearranged tourist or visit visas (single-entry or multiple-entry, varying durations)
  • Transit visas (48-hour or 96-hour) if you have a connecting Emirates/Etihad ticket and a qualifying layover
  • Visas arranged by a UAE-based sponsor (family, employer, or hotel)
  • Airline-assisted visas for passengers booked on eligible flights

GCC residents and special pathways

If you reside in a GCC country with a valid residence permit, some pathways may offer simplified entry to the UAE. That flexibility varies by the issuing GCC country and the traveller’s nationality and must be checked case-by-case. For Pakistani nationals the safest assumption is that residency in the Gulf may help only if you meet the specific programme conditions (for example, certain visa-on-arrival or electronic visa options tied to residency in particular countries).

Visa Types Explained: Which One Fits Your Trip?

Short-term tourism and visit visas

These are the most commonly used categories for Pakistani travellers visiting Dubai. They include single-entry 30-day visas, 60-day tourism visas, and similar short-term permits. Each has different fee structures and extension options. Typical requirements include a passport valid for at least six months, proof of accommodation, a return ticket, and sometimes evidence of funds.

Visas applied through major airlines often require an Emirates or Etihad ticket number or travel itinerary tied to the airline’s policy. When applying via embassy or consulate, you will submit hard copies of documents, and the processing time may be longer.

Transit visas (48-hour and 96-hour)

If you have a qualifying layover with Emirates or another eligible carrier, a 48-hour or 96-hour transit visa can be arranged. These are non-extendable and intended for travellers who plan a short stopover in Dubai while en route to another destination. They are useful if you want to leave the airport and explore the city for a day or two.

Multiple-entry tourist visas and longer stays

If you plan several visits within a set period—or if you expect to transit through the UAE multiple times—a multiple-entry 30- or 60-day tourist visa can be an efficient option. These visas are slightly more expensive but eliminate repeated application efforts.

Sponsored visas (family or company)

A UAE resident or a company can sponsor a visitor’s visa. For family visits, the sponsor typically provides relationship proof and contact details. Corporate visas for business visitors often carry different documentation requirements and may need a company letter and an invitation from a UAE-based entity. Sponsored applications are processed by immigration authorities and usually require the sponsor to be a registered UAE resident or entity.

Special categories: medical, student, work, and residence visas

These are handled through the appropriate UAE authorities (hospitals, universities, and ministries) and are not issued by airlines or simple travel agencies. If your reason for travel is medical treatment, study, or employment, follow the specific application route for those categories, which usually involves additional approvals and document attestation.

Where and How to Apply

Through the UAE Embassy or Consulate in Pakistan

Applying through the UAE Embassy in Islamabad or the Consulate General in Karachi is the traditional route. This process typically requires:

  • A typed and completed visa application form
  • Color passport photographs with a white background
  • A copy of your passport (machine-readable and valid for at least six months)
  • Proof of accommodation and return flight
  • Sponsor particulars if required
  • Any additional documents requested by the embassy (e.g., notarized affidavits for minors travelling alone)

Embassy applications are usually processed within a standard time frame (commonly up to 14 days for visa approvals), but you should submit your application well in advance—embassies often request at least two weeks prior to travel. If you are submitting a work, residence, or specialized visa category, consult the embassy’s visa section for exact guidance.

Airline-assisted visas (Emirates, Etihad, others)

For many Pakistani travellers the fastest option is to use an airline’s visa service when you book your flight. Major carriers offer integrated visa application services through “Manage Your Booking” or at ticketing offices. These services typically require:

  • A valid passport with six months’ validity
  • Payment of the visa fee online or at the ticketing office
  • A confirmed ticket on the airline (some services require the entire itinerary to be on the same PNR)
  • Hotel booking or sponsor details

Airline visa services can be faster (some are processed within a few days) and convenient because the passenger does not need to submit originals to the embassy. Note that Emirates and similar carriers may require a minimum number of working days before arrival to process the application.

Visa through licensed travel agencies and sponsors

Licensed travel agencies in Pakistan or the UAE can submit visa applications on your behalf. Similarly, a UAE sponsor (hotel, family member, or company) can apply for your visa directly within the UAE. These methods are frequently used for family visit visas and tourism visas and are practical when you have a confirmed sponsor in the Emirates. Confirm the agency’s license and reputation before paying fees.

Online and e-visa options

The UAE has advanced e-visa systems for several nationalities. While Pakistani nationals are not generally eligible for a visa-on-arrival, they can often complete online applications through airline portals or through the embassy’s designated partners (VFS Global or specific carrier channels). Always use the official channels recommended by the carrier or embassy to avoid scams.

Required Documents and Practical Checklist

Below is a single, actionable checklist you can use to prepare a visa application. Follow these steps closely; skipping any item is a common cause of delays or rejection.

  1. Confirm your passport has at least six months’ validity from your date of arrival and is machine-readable.
  2. Decide which visa type fits your trip (tourist 30/60 days, transit 48/96 hours, sponsored visit) and ensure your sponsor or airline supports that category.
  3. Book a refundable or confirmed return ticket that matches your proposed travel dates.
  4. Reserve accommodation and obtain a booking confirmation for the visa application, or ensure your sponsor provides tenancy documentation if staying with family.
  5. Prepare a clean, recent passport photo with a white background and the required dimensions.
  6. Gather proof of funds if requested (bank statements) and any additional documents required by the embassy for Pakistani nationals, such as NOC or attestation for minors.
  7. If applying via airline, ensure your flight itinerary is on the same PNR and submit the visa request through Manage Your Booking or the airline’s visa portal.
  8. If applying through the embassy or a travel agency, submit the typed application form, passport copy, photos, sponsor details, and any attested certificates as required.
  9. Keep electronic and hard copies of your visa approval and be prepared to present a printed copy during immigration screening at Dubai airport if requested.
  10. Check visa validity dates, permitted stay duration, and extension options; do not overstay—overstay penalties are strictly enforced.

(Use this checklist as a template. Specific requirements can vary, so confirm with the embassy, airline, or sponsor before submitting.)

Common Application Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

Missing or invalid passport validity

A frequently encountered reason for refusal is insufficient passport validity. Confirm six months’ validity and that the passport is machine-readable; handwritten passports are not accepted.

Incorrect or incomplete sponsor details

When a sponsor applies on your behalf, ensure their contact information, ID, and relationship proof are precise and complete. Embassies are strict about sponsor particulars and will return incomplete applications.

Mismatched travel and hotel dates

Your flight dates and hotel reservation must align with the visa application. Inconsistencies can trigger questions and delays; fix them before submission.

Using unlicensed agents

Cut-rate or unofficial services can lead to forged documents or visa fraud. Always use licensed travel agents, official airline channels, or the embassy/consulate for visa processing.

Not preparing for additional checks on arrival

Dubai Immigration sometimes performs visual screening and may ask for printed visa documents or copies of your booking. Have a printed copy of your visa approval and supporting docs accessible in your carry-on.

Overreliance on verbal promises

Do not rely on verbal assurances from sponsors or agencies. Get confirmations in writing and keep receipts of visa fees and agency services.

Arrival Intelligence: What Happens at Dubai Immigration

Documents you will be asked for

On arrival, you should be ready to present:

  • Passport (with visa or entry permit)
  • Printed visa approval or confirmation (if requested)
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Proof of accommodation or sponsor details
  • Adequate funds or proof of ability to support your stay, if requested

Immigration officers may ask questions about the purpose of your visit, length of stay, and where you will be staying. Answer confidently, provide concise details, and present the documents requested.

Visual screening and document printing

Dubai Airports conduct random visual screening for arrivals. If selected, you will be asked to proceed to a screening counter and provide a printed copy of your visa approval or visa number confirmation. If you don’t have a printed copy there may be a nominal fee to obtain one at the airport, payable in AED.

Customs and prohibited items

Customs rules are strict. Declare cash amounts above the permitted limit and avoid carrying prohibited medicines or narcotics without proper prescriptions and documentation. Personal medications that contain controlled substances must be accompanied by a medical prescription and possibly a prior approval.

Cultural etiquette on arrival

The UAE is an international society but also a Muslim country. Dress neatly and behave respectfully in public spaces. During Ramadan, avoid eating, drinking, or smoking in public during daylight hours. Greeting with "As-salamu alaykum" is common and appreciated.

Costs and Processing Times

Typical fee ranges

Visa fees vary by type and route—airline-assisted visas can have different service fees from embassy visas. As a ballpark, expect to pay anywhere from a modest fee for short transit approvals to higher amounts (sometimes over USD 100–200) for longer or multiple-entry tourism visas processed through commercial channels. Embassy rates are often published and may include non-refundable processing fees.

Processing timeframes

Processing commonly takes between 3–14 working days depending on the application method and the visa category. Airline visas can be faster if you meet all requirements and submit the request early. Embassy processing schedules can be longer during peak travel seasons.

Overstay penalties and fines

Do not overstay. Overstay fines accrue daily and can result in detention, deportation, and travel bans. If your plans change, extend the visa through the official channels before it expires, or prepare to depart and re-enter on a valid new permit.

Between Pakistan and the UAE: The Diplomatic Context

What the exemption for diplomatic and official passports changes

The bilateral memorandum that exempts diplomatic and official passport holders from pre-arrival visas eliminates a hurdle for official delegations and state travel. It streamlines intergovernmental meetings and short official visits. Ordinary travellers should not expect this policy to apply to their passports.

Practical implications for ordinary travellers

The policy’s publicity can create confusion. When planning travel as a private citizen, treat the visa requirements as unchanged: secure an appropriate visa before travel and ensure your documents are in order. If you are an official traveller, coordinate closely with your ministry or embassy to ensure that the exemption is recognized and recorded in the travel documentation.

Planning a Smooth Trip: A Day-by-Day Travel Framework

Before you book

Decide travel dates and identify the visa type that fits. If you plan a short holiday, compare the cost-benefit of an airline visa versus embassy processing. If you have a UAE-based sponsor, confirm their willingness and capacity to apply on your behalf. Bookmark authoritative resources and subscribe to travel updates; sudden policy changes do occur.

Booking your travel and visa at the same time

If you opt for an airline visa service, secure your tickets first. For embassy applications, prepare the hard-copy documents and submit well before your travel date. Keep electronic copies of every confirmation and approval.

Final preparation: 48–72 hours before departure

Print your visa approval and travel documents. Keep a set in your hand luggage. Double-check your passport validity and the names on your ticket and passport match exactly. Notify family or your sponsor of your arrival time and keep local contact numbers handy.

On arrival and first 24 hours

Expect standard immigration questions and random visual screening. Arrange official airport transport or a prebooked hotel transfer to avoid confusion. Once settled, register with your sponsor or the consulate if your stay is prolonged.

When Things Go Wrong: Contingency Planning

Visa rejection or delay

If a visa is rejected, identify the explicit reason and consult your sponsor or the issuing agency to determine if re-application with corrected documents is possible. Avoid reapplying without addressing the cause of rejection—this wastes time and money.

Lost passport abroad

Report a lost passport immediately to local police and your nearest Pakistani consulate or embassy. Start the emergency travel document process as soon as possible and avoid travel until you have valid identity documentation.

Health or medical emergencies

Ensure you have adequate travel insurance for medical evacuation and treatment. Some visas for medical purposes require pre-approval from a UAE hospital; if you travel for medical care, confirm that paperwork is complete before departure.

Practical Examples of Travel Scenarios (and the Best Visa Route)

Scenario A: Short business visit (48–72 hours)

If your trip to Dubai is a short business meeting and you have a single Emirates or other qualifying airline booking, a short-term transit or short tourist visa arranged through the airline is usually the fastest route. Airline services are convenient when your itinerary is on one PNR and you can meet the document requirements quickly.

Scenario B: Family visit or extended stay (30–60 days)

For family visits, a sponsored visa applied by your UAE-based relative offers the clearest path. Alternatively, a standard tourist visa submitted through a licensed travel agency or embassy application works if you prefer controlling the application yourself.

Scenario C: Multi-city Gulf trip including Saudi Arabia

Safety, Health, and Local Laws You Must Know

Dubai and the UAE are generally safe for international visitors, but there are strict laws and social expectations you should respect. Avoid public displays that might be considered indecent, comply with local drug and alcohol rules, and respect photography restrictions around people, government facilities, and sensitive infrastructure. Always carry identification when in public and follow traffic and public safety rules.

Health preparedness includes verifying that your routine vaccinations are up to date and checking any travel health notices before you leave. Secure travel insurance that covers medical evacuation, as healthcare costs can be high for uninsured foreign visitors.

FAQ

1. Can Pakistani travellers get a visa on arrival in Dubai?

No. Pakistani citizens generally must obtain a visa before arrival. Diplomatic and official passport holders have a separate exemption, but ordinary passport holders should apply through an embassy, licensed travel agency, a UAE sponsor, or an airline visa service.

2. How long does it take to process a UAE tourist visa for Pakistani nationals?

Processing times vary by application method. Airline-assisted visas can sometimes be issued within a few working days if conditions are met; embassy submissions may take up to 14 working days. Always apply well in advance of travel.

3. What documents are most commonly requested for a Dubai visa from Pakistan?

Common requirements include a passport valid for six months, passport-sized photographs, a confirmed return ticket, proof of accommodation, sponsor details if applicable, and occasionally proof of funds or relationship documents for family visits.

4. What should I do if my visa is rejected or delayed?

First, request the specific reason for the rejection. If it’s a documentation issue, correct and resubmit through the same official channel (embassy, airline, or sponsor). Avoid using unlicensed agents. If the issue is unclear, consult the issuing authority or a licensed travel agency to understand next steps.
