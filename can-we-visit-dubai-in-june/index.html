Managing the Heat: Safety, Health, and Practical Tips

Understanding Heat Risks

Heat exhaustion and heatstroke are real risks in Dubai during June. Dehydration, rapid fatigue, and dizziness can undermine your trip. High humidity increases the risk because sweating becomes less effective for cooling.

Day-to-Day Heat Management

Plan your days to minimize prolonged midday sun exposure. Prioritize:

  • Early-morning outdoor experiences (sunrise at the Burj Khalifa or Al Qudra Desert in early hours).
  • Indoor activities between 11:00â17:00 (museums, malls, indoor parks).
  • Evening desert safaris or waterfront promenades after sunset.

Hydration is paramount: carry a refillable water bottle, avoid alcohol in extreme heat during the day, and choose electrolyte-rich refreshments when needed. Sunscreen with high SPF and broad-brimmed hats remain useful, especially for short outdoor excursions.

Heat-Related First Aid Basics

Recognize early signs of heat illness: heavy sweating, weakness, headache, nausea, or fainting. Move to shade or an air-conditioned space immediately, elevate the feet, loosen clothing, and sip water slowly. If symptoms progress to confusion, loss of consciousness, or collapse, seek emergency medical care right away.
