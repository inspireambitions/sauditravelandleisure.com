The Legal Landscape: History And Recent Changes

A short legal history

Saudi Arabia has long maintained strict rules around alcohol based on religious and legal traditions. Public sale and consumption were banned in the mid-20th century and the prohibition has shaped social life and enforcement ever since. That long-standing framework began to shift in measured ways as the Kingdom pursued its Vision 2030 goals to build a global tourism and hospitality sector. These changes are deliberate and incremental: policymakers are balancing the desire to attract international visitors and investors against cultural norms and local sensitivities.

Diplomatic access and the Riyadh off-licence

One of the earliest visible manifestations of policy change was the opening of a licensed shop within the Diplomatic Quarter in Riyadh. This outlet operates under strict controls and is intended primarily for accredited diplomatic staff. Access is limited by registration, documentation checks, monthly quotas, and rules about attire and authorization. The establishment of a diplomatic shop was explicitly framed as a move to exert greater regulatory oversight over alcohol that was already entering the Kingdom through diplomatic channels, and to curb illicit markets.

The phased licensing plan for controlled venues

In recent policy announcements, the government outlined a controlled licensing approach that would permit the sale and consumption of certain alcoholic beverages in a set of designated environments. The important features of that plan are consistent across public statements: licenses will be issued to a limited set of venues (for example, high-end hotels, licensed resorts, some international tourist hubs, residential compounds for certain expatriates, and authorized embassies); permitted beverages will be limited to low- to moderate-strength categories (beer, wine, cider â with spirits and high-alcohol products restricted); and operations will be tightly regulated to prevent public consumption and social disturbance.

Authorities have been explicit that these changes are not a blanket liberalization. Instead, the model mirrors successful, tightly regulated approaches used by several neighboring countries: alcohol availability will be a curated, hospitality-focused feature rather than an open retail market.

What remains prohibited and the penalties

Even as regulations evolve, several core prohibitions remain clear. Sale, possession, or public consumption by those without authorization is illegal. Bringing alcohol into public spaces, attempting to disguise consumption, or distributing alcohol in unlicensed settings can lead to arrest, fines, detention, deportation for non-citizens, and other legal penalties. Travelers who assume rules are the same as in other Gulf countries do so at great risk; enforcement in Saudi Arabia remains strict where the law applies.

What This Means For Travelers in Riyadh

Short-term reality versus medium-term change

For most visitors arriving in Riyadh today, the practical reality is straightforward: do not expect to encounter open bars or retail liquor stores in the city center as you would in many other international destinations. Instead, plan for specific, licensed venues to be the only lawful locations where alcohol may be available to authorized individuals. Over the medium term, you can expect more hotels and select resorts to apply for licenses under the controlled framework, but rollout will be phased and venues will follow strict operational rules.

Who can legally buy alcohol in Riyadh today?

Current access primarily covers the following groups and contexts:

  • Accredited diplomatic staff with permission to use diplomatic channels.
  • In the phased license model, select licensed hotels, luxury resorts, and designated tourist hubs where the venue itself holds a license and follows all operational rules.
  • Certain residential compounds or expatriate housing that meet license criteria under regulatory frameworks (dependent on approvals and compound policies).

Tourists who are not diplomats or residents of licensed compounds should assume they cannot legally purchase alcohol in Riyadh unless they confirm in advance that a specific licensed venue is offering it in line with the regulations.

Rules inside hotels, resorts, and licensed venues

Where alcohol is permitted inside licensed hotels or resorts, expect numerous safeguards: segregated service areas, staff training, strict ID checks, and rules preventing the removal of alcohol from licensed premises. Consumption will be permitted only in designated private spaces; drinking in hotel lobbies, corridors, or private vehicles is likely prohibited. Service staff will follow protocols designed to respect local cultural norms, including limited hours of service and discreet presentation.
