Can You Get Alcohol in Riyadh?

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Introduction

Saudi Arabia’s tourism footprint has expanded rapidly in recent years, transforming Riyadh from a business-focused capital into a destination that welcomes a growing number of international visitors. That shift raises practical questions many travelers ask on arrival — chief among them: can you get alcohol in Riyadh?

Short answer: Yes — but only under strictly controlled, limited conditions. Alcohol is not freely available to the public in Riyadh; access has historically been restricted to diplomatic channels and, more recently, the government has begun rolling out tightly regulated licenses that allow sales and service in designated venues and circumstances. For most visitors, public purchase and open consumption remain prohibited and subject to enforcement.

This article explains what the current legal picture looks like, how recent policy changes affect visitors and residents, and what practical steps you should take when planning a trip to Riyadh. I’ll provide clear rules, step-by-step guidance for eligible people, alternatives for those who cannot access alcohol legally, and the cultural framework you need to travel with confidence and respect. As the lead voice for Saudi Travel & Leisure, my goal is to give you the blueprint for planning an informed, smooth stay in Riyadh that balances curiosity with local norms.

The Legal Landscape: History, Recent Changes, and What They Mean

A Brief Legal Background

Saudi law has historically prohibited the sale, possession, and public consumption of alcohol, rooted in the Kingdom’s interpretation of Islamic law and formalized by a nationwide ban in the mid-20th century. For decades the result was a strict, consistent enforcement posture: unauthorized importation, possession, or drinking could bring severe penalties including fines, detention, deportation for foreigners, and other legal consequences.

Diplomatic Exceptions and the 2024 Diplomatic Quarter Shop

Diplomatic personnel have long had unique arrangements that allow alcohol to enter the Kingdom under diplomatic privileges. In 2024 Riyadh opened an off-licence in the Diplomatic Quarter, available exclusively to non-Muslim diplomats under strict controls: registration, age limits, monthly quotas, and rules about transport and consumption. This step was framed as a way to curb illicit markets and formalize what had been a shadow supply chain for international missions.

That diplomatic shop does not equate to a broader public retail system, but it established an important legal precedent: the state can and will create narrowly defined exceptions if they are carefully regulated.

The 2025–2026 Policy Shift: Controlled Licensing

In 2025 Saudi authorities announced a plan to license alcohol sales in a limited number of venues across the Kingdom, with a rollout planned from 2026. The model being deployed is explicit about the boundaries:

  • Alcohol sales will be allowed only in tightly controlled, licensed venues, such as five-star hotels, luxury resorts, certain tourist hubs, and approved residential compounds for expatriates.
  • The permitted beverages will be restricted to beer, wine, and cider; spirits and drinks exceeding a specified alcohol content are excluded.
  • Public spaces, retail stores, private homes (for public consumption), and unlicensed distribution will remain off-limits.
  • Service and sales will be accompanied by strict licensing, staff training, monitoring, and penalties for violations.

This measured approach aims to open hospitality options for international tourists while preserving social norms and avoiding public intoxication and social tension.

What Remains Prohibited

Even with controlled licenses, certain activities remain illegal for the general public:

  • Public drinking or intoxication in open spaces.
  • Unlicensed sale, importation, or distribution.
  • Bringing in alcohol through unofficial channels.
  • Consumption by anyone under applicable age limits or in areas where service is explicitly banned (for example, certain religious or public venues).

Penalties can include fines, detention, deportation for non-citizens, and other administrative measures.

How These Rules Play Out Specifically in Riyadh

The Practical Picture for Travelers

Riyadh’s status as the political and administrative capital means changes are rolled out carefully and are often pilot-tested in restricted venues before wider adoption. For travelers in Riyadh today, the practical situation looks like this:

  • Non-Muslim diplomats and authorized diplomatic staff may purchase alcohol through diplomatic channels in the Diplomatic Quarter under pre-set quotas.
  • From 2026 onwards, certain five-star hotels, international resorts, and specially licensed event spaces in Riyadh are expected to be allowed to sell and serve certain alcoholic beverages. Availability will vary by property and event; hotels will apply for licenses and will have to adopt stringent controls.
  • For tourists without diplomatic status, the default assumption should be that you cannot walk into a shop or restaurant and casually buy alcohol. If you encounter alcohol availability, verify the license and rules before purchasing or consuming.
  • Enforcement is real and effective. Authorities will act on breaches, and the consequences for unauthorized consumption can be severe.

Authorized Sites: What to Expect

Licensed venues will operate under strong oversight. Typical features to expect when a venue is licensed:

  • Age verification and ID checks at the point of sale or service.
  • Designated, enclosed service areas where alcohol may be consumed only by authorized guests.
  • Limits on the types and strengths of alcoholic beverages served.
  • Staff training and record-keeping requirements.
  • Clear signage and compartmentalization to prevent service where prohibited.

If you are staying at an international hotel or resort, check ahead with the property’s reservations or concierge desk about whether they hold a license and what conditions apply.

Enforcement Realities

Enforcement in Riyadh balances visible regulatory control with community standards. Officials prioritize preventing public intoxication and unregulated supply chains. Travelers should assume that:

  • Carrying alcohol in public spaces is risky unless you can show legitimate proof that the alcohol was purchased and is being transported under approved conditions.
  • Drinking in private rooms within licensed hotels may be permitted by the property rules, but discretion is expected.
  • If you encounter law enforcement, be cooperative and present identification; public arguments or defiance can escalate outcomes.

Who Can Legally Buy and Consume Alcohol in Riyadh?

Eligible Groups

The categories that have had or will have lawful access, under regulation, include:

  • Non-Muslim diplomats and diplomatic staff operating under embassy privileges, currently able to access Diplomatic Quarter provisions.
  • Residents and guests of licensed five-star hotels, certain luxury resorts, and designated tourist hubs once the licensing regime is fully in force.
  • Occupants of licensed expatriate residential compounds, where permitted by compound management and subject to national restrictions.
  • Authorized staff and patrons at specific international cultural or business events that receive a temporary license.

For most short-term tourists, eligibility will be tied to whether the accommodation or event holds an active license and complies with the Kingdom’s rules.

How Licensing Affects Different Visitor Types

Business travelers who are guests of licensed hotels should confirm the hotel’s policies in advance and follow property guidelines. Long-term expatriates may have access through their compounds or employer arrangements, provided the compound or employer obtains authorization. Short-stay tourists should plan with the expectation that ease of access will be lower than in neighboring Gulf cities unless they are staying at explicitly licensed venues.

Step-by-Step: How Eligible Individuals Can Obtain Alcohol (List 1)

  1. Confirm eligibility: Verify you are covered either by diplomatic status, residency in a licensed compound, or by staying at a licensed hotel or event.
  2. Check venue licensing: Contact the hotel, resort, or event organizer to confirm a valid license, service hours, and the types of beverages permitted.
  3. Carry proper ID: Always have your passport and any diplomatic credentials or residency permits available for verification.
  4. Follow purchase rules: Adhere to quotas, limits, and record-keeping the venue requires; don’t attempt to leave licensed premises with alcohol if it’s prohibited.
  5. Consume discreetly and responsibly: Observe service rules, avoid public intoxication, and respect staff directions.

This procedure is deliberately stringent; it’s designed to protect both the traveler and the host in an environment where cultural sensitivity is paramount.

If You’re Not Eligible: Practical Alternatives and Travel Workarounds

Non-Alcoholic Options and Enjoying Riyadh Without Drinking

Riyadh’s dining and café scene has evolved with a sophisticated range of non-alcoholic offerings. Many high-end bars and lounges now serve premium mocktails, craft non-alcoholic beers, and zero-proof wines that replicate the social experience without breaking local laws. Exploring Riyadh’s modern culinary scene — from award-winning restaurants to contemporary coffee houses — can be a full, satisfying experience without alcohol.

Duty-Free and Transit Considerations

Bringing alcohol into Saudi Arabia without authorization is illegal. If you plan to visit neighboring countries with more permissive rules and then return to Riyadh, be mindful that transporting alcohol across Saudi borders is prohibited. Many travelers choose to enjoy beverages in transit hubs or neighboring countries and then return to Riyadh without bringing bottles back.

If you’re planning side trips, consider short trips to Bahrain or the UAE, where regulations for tourists are more permissive. For background on how neighboring countries manage alcohol differently, read about how neighboring countries regulate alcohol.

Social Hosting and Private Gatherings

Even when staying in a private residence, the legal environment remains restrictive. Hosting guests and serving alcohol in private homes can be illegal and carries risk. If you live in an approved compound where management allows licensed service, follow those rules strictly. Otherwise, choose non-alcoholic hosting options that are culturally respectful and comfortable for all guests.

Etiquette, Respect, and Safety: How to Behave in Social Situations

Cultural Sensitivities and Best Practices

Respect for local customs is the single most important rule when visiting Riyadh. Even where alcohol becomes available in licensed contexts, public modesty and conservative behavior are expected. Some key principles:

  • Be discrete: Avoid loud or boisterous behavior and public displays of intoxication.
  • Respect religious spaces: Never attempt to bring or consume alcohol near mosques, religious sites, or during prayer times.
  • Observe dress codes and local norms when entering licensed venues; some venues will have explicit rules on attire.
  • Don’t pressure locals to partake; many Saudi citizens abstain for religious or personal reasons.

Acting with restraint and cultural awareness minimizes friction and keeps your visit smooth.

Legal and Health Safety

Driving under the influence is strictly illegal and can have grave consequences. Use hotel transport, ride-hailing services, or chauffeurs if you are consuming within licensed venues and need to move around. If a medical emergency occurs, seek immediate help and be transparent with emergency personnel — medical staff will prioritize care.

Dealing with Law Enforcement

If confronted by police, remain calm and cooperative. Present identification when requested. Avoid confrontational language or actions. If you are a foreign national and face legal complications, contact your embassy or consulate promptly for consular assistance.

Long-Term Residents and Expat Life: Practicalities and Rights

Living in Compounds and Corporate Arrangements

Expats living long-term in Riyadh typically arrange accommodation through employers or private landlords. Many expatriate compounds will pursue approvals that allow residents controlled access to alcohol under defined conditions. If you plan to move to Riyadh, confirm with your employer or the compound management about their policies and whether they hold or intend to apply for relevant licenses.

Importing Alcohol for Personal Use

Importing alcohol into Saudi Arabia without authorization is illegal. Even if permitted via limited channels in the future, strict quotas and documentation will apply. For expatriate families and professionals, companies sometimes provide guidance or arrange licensed procurement where legally possible; always ensure any imports comply with the law and company policy.

Hosting International Guests

If you host international guests, consider the legal and social context. Hosting a gathering that includes alcohol in an unlicensed setting can expose both hosts and guests to legal risk. Opt for licensed venues to entertain or choose creative, alcohol-free hospitality alternatives that make guests feel welcome while respecting local rules.

Practical Travel Planning: A Saudi Travel & Leisure Framework

Step 1 — Research and Confirm Licenses in Advance

Before booking, ask hotels and event organizers if they hold an active license and what restrictions apply. Save written confirmations (email) of their license status or policy about on-site service and transportation rules.

Step 2 — Choose Accommodations Strategically

If access to licensed service is important to you, prioritize internationally branded five-star hotels or resort projects that are explicitly marketing licensed hospitality packages. For broader cultural experiences, choose a mix of properties and plan alternative evening options that don’t rely on alcohol.

Step 3 — Respect Local Laws and Company Policies

If you’re traveling for work, coordinate with your employer about company policies and support. If you require access due to diplomatic status, ensure your credentials are valid and that you understand purchasing quotas and transport restrictions.

Step 4 — Prepare for Alternatives

Familiarize yourself with local non-alcoholic alternatives and evening activities. Riyadh’s cultural calendar, restaurants, and entertainment venues offer many fulfilling options that don’t hinge on alcohol.

What to Expect in the Near Future

Controlled Expansion, Not Full Liberalization

The government’s approach is incremental. Expect careful expansion of licensed venues rather than sudden, sweeping normalizations. New licenses will emphasize training, monitoring, and compliance. Large-scale events and international sporting tournaments will have distinct rules: for example, Saudi authorities have indicated that major events such as the 2034 World Cup will not allow alcohol sales, even if some licensed hotels have permission in other contexts. Always verify event-specific rules before planning.

How the Tourism Strategy Fits

These changes are part of a broader economic and tourism strategy aimed at diversifying the economy and attracting visitors under Vision 2030. Hospitality infrastructure upgrades, international hotel partnerships, and pilot hospitality models in tourist hubs will continue shaping how alcohol policy is operationalized. For visitors, this means gradual increases in choice within defined limits, paired with a continued expectation of cultural respect.

Common Mistakes Travelers Make (And How To Avoid Them)

Many travelers assume rule parity with nearby Gulf cities and are surprised when access is restricted in Riyadh. Common errors include attempting to import bottles via luggage, consuming in public places, or failing to verify a venue’s licensing status. The best practice is to plan around the local context: confirm licenses, keep purchases only within licensed premises, and choose alternatives when access is not available.

Summary: Key Takeaways

Riyadh is navigating a new regulatory era around alcohol: carefully controlled access is emerging, but public availability remains restricted and enforcement is strict. Eligible groups include diplomats and patrons of licensed hotels and resorts; most travelers should expect limited direct access. Respect, discretion, and planning are essential. Use legal channels where they exist, favor licensed venues, and adopt alternatives where necessary.

Frequently Asked Questions

1) Can I buy alcohol in Riyadh duty-free at the airport?

No. Duty-free purchases of alcohol that are intended to be carried into Saudi Arabia are illegal unless you are moving through designated diplomatic or specially authorized channels. Don’t assume airport duty-free options allow import into the Kingdom.

2) If I stay at a five-star hotel, will I be able to order a drink?

Possibly, but only if the hotel holds a valid license for on-site sale or service. Confirm with the hotel before arrival and follow their documented rules on purchase, consumption, and transportation.

3) Are there alternatives for social drinking?

Yes. Riyadh’s high-end bars, lounges, and restaurants offer sophisticated non-alcoholic cocktails, zero-proof wines, and premium mocktails. These provide a social experience without legal risk.

4) What should I do if stopped by the police with alcohol?

Remain calm, present identification, and comply with requests. If you are a foreign national, ask for consular assistance as soon as is reasonable. Avoid confrontation and follow legal processes.
