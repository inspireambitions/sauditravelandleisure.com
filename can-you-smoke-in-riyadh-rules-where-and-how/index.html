Can You Smoke in Riyadh? Rules, Where and How

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Introduction

Saudi Arabia has seen a rapid rise in international visitors and expatriates over the past decade, and Riyadh sits at the center of that shift—an energetic capital where modern life and conservative traditions coexist. For travelers who smoke, a common and practical question arises: can you smoke in Riyadh? The answer affects how you plan flights, book hotels, enjoy cafés, and move through religious and cultural sites.

Short answer: Smoking in Riyadh is tightly regulated. Public smoking is widely restricted by national law and local regulations, with emphatic bans in airports, government buildings, hospitals, educational institutions, religious sites, and many tourist facilities. Designated smoking areas exist in some private venues and outdoors, but the safest approach for visitors is to understand the rules, respect signage, and plan where and when to smoke to avoid fines and social friction.

What the Law Says: National and Local Framework

National Tobacco Control and Relevant Regulations

Saudi Arabia has enacted a sequence of laws and executive regulations that create a strict national baseline for tobacco control. These rules cover where smoking is prohibited, packaging and labeling requirements, sale restrictions (including minimum age limits), and provisions related to electronic nicotine delivery systems. Many restrictions are broad and specific: smoking is forbidden inside airports, government buildings, hospitals, schools, and public transport facilities. There are also limitations on the sale and marketing of tobacco products, including plain packaging and large health warnings on packets.

The national framework is the place to start when planning a trip: it sets the legal ceiling for restrictions you’ll encounter across the Kingdom, including Riyadh, and determines the penalties for breaches. How Riyadh Applies and Enforces National Rules

Riyadh follows national legislation but also has local regulations and administrative practices that shape enforcement and permitted spaces. Local authorities and municipal bodies enforce bans more actively in some neighborhoods—especially around government districts, cultural centres, and popular tourist attractions. In practice, Riyadh’s approach combines signposted no-smoking zones with designated smoking areas in some malls, hotels, and private venues.

Where Smoking Is Prohibited in Riyadh — Clear No-Go Zones

Public Buildings and Government Spaces

Smoking inside government offices and most municipal buildings is not permitted. The ban includes both public and many private governmental facilities and extends to immediate outdoor perimeters in sensitive areas. If you’re visiting ministries, municipal offices, or any official service centre, assume smoking is prohibited unless a clearly marked outdoor smoking area is present.

Airports, Public Transport and Associated Facilities

Airports in the Kingdom have strict no-smoking policies in most terminals and public concourses. Designated smoking rooms can exist in certain airports, but signage will clearly indicate these. Similarly, public transport hubs—bus stations and any associated waiting halls—are smoke-free. If you’re flying in or moving across the city by coach or taxi, smoking in terminals, buses or stations is not allowed and can lead to fines.

Religious, Educational and Health Institutions

Smoking is categorically prohibited in and around places of worship and educational institutions. This includes the precincts around mosques and university campuses. Hospitals and healthcare facilities have both indoor and outdoor bans. Because Riyadh is a conservative city in religious terms, the expectation of no smoking around mosques is strong and widely respected.

This same principle applies in major religious cities elsewhere; restrictions around sites such as Makkah are especially strict and enforced with zero tolerance (such as Makkah). Likewise, near Medinah, smoking restrictions are treated seriously due to the religious significance of the area (near Madinah).

Tourist Sites and Cultural Centres

Tourism sites—museums, heritage sites, archaeological parks and many hotels used by tourists—now frequently restrict smoking inside buildings and sometimes around entrances and visitor flows. The tourism authority encourages smokeâfree visitor experiences at major destinations, and enforcement is often noticeable at ticket offices and cultural visitor centres.

Workplaces and Universities

Many workplaces and universities have internal smoke-free policies that go beyond national requirements. In Riyadh’s business districts and academic campuses, signage and HR policies typically prohibit smoking in entrances, lobbies and most indoor common areas. Visitors should assume that workplaces and university grounds are non-smoking unless designated outdoor areas are clearly announced.

Where You Can Smoke in Riyadh — Permitted Spaces and Best Practices

Designated Smoking Areas in Public Venues

While many indoor public spaces are smoke-free, some malls, larger hotels, and private clubs maintain designated smoking rooms or outdoor courtyards where smoking is permitted. These spaces are expressly signed. In modern shopping centres you’ll often find a sealed smoking room or an outdoor terrace where smoking is tolerated; however, the trend is toward fewer indoor smoking rooms and more outdoor-only options.

Hotels and Private Accommodation

Most major hotels provide smoking and non-smoking rooms, though the availability of smoking rooms is declining. Hotel websites or reservation platforms typically note whether rooms permit smoking, and many hotels now charge cleaning fees for guests who smoke in non-smoking rooms. In Riyadh, some hotels maintain designated smoking lounges or terrace areas where smoking and shisha (waterpipe) are permitted.

When you book, check hotel policies carefully and request a smoking room explicitly if you need one. Private Residences, Villas and Compounds

Private homes and many expatriate compounds permit smoking within private living areas or private balconies. However, building policies vary: some apartment blocks or managed compounds prohibit smoking in shared indoor spaces and some balconies to protect neighbouring units. Always check rules with your host or property manager before lighting up.

Outdoor Areas and Designated Terraces

Open-air public spaces that are not immediately adjacent to religious, educational or healthcare buildings are often where smokers congregate. Cafés with outdoor seating commonly allow smoking on terraces, but this depends on the venue’s own policy. Respect signage and staff instructions—if a terrace is shared with a family area or children’s play area, smoking may be restricted.

Cigarettes, Shisha and Vaping — What’s Treated Differently?

Traditional Tobacco (Cigarettes)

Cigarette smoking follows the broad bans described earlier. Packaging laws require graphic health warnings and plain packaging is enforced for retail. Sale restrictions prohibit single-stick sales and vending machines for cigarettes are largely phased out; retail is typically by packet only, and sale to under 18s is forbidden.

Shisha (Waterpipe)

Shisha cafés exist in Riyadh, but the legal and practical environment is nuanced. While private venues can operate shisha service in designated indoor lounges or open terraces, many tourist and hospitality sites now restrict shisha service or move it outdoors. Health warnings and regulations about molasses-type tobacco apply, and some venues limit flavours and promotional activity to comply with broader tobacco laws.

If you plan to enjoy shisha, favor licensed cafés and hotel terraces that clearly present their compliance and ventilation measures. Tourism-focused sites and attractions often prohibit any form of smoking, including shisha, so consult venue rules before assuming it’s permitted. For information about tourism facilities and where smoke-free policies are strict, explore advice on tourism destinations such as AlUla (tourism sites like AlUla).

E-Cigarettes and Vaping

E-cigarettes are legally allowed in Saudi Arabia under regulated conditions: the sale, import and marketing of vaping devices and nicotine-containing e-liquids are regulated, and nicotine limits and certain ingredient bans apply. Importantly, the use of e-cigarettes is prohibited in all spaces where smoking is banned. That means you cannot vape inside airports, government buildings, hospitals, universities, religious sites, or many tourist venues where smoking is banned.

If you vape, carry devices and liquids in original packaging when traveling, observe airline rules about batteries and liquids, and never assume vaping is allowed simply because e-cigarettes are sold locally. For regional differences, coastal cities may have a different social environment—coastal cities like Jeddah often have more open outdoor terraces where vaping is tolerated but still subject to the same legal no-smoking zones (coastal cities like Jeddah).

Penalties, Fines and Enforcement in Riyadh

Typical Fines and Legal Consequences

Enforcement has escalated over the years. Penalties for smoking in prohibited places are typically fines that start around 200 SAR and can reach 5,000 SAR for certain violations. There are also more severe penalties for offenses related to the sale of tobacco to minors, advertising violations, or selling prohibited forms of tobacco, with maximum fines reported in higher brackets for commercial infractions.

In addition to monetary fines, venues that host illicit tobacco promotions can face administrative sanctions. For travelers, the most realistic exposure is a fine for smoking in a clearly marked no-smoking area. Municipal enforcement officers and facility security staff carry out monitoring and issue fines or warnings.

How Enforcement Looks on the Ground

In Riyadh you are more likely to receive a warning or be asked to stop by security staff in private venues; municipal enforcement is more visible at public institutions, airports, and cultural sites. If a fine is issued, it will typically be processed through the municipal or police administrative system. Always comply politely with enforcement officers—contestations are best pursued through formal channels after you leave the scene rather than escalating in public.

What To Do if You Are Fined

If you receive a fine, request written documentation that details the reason, amount and payment instructions. Keep proof of identity and any relevant travel documents handy. If you believe a fine was wrongly issued, record the contact details of the issuing officer and the institution, and ask about appeal procedures. For travel insurance and assistance, keep a digital scan of documentation and contact details of your embassy or consulate if needed.

Enforcement can vary by city district and by the nature of the venue. In business districts, fines are usually administrative and processed quickly; in culturally sensitive areas the reaction may be firmer. Practical Strategies for Smokers Traveling to Riyadh

Before You Travel — Planning and Packing

Plan ahead so smoking does not interrupt your itinerary. Identify the hotels and venues that permit smoking or provide outdoor areas, and book rooms marked as smoking if they’re available. Pack supplies—lighters, a travel ashtray if you’ll smoke outdoors, spare batteries for vaping devices—and carry documentation about e-cigarettes if you intend to bring them. Check airline rules for batteries and liquids: most carriers treat vaping batteries as restricted items in checked luggage.

Confirm local availability of tobacco products if you rely on specific brands or e-liquids. Retail access varies; some international brands are available in Riyadh, but packaging and ingredients may differ due to regulatory requirements.

In Riyadh: A Daily Plan for Managing Smoking Needs

Riyadh’s heat and urban layout shape daily habits. Structure your day to include planned smoke breaks that align with permitted areas. Mornings visiting cultural sites? Leave smoke breaks for hotel terraces, designated outdoor café areas, or private compound spaces. Evenings in business districts? Use smoking rooms in certain malls or designated outdoor terraces of restaurants.

When attending formal events, tours, or visiting museums, assume no smoking is allowed and carry small breath-mints and nicotine gum if you use nicotine replacement. Many travelers find micro-planning—knowing the next permitted area within a 15–20 minute walk—makes the trip smooth.

Eating Out, Cafés and Nightlife

Cafés in Riyadh typically separate smoking and non-smoking sections; many modern venues offer an outdoor terrace for smokers. When visiting restaurants, ask staff about their policy—some upscale venues are fully smoke-free indoors, while smaller cafés may allow shisha on terraces. If you plan to spend evenings at hospitality venues, choose locations with explicit smoking terraces or book a private room where smoking is permitted.

Visiting Religious and Cultural Sites

Hold to no-smoking norms at mosques and cultural institutions. This is both a legal matter and a strong cultural expectation. Even if outdoor space seems available, avoid smoking near entrances, queues or family areas. If you want a smoke break near a cultural site, step well away from the premises into a public street or a designated outdoor area where smoking is allowed.

Etiquette and Cultural Sensitivity

Riyadh is socially conservative. Beyond legal compliance, cultural sensitivity greatly reduces friction. Refrain from smoking in mixed or family spaces, and be cautious around women and children—even where smoking is permitted, many locals prefer a smoke-free environment when families are present. When offered a shisha at a private gathering, follow the host’s cues about where and when it is appropriate.

Being discreet, polite and observant of signage will protect you from unwanted attention and ensures you leave a positive impression as a visitor.

Buying Tobacco and Vaping Products in Riyadh

Retail for cigarettes is through licensed shops and supermarkets; the sale of single sticks is banned and you’ll buy full packs only. Packaging is plainly labeled with health warnings. For vaping, regulated retail outlets and specialized shops sell devices and e-liquids in compliance with SFDA rules; nicotine concentrations and ingredients are controlled.

Never purchase tobacco products from unlicensed street sellers; packaging and contents could be non-compliant and may present legal risks if confiscated by authorities. If in doubt, buy from well-known retailers or hotel shops.

Health, Quitting Help and Alternatives

If restrictions make smoking inconvenient, consider short-term nicotine replacement options: patches, gum, lozenges and regulated e-cigarettes (used only where permitted) can be part of a travel plan. Saudi Arabia’s market for nicotine replacement exists and pharmacies may stock products; however, availability varies by brand. If you’re planning to travel with prescription medications linked to quitting, carry a doctor’s letter and original packaging.

For longer-term quitting support, public health campaigns and clinics in the Kingdom offer cessation programs. International travelers should consult their home-country health services before departure if they require prescription treatments.

A Traveler’s Quick Checklist: Do’s and Don’ts for Smokers in Riyadh

  • Always obey clearly posted no-smoking signs and instructions from venue staff.  
  • Avoid smoking near mosques, hospitals, schools, airports, government buildings and tourist attractions.  
  • Use designated smoking areas in hotels, malls or private venues; ask staff where to go.  
  • Carry identification and, if fined, request printed documentation and instructions for payment or appeal.  
  • Buy cigarettes and vaping supplies from licensed retailers; avoid single-stick sellers.  
  • Do not assume vaping is permitted indoors; treat vape devices like cigarettes regarding banned spaces.  
  • Be discreet and respectful in mixed-gender or family-oriented public spaces.  
  • If unsure, step away from entrances and crowds before smoking.

Sample Scenarios and How to Handle Them

Landing at King Khalid International Airport

Upon arrival, follow signage for designated smoking rooms if you need to smoke before baggage claim or transfers. Do not smoke in terminal concourses, near gates or in car parks attached to airport buildings where signage prohibits it. If you are transiting, check the airport’s map for smoker rooms and allow added time to reach them, as they are not always adjacent to gate areas.

Visiting a Museum or Heritage Site

Expect indoor smoke-free rules. If you need a break, plan to pause outside the main entrance in an area clearly away from queues and family spaces. Many museums also have outdoor cafés with terrace seating where smoking may be permitted—ask staff before lighting up.

Staying in a Hotel During a Conference

If your conference takes place in a hotel, smoking rooms may be limited and conference halls are always smoke-free. Use designated outdoor smoking terraces and, if necessary, the hotel’s smoking lounge. If you smoke in a non-smoking room, expect a cleaning fee and possible penalty.

Enjoying an Evening Café with Shisha

Choose licensed cafés with clear ventilation and seating away from family sections. Confirm whether the shisha lounge is indoor or outdoor and whether any smoking times are restricted. If accompanied by local friends, follow their lead on etiquette and placement.

Enforcement Differences Across the Kingdom — What Riyadh Visitors Should Know

Enforcement intensity can vary: Riyadh’s municipal authorities, airport security and cultural venues enforce with visible presence, while enforcement in other regions may have different emphases. For example, tourism sites often adopt strict no-smoking policies to preserve heritage and visitor experience, while some coastal or resort locations provide more outdoor smoking options. How to Reduce Risk: Practical Tools and Mindset

Treat each venue as an independent environment and adopt a proactive mindset: look for signage, ask staff, and walk to a clearly designated area if needed. Keep breath-fresheners and portable nicotine aids on hand for short-term reliance. When in doubt, delay smoking until you return to your hotel or a clearly designated terrace. This rule minimises the risk of fines and shows respect for the local culture.

Preparing for Emergencies or Disputes

If you encounter enforcement you believe is incorrect or unfair, remain calm and compliant. Request official documentation and the identity of the issuing officer. If you require consular assistance, keep your embassy or consulate contact details handy. Most issues are administrative and resolvable through formal channels.

For travel insurance, check whether administrative fines are covered under your policy before departure. If fines are not covered, prepare to pay the amount or follow the jurisdictional procedure for appeals.

Balancing Enjoyment and Responsibility — A Practical Framework

Travelers who smoke do best when they apply a simple framework I recommend to all visitors: Plan, Observe, Ask, and Respect.

  • Plan: Identify smoking-permitted venues and include them in your itinerary before you travel.  
  • Observe: Look for signs and respect physical boundaries and staff instructions immediately.  
  • Ask: When unclear, ask staff where the designated smoking area is—do not assume.  
  • Respect: Prioritise cultural sensitivity and the local community’s comfort; being respectful reduces conflict and fines.

This framework transforms uncertainty into a dependable travel routine that keeps your journey smooth and respectful.

Conclusion

Riyadh enforces strict, modern tobacco controls designed to protect public health and respect cultural norms. As a visitor, you will find smoking opportunities in hotels, certain outdoor terraces, private residences and a limited number of designated areas, but the dominant reality is that many public spaces are smoke-free with active enforcement and fines. The practical path is to plan ahead, use the framework of Plan, Observe, Ask and Respect, and rely on local signage and staff guidance. By doing so you can enjoy Riyadh’s world-class attractions and hospitality without legal or social friction.

FAQ

Can I vape in public places in Riyadh?

No. Vaping is treated the same as smoking in spaces where smoking is prohibited. E-cigarettes are legally sold and regulated, but their use is not allowed in airports, government buildings, hospitals, educational institutions, and many tourist sites. Always follow no-smoking signage.

Are there fines for smoking in Riyadh, and how much can they be?

Yes. Fines for smoking in prohibited areas typically start from a few hundred Saudi riyals and can reach several thousand depending on the offence and whether commercial or repeat violations apply. If fined, request written documentation and payment instructions from the issuing authority.

Where is it safest to smoke when I’m visiting Riyadh?

The safest spaces are designated smoking rooms or outdoor terraces at hotels, licensed cafés with explicit smoking areas, and private residences where smoking is allowed. Always look for signage or ask staff. Avoid smoking near mosques, hospitals, government buildings and cultural attractions.
