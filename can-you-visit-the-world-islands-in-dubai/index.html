Can You Visit the World Islands in Dubai?

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Dubai’s skyline is famous for superlatives and spectacle, but the World Islands sit offshore as a quieter, more curious kind of marvel. For travelers deciding whether those tiny country-shaped isles are open to visitors, the options are limited but real — and the experience can be surprisingly rewarding if you plan with the right expectations.

Short answer: Yes — but with important caveats. A small number of islands within the World archipelago are open to visitors: Lebanon Island offers day access for beachgoers, while the Anantara resort on the South America cluster provides overnight stays and a full-service island experience. Most of the artificial islands remain undeveloped or privately owned, so public access is restricted and usually requires booking through operators or resorts.

This article explains what the World Islands are, which specific islands you can visit today, and exactly how to plan transport, bookings, costs, and on-island logistics. It also offers practical, experience-focused advice — from best times to visit and what to pack, to realistic expectations about facilities, environmental considerations, and safety. As the KSA Travel Insider & Cultural Guide for Saudi Travel & Leisure, I’ll give you the blueprint to decide whether a trip to the World Islands fits your Dubai itinerary and how to execute it with confidence.

What Are the World Islands?

The Concept and Scale

The World Islands are an ambitious man-made archipelago positioned off the coast of Dubai. Conceived in the early 2000s, the idea was to craft clusters of reclaimed land that, when viewed from above, replicate a scaled map of the globe. The project originally comprised roughly 300 small islands of varying sizes, organized into continental groupings and named after countries, cities, and notable figures.

The sheer scale and novelty drew attention and investment, but the islands were built in phases, and financial setbacks slowed development. Today, the archipelago is a patchwork: a handful of islands have active developments or public access points, while many others remain undeveloped or privately owned.

Engineering and Challenges

Construction used dredging and land reclamation techniques similar to those employed on Palm Jumeirah. While the engineering allowed Dubai to create distinctive shoreline shapes, the project has faced long-term environmental and pragmatic challenges, including concerns about erosion, sea-level effects, and the massive costs of infrastructure (power, water, waste, and transport) for small, remote islands.

These challenges help explain why only a few islands are operational as hospitality or leisure venues and why visiting the World Islands requires careful, up-to-date planning rather than casual drop-ins.

Which World Islands Are Open To Visitors Today?

Lebanon Island: Day Access and Beach Club Vibes

Lebanon Island was one of the earliest completed islands and is the most visitor-accessible public slice of the archipelago. It operates as a day-visit destination with beach facilities, a swimming pool, food and beverage options, and water-sports operators. Practical points:

  • Typical visiting hours run during daylight, with some evening or adults-only slots managed separately.
  • Access is usually via scheduled boat transfers departing from Jumeirah Fishing Harbour or nearby marinas. Operators offer boat transfers, sunbeds, and lunch or beverage packages.
  • Lebanon Island can be busy on weekends and public holidays; bookings are recommended.

Anantara on Clarence Island (South America Cluster): Overnight Stays

The Anantara World Islands Resort, located in the South America cluster (often referred to by its island name), is the only true overnight resort operating within the World Islands archipelago. Staying here gives you the full island experience — private beaches, pools, spa services, and scheduled boat transfers to and from The Palm or mainland Dubai.

Key features include upscale dining, spa and wellness offerings, and curated island activities such as open-air cinema nights and masterclasses. Because accommodations are limited, reservations should be made well in advance.

Portions of the Heart of Europe and Other Developments

Some projects in the Heart of Europe cluster and parts of the archipelago have been announced or partially developed, but access varies by developer and stage of construction. When islands are part of a private resort development, access is typically restricted to guests or event attendees.

What You Cannot Expect

  • Most islands are not open for casual exploration. There are no public roads or bridges connecting the islands to the mainland — all access is by water or air.
  • There are limited public facilities outside of developed islands; don’t expect shops, clinics, or general services on undeveloped isles.

How To Visit: Transportation Options and Booking Steps

Getting There: Transport Methods Compared

You have four realistic transport options for reaching the World Islands: scheduled boat transfers, private boat or yacht charters, resort-arranged transfers, and aerial transfers (helicopter or seaplane). Choosing between them depends on budget, privacy needs, and the island you plan to visit.

  • Scheduled boat transfer: This is the most affordable and common way to reach Lebanon Island and the Anantara resort. Transfers typically depart from Jumeirah Fishing Harbour or The Palm and are included with day-pass bookings or resort reservations.
  • Resort-arranged transfer: If you’re staying at Anantara, transfers are bundled into your reservation. This offers predictability and a simple arrival/departure procedure.
  • Private yacht or speedboat: Ideal for groups seeking privacy, flexible timing, and an on-water experience; you’ll have direct access to the landing point arranged with the island operator.
  • Helicopter or seaplane: Fast and cinematic, aerial transfers are premium options for guests with time constraints or special occasions. Availability depends on the resort and local aviation operators.

Step-By-Step Booking Process

  1. Decide which island you want to visit (Lebanon for day access, Anantara for overnight).
  2. Check availability and official transfer options for your chosen day and time.
  3. Book your island access or resort stay through the island operator, resort website, or an authorized tour operator; confirm the meeting point and transfer schedule.
  4. Review cancellation and refund policies, weather contingency plans, and any requirements (ID, vaccination records, or guest lists).
  5. On the day, arrive early at the marina check-in point with valid ID and the booking confirmation.

Costs and What’s Typically Included

Visiting the World Islands can range from affordable to very expensive depending on your choice:

  • Day passes to Lebanon Island: moderate cost; often include boat transfer, sunbed, and sometimes light refreshments.
  • Resort stays at Anantara: premium nightly rates that include resort facilities, but not always transfers or premium experiences.
  • Yacht charters: variable and can escalate quickly, especially with added fuel, skipper, and crew fees.
  • Helicopter/seaplane: highest cost per person but fastest and most dramatic.

Confirm what’s included (meals, transfers, sunbeds, water-sports access) before booking to avoid surprises.

Practical On-Island Logistics and What To Expect

Facilities and Services

Expect resort islands to provide the basics of luxury hospitality: rooms or villas, restaurants, spa, pools, and beach access. Day-visit islands like Lebanon maintain public-facing facilities — sunbeds, beach shacks, washrooms, and sports rentals — but the scale and quality vary.

On undeveloped islands, there are no public services. If your boat tour includes an exploration of undeveloped islands, it will likely be a circumnavigation or a view from the deck rather than a landing with facilities.

Water-Sports and Activities

Beach and water-based activities on operational islands commonly include jet-skiing, parasailing, banana boating, paddleboarding, and snorkeling. Resorts offer curated experiences (private dining, sunset cruises, cinema under the stars) and, occasionally, guided marine activities led by trained staff.

Dining and Alcohol

Resorts typically have multiple dining options and licensed bars. Day-visit islands may operate casual beach shacks and cafes. If you plan to bring private food or alcohol, verify island policies — many public islands restrict glass and certain items for safety and environmental reasons.

Accessibility

Accessing the World Islands requires a boat or aircraft so mobility needs must be discussed with the operator or resort in advance. Resorts like Anantara strive to accommodate guests with reduced mobility, but infrastructure on small islands can present constraints (ramps, dock stability, transfer boats). Always request accessibility details before booking.

Health and Safety

  • First aid: Resorts maintain basic medical services; remote undeveloped islands do not. Bring essential medications.
  • Sea conditions: Transfers depend on weather and sea state. Rough seas can delay or cancel service.
  • Sun protection: There’s limited shade; sunscreen, hats, and hydration are essential.
  • Insurance: Check that your travel insurance covers marine transfers and watersports.

Environmental Considerations

The World Islands were built with intense engineering and have, at times, faced criticism for environmental impact. Responsible travelers should follow on-island guidance concerning wildlife, waste disposal, and marine safety. Resorts and operators increasingly emphasize sustainable practices; support them by choosing providers with clear environmental policies.

Best Time To Visit and Seasonal Tips

Dubai’s climate strongly dictates what kind of island trip you’ll enjoy. For sun-filled beach days, timing matters.

Seasonal Breakdown

  • October to April: The most comfortable months — mild temperatures, low humidity, excellent sea conditions. Ideal for beach days, water sports, and sunset photography.
  • May to September: Extremely hot and humid; daytime beach activities are less comfortable. If you visit in summer, plan early morning or late afternoon transfers and choose islands with strong shade and pool access.

Time of Day

Sunrise and early morning transfers offer calm seas and softer light for photography, but many island services open later. Sunset trips combine dramatic skyline views with cooler temperatures; verify boat schedules since some operators limit night access for safety.

Photography, Views, and Where to Stand

Iconic Vistas

From the World Islands you’ll capture unique perspectives of Dubai’s skyline: the Burj Al Arab, the Palm Jumeirah, and the high-rises of downtown. For aerial-style photos, the Anantara resort’s private beaches and elevated viewpoints are excellent. On day trips, the boat provides cinematic angles for panoramic shots of the archipelago mapped against the city.

Practical Photography Tips

  • Bring a polarizing filter to reduce glare and deepen the blue of the Persian Gulf.
  • Protect gear from sand and salt spray; use waterproof bags or covers.
  • For sunset shots, position the skyline to the left or right of frame to balance the seascape.
  • If using drones, confirm the resort or operator’s drone policy and UAE aviation rules; many areas require permits.

Costs, Budgeting, and Value Assessment

Typical Price Ranges (Indicative)

  • Lebanon Island day pass with transfers: moderate — mid-range per person.
  • Anantara overnight: premium resort pricing; plan as a luxury stay.
  • Private yacht charter: variable, often several hundred to thousands of USD depending on duration and size.
  • Helicopter transfers: premium per-person fares.

When evaluating value, weigh what matters to you: privacy, convenience, exclusivity, or a simple beach day. A well-priced day pass that includes transfers and sundecks can offer excellent value for casual visitors, while resorts offer more immersive experiences at a higher cost.

Safety, Rules, and Local Regulations

Entry Requirements and Visa Notes

For most tourists, visiting the World Islands does not require additional local permits beyond the UAE entry visa, if applicable. Ensure you meet UAE entry requirements for your nationality before travel. If you arrive in the UAE from Saudi Arabia or the wider Gulf, check current visa arrangements as they vary by passport and bilateral agreements.

On-Island Rules

Operators and resorts publish rules about safety, permitted items, and behavioral expectations — follow them. That includes restrictions on bringing your own alcohol and guidelines for marine and wildlife protection.

Emergency Protocols

Because islands are remote, confirm emergency procedures with your operator or resort. Resorts will provide basic medical services and, if necessary, evacuations to mainland medical facilities. For day tours, ask about the nearest clinic or hospital.

Accommodation Strategies: Stay On-Island Or Mainland?

Stay On-Island for Immersion

Choosing an overnight stay at Anantara (or similar resort developments if they become available) maximizes the island atmosphere. You’ll enjoy privacy, on-site activities, and dramatic sunrise/sunset views. On-island accommodation makes logistical sense if you want to disconnect from the mainland.

Stay on The Palm or Mainland for Convenience

If you prefer flexibility and more varied dining or shopping, base yourself on The Palm Jumeirah, Dubai Marina, Jumeirah Beach, or downtown Dubai. These areas offer easy access to marinas for scheduled transfers and a larger selection of hotels at varying budgets. Staying on the mainland can be more cost-effective and simplifies onward travel connections.

What To Pack — Essentials For a World Islands Trip

  • Valid passport and booking confirmations.
  • Lightweight sun-protective clothing, wide-brim hat, and sunglasses.
  • Reef-safe sunscreen and a reusable water bottle.
  • Swimwear, a quick-dry towel, and water shoes for rocky landings.
  • Small dry bag for phone, wallet, and camera.
  • Medications, seasickness remedies, and a basic first-aid kit.
  • Waterproof phone case and lens-cleaning cloth.

Environmental and Ethical Considerations

Respect the Marine Environment

The World Islands exist within a marine ecosystem; visitors should avoid littering, follow guidance on snorkeling or diving, and refrain from touching marine life. Resorts often have sustainability programs — choose companies that demonstrate responsible waste management and renewable energy initiatives.

Responsible Tourism Principles

  • Support local staff and on-island services by using licensed operators.
  • Avoid unauthorized landings on undeveloped islands to protect wildlife and fragile infrastructure.
  • Prefer vendors and resorts with clear environmental policies.

Expectation Management: What Visitors Often Misunderstand

Many visitors imagine the World Islands as a fully-developed archipelago with accessible beaches and shops across every island. The reality is more modest: most islands are undeveloped or private, and services are concentrated on a handful of operational islands. Treat the trip as an exclusive, managed experience rather than a hopping tour of multiple countries.

If your priority is extensive exploration of islands, consider other coastal options like island-hopping tours around the UAE’s natural islands or luxury private charters that can design a custom day on the water.

Comparing Alternatives: The World Islands Vs. Other Dubai Attractions

If you’re deciding whether to devote precious Dubai time to the World Islands, compare the experience to alternatives:

  • Palm Jumeirah: More developed, lots of beachfront hotels, restaurants, and easy access from the mainland.
  • Dubai Marina and Jumeirah: Great for nightlife, dining, and skyline views without needing a boat.
  • Offshore cruises: Sunset and skyline cruises offer waterfront views without the need for island access.

Each option delivers a different flavor: the World Islands are for privacy, novelty, and island solitude; Palm and mainland offers convenience and variety.

Frequently Changing Variables: How To Stay Updated

Because the World Islands’ accessibility can shift with new developments, operator changes, and construction activity, verify details before you travel:

  • Check the resort or island operator’s website for current opening hours and transfer points.
  • Confirm marina meeting locations and boat schedules 24–48 hours before departure.
  • Monitor weather forecasts for sea-state information, especially during transitional seasons.

FAQ

Can tourists land on most of the World Islands?

No. While a few islands are developed and accessible (Lebanon Island and Anantara’s island being the primary examples), most islands are private or undeveloped. Landings on undeveloped islands are generally not permitted without direct permission from owners or developers.

How do I book a day trip to Lebanon Island?

Day trips are booked through authorized operators and typically include boat transfers, sunbeds, and sometimes meals. Bookings should be confirmed in advance, and you’ll need to check the designated marina meeting point on your confirmation.

Is it safe to visit the World Islands regarding sea conditions and health services?

Visiting is safe when arranged through reputable operators. Sea conditions can affect transfers; resorts and operators have safety protocols. For serious medical needs, the mainland offers full medical facilities, so share your health concerns when booking.

Can I combine a World Islands visit with other UAE or Gulf destinations?

Absolutely. Many travelers combine a World Islands day with mainland Dubai activities or with visits to neighboring Gulf destinations.
