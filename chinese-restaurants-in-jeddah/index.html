10 Top Best Chinese Restaurants in Jeddah

Updated September 11, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Jeddah is one of Saudi Arabia’s most vibrant food cities. With its cosmopolitan mix of locals, expats, and visitors, the city offers a wide range of cuisines—and Chinese food holds a special place among them. From upscale fine dining to casual noodle joints, dim sum, spicy Szechuan, and sweet & sour classics—there’s something for everyone. Whether you want a refined night out or an affordable family dinner, this guide will take you through the best Chinese restaurants in Jeddah. Read on to discover your next go-to spot.

What Makes a Chinese Restaurant Stand Out

Here are key features to consider when looking for top Chinese dining spots:

1. Shang Palace (Shangri-La Hotel, Jeddah)

For a truly high-end dining experience, Shang Palace delivers the essence of Cantonese cuisine with luxury flair. The restaurant is set inside the Shangri-La Hotel overlooking the Red Sea, which makes it ideal for romantic dinners or business gatherings. The menu features dim sum, roast duck, and a wide selection of seafood prepared with authentic Chinese techniques. Prices are premium, but so is the experience. If you’re after a taste of China in an elegant setting, this should top your list.

2. Toki

Toki blends traditional Chinese recipes with modern presentation. The interiors are sleek and stylish, creating an atmosphere that feels both chic and comfortable. Guests rave about the Szechuan duck, hot soups, and artfully prepared dim sum platters. While the prices lean towards the higher side, the quality of flavors and service makes it worthwhile. Toki is perfect for date nights or group dinners where you want a touch of sophistication.

3. Baytoti

If you want Chinese food without the luxury price tag, Baytoti is your go-to. With multiple branches across Jeddah, it’s a household name for affordable Chinese dishes. Think sweet and sour chicken, beef in oyster sauce, stir-fried noodles, and comforting soups. Portions are generous, service is fast, and the setting is casual. Families and groups often choose Baytoti for weekday dinners or quick takeout.

4. Chinese Bistro

Chinese Bistro strikes a balance between affordability and atmosphere. It offers cozy interiors with round tables, making it family-friendly yet stylish enough for outings with friends. Their menu covers everything from spring rolls to sizzling beef platters. Buffet options (when available) make it a hit for variety lovers. Pricing is mid-range, and the food quality is consistently satisfying.

5. Gulf Royal Chinese

As one of Jeddah’s more established Chinese restaurants, Gulf Royal Chinese has built a loyal customer base over the years. The restaurant specializes in bold flavors—spicy stir-fries, saucy seafood, and traditional fried rice. The décor is modest compared to newer places, but the focus here is on food quality and hearty portions. Perfect for family gatherings or those who want no-frills, authentic flavors.

6. Chi Box

Sometimes you just need Chinese food on the go, and Chi Box is designed exactly for that. With its fast service and takeout-friendly packaging, it’s a popular choice for students, office workers, and anyone in a hurry. The menu includes quick favorites like chow mein, fried rice, and crispy chicken. Don’t expect fine dining here—it’s about convenience and tasty comfort food at budget-friendly prices.

7. Da Kekou

Da Kekou is a cozy spot known for its variety of soups, dumplings, and vegetarian options. The interiors are bright and family-oriented, making it a relaxed dining experience. It’s particularly loved by diners who enjoy lighter meals or want to try Chinese food beyond the usual sweet and sour dishes. Their desserts, such as fried ice cream, add an extra reason to visit.

8. Eat Chinese

Eat Chinese has become a go-to casual restaurant for locals. It’s not about fancy plating—it’s about serving tasty, consistent food. Popular picks include crispy chicken, fried noodles, and vegetable stir-fries. The vibe is lively, perfect for groups of friends looking for a casual night out. Service can get busy during peak hours, but the food is dependable and fairly priced.

9. P.F. Chang’s

An international chain with a loyal following, P.F. Chang’s offers a modern Asian-fusion twist. The menu includes their famous Dynamite Shrimp, hand-folded dim sum, and Mongolian beef. The décor is upscale yet welcoming, and the staff is attentive. Prices are higher than local restaurants, but the consistency in taste and global brand recognition make it a trusted choice for many.

10. Great Wall Chinese Restaurant

Great Wall is a classic spot located in Sharafiyah district. It has been around for years, serving traditional Chinese dishes that many expats and locals have grown fond of. From hot soups to stir-fried beef and chicken dishes, the flavors are hearty and familiar. The ambience is simple, but the food brings comfort and nostalgia. It’s a reliable choice for anyone who values traditional Chinese cooking in a laid-back setting.

Final Words

Jeddah’s love for Chinese cuisine is clear—you’ll find everything from fine dining restaurants serving authentic Cantonese delicacies to quick-service spots perfect for a weekday fix. If you’re looking for luxury and authenticity, Shang Palace and Toki are unbeatable. For casual meals, Baytoti, Eat Chinese, and Gulf Royal Chinese deliver comfort food at reasonable prices. And if you prefer a fast and simple bite, Chi Box will hit the spot.

No matter your budget or preference, Jeddah has a Chinese restaurant that can satisfy your cravings.

FAQs – Chinese Restaurants in Jeddah

1. What is the best Chinese restaurant in Jeddah for fine dining?

Shang Palace at the Shangri-La Hotel is the most recommended for high-end Chinese cuisine with a luxurious setting.

2. Are there affordable Chinese restaurants in Jeddah?

Yes, Baytoti, Eat Chinese, and Chi Box are all budget-friendly while still offering tasty meals.

3. Which places serve the best dim sum in Jeddah?

Shang Palace and Toki are the top choices for authentic dim sum dishes.

4. Do these restaurants have vegetarian options?

Yes, most Chinese restaurants in Jeddah offer vegetarian stir-fries, dumplings, and soups. Da Kekou is especially good for lighter, veggie-friendly meals.
