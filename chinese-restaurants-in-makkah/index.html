10 Best Chinese Restaurants in Makkah for Food Lovers

Updated September 11, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Makkah is best known as the spiritual heart of Islam, but for millions of visitors and residents, it’s also a place where cultures meet through food. While Middle Eastern flavors dominate the city’s dining scene, Chinese cuisine has carved out a special place. Whether you’re in the mood for comforting fried rice, spicy stir-fries, or flavorful seafood, you’ll find plenty of options around the city.

To help you choose, we’ve rounded up the 10 best Chinese restaurants in Makkah—from family-friendly favorites to elegant sit-down spots.

What to Look For in a Chinese Restaurant In Makkah

Before we dive into the list, here are a few things that make a Chinese restaurant stand out in Makkah:

10 Top Chinese Restaurants in Makkah

Here’s a closer look at the most talked-about spots in the city:

1. Jumbo Chinese Restaurant

Jumbo is a long-time favorite among locals who love Chinese seafood. The restaurant is well-known for its shrimp dishes and sizzling platters that arrive fresh and hot. With comfortable seating and a slightly upscale feel, it’s perfect for family dinners or small gatherings. Its location on the Makkah–Jeddah Road makes it easy to reach for both residents and travelers.

2. New Diamond Chinese Restaurant

New Diamond—sometimes referred to as Gulf Royal—offers a big menu that covers soups, sizzling beef, noodles, and more. The restaurant has an elegant interior with a family section for privacy, making it a go-to spot for group dinners. Portions are generous, and prices are fair compared to the serving size. If you want to sit down and enjoy a proper Chinese meal, this is one of the best options in Makkah.

3. Baytoti (Makkah Branch)

Baytoti is a popular chain across Saudi Arabia, and its Makkah branch is always buzzing. The menu focuses on classic Chinese comfort food—fried rice, chow mein, and sweet & sour chicken. It’s not fancy, but it’s reliable, fast, and reasonably priced. This makes Baytoti perfect for casual dinners, especially if you’re traveling with kids or want a no-frills dining option.

4. Al Atbaq Restaurant

Al Atbaq isn’t purely Chinese, but it’s a great pick if you’re dining with people who have different food preferences. The menu covers Indian, Pakistani, Arabic, and Chinese dishes, so there’s something for everyone. Their Chinese dishes, especially chicken chow mein and stir-fried vegetables, get good reviews. It’s family-friendly, affordable, and a solid choice for mixed groups.

5. Paradise Restaurant

Paradise is another multi-cuisine restaurant that includes Chinese favorites on its menu. It’s popular with families looking for an affordable meal in a relaxed setting. Sweet & sour chicken, crispy beef, and fried noodles are some of their well-liked dishes. While not a specialist in Chinese food, it’s reliable when you want a variety without spending too much.

6. Durrat China

Close to Makkah Mall, Durrat China offers a neat and straightforward dining experience. The menu highlights Chinese seafood, noodle dishes, and light soups. The environment is simple yet welcoming, making it a nice stop if you’re shopping nearby and want something filling. Many visitors enjoy the balance between price and quality here.

7. Family-Run Local Spots

Beyond the bigger names, Makkah has several small family-run Chinese restaurants. They may not have fancy interiors, but many serve surprisingly flavorful meals at affordable prices. These are great for quick dinners or takeaways when you want something authentic and budget-friendly. Exploring these hidden gems can often lead to new favorites.

8. Hotel Restaurants with Chinese Menus

Several hotels in Makkah feature international restaurants that include Chinese options on their menus. While not dedicated Chinese kitchens, they often serve well-prepared dishes like dumplings, kung pao chicken, or seafood stir-fries. For pilgrims or travelers staying in hotels near the Haram, this is often the most convenient way to enjoy Chinese flavors.

9. Casual Takeout Spots

If you just want fried rice, noodles, or a quick chicken dish, you’ll find many small takeout places around the city. They focus on speed and affordability, which makes them a hit with students, workers, and anyone short on time. While the menus are usually limited, the food is fast and satisfying.

10. Seafood-Focused Chinese Restaurants

Makkah diners love seafood, and some Chinese restaurants specialize in it. Jumbo and New Diamond stand out, but smaller places also serve dishes like chili prawns, garlic fish, and sizzling squid. If seafood is your preference, it’s worth asking what’s fresh on the day you visit.

Final Words

Chinese cuisine in Makkah might not be as widespread as in Jeddah or Riyadh, but the city has plenty to offer—from elegant restaurants like New Diamond and Jumbo, to casual favorites like Baytoti and Paradise. Whether you’re looking for a fine sit-down meal with family or a quick takeaway after a long day, you’ll find a spot that matches your budget and taste.

FAQs – Chinese Restaurants in Makkah

1. What’s the most popular Chinese restaurant in Makkah?

Jumbo and New Diamond are among the most recommended for full Chinese dining experiences.

2. Are there affordable Chinese food options in Makkah?

Yes, Baytoti, Paradise, and many small local outlets serve Chinese meals at budget-friendly prices.

3. Do these restaurants offer vegetarian dishes?

Most do. You’ll usually find vegetable stir-fries, soups, and noodle dishes that are vegetarian-friendly.

4. Which Chinese restaurant is closest to the Haram?

Hotel restaurants near the Haram often include Chinese options on their menus, making them the most convenient choice for pilgrims.

5. Do I need a reservation?

For larger restaurants like Jumbo and New Diamond, booking is a good idea during peak hours. Smaller and casual places generally don’t require reservations.
