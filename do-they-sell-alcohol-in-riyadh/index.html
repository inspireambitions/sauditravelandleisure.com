Do They Sell Alcohol in Riyadh?

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Introduction

Saudi Arabia’s tourism sector has been reshaped in recent years as the Kingdom opens key destinations, builds luxury resorts, and invites more international visitors under Vision 2030. That accelerating change naturally brings practical questions from travelers and expatriates: what is allowed, what isn’t, and how do you plan a trip that respects both the law and local culture?

Short answer: As of now, alcohol is not widely sold to the public in Riyadh. A tightly controlled off-licence in the Diplomatic Quarter serves a narrow group of non-Muslim diplomats, and broader licensed sales have been announced for a limited number of designated high-end and tourist locations under strict rules. For the typical visitor or resident, alcohol remains prohibited in public and private spaces unless you are inside an officially sanctioned, licensed environment or a diplomatic venue.

This article explains the legal framework, recent policy shifts, and the day-to-day realities you need to understand to travel in Riyadh with confidence. You’ll get practical, step-by-step advice on where alcohol can be legally purchased or consumed, how to avoid legal trouble, and how to plan hotel and dining choices that match your expectations. Throughout, I connect these operational details to the blueprint we use at Saudi Travel & Leisure to help travelers move from curiosity to a well-executed Saudi itinerary.

Legal Framework and Recent Policy Shifts

Historical context and the longstanding prohibition

Saudi Arabia’s legal posture on alcohol stems from longstanding religious and legal traditions. The public sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages have been prohibited for decades, and the law has been enforced strictly. Historically, foreign missions used diplomatic channels to bring limited quantities of alcohol into the Kingdom, but public commercial sales were banned after 1952. This history shapes both the current regulations and the sensitivity with which authorities approach any change.

Diplomatic exceptions and the diplomatic store

A notable, practical exception to the ban has always been diplomatic immunity. Embassies and missions have used diplomatic pouches and internal supplies to serve staff for years. In recent developments, Saudi authorities opened an authorized off-licence in the Diplomatic Quarter of Riyadh that sells alcohol to a narrow, pre-approved population: non-Muslim diplomats and accredited mission personnel. Purchases at this store are subject to registration, clearance procedures, and monthly quotas designed to curb unauthorized distribution. The aim behind the store is to reduce illicit trafficking through diplomatic channels while preserving the Kingdom’s public norms.

Announced reforms and limited licensing plans

More recently, Saudi officials announced plans to license the sale and consumption of certain alcoholic beverages in a limited number of carefully controlled locations. The proposed framework announced in 2025–2026 envisions licensing wine, beer, and cider (with stricter controls on spirits and high-alcohol products) at specific venues such as fiveâstar hotels, luxury resorts, certain tourist hubs, and private residential compounds for expatriates. These measures are being presented as a targeted program to improve the hospitality offering for international visitors while maintaining cultural norms and preventing public consumption.

The announced license program emphasizes strict controls: licensed venues must adhere to designated zones, train staff in responsible service, implement ID and access controls, and prohibit consumption in public spaces. While these policy shifts point toward a more regulated hospitality environment in the coming years, they do not represent an open market. Change is deliberately incremental, balancing economic goals and cultural considerations.

What the laws still prohibit

Under current law and customary enforcement in Riyadh, the following remain prohibited:

  • Public sale of alcohol in retail stores, restaurants, and open public spaces, except in narrowly authorized venues.
  • Possession, consumption, or transport of alcoholic beverages by the general public and Saudi citizens.
  • Unlicensed importation, sale, or distribution of alcohol.
  • Consumption or possession that leads to public disturbance, drunk driving, or offensive behavior (these are treated harshly).

Penalties for unauthorized possession or consumption can include fines, detention, corporal punishment in some cases, and deportation for foreign residents and visitors. The presence of diplomatic or licensed exceptions does not alter the strict legal environment for everyone else.

How “Selling Alcohol in Riyadh” Works Practically

The Diplomatic Quarter store — mechanics and limits

The Diplomatic Quarter off-licence is structured to serve only a clearly defined population: non-Muslim diplomatic staff and accredited personnel. Access requires registration and government clearance. Purchases are tracked, and quotas limit monthly acquisition; for example, authorities have publicly discussed point systems that translate liters of beer, wine, and spirits into monthly allowances to prevent diversion.

Operationally, expect strict procedures: identification at the door, purchase records tied to an accredited profile, limits on proxies, and clear rules on removal from diplomatic premises. The shop’s opening was described by officials as a measure to control previously untaxed and illicit exchanges of alcohol through diplomatic channels rather than a signal of open public availability.

Hotels, resorts, and designated tourist areas

The announced licensing framework anticipates that certain fiveâstar hotels, luxury resorts, and major tourist developments will obtain permissions to sell alcohol on-site. The licensing model being discussed places boundaries around where alcohol can be sold and consumed: inside licensed hotel restaurants and bars, in controlled resort zones (for instance, special zones being developed around tourist projects), and at specific events with designated permission.

For travelers, the practical implication is that the choice of accommodation and event matters. A stay at an authorized international resort, when and if licenses are active, could allow on-site purchases and consumption in a controlled setting. Until broader licenses are implemented, hotel offerings in Riyadh remain largely alcohol-free, and many international chains adapt by offering elevated non-alcoholic beverage programs.

Residential compounds and expatriate life

Some expatriate compounds have historically provided a social environment where residents could gather privately; however, such arrangements operate within complex legal boundaries. The announced licensing proposals sometimes reference compounds as potential licensed residential zones, but that is not yet the norm. If and when licensing expands to include certain compounds, it will come with rules about storage, consumption, and access.

If you live in a compound or lease long-term, confirm rules with management. Management must stay aligned with local licensing; informal allowances can be risky if not backed by official authorization.

Events, festivals, and international gatherings

Authorities are also considering controlled availability at designated international cultural and business events. Licensing for events would be narrow and subject to clear regulations. Note the specific, high-profile exceptions: at some major sporting events or religiously sensitive gatherings, alcohol may be explicitly banned regardless of general licensing policies. For example, officials have said there will be no alcohol during the 2034 FIFA World Cup events in Saudi stadiums, emphasizing that licensing decisions intersect with political, cultural, and event-specific considerations.

Enforcement realities and what that means for visitors

Enforcement in Riyadh is generally strict and predictable. Authorities focus on public order and community norms. That means that even if alcohol is legally available in a controlled venue, consumption that causes disturbance, displays public intoxication, or violates venue rules can still trigger severe consequences. Tourists and expats should conduct themselves with discretion and always follow venue-specific rules.

Practical Travel Advice: Planning Where and When to Drink

A short checklist of rules to follow

  • Carry valid ID at all times and expect ID checks in licensed venues.
  • Only purchase or consume alcohol inside authorized locations.
  • Never attempt to transport alcohol through public areas or across borders without proper authorization.
  • Avoid public intoxication and never drink-and-drive.
  • If you live in a compound, check official compound policy and do not assume private tolerance equals legality.

(Use this checklist as a quick reference; the sections that follow expand on each item with actionable steps.)

Choosing accommodations with confidence

When planning a trip to Riyadh, select accommodations based on their service offering and the clarity of their policies. Many international hotel chains operating in Riyadh will clearly state whether they are permitted to serve alcohol and under what circumstances. If a property claims to offer a licensed bar or alcohol service, confirm that it is an official license, what types of beverages are permitted, and any rules about room service or minibar availability.

If you prefer an environment where alcohol is readily available and legally served, consider traveling to neighboring Gulf destinations where hotel licensing is more established. However, for a Riyadh-centered itinerary that focuses on cultural attractions, museums, and dining, choose hotels that emphasize their beverage programs, whether alcohol-free or licensed.

For hyper-local planning, you can consult our Riyadh travel resources to compare neighborhoods and hospitality offerings when deciding where to stay and dine.

Dining and nightlife: what to expect

Riyadh’s dining scene has matured rapidly: creative chefs, modern cafes, and sophisticated restaurants deliver compelling non-alcoholic beverage programs, inventive mocktails, and fine dining without alcohol. Where licensed alcohol is available, it will usually be restricted to clearly marked bars or private dining rooms within the hotel or resort.

If you prefer a meal with alcohol, choose a licensed venue and confirm service windows and dress codes. Many upscale restaurants will partner with their hotels to provide private dining experiences where alcohol can be served in compliance with licensing regulations.

Transport considerations

Never drink and drive. Law enforcement takes this issue seriously with strict penalties for driving under the influence. Use ride-hailing apps and authorized taxis to get home from licensed venues. For longer stays, hire a driver or use hotel-arranged transport; many travelers in Riyadh rely on professional drivers when attending events or dining in the evening.

If you are flying in or out of Saudi Arabia with alcohol purchased in an authorized venue, check customs rules and airline policies carefully. Transporting alcohol across borders without proper documentation can lead to detention and confiscation.

Handling an encounter with authorities

If you are stopped by police or officials, remain calm, be respectful, and present identification when requested. Avoid argumentation or provocative behavior. If you face a legal situation, contact your embassy or consulate immediately and seek legal assistance. If you are a diplomat or accredited staff, follow your mission’s internal procedures and documentation.

Here is a practical list to follow if you are questioned regarding alcohol possession (this is the second and last list in this article):

  1. Stay calm, present ID, and cooperate.  
  2. Explain your status clearly (visitor, resident, diplomat) and show relevant documentation.  
  3. Request translation or consular assistance if needed.  
  4. If detained, ask for legal counsel and contact your embassy.

These steps prioritize de-escalation and access to legal protections.

Comparing Riyadh With Other Saudi Cities and the Region

Riyadh versus Jeddah, Makkah, and Madinah

Riyadh’s approach to alcohol regulation is shaped by both national law and the capital’s central role in policy implementation. Jeddah, a cosmopolitan port city with a long history of international interaction, typically reflects similar national constraints but may have different enforcement nuances. Makkah and Madinah, as the two holiest cities of Islam, have the strictest enforcement in matters of public morality and religion; any relaxation of alcohol rules is extremely unlikely in those cities, and special sensitivity applies to residents and visitors alike. When planning trips that include religious sites, visitors must assume zero tolerance for alcohol-related activity.

Regional comparisons: what neighboring Gulf countries do

Neighboring Gulf states have broad differences in how they regulate alcohol. Some countries permit sale and licensed consumption in hotels, restaurants, and specific retail outlets; others maintain strict bans. For travelers who prioritize easy access to alcohol, nearby destinations may offer greater predictability. However, the Kingdom’s announced licensing plans deliberately limit availability to controlled settings and emphasize cultural sensitivity, distinguishing its approach from neighbors’ models that allow broader public consumption.

How these differences influence travel choices

If having open access to alcoholic beverages is a central travel priority, choose your destination with that in mind. For travelers whose primary aim is cultural discovery, city exploration, and high-quality hospitality free of alcohol, Riyadh remains an exceptional and increasingly accessible destination. Carefully planned accommodation and event choices let you enjoy sophisticated dining and world-class hospitality while fully respecting local norms.

Cultural Etiquette and Responsible Behavior

Respect local norms

Understanding and respecting local customs is the single most important way to avoid legal and social difficulties. Saudi culture places a premium on modest behavior, discretion, and adherence to community standards. When licensed alcohol is present, consumption is expected to be private and respectful rather than public or boisterous.

Responsible service and training

Licensed venues are expected to train staff in responsible service, including age verification, sobriety checks, and refusing service when necessary. If a server or manager asks you to stop drinking or to leave a venue due to behavior concerns, comply immediately. Responsible behavior protects you and the people around you.

Dressing and conduct

Public conduct and attire still matter. Even in licensed venues, dress codes and standards of behavior may apply. If attending private or formal gatherings at licensed hotels or diplomatic functions, follow the host’s instructions and err on the side of conservative dress and decorum.

  4. Confirm dining options and private event rules with hotels or promoters.

Enforcement Risks, Missteps to Avoid, and How to Recover from Problems

Common mistakes visitors make

Many problems stem from assumption rather than information. Common mistakes include:

  • Assuming that what’s available in neighboring countries is available in Riyadh.
  • Believing private tolerance equals legal permission.
  • Failing to verify license status with hotels or event organizers.
  • Speaking publicly about illicit sources or offering alcohol to others irresponsibly.

Avoid these pitfalls by using reliable planning resources and confirming policies in writing when possible.

If something goes wrong

If you are detained or face charges related to alcohol, proceed calmly: present ID, ask for consular access, and consult legal counsel. If you are a foreigner, contacting your embassy is an immediate step. Document the encounter and follow local procedures; emotional reactions can complicate the situation.

Travel insurance and legal funds

Consider insurance add-ons that cover legal expenses and repatriation if relevant. Keep digital copies of passports, visas, and emergency numbers. For long-term residents, establish relationships with local attorneys who understand both Saudi law and expatriate circumstances.

Frequently Asked Questions

1) Can tourists buy alcohol in Riyadh?

No. Tourists cannot buy alcohol in public retail outlets in Riyadh. The only publicly reported retail outlet is limited to non-Muslim diplomatic personnel. Any broader retail sales are part of targeted licensing plans for specific venues and are not generally available to tourists across the city.

2) Are hotels in Riyadh allowed to serve alcohol?

As of now, most hotels in Riyadh do not serve alcohol openly. Licensing plans discussed by authorities envisage that a limited number of five-star hotels and luxury resorts could become licensed in specified zones. Always confirm directly with the hotel before booking and read the property’s policy.

3) What happens if someone is found with alcohol in Riyadh?

Possession or consumption of alcohol by the general public can lead to fines, detention, deportation for expatriates, and other legal sanctions. If you are detained, remain calm, present identification, and contact your embassy or consulate for assistance.
