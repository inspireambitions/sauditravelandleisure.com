Core Cultural Principles to Understand

The Legal Red Lines: Laws You Must Not Ignore

Alcohol, Drugs, and Contraband

Saudi Arabia enforces a strict prohibition on alcohol and most recreational drugs. Possession, consumption, or importation can lead to severe penalties including fines, imprisonment, or deportation. This is non-negotiable: do not bring alcohol, drink in private, or try to obtain banned substances while in Riyadh.

Speech and Social Media

Public criticism of the government, the royal family, or Islam can have legal consequences. Posting provocative content online or commenting publicly about sensitive matters is risky. Keep political discussion neutral and avoid sharing content that might be perceived as insulting or destabilizing.

Entry to Restricted Religious Areas

Mecca and parts of Medina are reserved for Muslim worshippers. Attempting to enter restricted zones is illegal. If you plan to travel beyond Riyadh, review guidelines and respect access restrictions; if in doubt, ask local authorities or your accommodation.

Photography Restrictions

Photos of military sites, certain government buildings, airports, and some public facilities are prohibited. Even in public spaces, avoid photographing people without permission. If youâre unsure whether a building is sensitive, err on the side of caution and ask.
