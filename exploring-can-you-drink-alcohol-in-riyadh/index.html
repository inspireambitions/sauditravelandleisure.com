The Current Legal Status of Alcohol Consumption

Right now, alcohol consumption in Riyadh is not allowed at all. This goes for both people who live there and tourists. Saudi authorities are strict when it comes to these alcohol laws. You cannot buy or drink alcoholic beverages anywhere, including your own home. If you break this rule, it is seen as a crime. The prohibition is for everyone, including non-Muslims and Saudi citizens. These laws help show the kingdom芒聙聶s dedication to supporting Islamic values.

If you wonder if there are any ways around this, the answer is simple. There is no hotel, restaurant, or shop in Riyadh that can legally serve or sell alcohol. You are not allowed to import or have alcoholic beverages with you, even in your own luggage. If you do, you could face big penalties.

The only small and controlled exception is for non-Muslim foreign diplomats. In January 2024, Saudi Arabia allowed its first alcohol shop to open in more than 70 years. The shop is in the Diplomatic Quarter of Riyadh. This alcohol shop is only for diplomats who are registered with the government. There are strict rules and limits, so drinks sold through this shop are carefully tracked. For everyone else, the ban on alcohol in the city is complete.

Right now, there are no public retail sales. There are also no places or venues where tourists or expatriates can get alcohol out in the open.

Still, the Vision 2030 development plan hints that there may be some changes in the future. These changes could happen at luxury resorts or in zones for tourists.
