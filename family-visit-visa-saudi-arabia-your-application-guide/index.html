Who Can Invite Family Members to Saudi Arabia?

A family visit visa application in Saudi Arabia can only work if you have a real sponsor in the country. This sponsor can be:

  • A Saudi citizen who can invite their close family or, in some cases, their friends.
  • An expat who lives and works in Saudi Arabia and holds a valid Iqama (it should not be a dependent or student).
  • The sponsor must be at least 18 years old and have a valid residency ID that lasts as long as the family visit.

Only people who have legal residency status and, for non-Saudis, a work visa, can ask someone to visit. The sponsor has to make sure the visitor follows all the visa rules and behaves well during the stay. For example, a Saudi engineer can invite his wife and kids, while an expat teacher can bring her parents for a visit. It is very important that the sponsor gives every detail the right way, as mistakes can slow things down or make the family visit visa form get rejected.

This is the way things work with family visit visas in Saudi Arabia. If you follow these rules and use the right sponsor, your Saudi visit visa application has a much better chance of going well.

Step-by-Step Application Process for a Saudi Family Visit Visa

Starting your application for a family visit visa to Saudi Arabia has a few important steps. First, the sponsor goes online to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs website. There, the sponsor sends in an invitation request and gets an authorisation slip. Once the Ministry of Foreign Affairs gives the approval, the visitor fills out the visa form, gets the needed health insurance, and gathers proof of relationship documents. Then, the person applying hands over the file with all papers to the embassy. Each of these steps needs to be done fully and carefully to cut down on any processing delays. Submitting the Application and Payment Procedures

Once your papers are all ready, you need to follow the official steps for submission and payment to finish your application.

Start by sending in all the needed papers. This will have your filled-out visa application form. Give these documents to the embassy of the kingdom of saudi arabia or a visa centre that is allowed to handle them. Make sure you attach the MOFA authorisation slip so it is easy to see. Most of the time, you pay the visa fees online. Some embassies let you pay at the office.

Key points to remember:

  • Bring the original documents and one copy for each one.
  • Visa fees are between $100 and $150 for every person. It depends on which insurance you pick.
  • You can pay with an international credit card or debit card. Some places also take cash.
  • Always keep the receipts for payments and the emails that show your payment was successful.

It usually takes 7 to 10 business days to process your saudi visit visa. This time can change, though. The best way is to apply early, make your payments right, and have your documents organised. How long is the family visit visa valid and can it be extended?

Family visit visas for Saudi Arabia are good from the date of issue. With this type of entry visa, you can stay in Saudi for about 30 to 90 days at one time. You may be able to extend your stay. To do this, you need to use the Ministry of Foreign Affairs site or the Absher platform. The approval will depend on the current rules and the nationality of the person applying for the visa.

What are common reasons for rejection of a Saudi family visit visa?

Rejections often happen when you do not have the right papers or if your proof of relationship does not match. If you use the wrong birth certificate, you could get denied too. Sometimes, they think people want the visa to work, which is not allowed. Not showing a clear marriage or birth certificate is also a reason people get rejected. If you are part of drug trafficking or have broken rules on your last visa, that can lead to a no as well.

Can you convert a family visit visa into a residence visa within Saudi Arabia?

You can't change a family visit visa into a residence visa while you are still in Saudi Arabia. If you want to stay in the country for a long time, you have to leave first. The applicants must go back to their home country, apply for a residence visa there, and then follow the rules for moving to Saudi Arabia. This is the way anyone on a visit visa to Saudi must do if they wish to live with their family.
