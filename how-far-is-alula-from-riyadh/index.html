How Far Is AlUla From Riyadh

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This page remains available for reference, but we consolidated this search intent under How Far Is Al Ula From Riyadh.

Introduction

Saudi Arabia’s tourism boom has made a weekend in AlUla one of the most sought-after escapes for residents of Riyadh and international visitors alike. More than ever, travelers ask one simple logistical question before they plan: how far is AlUla from Riyadh, and what’s the best way to get there?

Short answer: By air, the straight-line distance from Riyadh to AlUla is roughly 909 kilometers (about 565 miles), and nonstop flights take around 1 hour 30–1 hour 45 minutes; by road, expect roughly 900–1,000 kilometers depending on the route, which translates into a driving time of about 9 to 11 hours with sensible breaks. Bus services and guided tour transfers are also available and take significantly longer, though they offer a budget-friendly alternative or a relaxed way to take in the landscape.

This article will go beyond the simple distance. I’ll explain the exact distances by different measures, compare travel modes (flight, car, bus), provide realistic door-to-door time estimates, describe the key logistics you must plan, offer practical driving advice and safety tips, and map out ready-to-use itineraries so you can decide whether to fly for speed or drive for discovery. Where useful, I’ll point you to trusted resources and planning pages so you can convert inspiration into a seamless, well-planned trip.

My main message: Understanding the nuance between pure distance and door-to-door travel time is what will make your Riyadh-to-AlUla trip efficient, enjoyable, and culturally rewarding. With the right planning, this journey is an achievable short break or the gateway to a longer exploration of northwest Saudi Arabia.

How Distance Is Measured: Air Distance vs. Road Distance

Air (Great-Circle) Distance: What the number means

The commonly quoted figure—about 909 kilometers—refers to the great-circle, or “as-the-crow-flies,” distance between Riyadh and AlUla. This is useful for comparing flight durations, because aircraft follow more direct routes than cars. When you see “565 miles” or “909 km” in flight search results, that’s the metric used to estimate airborne time.

A nonstop flight covering this distance typically takes roughly 1 hour 30 minutes to 1 hour 45 minutes of airtime between King Khalid International (RUH) in Riyadh and AlUla’s airport (ULH). Add boarding, taxiing, and deplaning, and door-to-door time for flying is usually closer to 3–4 hours depending on how far you are from each airport and the efficiency of transfers.

Road Distance: Practical routes and why numbers vary

Road distance depends on the route chosen. There are two practical corridors you might use depending on your priorities:

  • The more direct interior route via Highway 65 and 50/55 that cuts across central Saudi landscapes and is often faster in terms of uninterrupted driving.
  • A slightly longer route that follows major paved highways but may offer more reliable services and better connectivity for stops.

Because of these options and measurements taken from different mapping tools, driving distances reported online range from about 900 km to 1,050 km. For planning, use a realistic range: expect 970–1,020 kilometers on the most common driving routes between Riyadh and AlUla.

Road distance matters more than straight-line for budgeting fuel, estimating breaks, and planning overnight stops.

Bus and Transfer Distances

Bus routes will follow the road network, so the distance mirrors the driving route. However, scheduled bus services can take longer because of fixed stops and less flexible departure times—expect journeys of 10–16 hours depending on operator, route, and any transfers.

Quick Travel Time Summary by Mode

  • Air: ~1h 30–1h 45m in the air; 3–4 hours door-to-door including security, transfers, and travel to/from airports.
  • Car: ~9–11 hours driving time for 970–1,020 km, depending on pace, route, and stops.
  • Bus: ~10–16 hours depending on operator and stops; budget option with fixed schedules.

Choosing How to Travel: Pros, Cons and When Each Option Makes Sense

Flying from Riyadh to AlUla — fastest and most predictable

Flying is the default choice for time-pressed travelers. Nonstop services operate regularly on the Riyadh–AlUla route through carriers such as Flynas and Saudia, with multiple weekly departures.

Advantages: fastest door-to-door time, minimal fatigue, convenient for short trips and weekend getaways, and often competitively priced if booked in advance.

Drawbacks: airport transfers add time; last-minute fares can be high during peak seasons; flights land at AlUla’s compact airport which can require ground transfers to hotels and sites.

When to fly: If you have 48–72 hours from Riyadh and want to maximize time on the ground in AlUla.

Driving — flexibility, discovery, and control

Driving converts a transfer into part of the experience. The landscape between Riyadh and AlUla is austere and beautiful in its own way—vast desert plains with distant escarpments—so a road trip is a different kind of reward.

Advantages: control over timing and stops, ability to detour to Madinah or other towns, lower cost-per-person for groups, easy to carry outdoor gear.

Drawbacks: long hours behind the wheel, potential for fatigue, need to plan fuel and rest stops, seasonal heat considerations, and rental car regulations (ensure your rental and insurance allow cross-region travel).

When to drive: If you value a slower pace, want to combine other stops like Madinah, or travel with a small group comfortable with long-distance driving.

Bus and coach transfers — budget-friendly and relaxed

Long-distance buses via operators such as SAPTCO run between major Saudi cities and can be a sensible budget option.

Advantages: lower cost, no driving stress, sometimes comfortable modern coaches.

Drawbacks: longer duration, limited schedules, less flexible stop-overs, potential crowding during peak travel windows.

When to take the bus: If budget is a top priority, you have time to spare, or you prefer a set schedule without driving.

Door-to-Door Time: How to Think About Your Total Journey

A realistic travel plan goes beyond airborne or in-car time. Consider these components:

  • Home to departure airport or car: Traffic in Riyadh can add significant time in morning and evening peaks.
  • Security and boarding: For domestic flights in Saudi Arabia, arrive 60–90 minutes before departure depending on your airline and class.
  • Flight time: ~1.5 hours in the air.
  • Arrival transfers in AlUla: The airport is small but hotels and archaeological sites are typically 30–60 minutes away by road.
  • Final transit: Taxis or hotel shuttles can affect total time.

Example door-to-door estimate for flying: 45–75 minutes transit to RUH + 60–90 minutes check-in + 1.5 hours flight + 30–60 minutes transfer in AlUla = roughly 4–6 hours total. For driving, add personal preparation and breaks to the core 9–11 hours of driving and plan for a full day on the road.

Step-By-Step Travel Planning: From Booking to Arrival

To keep this practical and directly actionable, follow this condensed stepwise framework for an efficient Riyadh–AlUla trip. (This is the second and final list in the article.)

  1. Pick dates and check major events in AlUla (festivals drive accommodation demand).
  2. Compare flight vs. driving times and costs for your party size; book flights early if traveling in winter.
  3. Reserve Hegra and AlUla site tickets in advance during high season.
  4. If driving, confirm rental company policies, fuel stops, and emergency contact info; map out rest stops roughly every 2–3 hours.
  5. Pack appropriately for desert conditions (sun protection, layers for nights) and download offline maps.
  6. Register trip details with your hotel and share your estimated arrival time.

Airports, Terminals and Ground Transfers

Riyadh: King Khalid International Airport (RUH)

King Khalid International is Riyadh’s major hub. Terminal procedures are efficient for domestic flights but can be busy during peak hours. Ride-hailing apps and airport taxis are widely used for transfers, as are private transfers arranged through hotels.

Tip: If you are traveling from central Riyadh, allow plenty of time to navigate city traffic—especially on Fridays and during evening peak periods.

AlUla: Prince Abdul Majeed bin Abdulaziz International Airport (ULH)

AlUla’s airport is purpose-built to serve tourism to the region. It’s compact and designed for efficient arrivals and departures. Many hotels and tour operators offer transfers from ULH; confirm pickup points and timings in advance.

Tip: If your flight lands late, reconfirm that your hotel provides late arrival service or arrange a pre-booked private transfer to avoid confusion.

Driving Routes and Practical Road Advice

The common driving corridor

Most drivers take a route that uses Riyadh’s artery northbound and then connects west toward AlUla using main highways that are well-paved and maintained. Services are available at major towns, but distances between service stations can be long—plan fuel stops deliberately.

Safety and comfort tips:

  • Drive during daylight for best visibility; desert highway lights are limited.
  • Observe speed limits; Saudi Arabia enforces speed and traffic rules with cameras and fines.
  • For solo drivers, plan relay driving or overnight stops to reduce fatigue risk.
  • Carry spare water, a basic first-aid kit, and a phone charger with a car adapter.
  • Check vehicle condition (tire pressure, coolant, oil) before long drives.

Scenic stops worth considering

A road trip can be enhanced by deliberate stops:

  • Medina (Madinah) is a logical historic detour and lies considerably closer to AlUla than Riyadh—consider an overnight there if you want to combine heritage with natural landscapes.
  • If traveling from the west or combining with coastal travel, Jeddah offers a coastal contrast and additional flight options.
  • Mountain towns such as Taif provide a different climate and greenery if you’re looking for variety en route.

Costs: Budgeting for Different Modes

Flying cost factors

Airfare varies by carrier, how far in advance you book, and seasonality. Economy fares on the Riyadh–AlUla route can be very reasonable if booked early; low-cost carriers sometimes have one-way deals that make short breaks economical.

Additional flight costs include luggage fees, airport transfers, and potentially higher rates during festival windows.

Driving costs

Calculate fuel assuming a typical fuel consumption for sedans or SUVs and use the driving distance estimate of approximately 1,000 km. Add tolls (if applicable on your chosen route), food stops, and overnight accommodation if you plan to break the journey. For groups, driving often reduces cost per person.

Bus costs

Buses are the most economical option but remember the trade-off is extended travel time and less flexibility.

Booking Tickets to AlUla’s Key Sites

AlUla’s major attractions, such as Hegra (Madain Salih), the old town, and guided experiences, often require pre-booked tickets—especially during the high season when cultural programs and festivals attract visitors. The Royal Commission for AlUla (RCU) manages many site bookings and tickets. Confirm entry requirements and whether guided tours are mandatory for certain sites.

Tip: If you’re traveling from Riyadh for a weekend, secure accommodation and any must-do site tickets at least 2–4 weeks in advance during winter.

Best Time to Travel Between Riyadh and AlUla

AlUla’s prime visitor months are the cooler months, roughly October through March. Daytime is comfortable for outdoor exploration, while evenings can be chilly in desert climates.

Riyadh’s urban heat in summer makes flying a more appealing choice in that season because you’ll minimize exposure to daytime heat during long drives. Winter months bring peak visitor numbers to AlUla for outdoor activities and cultural events; book early.

Accommodation and Where to Stay in AlUla

AlUla offers a range of options from boutique heritage hotels to luxury desert camps. Decide between staying near the oasis town center for convenience to restaurants and shops, or choose a remote desert lodge for immersive nights under the stars. Many lodges and hotels offer packages that include transfers and guided site visits; these can simplify logistics for travelers arriving from Riyadh.

Tip: If your priority is to visit Hegra early in the morning to avoid crowds, book a hotel close to the site or plan transfers that arrive the afternoon before.

Cultural Etiquette and Practical Tips for Traveling in KSA

Traveling between Riyadh and AlUla brings you into contact with Saudi culture and traditions. Respect local customs: dress modestly in public spaces and be mindful of local social norms. In smaller towns and rural areas, etiquette may be more conservative than in big cities. Public displays of affection are discouraged in public spaces; photography of people should be done politely and only with consent. Act respectfully at heritage sites and follow signage and guidance from local authorities and guides.

Combining AlUla With Other Stops: Smart Multi-City Plans

Riyadh — AlUla — Madinah

Madinah is a natural pairing for travelers who want to combine heritage and archaeology with spiritual or historic visits. Madinah is much closer to AlUla in road terms and is practical as a mid-point if you prefer to break a drive or wish to include a longer heritage component in your trip. If you fly, consider multi-city tickets or regional flights that hop between Riyadh, AlUla, and Madinah for efficiency.

Coastal detours: Jeddah and the Red Sea

For travelers with more time, a coastal extension through Jeddah provides seaside contrast and additional international flight options. If you’re planning a longer regional itinerary that includes the Red Sea, review flight and road logistics carefully—distances expand considerably, and travel time accumulates.

Practical Packing and Gear Advice for the Riyadh–AlUla Trip

Sun protection, breathable layered clothing, comfortable walking shoes, and a refillable water bottle are essentials. Nights can be cool, so bring a light insulating layer. If driving, include a small roadside emergency kit, extra water, and a physical map as a backup to offline GPS.

Safety, Healthcare, and Emergencies

Saudi roads are generally safe and well-maintained, but long-distance travel requires vigilance. Keep emergency numbers handy, ensure your health insurance covers travel within Saudi Arabia, and carry any necessary prescriptions in original packaging. Mobile coverage is extensive along major highways, but some remote stretches may have spotty reception—plan accordingly.

Sample Itineraries: How to Turn a Distance Into An Experience

24–48 Hour Sprint (Fly)

Arrive early from Riyadh, transfer to your hotel, and spend a day visiting Hegra, Elephant Rock, and the AlUla Old Town. This option maximizes time on the ground.

3–4 Day Road Adventure (Drive)

Leave Riyadh early, make a midpoint stop (for example, Medina or a regional town), arrive in AlUla the following day, and use two full days to combine guided archaeological tours with a desert hike or a stargazing experience.

Week-Long Cultural Loop (Combine Cities)

Fly into Riyadh or Jeddah for a multi-city route that includes AlUla, Madinah, and coastal or mountain detours. This is best for travelers who want depth and diversity in landscapes and cultural experiences.

Common Mistakes Travelers Make (And How to Avoid Them)

Most mistakes are logistical rather than dramatic: underestimating transfer times, failing to pre-book site tickets during peak seasons, and assuming fuel or services are always available on long highway stretches. Avoid these by planning conservative time buffers, booking key experiences in advance, and confirming vehicle and insurance terms if you’re renting.

Local Transport in AlUla: Getting Around Once You Arrive

Taxis and ride-hailing apps operate in AlUla, but for remote sites and a full program of exploration, most visitors rely on organized tours or rental cars. If your hotel offers included transfers for site visits, this can simplify logistics and let you focus on the experience rather than driving.

Practical Checklist Before You Leave Riyadh

  • Confirm flights or rental reservations and review cancellation policies.
  • Reserve accommodation and any guided tours or site tickets in AlUla.
  • Check vehicle documents and confirm fuel and maintenance if driving.
  • Download offline maps and emergency contacts.
  • Prepare clothing and sun protection suitable for variable desert temperatures.

Environmental Responsibility and Respect for Sites

AlUla’s archaeological sites are fragile. Respect barriers and signage, follow your guide’s instructions, and avoid removing anything from sites. Many visitor centers explain preservation efforts and how travelers can contribute—take a moment to learn and act responsibly.

Final Practical Tips and Insider Notes

  • Book Hegra tickets early if traveling in winter; demand rises dramatically during festival weeks.
  • For car rentals, verify cross-region permissions and whether satellite navigation is provided as standard.
  • If planning a last-minute weekend, check flight availability—but be prepared for higher prices.
  • Consider guided small-group tours if you want deep context combined with smooth logistics; many operators in AlUla specialize in history-led and geology-led itineraries.

Conclusion

Understanding the difference between the straight-line distance of roughly 909 kilometers and the practical road distance of about 970–1,020 kilometers—and the way those distances translate into real travel times—will help you choose the approach that suits your schedule, budget, and travel style. Whether you fly for speed or drive for discovery, the journey from Riyadh to AlUla is an accessible and rewarding way to experience Saudi Arabia’s landscapes and heritage.

FAQ

How long is the flight from Riyadh to AlUla?

A nonstop flight typically takes about 1 hour 30 minutes to 1 hour 45 minutes in the air. With check-in and transfers, allow roughly 3–4 hours door-to-door.

What is the driving time between Riyadh and AlUla?

Expect approximately 9 to 11 hours of driving for the roughly 970–1,020 kilometer road distance, depending on route, traffic, and stops.

Are there direct buses from Riyadh to AlUla?

Yes, long-distance bus services operate on this corridor, but they take longer than driving and flying—journey times can range from 10 to 16 hours depending on operator and stops.

Do I need to book AlUla site tickets in advance?

For peak season and organized experiences (especially Hegra), booking in advance is strongly recommended. Many guided tours and special events sell out early, so secure key tickets before you travel.
