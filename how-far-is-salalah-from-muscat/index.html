Route Details and On-the-Ground Guidance

This highway corridor stays closer to the sea for long stretches and connects major waypoints: Sur, Wadi Shab (if you take a detour), Duqm, Thumrait and finally Salalah. The road is largely modern and straight outside urban stretches.

Frequently Asked Practical Questions

Should I fly or drive between Muscat and Salalah?

If time is limited or you want to maximize sightseeing time in Salalah, fly one way and consider driving the return to experience the landscape. If the journey itself is a major part of your travel goals, drive the coastal route and plan for a one- or two-night stopover to avoid fatigue.

How long does a bus take and is it comfortable?

Official intercity buses take roughly 11芒聙聯13 hours and provide a budget-friendly, relaxed option with scheduled stops. Comfort depends on operator and coach class; book reputable operator seats in advance, especially during peak months.

Is it safe to drive at night on the route?

Road quality is generally good, but night driving increases the risk due to reduced visibility, potential wildlife or livestock near roads, and fewer services. Start early and aim to complete major stretches during daylight.

What paperwork and checks should I prepare before driving?

Carry your driving license, passport, visa documents, rental agreement, vehicle insurance, and emergency contact information. Confirm rental insurance covers long-distance travel and regional conditions.
