How Hot Does It Get in Riyadh

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Introduction

Riyadh's skyline of modern towers and wide boulevards sits above one of the Kingdom's most demanding climates. The city is a magnet for business travelers, new residents, and curious visitors alike—and knowing what the heat actually feels like is essential to plan your days, your wardrobe, and your itinerary with confidence.

Short answer: Riyadh regularly reaches daytime highs above 40°C (104°F) during its long hot season, with July and August commonly pushing peak daytime temperatures into the 45–48°C (113–118°F) range on the hottest days. Nights offer relief relative to the daytime but can still remain warm, often in the mid- to high 20s°C (mid-70s to mid-80s°F) through the summer months.

In this article you will find the full temperature picture by month, how "dry desert heat" compares with humid Gulf heat, what the real health and logistical risks are, and the exact strategies experienced travelers and locals use to stay comfortable and safe. This is the blueprint to travel—and live—well in Riyadh’s heat so you can plan outdoor activities, select appropriate clothing, and schedule your days to make the most of the Kingdom’s capital.

Climate at a Glance: What the Numbers Mean

Temperature Range by Season

Riyadh's climate is classically desert: very hot summers, mild winters, and low annual rainfall. Breaking the year into clear bands helps set expectations.

  • Winter (late November–February): daytime highs typically between 18–24°C (64–75°F), nighttime lows occasionally dip near freezing on unusually cold nights but more commonly sit between 7–12°C (45–54°F).
  • Shoulder seasons (March–April, October–November): warm days ranging from the high 20s to low 30s°C (80s°F), with comfortable nights.
  • Hot season (May–September): sustained heat, with average daytime highs above 38°C (100°F) from May and averages topping 43°C (110°F) in July and August. On extreme days, temperatures have been recorded above 48°C (118°F).

Geographically, Riyadh sits on a plateau around 600 meters (about 2,000 feet) above sea level on the Najd, which moderates humidity but does not reduce daytime heat intensity.

Monthly Averages and What to Expect

Numbers give clarity, but travellers want to know what those figures actually feel like:

  • May: rapid move into hot-weather mode—days often exceed 39–40°C (102–104°F), and shade becomes essential.
  • June–September: the peak heat period; average highs are commonly 42–45°C (108–113°F), with July typically the hottest month.
  • October: still hot in early October, but temperatures moderate quickly after the first half of the month.
  • November–March: ideal outdoor season—mild, blue-sky days perfect for sightseeing and long walks.

When planning, always look at recent daily forecasts for exact highs and lows since heatwaves can push temperatures above historical averages.

Records and Extremes

Riyadh is one of the hottest capital cities in the world. Practical planning should consider records: historically, extreme days have reached the upper 40s °C (118–120°F) during heatwaves. While rare, these days require special precautions and often lead to schedule changes for outdoor activities.

Why Riyadh Feels Different: Dry Heat vs. Humid Heat

The Role of Low Humidity

Riyadh’s heat is characterized by very low relative humidity, often in the single digits or low teens during summer. That dryness dramatically changes how heat feels and how the body cools itself.

When humidity is low, sweat evaporates efficiently, which can make high temperatures more tolerable than in humid coastal cities. However, this "dry heat" can be a false friend—evaporation causes rapid fluid loss, increasing the risk of dehydration even when you don't feel as sweaty.

Comparing Riyadh With the Coast

Contrasting Riyadh with coastal cities helps travelers choose packing and pacing strategies. For a coastal comparison and to understand how humidity changes comfort levels, check our overview of the coastal climate in coastal climate comparison.

Coastal cities like Jeddah and the Eastern Province (represented by cities such as Dammam and Al Khobar) combine high temperatures with high humidity. The same temperature will feel substantially hotter on the coast because sweat does not evaporate as readily, elevating apparent temperature or "heat index" and increasing perceived discomfort and health risk.

Daily Rhythm of Heat: Day vs Night

Peak Heat Hours

The hottest time of day is typically mid-afternoon, from roughly 12:00 to 16:00, when solar radiation and urban surface heating combine. In summer, staying outdoors during these hours is not recommended unless necessary and well-prepared.

Nighttime Cooling

Unlike humid climates, Riyadh usually cools at night, sometimes significantly. Even during July and August, night temperatures can drop into the mid-20s°C (mid-70s°F), offering respite. This diurnal range can be an advantage: evenings and early mornings are ideal for outdoor activities.

Heat Hazards: Health, Gaps in Infrastructure, and the Unexpected

Health Risks from Extreme Heat

Even with low humidity, the main medical concerns are dehydration, heat exhaustion, and heatstroke. Symptoms to watch for include dizziness, rapid heartbeat, headache, extreme weakness, confusion, and lack of sweating in the most serious cases.

Plan for prevention: regular hydration, scheduled breaks in air-conditioned environments, and prioritizing shaded routes and early-morning activity.

Vulnerable Groups

Children, older adults, and people with cardiovascular or respiratory conditions are especially vulnerable. Pregnant travelers and those acclimated to cooler climates should exercise caution and seek medical guidance when planning prolonged outdoor exposure.

Infrastructure Gaps and Urban Heat

While Riyadh is modern with excellent air-conditioned public spaces, side streets, parks, and construction zones can be exposed and extremely hot. Flash flooding can occur during rare intense rains, and sandstorms in spring reduce visibility and air quality—both factors that can complicate travel during certain times of year.

Practical Strategies to Stay Safe and Comfortable

Timing Your Day: The Heat-Aware Schedule

Adapting your day reduces stress and maximizes enjoyment. A practical daily rhythm looks like this:

  • Dawn–mid-morning (5:30–10:00): best time for outdoor exploring and walking tours.
  • Midday (10:00–16:00): plan indoor activities—museums, malls, markets, or a long lunch in an air-conditioned restaurant.
  • Late afternoon–evening (17:00–21:00): resume outdoor activities such as parks, gardens, or cultural districts when temperatures fall.

This framework lets you experience the city fully while avoiding the worst of the heat.

Clothing, Cultural Etiquette, and Sun Protection

Dressing for Riyadh is both practical and cultural. Lightweight, breathable fabrics in light colors are essential. For women, the traditional abaya is widely worn and comes in lightweight materials—choose breathable versions. Men typically wear a long-sleeved shirt or a light thawb to protect the skin from direct sun; loose styles promote airflow.

Sun protection tips:

  • Broad-brimmed hat or umbrella when walking outside.
  • Sunglasses with UV protection.
  • High-SPF, broad-spectrum sunscreen applied liberally and re-applied after perspiration.
  • UV-protective clothing if you plan extended outdoor time.

Hydration Strategy

Hydration must be intentional. The dry air masks fluid loss, so follow a hydration routine rather than drink only when thirsty. Carry a reusable water bottle and aim for regular small sips. For long outdoor stretches in summer, electrolyte solutions are helpful to replace salts lost through sweating.

Cooling Tools and Travel Gear

Small, portable items make a real difference: cooling towels, battery-operated portable fans, and insulated water bottles keep fluids cool. Pack lightweight, technical fabrics that wick sweat and dry quickly.

Quick heat-ready packing checklist:

  • Lightweight long-sleeve shirts and breathable abaya/thawb
  • Wide-brimmed hat or folding umbrella
  • High-SPF sunscreen and lip balm with SPF
  • Reusable insulated water bottle
  • Portable cooling towel or battery fan
  • Sunglasses with UV protection
  • Closed-toe walking shoes and sandals for shade breaks

Travel Logistics in Extreme Temperatures

Transport: Car, Taxi, and Public Transit

Riyadh is a car-centric city. If you’re driving, ensure the vehicle's air conditioning is working well and keep bottled water in the car. Park in shade where possible—interior temperatures in parked cars can climb to dangerous levels very quickly.

Taxi apps and ride-hailing services are abundant; always choose an air-conditioned vehicle for comfort and safety. For metro travel and city planning, consult local transit updates, as metro lines and services reduce time spent outdoors.

Accommodation Choices

Choose hotels or apartments with reliable air conditioning and good guest reviews about cooling performance. Rooftop pools and shaded courtyards are excellent amenities for managing the heat. Locations near green spaces or sheltered cultural districts allow more flexibility for evening outdoor plans.

Eating and Dining

Midday meals in Riyadh often take place indoors. Try local restaurants and cafés inside malls or cultural centers during peak heat hours. For pleasant outdoor dining, evenings are ideal once temperatures drop.

Activities and Itinerary Planning Around Heat

Morning Cultural Itinerary Example

Start early with a visit to significant cultural sites when temperatures are mild. Early-morning options might include an open-air historical site or a guided walking tour of a heritage district, followed by a late breakfast in a cool café.

Midday Indoor Options

Riyadh offers excellent indoor alternatives: museums, art galleries, large shopping centers, and contemporary entertainment venues. Plan your key indoor visits—such as museums or exhibitions—between 11:00 and 16:00.

Evening and Night Activities

Evenings in Riyadh come alive with outdoor dining, garden promenades, and night markets. Cooler temperatures and often clear skies make stargazing on the city outskirts or a dinner terrace an enjoyable experience.

Day Trips and Cooler Alternatives

For travelers seeking cooler or different landscapes, options like mountain retreats and desert experiences vary in temperature. A popular option for respite from city heat is visiting higher-altitude towns. Heat and Activity: Sports, Desert Excursions, and Special Events

Organized Desert Trips

Desert safaris and dune driving are signature experiences but are best scheduled early morning or late afternoon to avoid peak heat. Tour operators are experienced at timing activities to maximize comfort; choose operators that include shaded rest stops, water, and flexible schedules.

Outdoor Sports and Running

If you plan runs or outdoor workouts, early morning before sunrise is the best window. Many expats and locals make the most of shaded parks and indoor fitness centers with excellent climate control.

Festivals and Public Events

Seasonal festivals and public events in Riyadh are frequently timed to avoid extreme heat, often taking place in the cooler months or during evening hours. Check the current event calendar before booking to ensure dates align with comfortable weather.

Vehicles, Equipment, and Safety Considerations

Car Maintenance and Heat

High temperatures place stress on cars—engine cooling systems, tires, and batteries require attention. If you're renting, choose a vehicle with robust air conditioning. Carry extra coolant if driving long distances through remote areas and make sure your phone is charged and you have local emergency contacts saved.

Electronics and Heat

Electronics left in hot cars or direct sun can malfunction. Keep cameras, phones, and batteries in shaded, cool spaces and use insulated bags for long outdoor shoots.

Heat-Related Emergency Preparedness

Always know your hotel’s emergency procedures and the location of medical facilities. For longer stays, register in-country emergency numbers and know how to obtain local ambulance services. Carry basic first-aid items and make sure someone in your group knows how to recognize and respond to heatstroke.

Understanding Weather Events Unique to Riyadh

Sandstorms and Dust

Sandstorms are possible any time of year but are more common in spring. They reduce visibility and can aggravate respiratory conditions. On storm days, plan to stay indoors; masks and eye protection help if you must travel.

Flash Flooding

Although rainfall is sparse, intense rain events—especially in winter and spring—can produce flash floods in urban drainage-challenged areas. Check local weather alerts after rain events and avoid low-lying roads prone to flooding.

Thunderstorms

Thunderstorms with sudden gusts occur occasionally in transitional seasons. They can create localized weather hazards including dust storms and temporary road hazards.

Long-Term Stay: Living in Riyadh Through the Seasons

Acclimatization and Daily Life

For newcomers, a two-week acclimatization period usually suffices for the body to adapt to the dry heat. Practical daily adjustments—shifting exercise to early morning, planning outdoor time for evenings, and having reliable indoor workspaces—make life comfortable.

Housing and Utilities

Most modern housing has strong air-conditioning systems. Verify cooling capacity and maintenance routines before long-term rental commitments. Solar shading and insulated glazing in homes significantly improve comfort while reducing energy bills.

Work and Business Travel

Many business schedules adapt to heat patterns with midday breaks and evening networking. If your business involves fieldwork or site visits, coordinate timing with local contacts who understand seasonal constraints.

Planning a Trip: When to Go and How to Prepare

Best Months to Visit (Comfort Priorities)

If your priority is comfortable outdoor sightseeing and cultural exploration, the best months are November through March when days are mild and skies are clear. For cooler mountain excursions and flower seasons near Taif, spring months can be ideal. For a southern desert or archaeological focus, plan for shoulder months to avoid peak heat.

Booking Advice and Itinerary Tips

Book accommodations with good cooling and plan to schedule the most weather-sensitive activities in mornings and evenings. Allow flexibility for weather-related schedule changes. If combining Riyadh with coastal or eastern destinations, compare humidity expectations with our Eastern Province weather content to plan appropriately.

Special Considerations During Religious Observances

During Ramadan, daytime activity patterns adjust—many businesses and services operate on different schedules and outdoor activity rhythms shift, with evenings becoming more active. Respect local customs and adapt plans accordingly.

Common Mistakes Travelers Make—and How to Avoid Them

Many travelers underestimate Riyadh’s heat because it is a dry heat and believe the risk is less than in humid climates. The real mistake is underestimating fluid loss.

A second common error is scheduling all outdoor touring during midday. The simple reschedule to mornings and evenings increases comfort and safety dramatically.

Finally, travelers sometimes expect summer clothing alone to be sufficient. Including lightweight long sleeves and sun protection is necessary—exposed skin during long daytime exposure increases the risk of sunburn and heat stress.

The Special Role of Air Conditioning and Indoor Life

AC Is Essential—but Not Universal

Most hotels, malls, offices, and modern transport vehicles are well air-conditioned. Public spaces designed for tourists prioritize cooling, which is part of how daily life is organized. However, smaller shops, local markets, or temporary outdoor events may lack powerful cooling—plan accordingly.

Balancing Time Indoors and Outdoors

Enjoying Riyadh means blending indoor cultural immersion with outdoor moments timed to the city’s temperature rhythm. Use air-conditioned intervals as opportunities to explore longer museum exhibits or enjoy a relaxed lunch, then return outside when temperatures are more manageable.

Bringing It Together: A Blueprint for a Comfortable Riyadh Visit

Plan your trip with three practical pillars—timing, protection, and pacing.

  • Timing: schedule outdoor activities for early morning and evening. Reserve indoor attractions and longer transit for the midday heat.
  • Protection: pack sun protection, a hat, and an insulated water bottle; wear breathable layers that respect local customs.
  • Pacing: accept that midday is for rest, meals, or indoor culture. Resist the impulse to cram every daylight hour with outdoor sightseeing.

Conclusion

Riyadh’s heat is intense yet navigable with the right preparation. Understanding that summers bring frequent highs above 40°C (104°F), that nights can be significantly cooler, and that the dryness increases fluid loss shapes every practical decision—what to pack, how to structure days, and which activities to schedule. With access to modern cooling, shaded urban design, and a rhythm of early-morning and evening activity, travelers and residents alike can enjoy the city’s cultural richness without being overwhelmed by its climate.

FAQ

How high do Riyadh temperatures get in Celsius and Fahrenheit during the hottest months?

In July and August, average daytime highs commonly sit around 43–45°C (109–113°F), and extreme heat events have pushed daily maximums near 48°C (118°F). Nighttime temperatures usually drop into the mid-20s°C (mid-70s to mid-80s°F).

Is Riyadh’s heat more dangerous than coastal Saudi cities?

Both environments carry risks. Riyadh’s dry heat allows for more efficient sweat evaporation but masks dehydration, while coastal regions combine heat with high humidity, increasing perceived temperature and sweat retention. Risk mitigation differs: in Riyadh, prioritize hydration and shade; on the coast, prioritize avoiding prolonged exertion during humid afternoons.

What is the best month to visit Riyadh for outdoor activities?

November through March offer the most comfortable outdoor weather—mild days, cool nights, and clear skies—ideal for cultural sites, walking tours, and outdoor dining.

How should I adjust a sightseeing itinerary to avoid heat-related problems?

Structure outdoor sightseeing for sunrise through mid-morning, then use midday for museums, shopping, and air-conditioned experiences. Resume outdoor activities late afternoon and evening. Carry water, sun protection, and a small cooling towel for comfort during transitions.
