Transportation: Cars, Metro, and Getting Around

Driving Culture and Car Ownership

Riyadh is a car-first city. Personal vehicles are the primary way to get around, and many households employ drivers to manage errands and school runs. If you plan to drive, obtain a Saudi driving license after arrival; many expats convert their existing license within a prescribed timeframe.

Road conditions are good, but be prepared for aggressive driving behaviors and heavy traffic during school runs and evening mall hours. For safety, many locals recommend larger vehicles for visibility and road presence.

Taxis, Ride-Hailing, and the Emerging Metro

Ride-hailing apps like Careem and Uber operate widely and are a practical alternative to owning a car. Local taxis are available by app or phone and can be used for shorter trips.

The Riyadh Metro is an essential long-term development for the city. As the network expands, expect major improvements in commute times and a gradual shift in the urban mobility pattern. For now, plan commutes around the realities of car-based traffic.
