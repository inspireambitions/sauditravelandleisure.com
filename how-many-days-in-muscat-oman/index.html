Getting Around Muscat ГўВЂВ” Practical Transport

Muscat covers a long coastal strip. Public transport is limited, so most visitors prefer private hire, taxis, or rental cars.

Driving: Renting is straightforward and gives the most flexibility for day trips. Roads are generally excellent; highways are modern and signage is clear, but mountain roads can be narrow and winding. For desert driving and some mountain tracks, a 4x4 is highly recommended. Always check local rules on speed limits and watch for unmarked speed bumps and livestock outside urban zones.

Taxis and ride-hailing: Taxis are plentiful in Muscat; app-based services and hotel-arranged transfers offer convenience. For airport pickups, pre-booked transfers add peace of mind.

Organized tours: If you prefer an easier option, book local guided day trips for Wadi Shab, Daymaniyat snorkeling, or Nizwa excursions. These relieve navigation stress and often include local insight youГўВЂВ™ll miss on your own.

When planning cross-border driving (e.g., from the UAE), check insurance and border documentation; many rental contracts restrict cross-border travel.

Safety, Health, and Practical Tips

  • Health: Tap water in Muscat is generally safe but many travelers prefer bottled water. Bring sun protection and a basic first-aid kit.

  • Connectivity: Buy an Omani SIM on arrival if you need reliable data for navigation. Download offline maps for remote day trips.
