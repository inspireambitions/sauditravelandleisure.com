Embassy or Consulate Application

Applying through the UAE embassy or consulate in your country is a formal route often chosen for diplomatic, official, or other special visa categories. Processing times vary widely by office but often require several days to a couple of weeks.

Common Delays, Rejections, and How to Avoid Them

Even when processing windows are short, certain issues regularly lead to delays or denials. Anticipate and address the following:

  • Name mismatches: Your name on the visa application must match your passport and flight booking exactly. Minor inconsistencies can require correction and reprocessing.
  • Missing or incorrect supporting documents: Hotels, airlines, or immigration may request original documents at arrival or during processing. Always provide clear, legible scans when applying online.
  • Sponsor discrepancies: If you are using a company or family sponsor, confirm they have submitted their side of the paperwork correctly and that their contact details are current.

If your visa is delayed beyond the stated timeframe, escalate politely: contact the agency that submitted your application (airline or hotel), ask for reference numbers, and request written confirmation of the expected processing timeline.

For planning simplicity, if you know you may wish to stay beyond your initial permit, apply for a longer-duration tourist visa (where available) or confirm extension policies at application time.
