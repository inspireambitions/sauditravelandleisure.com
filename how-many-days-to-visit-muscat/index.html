Deep Dive: What To Do In Muscat (By Interest)

Cultural and Architectural Highlights

Sultan Qaboos Grand Mosque

This mosque is a modern architectural landmark and one of the most visited sites in Muscat. Non-Muslim visitors can enter during designated morning hours; women should cover head and limbs (abayas are usually available at the entrance). Visit early to avoid crowds and heat.

Mutrah Corniche and Mutrah Souq

The Corniche offers a gentle, scenic waterfront stroll with views across the harbor. Mutrah Souq is ideal for browsing frankincense, Omani silver, khanjars (traditional daggers), and textiles. Visit in late afternoon or early evening when temperatures drop and the souq feels most atmospheric.

Wadi Shab and Bimmah Sinkhole are popular day trips and can take most of a day each. Jebel Akhdar offers high-elevation hiking and terraced villages, but requires appropriate vehicle capabilities (often 4x4) and a couple of days for meaningful exploration.
