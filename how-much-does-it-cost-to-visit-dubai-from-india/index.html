How Prices Vary: Core Variables That Drive Cost

Practical Pitfalls and Mistakes to Avoid

Under-budgeting daily expenses

Many travellers forget to include smaller items‚Äîairport transfers, tourist taxes or resort fees, bottled water, and tipping. These add up. Add a 10‚Äì15% contingency to your estimated daily spend.

Leaving visa and insurance to the last minute

Visas can be delayed and insurance policies may have waiting periods. Secure both early to avoid last-minute price shocks or denied boarding.

Over-scheduling

Dubai can feel like a fast-moving city: skyscrapers, malls, and attractions. Build rest windows into your itinerary to recover from jet lag and really enjoy marquee experiences like a sunset desert safari or a leisurely Marina dinner.

Ignoring local customs and regulations

Dubai is cosmopolitan but observant of local laws. Know alcohol rules, public behavior expectations, and dress codes for certain places. It keeps you out of expensive fines and awkward situations.

Currency, Payments and Financial Tips

The UAE dirham (AED) is widely accepted. Credit cards are accepted almost everywhere in Dubai, but have some cash for small purchases, tips, and market stalls. Notify your bank for international travel and check card foreign transaction fees. ATMs are plentiful; withdraw larger amounts to avoid repeated ATM fees.

Carry a small buffer in cash for purchases at local markets and small taxis when card payment is not practical.
