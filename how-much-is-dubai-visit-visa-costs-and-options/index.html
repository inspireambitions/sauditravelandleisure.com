How Much Is Dubai Visit Visa: Costs and Options

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Dubai remains one of the world’s most visited cities, and for travelers planning a short visit the first practical question is simple: how much is Dubai visit visa? The price you pay depends on where and how you apply, your nationality, the length of stay you want, and whether you need a single or multiple entry permit. Understanding the different channels and cost drivers lets you budget accurately and avoid surprises at arrival.

Short answer: For many nationalities a short Dubai visit visa can be free on arrival; for prearranged visas typical fees range from roughly USD 10–USD 250 depending on type (transit, 30-day, 60-day, single or multiple entry) plus any service fees and VAT. Extensions, sponsor requirements, and special national rules can add charges, and overstaying carries a standard AED 50 per day fine.

This article explains the visa categories, who is eligible for visa-free entry, the common official fee ranges, the additional costs charged by airlines and visa service providers, and the step-by-step process to apply correctly. Along the way you’ll find practical checklists, common mistakes to avoid, and travel-planning advice—especially for visitors traveling from Saudi Arabia or combining Dubai with neighboring Gulf destinations. The goal is to give you a clear cost picture, the legal requirements, and the planning framework to secure the correct visa without overpaying.

Understanding Dubai Visit Visa Types

Visa On Arrival: Who Gets It, and For How Long

The UAE operates a tiered visa-on-arrival scheme. Passport holders from a large number of countries receive a stamped visit visa without any prior arrangements; others can get longer on-arrival visas if their passport is from an eligible country. The main on-arrival categories you’ll encounter are short-stay (30 days), longer stays (90 days multiple entry valid for six months), and in very limited cases, 180-day arrangements for specific nationalities.

Visa on arrival is free when it applies. The practical implication is that many travelers do not pay anything for entry beyond their flight and accommodation. Always check your passport eligibility before travel—rules are updated periodically and an otherwise eligible traveler could be asked for additional documents at the border.

Prearranged Tourist Visas: Short and Long Term Options

If your nationality requires a prearranged visa, or if you prefer to secure a visa before travel for peace of mind, there are several tourism visa types:

  • Transit visas (48 or 96 hours): issued for short layovers. Designed for travelers connecting through Dubai who want to leave the airport for a short time.
  • 30-day tourist visa (single or multiple entry): commonly used by leisure travelers.
  • 60-day tourist visa (single or multiple entry): for longer stays or combined business and leisure trips.
  • 90-day options may be available through specific channels or for certain nationalities, sometimes with different validity rules.

Each visa type has an official fee and may be sold through different channels—airlines, travel agents, or immigration authorities—so the out-the-door cost varies accordingly.

Transit Visas: A Low-Cost Option for Short Stops

Transit visas are the cheapest prearranged option if you need to leave the airport during a stopover. They are typically valid for 48 or 96 hours and offered by airlines (when your itinerary meets the carrier’s criteria) or by accredited tourism offices. If you are on a valid connecting Emirates flight, for example, you can often apply for a transit permit through their system at a modest fee.

Sponsorship Visas: When Someone in the UAE Applies for You

Family members or companies living in the UAE can sponsor a visitor. In such cases, costs and procedures may differ slightly and could include refundable security deposits depending on the sponsor’s relationship to the visitor and their nationality. This route can be useful for visitors staying with relatives, but it requires local paperwork, and sponsors are responsible for fines if the visitor overstays.

How Much Is Dubai Visit Visa — Costs Explained

Determining the exact cost requires identifying the visa channel and visa type. Below I break down the typical official fees, common airline fees, and typical service charges charged by agencies—so you can see where your money will go.

Official Government Fees and Typical Ranges

Government and immigration authorities publish fees that vary by emirate and visa class. The figures below reflect typical ranges and widely used channels but note that exact charges can change and differ by applicant nationality.

  • Visa on arrival: 0 AED (free) for eligible passport holders.
  • Transit visa (48 hours): nominal fee (sometimes USD 10 via airline channels).
  • Transit visa (96 hours): typically low-cost, often in the USD 40–USD 55 range when issued through airlines or accredited offices.
  • 30-day tourist visa (single entry): common official rates seen through airline channels are around USD 90 (or equivalent AED ~330), but other channels may list roughly AED 200 in different contexts.
  • 60-day tourist visa: commonly encountered rates in international airline visa services run around USD 177 for single-entry, and somewhat higher for multiple entry (USD 231).
  • 90-day visit visas: for some nationalities or via special arrangements, fees can be higher; processing and deposit requirements will apply.
  • Extension fees: extensions vary by visa type; a standard extension price commonly seen through airline channels is around USD 230 per extension for certain tourist visas, while authorities may apply different administrative rates in other contexts.

Government fees are sometimes presented in AED and sometimes in USD by airlines and agents. VAT (5%) generally applies to service components and in some cases to the visa fee charged by service providers.

Airline and Agency Service Fees

Airlines that offer visa facilitation (for example, when you book and travel on their flights) often show bundled visa rates and processing fees. Those published airline fees include the immigration fee plus the carrier’s facilitation charge and any VAT. Travel agents and visa specialists will similarly add a service fee that reflects their convenience, speed, and any premium for handling documentation.

Expect to pay an extra USD 10–USD 70 on top of official fees when you use an airline or third-party agency, although this varies widely by provider, speed of processing, and whether any additional documents are needed.

Refundable Deposits and Additional Charges

In some sponsorship scenarios, authorities require a refundable security deposit—commonly cited at around AED 1,000—especially when the sponsor applies on behalf of a visitor. When applying through private visa centers or for some nationalities, additional government charges like knowledge or innovation fees (small AED amounts) may be added if the application is processed inside the UAE.

Overstay Fines and Cancellation Charges

One of the biggest hidden costs is overstaying. The standard fine has been standardized at AED 50 per day for visit and tourist visa overstays. If you plan uncertain dates, build in a clear buffer and understand the extension process to avoid these fines.

Cancellation policies vary: many visa fees are non-refundable once issued, and agents often charge a cancellation fee (for example, a nominal AED 110 in some centers), so confirm refund rules before paying.

Who Pays What: Comparing Channels

When you ask how much is Dubai visit visa, a practical way to think about cost is by channel:

  • Visa on arrival: cost = 0 (eligible passports only).
  • Direct government application (GDRFA/DNRD through accredited channels): cost = published government fee + any local service charges.
  • Airline-managed visa: cost = airline published visa fee (includes government fee + carrier processing).
  • Visa agency: cost = government fee + agency service fee + VAT.
  • Sponsor in UAE: cost = government fee + potential refundable security deposit + sponsor’s administrative handling.

Because channels bundle fees differently, always ask the provider to break down the government fee vs. service fee so you understand what you are paying for.

Step-By-Step: How to Apply for the Correct Dubai Visit Visa

  1. Confirm whether your passport qualifies for visa on arrival. This is the fastest and cheapest outcome; if eligible, no pre-arrangement is needed.
  2. If a prearranged visa is required, decide whether to apply through your airline, an accredited visa center, or a sponsor in the UAE. Each has specific document requirements and submission methods.
  3. Collect required documents (clear passport copy with at least six months validity, color photo, confirmed onward or return ticket, hotel booking or host tenancy contract, and any nationality-specific IDs).
  4. Pay the visa fee and any service charges. Request a fee breakdown.
  5. Receive the entry permit (often emailed), print or download a copy for presentation during transit or at immigration if requested.
  6. On arrival, present the visa or passport for stamping. If directed for visual screening, carry hard copies of your visa and travel documents to avoid copying charges at the airport.
  7. If you need to extend, start the extension process well before the expiry—extensions may require presenting the original passport and paying the extension fee at an official office.

Documents You Must Prepare

A correct visa application depends on clean, consistent documentation. Typical requirements include:

  • A passport valid for at least six months and machine-readable.
  • A recent color photograph on white background (specifications vary by provider).
  • Confirmed return or onward ticket (some agents insist on an Emirates ticket when applying through the airline portal).
  • A confirmed hotel booking or a tenancy contract/host’s passport and residency page if staying with family.
  • Additional nationality-specific documents (national ID cards, proof of relationship for family applications, and supporting business letters for official visits).

If you are traveling from Saudi Arabia or combining Dubai with visits to Saudi cities, align your travel and accommodation dates before applying to avoid mismatched travel records.

Timing and Processing Expectations

Processing time depends on the channel:

  • Visa on arrival: immediate on landing (if eligible).
  • Transit visas via airlines: usually processed within a few days; apply as early as possible.
  • Standard tourist visas via airline portals: often processed in 3–4 working days; some channels offer expedited processing for an extra fee.
  • GDRFA and printed embassy-style visas: times vary; always factor in at least 3–5 working days for secure processing.

If your flight is imminent, use an airline-managed service that offers faster turnaround, but be prepared to pay a convenience premium.

Common Pitfalls and How To Avoid Them

Mistakes when applying for a Dubai visit visa are common but avoidable with simple precautions. The most frequent issues include:

  • Applying with a passport that does not have the required validity or machine-readable format—always check six-month validity and passport type.
  • Using a travel agent or airline without confirming the fee breakdown—ask for a line-by-line invoice to verify government vs. service charges.
  • Relying on a visa-on-arrival assumption without double-checking current eligibility—rules change and can be enforced at immigration.
  • Late applications and poor timing—begin visa processes well ahead of travel dates, particularly when traveling during peak times.
  • Overlooking extension rules—if you plan to extend, understand the fee, timing, and whether you must apply from inside the UAE.

Address these by preparing documents early, choosing a reliable application channel, and checking eligibility status directly with immigration or airline portals if in doubt.

Cost Examples By Scenario

To make the numbers less abstract, here are realistic cost scenarios you may encounter. These examples illustrate how the channel and visa type influence the final price:

  • Eligible Passport Holder, Short Stop: Visa on arrival = 0 AED. You only pay transport and accommodation.
  • Middle East/Asian National on Emirates Booking, 30-Day Tourist: Airline-managed 30-day visa ~USD 90 (includes official fees and airline processing). Add any agent fee if you used one.
  • Long Stay Visitor from a Non-Eligible Country, 60-Day Multiple Entry: Prearranged visa ~USD 177–231 depending on single vs. multiple entry and service charges. Extensions add per-extension costs.
  • Transit Visitor Requiring 96-Hour Permit: Airline transit visa ~USD 40–55 from airline or accredited visa office.

These are indicative; always ask the provider for the exact total you will be charged in your currency.

When To Use A Sponsor Versus Applying Independently

A sponsor can be an employer, family member, or tourism office in the UAE. Sponsor-based applications are useful when you are staying with family or visiting for a specific purpose that requires local backing. Independent applications through airlines or visa centers suit leisure tourists who prefer direct control.

Choose sponsorship when:

  • You have an immediate relative who is a UAE resident and can provide the required guarantees and documentation.
  • Your travel purpose is official, personal, or involves stay with family, and the sponsor agrees to be responsible for any fines or administrative follow-ups.

Choose independent application when:

  • You prefer speed and control over the process.
  • You want to avoid refundable security deposits or local sponsor obligations.
  • You can satisfy standard document requirements (passport, onward ticket, hotel booking).

Insurance, Health, and Entry Requirements

Some visa channels may ask for proof of medical insurance valid in the UAE. Even when not mandatory, having travel insurance that covers medical expenses in the UAE is strongly recommended for all visitors. Make sure any insurance policy covers the full duration of stay and repatriation if needed.

Additionally, keep up to date with public health requirements: immunization or testing rules can change based on global conditions. Check authoritative channels and your airline well before travel.

Avoiding Extra Charges at the Airport

Airport immigration may request printed copies of your visa or travel documents for visual screening. If you cannot produce a hard copy, some airports may provide a photocopy service for a nominal fee (e.g., AED 30 per copy at certain terminals). The best preventive move is to carry printed copies of your visa confirmation, passport bio page, and return ticket to avoid last-minute charges or delays.

How Extensions Work and What They Cost

If you plan to stay longer, understand extension rules before travel. Many tourist visas are extendable once or twice for a fee. Extension costs vary by visa type and where you apply—airport visa offices and immigration centers process extensions but may require presentation of the original passport and payment in person. Extensions can be more expensive than initial issuance, so treat initial stay planning seriously to avoid surprise fees.

Comparing Dubai Fees To Neighboring Gulf Options

Dubai’s visa pricing and application convenience often compare favorably to other Gulf states. For example, some neighboring destinations also offer visa-on-arrival for certain nationalities but differ in lengths and fees. If you are planning a multi-country Gulf trip, compare visa costs and rules for each country early in your planning so you can pick a route that minimizes administrative overhead.

Real-World Planning Framework: Budgeting For The Visa Component

When budgeting for a Dubai visit, treat the visa as one line item among these costs:

  • Flight (book early for best fares).
  • Accommodation (align booking dates to visa validity).
  • Visa fee (and potential agent or airline service fees).
  • Travel insurance and health-related costs.
  • Local transport and incidental charges (including the possibility of an airport copying fee).

A realistic visa budget for most travelers is between USD 0 (visa on arrival) and USD 300 (longer multi-entry or if agent fees and extensions are included). If you have an uncertain itinerary, budget a cushion for possible extensions or expedited processing charges.

Choosing a Trusted Provider

If you do not apply directly through immigration, choose an airline or agent with verifiable customer service and transparent fee breakdowns. Check recent traveler reviews, ask for a written invoice that separates government fees from service charges, and confirm processing times and refund policies.

Safety, Legality, and Cultural Respect

A visa is permission to enter; it is not permission to break local laws. Familiarize yourself with UAE laws and local cultural norms before arrival. Simple behaviors that are acceptable in one country may be restricted in the UAE; understanding this protects you from fines and legal issues that could affect future travel to the region.

Practical Example Scenarios: Which Visa to Choose

  • Short Business Stopover (24–72 hours): If your nationality isn’t eligible for visa on arrival, apply for a 48- or 96-hour transit visa through your carrier to avoid disruption.
  • Leisure Trip for Two Weeks: If eligible for on-arrival, no visa fee applies—simply confirm passport validity and lodging. If not, a 30-day tourist visa via your airline is a convenient single-payment option.
  • Visiting Family and Staying Longer: Sponsor route may be appropriate, but agree terms with your sponsor about deposits and responsibilities before travel.
  • Multi-Country Gulf Vacation: Plan all visa requirements together, prioritize visas that allow multi-entry where possible, and align ticket bookings to visa validity windows.

These decision frameworks aim to turn the abstract question “how much is Dubai visit visa” into a practical selection process that fits your trip profile.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a typical 30-day Dubai tourist visa take to process?

Processing depends on the application channel. Airline-managed visas are often processed within 3–4 working days, while some visa centers can expedite faster for a fee. Visa on arrival requires no pre-processing for eligible passports.

Are visa fees refundable if my plans change?

Generally, visa fees are non-refundable once the visa has been issued and used. Cancellation policies vary; some application centers may allow refunds before processing with a deduction. Always confirm refund and cancellation terms before payment.

What happens if I overstay my Dubai visit visa?

Overstaying a visit visa typically incurs a standard fine of AED 50 per day. Repeated or prolonged overstays can result in additional penalties and may affect future visa eligibility.

Can I extend my Dubai visit visa while in the UAE?

Yes, many tourist visas are extendable, but extension rules and fees vary. Extensions typically require presenting the passport and paying the extension fee at designated immigration offices or airport visa centers well before expiry.
