How to Apply Dubai Visit Visa From Pakistan

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Across the Gulf, travel between Pakistan and the United Arab Emirates remains one of the busiest regional corridors—millions of Pakistanis travel to the UAE each year for tourism, family visits, business and medical treatment. If you are planning a short trip to Dubai, the visa process is straightforward when you know the right routes, documentation and timing.

Short answer: Pakistani nationals must obtain a prearranged visit visa for the UAE unless they qualify for a specific visa-on-arrival exemption. You can apply through an approved travel agent, through certain airlines’ online visa services, via a sponsor (family or employer) in the UAE, or in limited cases by submitting documents to the UAE Embassy in Islamabad. The correct route depends on your travel purpose, passport type and how fast you need the permit.

This article explains every step a Pakistan-based traveler needs to take to secure a Dubai visit visa: the types of permits available, which application channels to use, a practical step-by-step application process, the exact documents you must prepare (including attestation steps in Pakistan), timelines, fees, common pitfalls and how to handle special cases such as children, medical travel and transit. You’ll leave with a clear, confident plan to turn your travel idea into a valid visa and a smooth arrival in Dubai. Understanding Dubai Visit Visas: Types and When to Use Each

What Is a "Visit Visa" in the UAE Context?

A visit visa (often called a tourist visa) grants foreign nationals permission to enter the UAE for short-term stays for tourism, visiting family, medical treatment or attending short business meetings. Visit visas differ from residency, work or student visas and are issued for defined durations, single or multiple entries, and with varying extension rules.

Common Visit Visa Types Relevant for Pakistani Applicants

There are several visit visa variants that Pakistanis most frequently use:

  • Short-stay (30 days, single-entry): Ideal for brief tourism or family visits.
  • Long-stay (60 days, single-entry or multiple-entry): Suitable for longer holidays or multi-stop itineraries.
  • Transit visas (48-hour or 96-hour): For travelers passing through Dubai with a confirmed onward itinerary.
  • Pre-approved single-entry or multiple-entry permits issued by airlines or approved travel agencies.

Which you choose depends on your travel dates, whether you need flexibility to leave and return to the UAE, and whether a sponsor is involved.

Who Can Sponsor a Visit Visa?

A visit visa must be sponsored by one of the following:

  • An authorized UAE-based sponsor (a resident, company or family member).
  • An accredited travel agency in the UAE.
  • Certain international airlines when you book the relevant flights (e.g., Emirates, Etihad) offer visa facilitation for eligible passengers.

If you do not have a UAE sponsor, using an accredited travel agent or the airline’s visa service is the most common pathway for applicants from Pakistan.

Which Application Channels Are Available from Pakistan?

Embassy of the UAE in Islamabad

The UAE Embassy in Islamabad handles certain visa categories and provides guidance on documentation, attestation and consular procedures. Some applicants—especially government officials, those traveling on special diplomatic or official passports, or people requiring official notes—must file through the embassy according to precise rules.

Accredited Travel Agents and Airlines

Certified travel agents in Pakistan regularly process tourist visas on behalf of customers. Airlines such as Emirates and Etihad also offer online visa services to passengers with confirmed tickets, which allow you to apply for many types of visit permits without physically submitting your passport.

VFS Global and Commercial Visa Centres

For some visa pathways and document collection, authorized third-party visa service centres may handle submissions and biometric collection where required. These services vary by country and by visa type.

Sponsors in the UAE

If you have friends or family who are UAE residents, they can apply on your behalf through local channels. The sponsor must provide accurate details and, in some cases, a security deposit depending on the visa category.

Step-By-Step Application Process

Below is a clear step-by-step process to follow when applying for a Dubai visit visa from Pakistan. Use this as your operational checklist and timeline. (This is one of two lists in the article—the second will be the documents checklist.)

  1. Decide the visa type you need (30-day single, 60-day single or multiple, or transit) and confirm your travel dates and flexibility.
  2. Choose the application channel: an accredited travel agency in Pakistan, an airline visa service if you have a qualifying ticket, a UAE sponsor, or the Embassy of the UAE in Islamabad for eligible categories.
  3. Book refundable or changeable flights and a provisional hotel reservation that match your proposed dates. Avoid nonrefundable bookings until the visa is approved.
  4. Collect and prepare required documents (see the documents checklist below), including all necessary attestations from Pakistani authorities if applicable.
  5. Submit the visa application through your chosen channel and pay visa and service fees. Ensure you receive a reference or application number.
  6. Track the application and be ready to provide additional documents if requested. Typical processing is from 3–14 working days depending on route and visa type.
  7. Once approved, receive the e-visa or visa number (often emailed). Print a copy to carry to immigration.
  8. Travel to Dubai on the scheduled dates, present the visa copy at immigration and follow arrival procedures.

Follow these steps exactly, and you’ll avoid most common delays. If your circumstances are unusual—diplomatic status, minor traveling alone, or urgent medical travel—communicate these at the outset to the agent or embassy to ensure specialized instructions are followed.

Documents Checklist: What You Must Prepare

Below is the second and final list in this article—a compact checklist of documents you must have ready. Use page-quality scans and clear, legible photographs.

  • Valid passport with at least six months’ validity from the date of intended travel and at least one blank page.
  • Clear color copy of the passport information page.
  • Recent passport-size photograph with white background (follow the exact size requirements given by the processing party).
  • Completed visa application form (typed where required by the embassy).
  • Confirmed travel itinerary or provisional flight booking. If applying via Emirates’ online service, your ticket number often must start with the airline’s designated code.
  • Confirmed hotel booking or tenancy contract (if staying with family, provide a tenancy contract or sponsor’s Emirates ID and residency proof).
  • Proof of funds if requested (bank statements).
  • Travel insurance valid in the UAE, especially for medical travel.
  • For minors traveling alone or with only one parent: an affidavit/no-objection certificate, attested by Pakistani authorities and translated where required.
  • Additional attestations or documents as requested for special visa categories, such as note verbale for government officials or employment letters.

Always verify the exact document requirements with the entity you’re applying through—airline, travel agent or the embassy—before submission.

Fees, Processing Times and Payment Options

Typical Fee Ranges

Visa fees vary by visa type and channel. Approximate ranges (subject to change) are:

  • Transit (48/96 hours): Low single-digit fees in USD to cover processing.
  • 30-day tourist visa: Fees typically about USD 90, excluding agent/VFS charges.
  • 60-day tourist visa: Higher, often around USD 170–230, excluding value-added taxes and service charges.

Add-ons such as visa stamping, Knowledge Dirham or Innovation Dirham (applied in some UAE emirates), travel agent fees and VFS handling charges will increase the total.

Processing Times by Channel

Processing timelines depend on channel and visa type:

  • Airline e-visa services (when eligible): typically 3–7 working days if all documents are correct.
  • Travel agent submissions via UAE partners: often 3–10 working days.
  • Embassy-in-Islamabad processing for special categories: up to 14 working days or longer for cases requiring formal approvals.
  • Emergency or expedited services might be available at higher cost; confirm availability before relying on them.

Payment Methods

Visa and service fees are usually payable at the time of application through online payment gateways, bank transfers, or in-person cash/card payments to service centres. Keep receipts and transaction references.

Applying Through Airlines vs. Travel Agents vs. Embassy: Pros and Cons

Airline Visa Service (e.g., Emirates, Etihad)

Applying through a qualifying airline after you book a ticket can be the quickest route for many travelers because it removes the need to submit your passport physically. The airline verifies your booking and issues the permit electronically.

Pros: Fast, convenient, no embassy visits, often fully online. Cons: Usually requires an all-Emirates itinerary or qualifying partner ticket; stricter eligibility rules; added airline service fees.

When you book an eligible ticket, use the airline’s "Manage Booking" visa feature and have your documents ready.

Accredited Travel Agents in Pakistan

Travel agents are the most common choice for Pakistanis. They handle forms, attestations, booking coordination and liaison with UAE partners.

Pros: Hands-off experience, helpful for complex cases, good for group applications. Cons: Service fees, potential for variable turnaround times, ensure you choose a certified agent.

Always verify the agent’s accreditation and insist on written receipts showing the visa application number.

Embassy Route (UAE Embassy, Islamabad)

Certain categories must go through the embassy in Islamabad, such as government officials, diplomatic passport cases and specific visa requests requiring consular verification.

Pros: Official channel for sensitive or special cases. Cons: Longer processing, stricter appointment and document requirements, mandatory attestations and visa fees paid upfront.

If you need to use the embassy, follow their listed submission hours, produce typed forms, and provide any required note verbale or official letters.

Attestation and Document Legalization in Pakistan

If your application requires certified documents—for example, affidavits for minors, educational certificates for long stays, or official letters—you must follow Pakistan’s attestation chain. This typically involves attestation by local authorities, then the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) in Islamabad, and, in some cases, attestation by the UAE Embassy.

Educational certificates often require HEC or IBCC verification before MOFA attestation. Documents such as marriage certificates, birth certificates or power of attorney commonly require NADRA attestation and MOFA stamps as well.

Note that the UAE Embassy in Islamabad publishes precise attestation requirements for different documents. Missing a required attestation is a frequent cause of delays; plan extra time if your visa type requires document legalization.

Special Cases and Common Pitfalls

Children Traveling Alone or With One Parent

Minors under 21 traveling without their father or with only one parent often need a notarized NOC/no-objection certificate from the absent parent. This affidavit must be on appropriate stamp paper, translated into Arabic where required, attested by MOFA and sometimes attested by the UAE Embassy. Prepare these documents well in advance.

Government Officials and Diplomatic Passport Holders

Government officials often require a note verbale from Pakistan’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs as part of the visa submission. These notes must be recent and contain sponsor details and purpose of travel.

Applicants With Previous Overstays or Travel Issues

Previous visa overstays or immigration violations can complicate new visa approvals. Be honest in the application form and, if necessary, consult the embassy or a qualified agent to resolve outstanding fines or clearances before applying.

Mismatched Dates and Reservations

One of the most common causes of visa refusal is mismatched dates between flight tickets, hotel bookings and the visa application. Ensure your return ticket date aligns with the duration on the visa application.

Using Non-Accredited Agents or False Documents

Never use unverified agents or submit false documents. Besides the legal risk, doing so often results in visa refusals, fines, and potential travel bans.

Extensions, Overstays and Penalties

Visit visas are sometimes extendable once in the UAE, depending on the visa type and emirate-specific rules. Extensions require visiting the local immigration office and may require providing the original passport.

Overstaying a UAE visa carries fines that accrue daily. Severe or prolonged overstays can result in detention and deportation, additional fines, and a ban on future entry. If you expect to overstay or if emergencies occur, contact your sponsor or the relevant UAE immigration office immediately to seek an approved extension and avoid penalties.

Arrival in Dubai: Immigration, Screening and What to Carry

When you land in Dubai, have the following ready to present to immigration staff:

  • Printed copy of the e-visa or visa number confirmation.
  • Original passport.
  • Return or onward ticket.
  • Hard copy of hotel reservation or sponsor contact details if staying with family.

Be prepared for brief security or visual screening, and in some cases the authorities may request to see a printed copy of your visa. Keep digital and printed copies accessible. If you are selected for additional checks, follow instructions politely and present documents promptly—this is a standard security routine at major international airports.

Timeline and Practical Planning Blueprint

Planning your Dubai trip from Pakistan should follow a conservative timeline that allows for any document legalization and processing delays. A practical schedule:

  • 4–6 weeks before travel: Decide travel dates, choose visa type, and select application channel. Begin attestations if required.
  • 3–4 weeks before travel: Book provisional flights (refundable/changeable) and hotel reservation. Start the application with your airline or agent.
  • 2–3 weeks before travel: Submit final documents and pay visa/service fees. Monitor application and provide any requested additional documents immediately.
  • 3–10 working days before travel: Expect visa approval. Print the e-visa, confirm final travel bookings and purchase travel insurance.
  • Arrival: Keep copies of visa and bookings ready and be prepared for routine screening.

This timeline builds a buffer for possible embassy requirements or document attestation steps. If you have less time, prioritize airline visa services (if eligible) or expedite through reputable agents but be prepared for higher fees.

Post-Visa Approval: Final Steps Before Departure

Once your visa is approved, confirm the following:

  • Print or download the e-visa confirmation and save multiple copies.
  • Reconfirm flights and check-in requirements (some airlines ask to see the visa at check-in).
  • Carry all supporting documents when you travel: passport, visa printout, hotel bookings, travel insurance and sponsor contacts.
  • Keep emergency contact numbers (your travel agent, sponsor or the Pakistani consulate in Dubai) easily available.

On arrival, follow the standard immigration process and provide the requested documents. If you encounter any issues with entry, contact your sponsor or the airline immediately for assistance.

Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid these repeated mistakes that delay or derail visa approvals:

  • Missing or expired passport validity (passport must have at least six months validity).
  • Incomplete or handwritten application forms when typed forms are required.
  • Incorrect or mismatched travel dates between ticket and visa application.
  • Using non-accredited agents or failing to secure attestation for necessary documents.
  • Submitting photocopies without certified attestation when originals are requested.

Double-checking these items before submitting your application saves time and stress.

The Role of Travel Insurance and Health Requirements

Travel insurance valid in the UAE is increasingly important and, for certain visa categories, mandatory. Ensure your policy covers medical expenses and repatriation. If you are traveling for medical treatment, prepare specific medical letters and appointment confirmations and make sure the insurer’s coverage matches the visa requirements.

Health entry rules can change; check vaccination or testing requirements shortly before travel and have documentation readily available.

When to Contact the Embassy or Consulate

Contact the UAE Embassy in Islamabad in these situations:

  • You are a government official needing a note verbale.
  • You hold a diplomatic or official passport with special visa rules.
  • You require embassy attestation for documents that are mandatory for visa issuance.
  • Your visa route is unclear or your application has been refused with no clear resolution provided by the agent.

Follow the embassy’s published office hours and submission rules. For many routine tourist visas, the embassy is not the primary channel, but its guidance is essential for the special categories outlined earlier.

Case Scenarios and Decision Framework

When deciding which route to take from Pakistan, use this decision framework: urgency, eligibility, complexity, and cost.

  • Urgency: If you need a fast turnaround and you qualify, airline visa services are often fastest.
  • Eligibility: If you have a UAE resident sponsor, sponsor routes are straightforward.
  • Complexity: For complex situations (minors, officials, medical treatment), embassy or experienced agents are recommended.
  • Cost: Travel agents may charge more but handle complexity; airlines charge reasonable fees but have stricter eligibility.

Apply this framework to pick the channel that best matches your priorities and constraints.

Avoid common pitfalls like mismatched dates, missing attestations and incomplete forms. FAQ

Do Pakistani passport holders get visa on arrival in Dubai?

Pakistani passport holders generally require a prearranged visa. Visa-on-arrival exemptions are limited to specific nationalities and circumstances. Pakistani applicants should apply through a sponsor, accredited travel agent, qualifying airline service, or consult the UAE Embassy in Islamabad for special cases.

Can I apply for a Dubai visit visa through Emirates if I have a third-party carrier on one leg of my trip?

Airline visa services normally require the qualifying airline to operate the relevant legs of the itinerary or for the booking to meet the airline’s conditions. If your ticket includes other carriers, the airline may not process the visa. Check eligibility on the airline’s visa application page or consult a travel agent.

How long will it take to get the visa approved?

Typical processing ranges from 3 to 14 working days depending on the route and visa type. Airlines and online services may complete approvals faster. Embassy submissions and special categories can take up to 14 working days or more.

What if my visa is refused?

If a visa is refused, the issuing authority will usually state the reason. Do not reapply with the same supporting documents immediately. Consult your agent or the embassy to clarify the refusal reason and resolve any outstanding issues—such as missing attestations or discrepancies—before reapplying.
