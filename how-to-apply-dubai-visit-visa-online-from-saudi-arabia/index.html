How To Apply Dubai Visit Visa Online From Saudi Arabia

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Saudi Arabia is a region of growing outbound travel, and many residents are choosing Dubai as a top short-break or weekend destination because of its proximity, direct flights, and wide range of leisure and business offerings. Whether you hold a Saudi Iqama or are a resident of another GCC country living in Saudi Arabia, the UAE’s visa rules have specific steps and documents you must follow to secure a visit permit before you travel.

Short answer: If you hold a Saudi residence permit (Iqama) you can apply for a Dubai visit visa online through the GDRFA (General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs) smart portal or via approved airline visa services. The process requires a valid Iqama with sufficient remaining validity, a passport with at least six months’ validity, a passport-sized photo, and payment of the applicable fee; GCC resident permits are eligible for a 30-day entry permit that can often be extended once. This article explains eligibility, which online channels to use, a detailed step-by-step application workflow, common pitfalls and solutions, extension and entry rules, and practical planning tips to move from application to arrival with confidence.

Who Needs a Dubai Visit Visa from Saudi Arabia?

GCC Residents Living in Saudi Arabia

Residents of GCC countries who live and work in Saudi Arabia, including those holding Saudi Iqamas, must generally obtain an entry permit before arriving in the UAE. That rule has been enforced since 2016 to streamline pre-arrival checks and reduce immigration delays. If you hold a Saudi residence permit, you are eligible to apply for a GCC Resident entry permit to the UAE, which is normally valid for 30 days from date of entry and extendable once.

Saudi Citizens and GCC Nationals

Saudi citizens and many GCC nationals can enter the UAE without a pre-arranged visa, depending on nationality. Always verify your passport’s visa-on-arrival eligibility before travel; if your passport requires a prearranged visit permit, follow the online application channels highlighted below.

Expatriates With Non-GCC Residency

If you are not a GCC resident but live in Saudi Arabia on a work visa or other permit from a non-GCC country, you will need to check the UAE’s visa rules for your nationality. Some nationalities can obtain visas on arrival, others need pre-arranged tourist visas through airlines or UAE visa channels.

Which Visit Visa Types Apply To You?

GCC Resident Entry Permit (30 Days)

The GCC resident entry permit is the most common for Saudi Iqama holders. Key characteristics:

  • Valid for 30 days from the date of entry.
  • Can typically be extended once for an additional 30 days.
  • Issued after evaluating the applicant’s profession, residence validity, and passport validity.
  • A GCC resident permit must have at least one year remaining in many cases; check the current rules at the time of application.

Tourist Visas (30- and 60-Day Types)

For non-GCC residents and nationals requiring prearranged tourist visas, the UAE issues single- and multiple-entry tourist visas valid for 30 or 60 days after entry. These are available through airlines (if you book with them), travel agents, or GDRFA-approved channels.

Short Transit Visas (48-hour, 96-hour)

If you're transiting Dubai on your way to another destination and meet the airline and ticket requirements, short transit visas (48-hour or 96-hour) can be issued via airline portals like Emirates’ Manage Your Booking. These are limited to specific ticketing conditions.

Where To Apply: Official Channels and Alternatives

GDRFA Smart Portal (Primary Official Online Route)

If you are applying from Saudi Arabia with a Saudi Iqama, the GDRFA smart portal is the official, most direct route for GCC resident entry permits. The portal supports registration, application submission, document upload, fee payment, and tracking. Using the official channel reduces the chance of delays caused by third-party intermediaries.

Airline Visa Services

Airlines such as Emirates and Etihad offer online visa services for passengers who hold tickets with those carriers. These services are convenient if your travel itinerary is on the airline’s ticketing and you prefer to consolidate booking and visa processing. Airline services can be faster for certain nationalities, but they often require that your entire itinerary be on that carrier.

Embassy and Consulate Services

For special cases—diplomats, VIPs, or complex applications—applications may be routed through UAE consulates or embassies. These channels are less common for routine GCC resident permits but remain an option for exceptional circumstances.

Travel Agencies and Visa Service Providers

Licensed travel agents and visa facilitators can complete applications on your behalf. They are useful if you need help with document translations or if your application requires additional supporting materials. Use reputable agents—preferably those with verified relationships with the GDRFA or airlines.

Step-By-Step: How To Apply Dubai Visit Visa Online From Saudi Arabia

(One concise numbered list to capture the operational steps; detailed explanations follow in paragraphs.)

  1. Register and log in to the GDRFA smart portal or select the airline visa service you will use.
  2. Complete the online application form for the appropriate visa type (GCC Resident Entry Permit).
  3. Upload required documents: passport scan, Iqama copy, passport-style photo, and any sponsor documents.
  4. Pay the visa fee using a debit/credit card.
  5. Track the application status and respond to requests for additional documents.
  6. Print the approved e-visa and carry it with you when you travel; ensure Saudi exit/re-entry permissions are valid.

Registering on the GDRFA Smart Portal

Begin at the GDRFA smart portal. Create a personal account with an email address and a secure password. The portal requires identity verification via the email code that’s sent to you. Once activated, you’ll access services and be able to choose “New GCC Entry Permit” and then “GCC Residents” as the person type. Registration is straightforward but fill the details exactly as in your passport and Iqama to avoid mismatches.

Completing the Application Form: Fields to Expect

The form is structured and asks for:

  • Visa Details: Person type (select GCC Residents), arrival emirate (choose Dubai if that is your destination), port of arrival.
  • GCC Residency Details: Iqama number and its issue and expiry dates. Ensure your Iqama has the required remaining validity.
  • GCC Trip Details: Date of arrival, where you are arriving from within Saudi Arabia, and a local UAE address (hotel booking or host address).
  • Sponsor Details: For Iqama holders, select “Establishment” and enter your employer’s company name and address as on your Iqama.
  • Passport Details: Passport number, nationality, passport issue and expiry dates.
  • Applicant Details: Mother’s name, marital status, occupation, religion, education—complete accurately; some fields auto-populate when you search for your data in the portal.
  • Contact Details: Saudi phone number, email, and address outside the UAE.

Small inconsistencies—like a misspelled mother’s name or mismatch in sponsor name—can delay approval, so cross-check before submitting.

Document Uploads: What To Prepare

(Second list — a short checklist of documents; keep lists to two only.)

  • Passport copy (clear, A4-sized scan; passport must be valid for at least six months).
  • Saudi Iqama copy (valid and ideally showing at least one year remaining where required).
  • Passport-style photo (white background).
  • Confirmed hotel reservation or local address in the UAE.
  • Proof of return travel (recommended, and sometimes required for airline-processed visas).

Upload high-quality images (300 dpi if possible). If the portal requests additional documents, submit them promptly to avoid processing delays.

Fees and Payment

The standard fee for the GCC resident entry permit is approximately AED 250 plus VAT (check the portal for the exact current fee). The GDRFA portal accepts international debit and credit cards. Keep your payment card details secure and ensure your bank allows international transactions.

Processing Time and How to Track

Typical processing time for GCC resident permits through the GDRFA is around 48 hours, though it can vary. Once you submit an application and payment, you will receive an electronic receipt and a tracking reference. Log back into the portal to check for status updates and messages from the GDRFA. Approved permits are emailed to the applicant as a PDF; print a copy to present on arrival or save an offline copy on your mobile device.

Common Reasons for Rejection and How to Avoid Them

Expired or Insufficient Iqama Validity

If your Iqama is expired, close to expiry, or shows residency changes (a recently altered profession), the application may be declined. Ensure your Iqama meets the minimum validity requirement before applying.

Passport Validity Issues

A passport with less than six months of validity is a frequent cause of rejection. Renew your passport before applying if needed.

Profession Not Listed or Disallowed

GDRFA evaluates some professions for eligibility. If your occupation is not accepted, the application may be rejected; consult your HR or a qualified travel agent for alternative routes (such as applying via an airline if eligible).

Mismatched Personal Details

Small discrepancies between passport spelling, Iqama details, and the application form can lead to delays. Double-check names, dates, and numbers before submission.

Sponsor or Booking Problems

If you list a sponsor who is not traveling with you or provide incomplete sponsor details, the application can be denied. For hotel stays, provide valid, confirmed booking data.

If an application is rejected, the GDRFA usually provides a reason, and you can reapply after resolving the issue.

Extending, Cancelling, and Changing Your Visa

Extending a GCC Resident Entry Permit

A GCC resident visit permit is commonly extendable once for an additional 30 days through GDRFA channels. Extension rules and fees vary; apply for an extension well before your current permit expires and prepare the original passport for any in-person checks if required.

Cancelling or Adjusting Travel Dates

If your plans change, some portals allow cancellation requests; policies differ based on the visa type and the issuing entity (GDRFA vs airline). Refunds on fees are generally limited; non-refundable fees are common once the visa is processed.

Arrival Procedures and Practical Airport Guidance

Documents to Carry at Immigration

Carry printed copies of the approved e-visa, your passport, Saudi Iqama, hotel reservation, and return ticket. Immigration officers sometimes request a printed visa copy or charge a small fee to print it at the airport.

Visual Screening at Dubai Airports

Be prepared for possible visual screening checks at Dubai International. Officials may request a printed copy of your visa and will verify documents. Keep a clear, printed set of your visa documents and a passport-sized photo.

Border Crossing by Road (Hatta Border)

If you plan to drive to the UAE from Saudi Arabia, select the Hatta border as your point of arrival in the application. Ensure your car insurance, vehicle documents, and driver’s license are valid for travel into the UAE. Border crossing rules and vehicle requirements differ from airport arrival procedures.

Saudi Exit and Re-Entry Requirements

Before you leave Saudi Arabia, ensure you have the required exit and re-entry permissions from your Saudi sponsor or employer where applicable. Saudi Iqama holders often need an exit/re-entry visa; missing this step can cause denied departure or problems on return. Confirm with your HR or sponsor that the necessary permits are issued and active.

Special Cases and Practical Tips

Travelling With Family or Minors

Minors and family members often require additional documentation. For children under 18, guardian consent forms and copies of parents’ IDs may be requested. When traveling with dependents, prepare birth certificates or family books if applicable and ensure entries match across passports and application fields.

Using an Airline to Process the Visa

If you prefer, book your flight with an airline that offers visa services and apply through their “Manage Your Booking” or visa portal. Airlines can be convenient if you have non-standard passport conditions, but ensure your itinerary meets the airline’s eligibility criteria (e.g., ticket number prefixes).

When to Use a Travel Agent

Use a travel agent if your application requires translations, attestations, or if you face repeated rejections. Agents can help present documents in the required format and expedite corrections.

Travel Insurance and Health Requirements

Purchase travel insurance that covers UAE travel. Check current health entry requirements, vaccination mandates, or testing rules before traveling, as these can change.

Cost Breakdown and Budgeting

Visa fees differ by visa type and issuing channel. Expect to pay the GCC resident entry permit fee (roughly AED 250 plus VAT as a reference point), plus any service fees if you use an agent or airline. Add travel insurance, hotel booking deposits, and any border-related vehicle costs if driving. Budget for contingencies—bank transaction issues or document translation fees.

Linking Your Visa Plan to Your Trip Itinerary

Once your visa is approved, build an itinerary that makes the most of your time in Dubai and the UAE. Dubai is an easy short break from Saudi cities; a well-planned 3-5 day itinerary can mix iconic sights, modern experiences, and a pace that fits weekend travel.

For readers departing from Riyadh, Jeddah, Dammam, or Al Khobar, plan flights that minimize transit time to maximize your stay. For cross-regional travelers who wish to combine Dubai with a Saudi domestic trip—such as a return to Riyadh or a detour to AlUla’s landscapes—use airline connections logically and leave buffer time in case of visa-related questions at the border.

Troubleshooting: If Your Application Is Delayed or Rejected

Immediate Steps After a Rejection

If you receive a rejection notice, read the explanation carefully. Often the issue is a missing document, passport validity, or Iqama validity. Correct the problem—renew your passport, request an updated Iqama from your employer, or provide clarifying documents—and reapply. Keep records of prior submissions to show any corrections.

Contacting GDRFA Support

Use the contact and support options on the GDRFA smart portal. In urgent cases—such as immediate travel for business—explain the time-sensitive nature of your trip and provide supporting documents proving urgency. For complex inquiries, visit UAE consulate assistance channels.

Banking and Payment Issues

If payment fails, try paying with another card or contact your issuing bank to authorize international transactions. Some banks restrict cross-border transactions for security; inform your bank before initiating the payment.

Safety, Cultural Considerations, and Etiquette in the UAE

Dubai is cosmopolitan and accustomed to international visitors. Still, observe local laws and cultural norms: dress modestly in public areas, respect public behavior codes, and be mindful of local customs during holidays and prayer times. Keeping copies of travel documents and emergency contact numbers with you is prudent.

Case Scenarios: Choosing the Right Channel

Opt For GDRFA When…

You hold a Saudi Iqama, need the standard GCC resident entry permit, and prefer the official channel with direct processing and transparent status updates. The GDRFA route reduces middleman fees and keeps your application within the UAE’s official processing system.

Opt For an Airline When…

Your airline offers visa processing tied to your ticket (such as Emirates) and this simplifies visa issuance—especially when the airline requires the ticket to be part of the same booking. This route is often convenient for non-GCC nationals who must meet airline-linked criteria.

Use a Travel Agent When…

Your application requires document translation, attestations, or has complex family or dependent scenarios. An experienced agent can accelerate corrections and manage additional documentation.

Checklist Before You Submit Your Application (one paragraph)

Before you click submit, confirm your passport is valid for at least six months, your Saudi Iqama is current and meets the authority’s validity requirements, names and details match across all documents, you have a confirmed UAE address and return travel details if requested, and you have electronic copies of a passport photo, passport, and Iqama scanned to the required clarity. Ensuring these elements reduces the risk of delays and helps you move from application to travel with confidence.

Conclusion

Applying for a Dubai visit visa online from Saudi Arabia is a straightforward, manageable process when you follow the correct channel, prepare accurate documents, and time your application with your travel plans and Saudi exit/re-entry permissions. Use the GDRFA smart portal for the GCC resident entry permit if you have a Saudi Iqama, or apply through an airline when your ticketing conditions favor that route. Keep passport and Iqama validity, sponsor information, and hotel bookings in order, and track your application so you arrive in Dubai ready to enjoy the trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How long does it take to process a Dubai visit visa for Saudi Iqama holders?

Processing times vary but the GDRFA smart portal typically processes GCC resident entry permits within about 48 hours. Allow extra time for weekends, public holidays, or if additional documents are requested.

2. What if my Iqama expires while I’m in the UAE?

Do not travel if your Iqama is expired. You risk denied entry. Renew your Iqama before applying for a UAE permit. If you are already in the UAE and your Saudi residency expires, coordinate immediately with your Saudi sponsor to resolve the status before attempting to return.

3. Can I extend my Dubai visit visa while in the UAE?

GCC resident entry permits are usually extendable once for an additional 30 days through the GDRFA. For other tourist visas, extension policies vary and may require in-person processes at designated offices. Apply early and follow the official portal guidance.

4. Do I need a printed copy of my e-visa?

Yes. While many immigration counters accept digital copies, Dubai immigration may request a printed copy or charge a small fee to print it at the airport. Carry both digital and printed copies of your approved visa, passport, Iqama, and hotel booking.
