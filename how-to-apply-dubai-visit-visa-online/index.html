How To Apply Dubai Visit Visa Online

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Gulf tourism is booming: millions of visitors pass through the UAE each year, and Dubai remains one of the world’s most visited cities for leisure, business, and stopovers. If you’re planning a trip to Dubai, understanding the online visit visa process saves time, removes uncertainty, and lets you move from planning to enjoying the city with confidence.

Short answer: You can apply for a Dubai visit visa online through several official channels—airlines (like Emirates), immigration portals (ICP/GDRFA), or through a UAE-based sponsor—by completing an application, uploading required documents, paying the fee, and receiving an electronic entry permit. Processing times range from a few hours for transit permits to several days for longer tourist visas, and the exact steps depend on your nationality, travel plans, and the route you choose to apply.

This post explains every step, decision point, and practical tip you’ll need for how to apply Dubai visit visa online. You’ll learn which route fits your situation, which documents to prepare, how to avoid common mistakes, how to track your application, and what to expect at arrival. The goal is to give you the blueprint for a smooth, predictable UAE entry so you can focus on what matters—your trip.

Why Apply for a Dubai Visit Visa Online?

Convenience, speed, and certainty

Applying online eliminates the need to visit embassies or queue at airport counters. When done correctly, the process delivers an electronic visa (e-permit) that you can print or store on your phone. That e-permit is accepted at immigration and reduces the risk of last-minute problems on arrival.

Multiple official routes with different advantages

There isn’t a single “one-size-fits-all” online application. You can apply via your carrier (if you’re flying Emirates), directly through federal or emirate-level portals (ICP or GDRFA Dubai), or through a UAE-based resident sponsor or hotel. Each route has slightly different requirements, fees, and eligibility rules. Choosing the right route improves speed and lowers risk.

When an online application is required

Nationals of many countries receive visas on arrival, but if your passport doesn’t qualify, or if you need specific validity or multiple-entry options, prearranging an online Dubai visit visa is essential. GCC residence permit holders and travelers from some countries must obtain an entry permit before arrival.

Who Can Apply Online — Eligibility and Visa Types

Visa on arrival vs prearranged visit visas

The UAE offers visa-on-arrival for certain passport holders (commonly 30-day or 90-day stamps issued at the airport). If your nationality is not listed for visa on arrival, or if you want a longer or multiple-entry permit, you must apply in advance.

Common types of visit visas available online

Tourist and visit visas are offered in short transit forms and longer tourist formats. Typical categories you’ll encounter include:

  • Transit permits (48-hour, 96-hour): For short layovers or stopovers.
  • Short-term tourist visa (30 days): Single or multiple entry, often extendable.
  • Long-term tourist visa (60 days): Usually extendable once.
  • Multiple-entry tourist visas (30 or 60 days): For visitors planning repeated short trips within the validity period.
  • Special preapproved entry permits for certain nationalities (e.g., new provisions that affect Indian nationals and other categories).

Which one suits you depends on how long you’ll be in Dubai, whether you need multiple entry, and your passport’s visa policy.

Residency-based eligibility and special cases

If you hold a valid residence permit from a GCC country or certain Western nations (some conditions apply), you may be eligible for a simplified entry permit. Additionally, holders of valid US visas, UK visas, or EU residence permits sometimes qualify for streamlined entry or special preapproved permits—check the latest rules for your nationality.

Official Online Routes — Where To Apply

Airline-managed visa services

If you book with certain carriers, you can apply through the airline’s “Manage Your Booking” section. Airlines like Emirates and Etihad offer visa application services for passengers with confirmed tickets. This option is convenient when your flight itinerary is entirely on that carrier and complies with their requirements.

Applying via an airline is best when you already have an airline booking and you want an end-to-end service. Airlines collect the visa fee, process the application, and notify you when the permit is issued.

Federal and emirate-level government portals

There are two primary official digital routes for UAE entry permits:

  • Federal ICP (Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Ports Security): ICP manages national entry permits and visa-related services.
  • GDRFA Dubai (General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs - Dubai): GDRFA issues permits specific to Dubai and handles arrivals at Dubai airports.

You can apply directly through ICP, GDRFA Dubai, or their mobile apps (e.g., DubaiNow and ICP App) when you meet the eligibility criteria. These portals accept online applications, require document uploads, and issue electronic entry permits that can be shown at immigration.

Sponsor or resident application

If you have a host in the UAE—family member, employer, or hotel—the sponsor can apply on your behalf. Residents can use local channels to obtain permits for relatives or guests. This route is common for those staying with family or when employers arrange business visit permits.

Third-party visa agencies and travel platforms

Numerous private agencies offer a “complete service” to arrange Dubai visit visas online. They can be faster for complex cases but may charge additional service fees. Use reputable providers, verify their credentials, and prefer those who clearly state processing times and refund policies. When to use each route — practical guidance

Choose airline service if your flight is with that carrier and you prefer a one-stop booking. Use GDRFA/ICP for direct, official processing when you meet the online eligibility or when you require specific Dubai entry requirements. Use a sponsor if a UAE resident is managing travel logistics. Use a trusted broker if your situation is complex, but insist on transparent fees and timelines.

Documents, Fees, and Processing Times

Standard documents required for online applications

Requirements vary by route and nationality, but the core documents are consistent:

  • Passport copy (machine-readable, valid for at least six months from travel date in most cases).
  • Passport photo (recent color photograph meeting biometric criteria).
  • Proof of confirmed travel (return ticket or onward ticket).
  • Hotel reservation or host invitation/tenancy contract if staying with family.
  • For some itineraries: proof of relationship when staying with family (marriage certificate, birth certificate).
  • Additional documents depending on nationality (national ID for some countries, residency permits).

To reduce confusion at submission, prepare high-quality scans and ensure names and passport numbers match exactly.

Quick application checklist

  • Valid passport with minimum required validity.
  • Recent passport-style photo (digital).
  • Flight booking or itinerary confirmation.
  • Hotel booking or sponsor letter.
  • Payment card for visa fees.
  • Copies of residency permits or supporting documents if required.

(That checklist above is the single list included in this article to keep your application organized.)

Visa fees and payment

Fees vary by visa type (transit, 30-day, 60-day, single vs multiple entry). Emirates and other airlines publish fee tables for their visa offerings; government portals list the official visa charges and taxes. Private providers add service charges. Payments are typically made online via card during application. Visa fees are generally nonrefundable once the application is processed.

Processing times and validity

Transit permits can often be approved the same day or within a few hours. Short-term tourist visas commonly take 1–7 business days depending on the route and nationality. Once issued, some visit visas are valid to use within a set window (e.g., entry within 60 days from date of issue for many prearranged permits). Always verify the “valid before entry” and “valid after entry” conditions to avoid expired-permit issues on arrival.

Step-By-Step: How To Apply Dubai Visit Visa Online

Step 1 — Determine eligibility and the right visa type

Begin by confirming whether you qualify for visa on arrival. If not, decide whether a 48/96-hour transit permit, 30-day tourist visa, or 60-day tourist visa meets your needs. Consider whether you need single or multiple entry.

Step 2 — Choose your application route

Select one of the official routes: airline-managed service, ICP/GDRFA portal, sponsor, or a vetted private agency. If you already have an Emirates ticket, the airline portal may be easiest. If you need a Dubai-specific entry, GDRFA Dubai can be appropriate. A sponsor is effective when staying with a UAE resident.

Step 3 — Prepare documents and digital files

Scan your passport (biographic page), take a compliant digital passport photo, and gather travel confirmations. Name fields must match your passport exactly. Save files in the formats requested (usually JPG/PNG for photos and PDF for documents).

Step 4 — Complete the application form

Fill the online form carefully: personal details, passport number, travel dates, sponsor details if applicable. Double-check spellings, passport expiry dates, and contact details.

Step 5 — Pay fees and submit

Pay the visa fee online. Keep the payment confirmation and reference number. If using a third party or a sponsor, ensure they provide a tracking or reference number for your application.

Step 6 — Track the application and download the permit

Official portals and airlines provide tracking tools. Once approved, you’ll receive an e-permit or visa PDF. Save and print one copy; store a digital copy accessible from your phone. If selected for visual screening at Dubai immigration, having a printed permit speeds processing.

Step 7 — Travel and present your permit at immigration

Carry your passport, printed e-permit, onward/return tickets, and hotel confirmation. Be prepared to present additional documents if asked (e.g., proof of funds). If requests for further checks occur, calmly follow instructions—immigration decisions rest with UAE authorities.

Common Pitfalls and How To Avoid Them

Mismatched names or passport numbers

Even small differences (missing middle names, hyphenation) can cause delays or rejection. Use the exact name order and spelling as shown on your passport.

Insufficient passport validity

Most online routes require a minimum of six months’ validity. Renew your passport before applying if you’re close to the limit.

Booking nonrefundable flights before visa approval

If your visa application might be delayed or rejected, avoid nonrefundable bookings. Where possible, use refundable fares or confirm the visa before committing.

Low-quality scans or wrong file types

Portals reject imperfect files. Use high-resolution scans and follow file type and size instructions closely.

Not checking nationality-specific requirements

Some countries require national ID cards or additional documentation. Review the portal’s nationality-specific checklist before applying.

Missing or incorrect sponsor information

If you’re applying through a host, ensure the sponsor’s details (residence number, relationship proof) meet the requirements. Misreported sponsor data will lead to delays.

Overstays and extensions

Understand the permitted length of stay and extension rules. Extensions often require visiting specific offices or meeting additional conditions. Overstaying can result in fines and travel limitations.

Tracking, Approval, and Receiving Your E-Visa

How approvals are communicated

Approvals are typically sent via email and made available in the applicant’s portal account. Airlines also notify passengers through their booking systems. Save the confirmation email and download the attached e-permit.

What the e-permit contains

The electronic permit includes the visa number, validity dates, type of visa, and entry conditions. Immigration officials may request the printed permit during random checks or visual screening.

If your application is delayed or rejected

Delays happen. Contact the processing entity (airline, government portal, sponsor) for clarification. For rejections, the application portal sometimes provides a reason. You can correct the issue and reapply. Avoid speculative online “fixes”—correct documentation and exact details are the reliable remedy.

Arrival Procedures: What To Expect at Dubai Immigration

Documents to present

Present your passport, printed e-permit, travel itinerary, and hotel confirmation or sponsor details. If asked, show proof of funds or onward travel.

Random visual screening and photocopy requirements

Dubai immigration sometimes conducts visual screening. Visitors may be required to produce a printed copy of the visa or pay a small fee to print one at the airport. Carry a printout to avoid unnecessary steps.

Entry stamps vs e-permit validation

Some visitors still receive an entry stamp alongside their e-permit approval; others rely solely on the e-permit record. Immigration will record your entry electronically.

Extending a Visit Visa and Overstay Rules

Extension possibilities

Many tourist visas can be extended once for an additional period (commonly 30 days), but extension rules change and may require visiting immigration offices in person with original passport and ticket copies. Extensions may incur fees.

Overstay fines and consequences

Overstaying accrues fines per day and may result in detention, deportation, or future entry restrictions. If you anticipate needing more time, begin extension procedures before your permit expires.

Special Considerations

Transit visas for short stopovers

If you have a short stopover in Dubai and meet the eligibility, you can apply for 48-hour or 96-hour transit permits. Airlines often provide these for passengers with connecting itineraries.

Indian nationals and recent preapproval rules

There have been evolving provisions for specific nationalities that offer streamlined short-term permits under certain conditions. Eligibility often requires valid third-country visas or residence permits. Always check the latest requirements before applying.

Traveling with minors and family groups

When traveling with children, carry original birth certificates and any custody documents if parents are traveling separately. Minor names must match travel documents exactly. Some applications may require parental consent forms.

Medical travel and business visits

If your purpose is medical care or business, prepare supporting documents such as hospital appointment letters or business invitations. These documents strengthen the application and sometimes change processing categories.

Refunds, Cancellations, and Dealing With Rejections

Refund policies

Visa fees are typically nonrefundable if the application is processed. Refunds before processing may incur administrative deductions. Confirm the service provider’s policy before paying.

Re-application strategy

If rejected, identify the reason, correct any issues, and reapply. Do not submit multiple duplicate applications simultaneously—this can complicate processing.

Escalation and contacting authorities

If you need to escalate, use official platform contact channels (ICP, GDRFA, airline support). Keep all application references and payment receipts handy for any inquiries.

Safety, Insurance, and Health Considerations

Travel insurance recommendations

Purchase travel insurance that covers medical expenses, trip interruptions, and lost luggage. Insurance may not be mandatory for visa approval, but it is a sensible precaution.

Health advisories and vaccinations

Check for routine vaccinations and any health advisories before travel. While pandemic-era entry rules have relaxed in many places, remain aware of testing or health declaration requirements that can emerge unexpectedly.

Data privacy and sharing documents

Only submit personal documents to official portals or verified service providers. Avoid sending copies via public or insecure channels. When using a sponsor or travel agency, ensure they have clear data-handling policies.

Cost-Saving and Time-Saving Strategies

Apply early but not too early

Apply early enough to allow processing delays but avoid applying months in advance if the permit has a short “valid before entry” window. The right timing depends on the visa type.

Choose the route that matches your booking status

If you already have a ticket with a carrier that offers visa service, that often simplifies the process. If your itinerary includes multiple carriers or open-jaw tickets, the government portal or a sponsor may be better.

Avoid unnecessary premium services

Premium or rush services can be useful for last-minute plans but often carry high fees. Plan ahead to avoid paying extra.

FAQ

Do I always need to apply for a Dubai visit visa before I travel?

Not always. Citizens of visa-on-arrival countries receive a stamp at the airport. If your passport does not qualify for visa on arrival or you require a special permit (longer stay, multiple entry), you must apply online before travel.

How long does it take to get an online Dubai visit visa?

Transit permits can be issued within hours; standard tourist visa processing typically ranges from one to several business days. Airlines and government portals will provide estimated processing times during application.

Can I extend my Dubai visit visa after arrival?

Some tourist visas are extendable once. Extension rules vary and often require visiting immigration offices with your original passport. Start the extension process before the permit expires to avoid fines.

What should I do if my visa application is rejected?

Review the rejection reason, correct any document or data errors, and reapply with accurate documentation. Contact the processing authority (airline support, GDRFA, ICP, or your sponsor) for guidance and next steps.
