Who Is Eligible for a 90-Day Visit Visa?

Visa-On-Arrival vs. Pre-Arranged Visas

The UAE operates several entry frameworks:

  • Nationals of certain countries receive a visa on arrival. For many European, North American, and several other passports, immigration will stamp a 90-day multiple-entry visit visa valid for six months from issuance. If you hold one of those passports, you often do not need to apply in advanceâyour passport will be stamped at the airport.
  • Nationals who do not enjoy visa-on-arrival privileges must get a pre-arranged visa. Pre-arranged visas can be processed by UAE-based airlines for their passengers, by hotels and licensed travel agencies, or through the official government portals (ICP or GDRFA-D depending on who sponsors the visa).
  • UAE residents can sponsor first- and second-degree relatives and invite them via the Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Ports Security (ICP) system. That route commonly results in a 90-day single-entry visit visa for guests.

Special Cases and Conditional Eligibility

Some nationalities have conditional arrangements: for instance, Indian nationals with a valid US visa or green card have simplified entry paths and short-term visas on arrival in some scenarios. Similarly, GCC residents or holders of certain residence permits may need to apply in advance if their status has changed.

If you are a resident of another Gulf country or are transiting the region, be mindful that residency rules and visa sponsorship differ between emirates and between the federal ICP system and individual emirate portals (for example Dubaiâs GDRFA). Extensions and Overstays: What You Need to Know

Common Application Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

Incomplete or Incorrect Documentation

Missing attestations, a passport with insufficient validity, mismatched names between passport and ticket, or hotel bookings that do not match travel dates are the most common causes of visa delays or refusals. Always cross-check names, dates, and document quality before submission.

Using Unlicensed Agents

Only use licensed travel agents, hotels, or airline services. Unlicensed agents can take payments and disappear or submit incorrect data, leading to rejections and lost funds. If in doubt, ask your hotel or airline for confirmation of their licensing.

Timing Errors

Applying too close to departure increases the risk of delayed processing. For ICP-sponsored visas, allow up to 5 business days; for airline services, apply at least 4 working days in advance. For complex cases (attestations, bank proofs), start weeks in advance.

Misunderstanding the Sponsorâs Role

A sponsorâs job may include providing a refundable security deposit and presenting attestations. Clarify whether the sponsor is required to be present at any stage and whether employer-level criteria apply.

FAQ

Can I extend a 90-day visit visa while in the UAE?

Extensions depend on the visa category. Some short-term tourist visas allow extensions processed in the UAE (airport immigration or GDRFA), while many single-entry 90-day visitor visas are non-extendable. Always check the visa text and speak to immigration officials before the visa expires.

How long does ICP processing usually take for a sponsored 90-day visit visa?

ICP processing commonly takes about 2â5 business days from submission, assuming correct documentation. Complex cases or additional document requests can extend this timeframe.

What happens if my passport expires while Iâm in the UAE?

You must renew your passport via your countryâs diplomatic mission in the UAE and notify immigration as required. If your passport expires, you may face difficulties leaving the country and renewing your visa status; plan passport renewals before travel.

Can I work on a 90-day visit visa?

No. Visit visas are for tourism and visiting purposes only; working on a visit visa is illegal and can lead to fines, deportation, and future entry bans. If you intend to work, secure the appropriate work permit and residency visa before starting employment.
