How to Apply for Dubai Visit Visa From India

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

India and the UAE share an intense flow of travelers—tourists, visiting family, and business travelers—making Dubai one of the easiest and most popular short-haul destinations for Indian passport holders. Whether you are planning a short stopover to experience Dubai’s skyline and beaches or arranging a longer vacation, knowing the correct visa pathway from India saves time, money and stress.

Short answer: Most Indians obtain a Dubai visit visa through an airline, an approved travel agency or a UAE-based sponsor. Eligible Indian passport holders with valid US visas or certain residence permits can also obtain a short visa on arrival under recent policy changes. This article explains every practical step—from choosing the right visa type and gathering documents to applying, tracking and preparing for arrival—so you can complete the process confidently and travel without surprises.

This post will cover eligibility rules, the different application channels available in India, exact document checklists, step-by-step processes for the most common application routes, timelines, fee expectations, tips for preventing delays or refusals, and how to coordinate Dubai travel with wider Gulf or Saudi Arabia plans. Use this blueprint to move from curiosity to a fully planned trip.

Why Getting This Right Matters

Applying for a Dubai visit visa is straightforward when you know which channel to use, what documents to present and how decisions are communicated. Mistakes commonly lead to delays, non-refundable fees, or stress at the airport. The approach below focuses on avoiding those pitfalls by using airline-managed services or licensed agencies, meeting immigration expectations, and building a simple contingency plan. Understanding Visa Options for Indians

Overview of Visa Types Available to Indian Nationals

Dubai (and the wider UAE) issues several visit-related visa categories. Pick the one that matches your purpose and length of stay:

  • Short transit permits (48-hour or 96-hour) intended for passengers with a planned short stopover.
  • 14-day visa on arrival for eligible Indian nationals who hold a valid US visa/green card or certain residency cards (introduced to simplify short visits).
  • Single-entry tourist visas (30 days) and long-term tourist visas (60 days).
  • Multiple-entry tourist visas that allow repeated visits within the visa validity.
  • Longer residency or family visas (beyond the scope of this article but important for family reunification and work).

Each visa has distinct rules for validity (how long it can be used from issue), permitted stay (days allowed within the country), and whether it is extendable. Fees and processing channels differ as well.

New Arrival Rules That Affect Indian Travelers

From January 2024 the UAE introduced a 14-day visa-on-arrival route for eligible Indian passport holders who carry qualifying foreign visas (for example, a valid US visa or green card) or specific residence permits from certain countries. The fee and eligibility criteria must be checked before travel because the policy is document-specific and can change. If you qualify, this route removes the need for prior application in many cases, but you should still prepare printed copies of your supporting documents for immigration.

Which Visa Type Is Best For You?

Decide based on trip length, number of entries, and how you’re traveling:

  • If you are transiting Dubai for less than four days, apply for a transit visa (48/96 hours) through your airline or travel agent.
  • If you qualify for the 14-day visa-on-arrival, that is the fastest option—carry your qualifying visa/residence card, pay the fee on arrival, and proceed through immigration.
  • For holidays of 30–60 days, choose the single-entry or multiple-entry tourist visa according to how often you plan to re-enter.
  • If someone in the UAE (company, family, or hotel) is sponsoring you, a sponsored visa may be used and can sometimes be faster.

Application Channels From India: Pros, Cons, and When To Use Them

Airline-Managed Visas (Emirates, Etihad and Others)

Many Indian applicants choose to apply via their airline—Emirates, Etihad, or flydubai—because the carrier submits the application on your behalf and the process is streamlined when your flight ticket is already with them. Airlines typically offer online application through a “Manage Booking” page or via their local ticketing offices.

Advantages:

  • Fast and integrated with your flight booking.
  • Minimal paperwork beyond passport copy, photo, hotel booking and ticket details.
  • Clear processing windows and predictable fees.

Limitations:

  • Some airline-managed services require an airline ticket number and itinerary that meets specific conditions (for example, booking with the issuing carrier).
  • Not all ticket types or carriers support all visa categories.
  • If your flight itinerary changes, you must update the visa details.

When to use:

  • You are flying with the same carrier that provides the visa service and your itinerary meets the carrier’s rules.
  • You need a simple tourist or transit visa and prefer convenience.

UAE Embassy / Consulate Or GDRFA / ICA e-Services

The UAE issues visas through federal and emirate-level authorities. Indian applicants can sometimes apply via official channels or through approved typing centers linked to ICA or GDRFA portals. These channels are generally used for specialized visas or by applicants who want to work directly with government systems.

Advantages:

  • Direct route to the issuing authority.
  • Necessary for specific visa classes that must be handled at government level.

Limitations:

  • More complex paperwork and sometimes longer processing times.
  • Typically requires assistance of a sponsor or a local typing center.

When to use:

  • You are applying for a specialist permit or have a local sponsor handling the application.

Travel Agencies / Visa Service Providers (VFS and Licensed Agents)

Licensed travel agencies and visa middlemen in India provide visa application services for Dubai. They collect documents, submit to the appropriate authorities or airline partners, and return approved e-visas to applicants.

Advantages:

  • Helpful for first-time applicants or those needing an agent to coordinate bookings and documents.
  • Agencies often have express options and local support.

Limitations:

  • Additional service fee on top of government/airline visa costs.
  • Variable quality—choose accredited and well-reviewed agencies.

When to use:

  • You want a full-service option that includes ticket and hotel bookings or need help matching the right visa category.

Sponsored Entry (UAE Resident, Company, Hotel)

A UAE resident or registered company can sponsor your entry and apply on your behalf. Sponsors can be family members, employers, or hotels (in some cases hotels book permits for guests).

Advantages:

  • Often the fastest route if the sponsor is reputable and submits correct documents.
  • Useful for family visits or invitations.

Limitations:

  • Requires a trustworthy sponsor and proof of relationship (for family visas).
  • The sponsor assumes responsibility for the visitor, including potential fines for overstays.

When to use:

  • When visiting family, attending business meetings, or relying on a company arrangement.

Documents You Must Prepare (Clear, Practical Checklist)

Below is a concise checklist you will reference throughout the application regardless of channel. Keep scanned copies ready and ensure the originals are available for travel.

  1. Valid passport (minimum six months validity from date of travel).
  2. Passport-sized colour photograph matching UAE photo specifications.
  3. Clear colour scan of passport biodata page and any previous UAE visas or relevant residence permits.
  4. Confirmed round-trip airline ticket or confirmed onward ticket showing travel dates.
  5. Confirmed hotel reservation matching flight dates (if staying in a hotel).
  6. Proof of sufficient funds (bank statements may be required in rare cases).
  7. Proof of relationship for family-sponsored visas (marriage certificate, birth certificate).
  8. If applying for visa-on-arrival via US visa/green card/residence permit route, carry the original qualifying visa or residence card.

(Keep these documents organized in a single folder; airlines and agents will request only what is required for the chosen route.)

Step-by-Step Application Process (Airline Route — Most Common)

  1. Confirm your travel dates and book a refundable or changeable ticket with an airline that offers visa services.
  2. Decide which visa type you need (transit/14-day/on-arrival/30/60-day).
  3. Gather and scan the documents listed above.
  4. Visit the airline’s “Manage Booking” visa portal or local ticket office and fill out the visa application form. Pay the visa fee online or at the office.
  5. Receive the visa reference number and tracking information. Wait for processing (typical 3–5 working days for standard tourist visas).
  6. On approval, download and print the e-visa copy. Keep both digital and printed copies for immigration.
  7. Travel with your travel documents and ensure you present the e-visa at immigration along with any requested supporting papers.

This numbered sequence provides a clear flow for the most common and dependable application route available to Indians.

How to Apply If You Qualify for Visa-On-Arrival (14-Day Route)

If you are an Indian passport holder carrying a valid US visa/green card or a qualifying residence permit, here is the practical approach:

  • Before you travel, confirm the qualifying status of your US visa or residence permit and make digital and printed copies.
  • Ensure your passport has the required minimum validity.
  • Upon arrival in Dubai, proceed to the immigration desk for visa-on-arrival processing. Present your passport, the qualifying visa/residence permit, and any requested documentation.
  • Pay the arrival visa fee (if required) in the accepted currency. Have a small amount of cash or a card as per the airport’s accepted methods.
  • Receive the 14-day permit and keep copies both digitally and printed.

Note: Policy updates can change eligible document lists; always verify immediately before travel.

Processing Times, Fees and Extensions

Typical Processing Times

Processing often depends on the route chosen:

  • Airline-managed visas: 2–7 working days, express options available for additional fees.
  • Travel agency submissions: 3–7 working days depending on the provider.
  • Transit visas (48/96 hours): Usually processed within 24–72 hours but must be applied for before travel.
  • Visa-on-arrival: Immediate processing at immigration if eligibility is confirmed.

Plan with a buffer. Submit applications at least one to two weeks before travel; for complex situations, allow three weeks.

Cost Expectations

Fees vary by visa type, processing speed and provider. Typical cost bands (indicative, subject to change):

  • 48-hour transit visas: modest fee (varies).
  • 14-day visa-on-arrival for eligible Indians: a modest arrival fee.
  • 30-day single-entry tourist visas: moderate fee.
  • 60-day and multiple-entry visas: higher fee.
  • Agency and express processing fees: additional charges apply.

Always confirm fees before submission, and understand the refund policy: many visa fees are non-refundable once a decision is taken.

Extending Your Stay

Some visa types are extendable (for example, certain tourist visas can be extended once at a local immigration office), but rules change. Extensions typically require visiting the immigration or service center in the UAE and paying additional fees. Overstaying without extension results in fines per day.

Common Reasons For Visa Delays or Refusals (And Remedies)

Mistakes or missing documents are the main culprits. Anticipate and address these common issues:

  • Passport validity under six months: remedy by renewing passport prior to applying.
  • Mismatched names, dates or details between passport, ticket and hotel bookings: ensure all documents use identical spellings and formats.
  • Handwritten passports or unclear scans: provide machine-readable passport scans and clear photos.
  • Incorrect photo specifications: follow the UAE’s photo requirements.
  • Using an unlicensed agent: always verify agent accreditation and read reviews.
  • Criminal records or entry bans: resolve legal or documentation problems before application.

If a visa is refused, ask the issuing authority or agent for the reason. Depending on the cause, you may correct details and reapply.

Airport Arrival: What Immigration Will Expect

When you arrive in Dubai, have these ready and organized:

  • Passport and printed e-visa.
  • Airline boarding pass or return ticket.
  • Hotel reservation and local contact details.
  • If relying on visa-on-arrival, the qualifying supporting visa/residence card.
  • Proof of sufficient funds if requested.

Immigration officers may conduct random checks. Always maintain a polite and clear explanation of your travel purpose. Carry copies of any supporting documents you used in the application.

Practical Tips to Avoid Problems

  • Always apply through official or accredited channels. Avoid informal offers that seem cheaper but lack paperwork.
  • Match dates across ticket, hotel and visa application.
  • Register e-visa copies both digitally (phone) and printed.
  • Keep emergency contact numbers for your airline and the Indian consulate in the UAE.
  • Consider travel insurance to cover medical and trip issues.
  • If you plan to visit both the UAE and Saudi Arabia on the same trip, coordinate visas and flights in advance and consult travel resources for both countries to minimize layover-related visa confusion. How to Choose Between Airline, Agency and Sponsor Routes

Consider these questions to choose the best route:

  • Is your airline offering a visa service tied to your ticket? If yes and your itinerary matches the airline’s rules, apply there for speed and convenience.
  • Do you prefer a full-service option with document handling and hotel/ticket bookings? Choose a licensed travel agency.
  • Do you have family, employer, or business contacts in the UAE who will sponsor you? A sponsored application often simplifies the process.
  • Are you eligible for the 14-day visa-on-arrival? If yes, confirm all documents and prefer that route for last-minute travel.

When in doubt, prioritize transparency: use an authorized provider and confirm refund, processing time and service level before you pay.

Cost-Saving Strategies and When to Pay Extra

Saving money is possible, but sometimes paying extra is worth the speed and certainty:

  • Book round-trip flights directly with an airline that provides visas. This can reduce paperwork and coordination time.
  • Combine visa application with your travel booking to avoid duplicate fees and mismatched dates.
  • Avoid the cheapest unverified agents; pay a modest premium for trusted agencies that will resolve problems quickly.
  • For group travel, request group-applying discounts with licensed agents.

If your travel dates are flexible, avoid express processing fees by applying early.

After Your Visa Is Approved: Final Pre-Departure Checklist

Before you fly, finalize these items:

  • Print at least one physical copy of your e-visa and carry digital backups.
  • Re-check passport validity and visa dates.
  • Confirm the hotel check-in and ensure your name is on the booking.
  • Keep copies of your application receipt and any communication with the issuing agency or airline.
  • Notify family or emergency contacts of your itinerary.

At this stage, your remaining task is logistics—packing, insurance, and local transport planning.

Troubleshooting: If Your Visa Is Delayed or Rejected

If you’re notified of delay or rejection:

  • Request a clear explanation and document the communication.
  • If it was a clerical error (name mismatch, wrong dates), correct and reapply quickly.
  • If additional documents are required, supply them immediately and track the application.
  • If rejected due to eligibility concerns, ask for a formal refusal letter to check options for appeal or reapplication.
  • For urgent travel, explore alternative routes like airline-managed express visas or applying through a different accredited agency.

Keep copies of all submissions and communications; they help expedite reapplications.

Security, Health, and Insurance Considerations

Dubai is a safe city, but sensible precautions apply:

  • Obtain travel health insurance that covers the full duration of your stay and potential medical evacuation.
  • Check immunization and health recommendations and understand local healthcare access points.
  • If carrying prescription medications, carry prescriptions and ensure the medicine is permitted in the UAE.

For travelers continuing on to Saudi Arabia, ensure your health documents and insurance meet both countries’ entry requirements.

Practical Itineraries After Arrival (Ideas To Make The Most Of Your Stay)

Once your visa is approved and travel is booked, plan days that match your visa length. For a 7–10 day visit, balance iconic Dubai experiences—downtown Dubai, Palm Jumeirah, a desert safari and a day trip to Abu Dhabi. If you have a longer multi-entry visa, alternate urban exploration with a short road trip to neighboring emirates. Final Legal & Practical Notes

Visa rules can and do change. Always verify up-to-date eligibility and fees before applying. Use official airline portals, licensed agencies, and government channels. Keep receipts and confirmations of payments and correspondence until after your trip is complete.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I get a Dubai visa on arrival as an Indian passport holder?

Yes—eligible Indian passport holders who carry a valid US visa, US green card, or certain qualifying residence permits may obtain a 14-day visa on arrival. Confirm your eligibility immediately before travel and carry printed copies of the qualifying documentation.

2. How long does a standard Dubai tourist visa take to process when applied from India?

Processing typically takes 2–7 working days through airline-managed services or licensed agencies. For peace of mind, apply at least one to two weeks before travel. Express services are available at extra cost.

3. What happens if my passport is due to expire within six months?

You must renew your passport before applying. UAE authorities require at least six months’ validity from the date of arrival for visa issuance and entry.

4. Can I extend my tourist visa after arriving in Dubai?

Some tourist visas are extendable once through UAE immigration for a fee, but extension rules vary by visa class and change periodically. Check the visa’s extendability at approval and plan accordingly.
