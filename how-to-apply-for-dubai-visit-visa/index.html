How to Apply for Dubai Visit Visa

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Travel between the Kingdom and the UAE is growing fast: millions of leisure and business visitors cross the Gulf every year, and Dubai remains one of the region’s most visited cities. If you are planning a short break from Saudi Arabia, a business trip, or a stopover en route to another destination, understanding exactly how to apply for a Dubai visit visa is the single best way to avoid delays and travel stress.

Short answer: If your nationality does not qualify for visa-free entry or visa on arrival, you must obtain a prearranged visit visa before you travel. You can apply through an airline (when flying with carriers that offer visa services), through a UAE-based sponsor (family, employer, or government entity), or via approved visa service providers. Requirements vary by nationality, visa type and sponsor; prepare a valid passport, passport-quality photo, proof of accommodation, return or onward ticket, and in many cases medical insurance and a recent bank statement.

This article explains the visa types available for visitors to Dubai, the precise steps to apply from Saudi Arabia or elsewhere, the documents immigration officers expect, common pitfalls and how to avoid them, and practical tips for arrival, extensions and contingency planning. As the KSA Travel Insider & Cultural Guide for Saudi Travel & Leisure, I’ll walk you through the exact process so you can plan confidently and move from curiosity to a well-executed Dubai trip.

Who Needs a Dubai Visit Visa?

Understanding visa-free vs visa-required nationalities

Dubai welcomes travelers under different entry regimes. Nationals of many countries can enter the UAE without a pre-arranged visa and receive a visa stamp on arrival valid for 30, 90 or 180 days depending on passport. Other nationalities must obtain a visit visa in advance. Whether you need a visa depends on:

  • The nationality shown on your passport.
  • The type of passport (diplomatic or ordinary).
  • Residency in countries that grant special entry privileges (for example, holders of valid US visas or GCC residence permits may have simplified entry for certain nationalities).
  • The purpose and length of stay (tourism, visiting family, attending a mission, transit).

If you’re unsure, begin by checking official visa rules for the UAE; for a Saudi-focused planning approach, our planning your Saudi itinerary resources highlight typical documentation and timelines for Gulf travel and how to coordinate multi-country trips.

Common cases for Saudi residents and expatriates

Saudi nationals do not need a visa to visit the UAE for tourism; they receive entry directly. If you are a Saudi resident but hold a different passport, check whether your passport qualifies for visa on arrival or whether you must apply for a prearranged visit visa. Many expatriates living and working in the Kingdom manage their Dubai visas through airlines or travel agencies in Saudi Arabia; later sections explain how to do that safely and efficiently.

Types of Dubai Visit Visas Explained

Visa on Arrival

For passport holders from specified countries, the UAE provides visa-on-arrival services at entry points. The most common durations are 30 days and 90 days, with a rare 180-day facility for certain passports. Visa on arrival is automatic at immigration if you meet nationality eligibility and passport validity requirements. Typical conditions include:

  • Passport valid for at least six months.
  • A confirmed onward or return ticket.
  • No disqualifying immigration history.

If you qualify, visa on arrival is the fastest entry option. However, you should carry printed copies of accommodation bookings and return tickets to satisfy random screens.

Prearranged Visit Visas (Tourist Visas)

Prearranged or preapproved visit visas are required when visa on arrival or visa-free entry is not available for your nationality. These can be single-entry or multiple-entry, short-term (30 days) or longer (60 or 90 days), and may be issued by airlines, hotels, approved travel agencies, or directly by relevant UAE immigration authorities.

Breakdown of common prearranged visas you will encounter:

  • Transit visas (48-hour or 96-hour): For travelers stopping in Dubai en route. They are short validity, non-extendable, and often issued only to passengers holding tickets with qualifying airlines.
  • Tourist short-term (30 days): Standard option for most short stays. Often extendable once at local immigration for an additional fee.
  • Tourist long-term (60 or 90 days): For longer stays; usually single or multiple entry with a higher fee and stricter documentation.
  • Multiple-entry long-term tourist visas: Introduced more recently, including a five-year multiple-entry visa for applicants meeting financial and insurance criteria. These permits allow multiple 90-day stays per entry and are attractive for frequent travelers.

When you read visa descriptions, pay attention to “valid before entry” vs “valid after entry” windows (how long you have to travel after the visa is issued) and to extension rules.

Sponsorship-Based Visit Visas

A UAE resident—often a family member, friend, or employer—can sponsor a visit visa on your behalf through local immigration offices. Government entities can also sponsor foreign visitors for official business. Sponsorship often requires the sponsor to submit copies of their own ID, proof of residency, and a formal undertaking. If your sponsor handles the application, confirm they will send you a copy of the visa approval before departure.

Airline and Hotel-Sponsored Visas

Major carriers and hotels issue tourist visas as part of their passenger services, provided you travel on qualifying tickets and maintain confirmed reservations. Airlines such as Emirates and Flydubai offer streamlined application processes for passengers who book their flights with them. Hotels can also arrange visas if you purchase a stay package. This route is convenient for travelers booking through travel agents or directly with the airline or hotel, but it’s important to verify fees, refund policies, and processing timelines before payment.

Governmental and Special Visas

If you’re traveling for medical treatment, business missions, training or study visits, or you are eligible for diplomatic or UN visas, specific procedures and documentation apply. Government-sponsored visas typically need prior approvals and invitation letters.

Step-By-Step: How To Apply For A Dubai Visit Visa

Before the lists below: ensure your passport meets the six-month validity rule and that you have digital-quality passport photos. Use the following practical process whether you apply from Saudi Arabia or elsewhere.

Step 1 — Confirm your visa eligibility and choose the best route

Start by confirming whether you need a visa at all. If you do, choose how you will apply: through an airline (if you have a qualifying booking), a UAE-based sponsor, a licensed travel agent or hotel, or through approved online channels via UAE immigration services. Each route has pros and cons:

  • Airline application: Fast and integrated with your ticket; often the easiest for travelers booking flights with Emirates or Flydubai.
  • Hotel or agency: Good for package travelers who want the operator to manage the paperwork.
  • Sponsor: Required for some nationalities and ideal when family or business hosts are in the UAE.
  • Direct official application: For those who prefer handling the process themselves and meeting ICP/GDRFA requirements.

For practical, locally oriented planning, our detailed Dubai travel resources explain which routes suit distinct traveler profiles.

Step 2 — Gather required documents

Documentation varies by visa type and nationality, but universally expected documents include:

  • A passport copy (color), with at least six months validity from entry date.
  • Passport-sized photograph that meets biometric standards.
  • Proof of a confirmed return or onward ticket.
  • Hotel reservation confirmation or proof of staying with a sponsor (tenancy contract or family ID where applicable).
  • Proof of travel medical insurance for certain longer-term visas.
  • Bank statements or financial evidence for some long-term or multiple-entry visas.

  • Passport-style color photo (biometric quality)
  • Confirmed return or onward ticket
  • Hotel reservation or sponsor’s proof of accommodation
  • Travel medical insurance (if required)
  • Recent bank statement (may be required for long-term visas)
  • Any sponsor letters, marriage or birth certificates (where family sponsorship is used)

Step 3 — Complete the application and pay fees

If applying via an airline: log into the airline’s Manage Booking portal after your ticket is issued and follow the visa application flow. Airlines will list fee options (transit, 30-day, 60-day, 90-day), and processing times. If using a travel agency or hotel, submit documents to them and confirm the timeline and refund policy.

If applying through a sponsor: your sponsor will upload your documents to the local immigration office system. Always request a copy of the visa approval or reference number before traveling.

Processing fees differ by visa type and sponsor channel. Keep printed or digital receipts and the application reference number. For those using direct official channels, most long-term tourist visas are extendable for a published extension fee; transit visas are non-extendable.

Step 4 — Receive visa approval and verify details

Once the visa is approved, you’ll receive an electronic entry permit or an approval number. Verify the visa type, number of allowed entries, validity window and permitted length of stay. Print a hard copy of the visa approval to carry to the airport; some airports conduct random visual screening and may ask for a printed visa confirmation.

Step 5 — Arrival paperwork and immigration

At Dubai entry points, immigration officers check passports, visas, onward tickets and may request proof of accommodation or funds. For travelers from Saudi Arabia or other Gulf countries, be prepared for occasional random interviews and visual screening. Keep digital copies of your visa, passport, and bookings accessible, but also carry a printed set to present at screening counters when requested.

Step 6 — Extensions and exit

If you need to extend a visit visa, check local immigration rules for eligibility and fees. Many tourist visas can be extended once for a set fee, but transit and some short-stay visas are non-extendable. Always respect your visa’s exit date to avoid fines. If you overstay, expect fines calculated per day and potential future entry restrictions.

Applying From Saudi Arabia — Practical Options and Timelines

Applying via airlines based in Saudi Arabia

If you book an Emirates, Flydubai, Etihad or other Gulf carrier from Riyadh, Jeddah or Dammam, the airline may allow you to apply for a UAE entry permit with your ticket. This is particularly convenient if you prefer an integrated experience: book flights, upload documents and receive visa approval without visiting consulates.

When you apply through the airline, follow these steps:

  • Confirm that your itinerary qualifies for the airline’s visa service.
  • Upload a clear passport copy and photograph.
  • Provide travel dates and hotel bookings.
  • Pay the airline’s visa fee (which typically includes VFS or processing fees).

Processing time is usually 3–5 business days; plan for at least a week if you have complicated documentation or if you require sponsor approvals.

Applying through Saudi travel agencies and hotels

Licensed Saudi travel agents commonly handle UAE visa applications for customers. If you go this route, ask for:

  • A written breakdown of service fees and the government visa fee.
  • The expected processing timeline and refund policy if the visa is denied.
  • A copy of the visa authorization letter or entry permit before travel.

When using a hotel to sponsor your visa, be certain the hotel reservation is refundable or modifiable in case your travel dates change.

Direct embassy/consular application (less common)

The UAE embassies and consulates typically do not issue tourist visas directly to individual applicants in most cases. Instead, they provide support for diplomatic, governmental and some special cases. For standard tourist visas, airlines, hotels or sponsors remain the primary channels.

Preparation timeline

For routine tourist visas, allow at least 5–10 working days from document submission to visa approval. For more complex cases—such as government sponsorships, family group applications, or long-term multiple entry visas—allow several weeks for processing and approvals.

For Saudi residents who travel frequently to Dubai, consider securing longer multiple entry options if eligible, such as the new five-year multiple-entry resident tourist visa (requirements include proof of funds and insurance).

Fees, Processing Times, Extensions and Overstay Penalties

Typical fee ranges and what they cover

Visa fees depend on the type and channel of application. Expect to pay anything from nominal transit fees for a 48–96 hour permit to higher amounts for 60– or 90-day visas. Airlines and agents charge service or processing fees on top of government visa fees. Fees are generally non-refundable once processing begins.

Indicative fee examples (these are frequently updated and vary by channel and nationality):

  • Transit visa (48 hours): modest fee, usually non-extendable.
  • Short-term tourist (30 days): moderate fee, often extendable once for a higher rate.
  • Long-term tourist (60–90 days): higher fee, often extendable.
  • Multiple-entry long-term or 5-year visa: significantly higher, with financial eligibility criteria.

Always confirm exact fees with the agency or airline you use.

Processing time expectations

Standard processing turns around in 3–7 working days for most airline or agency-submitted applications. Government sponsorships sometimes take longer due to required internal approvals. During peak travel seasons or national holidays, expect delays and plan extra buffer days.

Extensions and overstays

Short tourist visas are sometimes extendable once within the UAE, subject to fees and documentation. Transit visas are usually non-extendable. Overstaying a visa triggers daily fines and may complicate future entry; fines accumulate quickly and can result in deportation processes in extreme cases. If your plans change, contact the local immigration office early to explore legal extension options.

Eligibility Nuances and Special Cases

Indian nationals — recent changes and specifics

India has seen several entry-rule updates affecting visa duration and on-arrival eligibility, including new facilities where eligible Indian passport holders with qualifying residency or US visa status can receive short visit permits on arrival or through airline-managed preapprovals. If you hold an Indian passport, confirm whether your specific residency stamps or visas (US, UK, EU, or GCC residence permits) make you eligible for on-arrival entry or a preapproved short stay. Document validity (six months minimum) remains essential.

US, UK, Schengen and GCC residence holders

Holders of long-term residence permits (for example, US green card) or valid visas from certain countries may be eligible for facilitated entry or on-arrival visas. Check the specific combination of your passport nationality and residence status. These rules can permit short 14-day or longer stays with simplified documentation.

Minors, family travel and special documents

Traveling with children or being a parent sponsoring a minor requires specific authorization documents and birth certificates. Some visas may not be available for unaccompanied minors unless traveling with parents or legal guardians. When traveling with family members, prepare marriage and birth certificates if the sponsor is a family member.

Medical treatment, business missions and official visits

If your visit is for medical reasons, training, study or official government business, the documentation and processing timelines differ. Medical visas often require hospital letters and appointment confirmations; official business invites require government or company letters.

Common Mistakes and How To Avoid Them

Mistake: Traveling without printed visa approval

Some travelers rely entirely on digital copies and are surprised when asked for a printed visa confirmation during random airport screening. Always carry a printed copy of your visa approval and your booking confirmations. Dubai immigration sometimes conducts visual checks that require a printed permit.

Mistake: Misunderstanding visa validity windows

A visa’s date of issue and “valid to enter” window can differ. For example, a prearranged visa might be valid for 60 days from issue—if you do not travel within that period, the visa will expire. Verify entry windows and match your travel schedule to the visa’s effective dates.

Mistake: Not checking passport validity

A passport with less than six months validity will be rejected at immigration. Check your passport expiry well ahead of travel and renew when necessary.

Mistake: Using an unlicensed travel agent

Unlicensed agents sometimes promise fast visas but fail to provide legitimate approvals. Use established airlines, certified travel agencies, hotels with visa services, or sponsor arrangements through known residents. If in doubt, contact the UAE embassy or consult credible resources such as our regional Gulf travel comparisons for recommended channels.

Mistake: Ignoring insurance and bank proof requirements for long-term visas

Some multi-entry or long-term tourist visas require medical insurance or minimum bank balances in the applicant’s account. Prepare these documents in advance to avoid rejection.

Arrival, Immigration Processing and Practical Tips

What to expect at the airport

On arrival at Dubai International or other UAE entry points, present your passport, printed visa permit and return ticket. Keep accommodation booking and proof of funds accessible. Expect a short interview in some cases and random visual or document screening. If flagged for random checks, presenting clear documentation and remaining calm will smooth the process.

Cultural and practical arrival tips

Respect local customs on arrival and during your stay. Dress modestly in public spaces, especially in traditional neighborhoods, and be mindful of public behavior rules. When engaging in business or family visits, confirm local expectations in advance and carry business cards for formal meetings.

If you plan an inter-emirate itinerary, include time for travel between Dubai and Abu Dhabi or other emirates; our Abu Dhabi practical tips page outlines practicalities for visitors who wish to combine stops.

Transiting through Dubai

If Dubai is a transit point for your onward travel, check transit visa rules carefully. Some transit visas require a minimum connection time and an eligible ticket. Airlines often handle short transit visas as part of their service packages.

Applying for Longer or Multiple-Entry Visas

Multiple-entry and long-term tourist visas

The UAE now offers options for multiple-entry long-term tourist visas, including a five-year multiple-entry permit with eligibility that typically requires proof of financial means (for example, a minimum bank balance over a specified period), valid travel medical insurance and confirmed ticketing. These are suitable for frequent travelers who visit the UAE regularly and wish to streamline future trips.

When applying, expect to provide:

  • Six months of bank statements showing the required balance.
  • A valid travel insurance policy that covers the UAE.
  • Accommodation proof for initial entry.

If you travel frequently between Saudi Arabia and the UAE, multiple-entry visas can be a practical investment.

Business and mission-based long stays

If entering on a business mission or planned training, obtain an invitation and supporting letters from the host company or government entity. Government-sponsored visits often have distinct visa categories and processing timelines.

Troubleshooting: Denied Visas, Delays, and Lost Documents

If your visa application is denied, you will usually receive a reason. Common causes include insufficient documentation, prior immigration violations, or inaccuracies in the application. Remedies include:

  • Reapplying with corrected documentation.
  • Sponsorship through a UAE resident or government entity.
  • Consulting a licensed immigration advisor or the embassy when refusals relate to complex immigration histories.

If you lose your passport while in the UAE, contact your embassy or consulate immediately for emergency travel documentation and report the loss to local police. If your visa approval is lost before travel, request the issuing airline or sponsor to resend the electronic approval.

Safety, Insurance and Health Requirements

Travel medical insurance

Certain UAE visa categories require travel medical insurance valid in the UAE. Even when not mandatory, insurance is strongly recommended: it covers emergency healthcare costs and can be essential for long-term stays. Verify that your insurance policy clearly states coverage in the UAE and the policy number and contact details are available.

Health regulations and COVID-era updates

Entry health rules can evolve, especially during global health events. Always check the latest entry requirements for health checks, vaccination certificates, or testing rules before travel. Airlines and immigration services publish updated directives that you should confirm within days of departure.

Cost-Saving Tips and Smart Planning

  • Book a flight that offers airline visa services if you prefer a bundled application process—this typically reduces the paperwork you handle.
  • If you have frequent short visits, evaluate whether a multiple-entry visa or longer-term tourist visa is more cost-effective.
  • Avoid last-minute processing: rush applications attract premium fees and risk denial if documents are missing.
  • Use trusted travel agencies and hotels with clear refund and rescheduling policies.

Decision Framework: Which Application Path Fits You?

Option A — Visa on arrival eligible

If your passport qualifies for on-arrival entry, you usually don’t need to do anything before travel beyond ensuring passport validity and holding return tickets and accommodation proof.

Option B — Airline-managed visa (recommended for simple itineraries)

If you book with an airline offering visa services, this is often the easiest route. It centralizes ticketing and visa processing and reduces the risk of mismatched travel documents.

Option C — Sponsor application (necessary for certain nationalities)

When required by your nationality or if a family member is hosting, a UAE sponsor is the standard path. Confirm the sponsor’s responsibility and that they will provide the electronic visa copy.

Option D — Hotel or travel agency (suitable for packaged travel)

Hotel and agency applications are excellent for packaged tourists. Ask for written assurances about fees and refund conditions.

FAQ

Q1: How long does a typical tourist visa take to process? A: Most airline- or agency-submitted tourist visas process within 3–7 working days, though some cases can take longer depending on nationality, sponsor approvals, or peak travel periods. Always allow extra buffer time before your intended travel dates.

Q2: Can I extend a Dubai visit visa while in the UAE? A: Many short-term tourist visas can be extended once for a published fee, but transit visas are generally non-extendable. Check the extension rules for your specific visa type and contact local immigration early if your plans change.

Q3: Do I need travel medical insurance for a Dubai visit visa? A: Some visa categories, particularly longer-term and multi-entry tourist visas, require medical insurance valid in the UAE. Even when not mandated, insurance is strongly recommended to cover unexpected medical costs.

Q4: What happens if my visa application is denied? A: You’ll typically receive a reason for denial. Remedies include reapplying with corrected documentation, using a UAE-based sponsor, or seeking a different visa category. In complicated cases, consult the sponsoring entity or a licensed immigration advisor before rebooking travel.
