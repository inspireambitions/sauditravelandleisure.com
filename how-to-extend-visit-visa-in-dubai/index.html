How to Extend Visit Visa in Dubai

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Dubai remains one of the world’s most visited cities for business, leisure and family visits, and travelers from the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia are among the most frequent guests. If your plans change or you simply want to stay longer to savor the city’s beaches, events and business meetings, knowing precisely how to extend a visit visa in Dubai is essential to avoid fines, stress and unexpected travel.

Short answer: You can extend most Dubai visit visas from inside the UAE using official channels such as GDRFA (General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs) or the federal ICP platforms. Extensions are applied in fixed increments, require a valid passport and visa copy, and carry fees (typically AED 600 plus VAT and local levies); total stay in the UAE must not exceed 180 days in a calendar year. Processing is usually quick when documents are correct, but you must start the process before your current visa expires.

This article explains the step-by-step process for extending a visit visa in Dubai, the documents and fees you’ll need, the options if you’ve already overstayed, and practical planning advice tailored for travelers from Saudi Arabia and the wider Gulf region. As Saudi Travel & Leisure’s KSA Travel Insider, I’ll combine local insight and practical checklists so you can plan a smooth extension and continue exploring Dubai with confidence.

How Dubai Visit Visa Extensions Work: The Fundamentals

Which Visit Visas Can Be Extended?

Dubai offers several short-term entry permits that are commonly called “visit visas.” The most relevant types for visitors who may need an extension are:

  • Short tourist visit visas (commonly 30-day or 60-day permits).
  • Long-stay visit visas (90-day single-entry permissions).
  • Multiple-entry tourist visas issued for specified periods.
  • Visit visas sponsored by hotels, airlines, or private sponsors (family, employers, companies).

Most of these visit visa categories allow in-country extensions, but the rules vary by visa type and issuing authority. The General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA) in Dubai and the federal ICP handle approvals and processing. The most important limits to remember are that total permitted stay in the UAE cannot exceed 180 days in a calendar year and that you must apply before your visa’s expiry to avoid fines.

Legal and Practical Limits: Duration, Renewal Count, and Total Stay

If you’re asking how to extend visit visa in Dubai, know this: extensions are finite and governed by two parallel constraints. First, individual extensions are allowed in set increments (commonly 30 days at a time for many visit visas). Second, the combined total time a non-resident may remain within the UAE across the calendar year is capped—180 days. Immigration authorities reserve the right to approve or deny extensions, especially if the original purpose of the visit appears to have changed.

Channels to Apply: Digital vs. In-Person

You can extend a visit visa through several channels:

  • Online via the GDRFA Dubai smart services or the federal ICP/Tasjeel platforms (recommended for speed and documentation tracking).
  • In person at GDRFA service centers, Amer Centres, or designated visa service centers in airports.
  • Through the issuing agency (e.g., accredited travel agency or tourism office that originally issued the visa).
  • Via an authorized third-party visa service or registered PRO if you prefer someone to handle logistics.

Digital applications are increasingly preferred for their speed and automations. When applying online, you’ll need an account—often authenticated by UAE Pass—or your sponsor/agent’s credentials.

Step-by-Step Process: How to Extend Visit Visa in Dubai (In-Country)

Preparing Before You Apply

Begin with a careful review of your current visa: check the expiry date, the visa type, and whether it allows extensions. Do not wait until the last day; begin preparations at least one week prior to expiry to avoid rushed fees or the risk of overstay.

Gather core documents and ensure they meet the criteria (passport validity, clear scans, visa copy). Confirm whether your visa was issued by a hotel or travel agency—if so, you may need to route the extension request back through that sponsor.

Required Documents

  1. Valid passport copy (page with personal details) — passport should be valid for at least six months from the date of application.
  2. A clear copy of your current visit visa (entry permit or visa page).
  3. Completed application form (online form filled or printed form for in-person submission).
  4. Payment method for fees (credit/debit card for online; cash/card at centers).
  5. Any additional documents requested by the issuing authority or sponsor (e.g., sponsorship letters, hotel booking confirmation, or proof of onward travel).

(For convenience, see the Documents checklist below in list format.)

Documents Checklist

  • Passport scan (bio page)
  • Current visa copy (entry stamp or visa page)
  • Filled application (digital or paper)
  • Proof of sponsor if required (hotel booking, invitation letter)
  • Payment details

Submitting Your Application Online

The online route is recommended. Here’s the practical sequence:

  1. Access the GDRFA Dubai smart services portal or ICP online service; sign in using UAE Pass or your registered credentials.
  2. Search for the “Extend Visit Visa” service or equivalent.
  3. Fill in the application fields carefully: name, passport number, visa number, entry date, sponsor details if applicable.
  4. Upload the documents in the prescribed formats and resolutions.
  5. Pay the prescribed fees via the portal (card or e-payment).
  6. Submit the application and retain the payment/transaction confirmation.

After submission, you’ll receive a reference or application number. Use it to track status through the portal.

Submitting Your Application In-Person

For visitors preferring face-to-face assistance:

  1. Visit a nearby GDRFA service center, Amer Center, or an authorized visa center.
  2. Take your documents and passport (original may be required for verification).
  3. Fill the paper form if required; the agent will accept documents and explain any sponsor-specific steps.
  4. Pay the fee and receive an application receipt and tracking number.

In-person processing timelines can be slightly longer than online but are helpful for complex cases or if you need immediate assistance.

What Happens After Submission: Approval and Stamps

When the extension is approved, the updated visa will be issued electronically. Your passport may not get a physical sticker; instead, the new end date is recorded in immigration systems. If the extension requires an exit/entry stamp later, the immigration officer will advise during departure.

Fees, Timelines and Practical Cost Examples

Standard Fee Components

Expect these common charges when you apply for an in-country visit visa extension in Dubai:

  • Extension fee per 30-day increment: typically AED 600 (subject to periodic updates).
  • Value Added Tax (VAT): 5% applied where relevant.
  • Knowledge and Innovation Dirham fees: small nominal municipal fees (e.g., AED 10 each).
  • Additional in-country processing fee (applies in some cases): e.g., AED 500.
  • Sponsor or agency service charges if you use a private consultant or PRO.

Fees can vary by visa type, sponsor, and whether you’re applying online or in person. Always check the final payable amount on the submission screen before you confirm.

Typical Timelines

Processing times usually range from 48 hours to five working days, depending on document completeness and the specific channel used. Online applications, when documents are correct, will often complete faster. If you apply through a third-party sponsor or travel office, their internal processing time adds to the overall timeline.

Example Cost Scenarios

  • Extending a 30-day tourist visa for one additional 30 days (standard online): AED 600 + 5% VAT + small dirham fees = approximately AED 650–700 total.
  • Extending from inside the UAE with extra in-country fee: AED 600 + AED 500 + VAT and dirham fees = approximately AED 1,200–1,300 per 30-day increment.

Be aware that multiple increments add up quickly. If you anticipate a long stay, evaluate whether switching to another visa type (e.g., a long-stay visa or a residency permit via an employer) is more cost-efficient.

Common Questions and Situations: What Travelers Ask Most

Can I Extend My Visa Multiple Times?

Yes, extensions are possible multiple times within limits set by the issuing authority and subject to the 180-day calendar-year cap. Approval is not guaranteed and depends on your visa category and immigration discretion.

What If I Miss the Expiry Date?

If you overstay, the UAE charges fines for each day beyond the visa expiration (commonly AED 50 per day, subject to updates). Persistent overstay can result in additional penalties, deportation orders, or bans. If you find yourself in this situation, address it immediately—visit the nearest GDRFA or Amer center and seek a formal resolution. If a fine applies, you will need to pay it; unresolved overstays create complications for future UAE travel.

Can I Apply for an Extension from Outside the UAE?

Typically, extension applications are processed in-country or by the agency that issued the visa. If you leave the UAE and your visa has expired, a re-entry will require a new permit or reissuance. Some visa types can be renewed by the issuing agency while you’re outside, but that process varies by sponsor and visa origin.

Does My Nationality Affect Extensions?

Visa issuance rules at entry are nationality-dependent, but extension permissions are based more on the visa category than your passport. Some nationalities receive visas on arrival and can still apply for extensions through the proper channels. Check whether your initial visa was issued on arrival and whether that variant allows in-country extensions.

Avoiding Common Mistakes When Extending a Dubai Visit Visa

Mistake: Waiting Until the Last Minute

Applying on the final day invites problems: payment errors, evidence requests, or slow processing can leave you inadvertently overstaying. Begin the extension process at least one week before expiry. If your stay plans are uncertain, consider applying earlier; many systems allow you to request an extension while weeks remain.

Mistake: Uploading Poor-Quality Documents

Smudged passport scans, wrong file formats, or cropped visa images are common reasons for delays. Scan at 300 DPI where possible, keep images under portal size limits but clear, and verify that all text is legible.

Mistake: Assuming All Visa Types Are Identical

Sponsorship and issuing authority matter. If your visa was arranged by a hotel, airline, or government sector, follow the sponsor’s required steps rather than trying a generic online route.

Mistake: Not Tracking the Application

Always save the application number and payment confirmation. Check the portal regularly and respond promptly if additional documents are requested.

Alternatives If an Extension Is Denied or Not Possible

Exit and Re-Enter (Visa Run)

One traditional option is to exit the UAE and apply for a new entry permit from abroad. That can be done via consulates, airline-issued visas, or online visa services. This approach is viable for many nationalities, but be mindful of the 180-day annual limit and any rules about multiple-entries under the same visa.

Apply for a Different Visa Type

If you need long-term presence (for business assignments, family reasons, or extended work), consult a sponsor or PRO about converting to a work permit, a residence visa, or a longer-duration visit visa. Conversion rules are more complex and may require employer sponsorship, medical checks, and Emirates ID processing.

Use a Local Sponsor or Employer

A local sponsor—family member, company or government entity—may help transition your status legally. This often involves a formal application and can be the most direct route to extended residency eligibility.

Seek Professional Assistance

When visa matters are time-sensitive or complicated (overstay, sponsorship disputes, or penalties), licensed PROs and immigration consultants can provide structured help. If you prefer guidance, you can consult reputable service providers in Dubai who specialize in residency and visa services.

Practical Timeline Example: How to Plan an Extension During Your Trip

A simple, example timeline helps you visualize the extension path:

  • Day 0–7 after arrival: Decide whether your stay will exceed your original visa. If yes, prepare documents and pay.
  • Day 7–10 before visa expiry: Submit the online extension application and pay fees.
  • Day 2–5 after submission: Await approval; check the portal daily. If additional documents are requested, supply them immediately.
  • Day of approval: Confirm the new end date and retain proof of the updated visa in your phone and email.
  • If denied: Immediately consult the issuing sponsor or visit a GDRFA/Immigration counter for next steps.

(First list — Required Documents)

  • Passport copy (bio page), valid for at least six months
  • Copy of current visit visa/entry stamp
  • Filled extension application form (online or paper)
  • Proof of sponsor if applicable (hotel booking, invitation letter)
  • Payment for extension and any local fees

(Second list — Final Quick Checklist Before Submission)

  • Confirm passport validity and visa copy
  • Scan clean images and name files clearly
  • Save payment method and ensure sufficient funds
  • Note your application number and save confirmation
  • Set a calendar reminder to check status and collect final approval

Note: These two lists are intentionally compact to give precise, actionable items without distracting from the detailed prose above.

Red Flags and When to Contact Authorities or a Consultant

If any of the following apply, seek immediate assistance:

  • Your extension is still pending on the expiry date.
  • You received a notification of rejection without clear reasons.
  • You have already overstayed and face fines or a hold on your passport.
  • The sponsor who issued your original visa is not responding.

In these cases, visit the nearest GDRFA or Amer center, or engage a licensed immigration consultant. When choosing an external service, check reviews and ensure they are registered to interact with UAE immigration on behalf of clients.

How to Monitor Status and Keep Records

Keep both digital and physical records. Save screenshot receipts, email confirmations, and the updated visa notification in multiple places: your phone, email and a cloud backup. Use the application number to track status through the GDRFA or ICP platforms, and print any final approval notices before the date you plan to travel or depart.

Mistakes to Avoid After Approval

Approval doesn’t mean complacency. Ensure the new end date is recorded in your passport system (check online or with the immigration desk). Keep proof of the extension with you. If you plan an exit and re-entry before your new expiry date, confirm the permissions for multiple entries exist on your specific visa type.

Planning Longer Stays: When Extensions Become Inefficient

If you are extending a tourist visit repeatedly, it may be preferable to evaluate alternate stays such as a residency permit, employment visa, or long-term visit visa. Repeated extensions cost more over time and create administrative overhead. Consult local sponsors or PROs about converting your status rather than continuing serial extensions.

Final Practical Scenarios and How to Handle Them

Scenario: Your Visa Is Approved but Travel Plans Change

If your extension is approved and you later need to leave before the new expiry, ensure your passport has the appropriate exit stamp. Keep the extension confirmation should immigration officers ask.

Scenario: Your Extension Is Rejected

If rejected, find out the precise reason immediately. Sometimes rejections are document-related and can be rectified quickly. If the reason is substantive (e.g., immigration discretion), consult the issuing sponsor for next steps; you may need to exit the country and apply for a new entry permit.

Scenario: You Need an Immediate Solution for Overstay

Visit immigration in person, pay fines, and seek a formal exit permit if applicable. Avoid further travel until the issue is resolved to prevent travel holds or future entry bans.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How long does it take to get a Dubai visit visa extension approved?

Processing typically takes from 48 hours up to five working days depending on the channel used (online versus in-person) and whether additional documents are requested. Submit early to avoid last-minute delays.

2. What are the total allowed days I can stay in the UAE in a year?

A non-resident’s combined stay in the UAE cannot exceed 180 days in a calendar year, including original visa time and any approved extensions.

3. Can I extend my visa after I have already overstayed?

You cannot extend a visa while you are overstaying without first resolving fines and penalties. Visit the nearest immigration office to clarify your status, pay any due fines, and seek guidance on the correct remedy.

4. If my visa was issued by a hotel or airline, can I still extend it myself?

Often you will need to route the extension request through the original sponsor (hotel, airline, or agency). Check your visa paperwork and contact the issuing entity to confirm the correct process for extensions.
