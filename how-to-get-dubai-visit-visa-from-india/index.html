How to Get Dubai Visit Visa From India

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Dubai remains one of the top destinations for Indian travelers—whether for weekend escapes, business stopovers, or family holidays. Each year millions of visitors fly between India and the UAE, drawn by Dubai’s skyline, modern comforts, and regional connectivity. Navigating the visa process is the first practical step that turns that desire into a confirmed trip.

Short answer: Indian nationals can obtain a Dubai visit visa through several channels: visa on arrival (for a narrow set of eligibility conditions), airline-arranged preapproved permits, online e-visa services, visa application centres such as VFS, or sponsors (hotels, tour operators, or UAE residents). The fastest routes are airline-managed entry permits if you have an eligible booking, or the visa-on-arrival for those who meet the new eligibility rules introduced in recent years. This article explains each pathway, documents required, timelines, costs, common pitfalls, and a complete planning framework to secure your Dubai visit visa from India with confidence.

This post aims to be your practical blueprint: clear steps you can follow, decision points to weigh, and real-world tips from the KSA Travel Insider perspective so you travel smoothly between India and the Gulf. Along the way I’ll also show how to combine planning for Dubai with regional travel planning resources to help you expand a short visit into a well-crafted Gulf itinerary.

Why the Visa Process Matters: A Practical Frame

Getting a visa is more than paperwork—it's the hinge on which timing, budget, and itinerary depend. Approaching the process as a planning exercise reduces last-minute stress: decide your travel dates first, choose the most efficient visa channel for those dates, and collect documents with buffers for processing and unexpected requests.

My approach as the KSA Travel Insider is to combine local knowledge and a project-like checklist so you complete visa steps efficiently and avoid common administrative traps. Below we translate that into actionable sections you can follow step-by-step.

Who Can Enter Dubai Without a Preapproved Visa?

Visa On Arrival: Who Is Eligible and What Changed Recently

Historically, the UAE allowed nationals of many countries to receive visas on arrival. For Indian passport holders, the landscape evolved with new rules and expanded eligibility for entry permits:

  • Indian nationals who hold valid residence permits or visas from certain countries (for example, US, UK, Schengen states, and some OECD countries) have been granted special entry permits or visas on arrival under specific conditions. As of early 2024, eligible Indian travelers with a qualifying US visa or green card, or valid residence permits from countries such as the UK, EU member states, Australia, Canada and a few others, can obtain a single-entry 14-day visa on arrival for a small fee. The permitted combinations and validity vary, so confirm eligibility before travel.
  • For most Indian passport holders without those third-country permits, a prearranged visa remains necessary before arrival.

If you are unsure whether your passport and residency/visa status qualify for an on-arrival permit, the safest move is to check with the airline or apply for a preapproved permit through one of the channels below.

Visa Channels: Which One Should You Choose?

There are five practical routes to getting a Dubai visit visa from India. Each has trade-offs in speed, control, documentation, and cost.

1) Airline-Arranged Preapproved Entry Permit

Airlines such as Emirates and Etihad offer visa processing linked to your ticket booking. If your itinerary is issued on the airline’s ticket stock and matches their conditions, you can apply through the airline’s “Manage Booking” portal or at a ticketing office.

Advantages:

  • Fast, mostly online process.
  • Ideal for travellers already booked on eligible flights.
  • Immigration may accept e-visas without passport stamping.

Limitations:

  • You must have an eligible ticket (e.g., full Emirates itinerary for Emirates visa service).
  • The airline may require specific ticket numbers, and changes to the itinerary can complicate the application.
  • Fees and VFS processing charges may apply.

2) VFS Global or Commercial Visa Centres (In-Country Application)

VFS and other visa handling agencies represent UAE authorities in India. They accept applications, verify documents, and submit to UAE immigration.

Advantages:

  • Clear, structured process handled locally.
  • Offers both standard and express processing options.
  • Physical centres allow document submission and queries.

Limitations:

  • Requires visits to the centre for biometric or document submission in some cases.
  • Fees can be higher for express service.
  • Processing times vary by centre and season; plan ahead.

3) UAE Government E-Visa (ICA / GDRFA Channels)

You can submit a direct application to the UAE’s immigration authorities (ICA or the relevant emirate’s General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs) via approved portals or partnered service providers.

Advantages:

  • Direct with the authority that issues the visa.
  • Clear rules about validity, duration, and extension.

Limitations:

  • Not all nationalities or visa types are eligible for direct online submission from India.
  • The process can require specific supporting documents (hotel bookings, return tickets).

4) Hotel or Travel Agent Sponsorship (Visa on Behalf)

Hotels, tour operators, or travel agencies can apply for a visitor visa as a sponsor. Many UAE hotels and inbound tour operators provide visa processing as part of a package.

Advantages:

  • Convenient—packaged with booked hotels or tours.
  • Useful for travellers unfamiliar with visa procedures.

Limitations:

  • Higher fees due to service charges.
  • You must trust the provider to handle your personal data securely.

5) UAE-Based Family Member or Employer Sponsorship

A UAE resident (a relative or employer) can sponsor your visit. This requires the sponsor to submit the application from within the UAE.

Advantages:

  • Reliable if you have a known sponsor.
  • Sometimes faster if the sponsor has an established local status.

Limitations:

  • Not applicable for independent travelers without UAE contacts.

Visa Types, Validity, Fees, and Extensions

Common Visit Visa Types and What They Mean

Understand the core visa categories so you pick the right one for your trip purpose and duration:

  • Short-term Tourist Visa (30 days single-entry): Most common for Indian leisure travelers. Usually valid for entry within a set period after issuance and allows a stay of up to 30 days from arrival. Many 30-day visas can be extended once for a fee, though rules change—confirm at time of application.
  • Long-term Tourist Visa (60 days single-entry): For longer stays or slower itineraries.
  • Multiple Entry Visitor Visas (30 or 60 days): Useful if you plan multiple short trips to the UAE within the visa’s validity.
  • Transit Visas (48 or 96 hours): For travelers transiting through Dubai with confirmed onward tickets.
  • Visa On Arrival (14 days for eligible Indian nationals with certain third-country visas/residence permits): Fastest path for eligible travelers but limited to qualifying cases.

Fees depend on the type, channel, and service provider. As a general reference, typical tourist visa fees range from roughly USD 90 for a single-entry 30-day visa to more for 60-day visas and multiple entry permits. Transit visas are usually much cheaper. Additional service fees apply when applying through airlines, VFS, or agents.

Always check the exact fee at application because VAT and processing charges may be added.

Extensions, Overstay, and Cancellation Rules

Visa extensions are possible for many tourist visas but commonly require visiting an immigration office in the UAE or applying through a sponsor. Extensions are not always automatic and may require the traveler’s physical presence and original passport.

Overstay penalties are strictly enforced. Fines accumulate per day and can escalate to bans or deportation for significant overstays. The sponsor or applicant is responsible for fines, so factor an extension plan if your itinerary may change.

Cancellation policies for visa fees vary. Many visa fees are nonrefundable once processed—even if the visa is later cancelled—so verify refund rules before paying.

Step-By-Step: How to Apply from India (Decision Framework and Actions)

Follow this project-style plan to avoid missteps. Use the “decide, prepare, submit, confirm” framework.

Decide: Pick the Right Channel (1–14 Days to Decide)

Start by answering four questions:

  1. Do you already have an eligible third-country visa or residency? If yes, consider the on-arrival 14-day permit if it’s the fastest fit.
  2. Do you have an eligible airline booking that permits airline visa service? If yes and your dates are firm, airline-led application may be the fastest.
  3. Are you booking a hotel or package that includes visa handling? That can simplify the process.
  4. Do you need multiple entries or a longer stay (60 days)? If so, plan for a 60-day visa with more documentation.

Choose the channel that aligns with your documents and timeline.

Prepare: Documents and Timing (Allow Buffer)

Essential Documents Checklist:

  • A passport valid for at least six months from the date of travel.
  • Passport-size colour photograph that meets UAE specs.
  • Clear colour copy of passport photo and bio pages.
  • Confirmed return ticket (or onward ticket) — if applying for transit visa, an onward confirmed ticket is mandatory.
  • Confirmed hotel reservation matching travel dates or sponsor letter if staying with family.
  • For applicants relying on third-country visa eligibility (e.g., US visa or US green card), include a clear copy of the valid visa/residence permit.
  • Proof of relationship for family-sponsored visas (birth certificate, marriage certificate).
  • Payment method for visa fees (credit/debit card, or local payment at VFS centre).

Timing tips:

  • Airline-managed permits can often be issued within 3–4 working days; apply as soon as your ticket is issued.
  • VFS or embassy processes can take up to one to two weeks; add more margin during holidays.
  • If you’re eligible for on-arrival entry, still prepare digital and physical copies of supporting visas/residence permits to show at immigration if requested.

Submit: Application and Payment

  • If applying through the airline: Use “Manage Booking” or contact the airline’s visa team. Provide passport details exactly as in the passport, upload photos and supporting documents, and pay the fee online. Save the confirmation PDF.
  • If using VFS: Book an appointment, bring originals and copies, submit the application, and pay service fees. Track application with the reference number.
  • If sponsored by a hotel or UAE resident: Provide the documents the sponsor requests and confirm they will notify you when the visa is issued.
  • If using an online government portal or authorised service agent: Follow their upload and payment workflow; note any document-size and format restrictions.

Confirm: Get the Visa and Prepare to Travel

  • Once the visa is issued, you’ll receive an e-visa PDF. Print a copy to carry with you; while many immigration counters accept digital versions, Dubai authorities sometimes request a printed copy at the visual screening counters.
  • Check entry validity dates and length of stay. Ensure your passport has the required blank pages.
  • If you booked through an airline, verify the visa number and keep the booking and ticket details handy in case of airport screening.

Arrival in Dubai: Immigration and Practicalities

At the Airport

Immigration in Dubai conducts standard checks; some arrivals are randomly selected for visual screening where a printed visa copy may be requested and a small fee charged for printing if you don’t have one. Present your passport, printed visa or visa number, arrival card if required, and any supporting documents upon request.

Customs rules: If you carry high-value items or restricted goods, declare them. Check UAE customs guidance if you are unsure.

Health Insurance and Medical Requirements

Although the UAE doesn’t require vaccination certificates for most travelers, travel insurance that covers medical expenses (including COVID-related expenses if policies require) is strongly recommended. Some visa types or hotel packages may request proof of insurance.

Staying Within the Rules

  • Observe the permitted length of stay.
  • If you need to extend, apply before your visa expires to avoid fines.
  • Respect local laws and cultural norms: public behaviour, dress codes in certain spaces, and restrictions on substances.

Costs and Payment Practicalities

Visa fees vary by route and visa type. Expect to pay:

  • Transit visas (48–96 hours): modest fees.
  • 30-day tourist visas: roughly USD 90 and up, excluding service or processing charges.
  • 60-day visas and multiple-entry options: higher fees.
  • Airline and agent service fees: variable; some offer express options for an extra charge.

Payment is typically required at the time of submission. Keep receipts and payment confirmation emails until after travel.

Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

To save time and money, avoid these frequent errors:

  • Mismatched names or passport details: Ensure your name fields and dates in the visa application match your passport exactly; minor differences can lead to rejection.
  • Booking refundable/non-matching tickets: If the visa requires ticket numbers from a specific airline (e.g., Emirates ticket number format for their visa service), ensure the ticket meets those requirements. Avoid non-refundable routing mistakes when visa processing is uncertain.
  • Ignoring document expiry windows: A passport must be valid for at least six months at entry; some service providers require six months from the date of application.
  • Not printing the e-visa: While many accept digital copies, a printed visa often eases immigration screening.
  • Applying too late: Peak travel seasons slow processing queues—apply with a comfortable buffer.

Choosing Between Channels: A Decision Matrix

Weigh these pros and cons when selecting your application path:

  • Airline Visa Service: Best if you have an eligible ticket and need speed with minimal paperwork. Less flexible if itinerary changes.
  • VFS/Commercial Centre: Best for clarity and formal support but requires visits and can take longer.
  • Hotel/Agent Sponsorship: Best for convenience—useful if you want help packaging visas with hotel bookings—but costs more.
  • On-Arrival (if eligible): Fastest and cheapest if you meet the specific residency/visa conditions.
  • UAE Sponsor: Best if you have a reliable UAE contact; not available to independent travelers without contacts.

Apply the decision matrix against your timeline and risk tolerance. For most Indian travelers with no qualifying third-country visas, applying through the airline (when eligible) or through a VFS centre offers the optimal combination of cost and reliability.

Security, Privacy, and Data Handling

When you submit personal documents to airlines, VFS centres, or hotel agents, confirm they use secure transmission channels and official submission portals. Keep passport scans and copies secure, share them only through official application platforms, and store printed confirmations. If you suspect an application portal is fraudulent, contact the UAE embassy or your airline immediately.

Tips for Smooth Immigration Experience

  • Carry a printed copy of your e-visa and your return or onward ticket.
  • Have proof of accommodation ready (hotel printout with matching names and dates).
  • Keep digital copies of all documents on your phone and in cloud storage for quick retrieval.
  • Dress and act respectfully in line with local customs during immigration interviews.
  • If asked for further documents at entry, present them calmly and ask for clear instructions about where to inquire within the airport.

Common Questions Addressed Proactively

How long will a typical visitor visa take?

Processing times vary by channel. Airline-managed permits may take 3–4 working days; VFS and embassy channels can take 3–10 working days for standard service. Express options exist but cost more. Apply early to avoid last-minute stress.

Can I change my travel dates after the visa is issued?

Some visas tie validity windows to the dates you provided. Minor date changes may be allowed, but significant changes often require a new application. Check the specific visa conditions and consult your visa service provider before changing tickets.

Are there nationality-specific additional documents?

Yes. Certain nationalities face extra documentary requirements. If your situation is complex, a VFS centre or visa officer can list country-specific needs. Always supply any extra documents promptly to avoid delays.

What happens if my visa is rejected?

Visa refusal is possible for various administrative reasons. If rejected, the agency usually returns the documents and explains the reason. Refund policies vary; many fees are nonrefundable. Review the rejection reason carefully and, if appropriate, reapply with corrected documentation or through an alternate channel.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I know if I’m eligible for the 14-day visa on arrival as an Indian national? A: Eligibility typically depends on holding a valid visa or residence permit from specified third countries (for example, US visa or green card, UK residence, certain EU country permits, or residence from countries such as Australia and Canada). Confirm the current eligible countries and required minimum validity on the airline or UAE immigration portal before travel. Carry printed copies of the qualifying visa/residence permit at immigration.

Q: Can I apply for a Dubai visa myself from India without an agent? A: Yes. You can apply directly through airline portals if you have an eligible booking, or through VFS/official e-visa services where available. Agents or hotels can streamline the process but are not mandatory.

Q: What should I do if my passport is due to expire within six months? A: Renew your passport before applying. Many visa channels require at least six months of validity from the date of entry, and some processing systems validate passport expiry before accepting an application.

Q: Can I extend my Dubai tourist visa once I’m in the UAE? A: Many tourist visas offer at least one extension, but this depends on the visa type and immigration rules at the time. Extensions usually require a visit to an immigration office or application through a sponsor and may incur additional fees. Apply for extension before your current visa expires to avoid fines.
