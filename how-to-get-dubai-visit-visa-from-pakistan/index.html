How to Get Dubai Visit Visa From Pakistan

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Dubai draws millions of visitors each year with its skyline, shopping, cultural experiences and short-haul connectivity across the Gulf. For Pakistani travellers planning a holiday, family visit, or short business stop in the UAE, the visa process is a straightforward administrative step when you know the right route, the exact documents required, and the timing that prevents last-minute stress.

Short answer: Pakistani passport holders cannot obtain a visa on arrival in Dubai and must secure a prearranged visit visa before travel. You can get a Dubai visit visa from Pakistan through several channels—airlines (like Emirates or Etihad) when paired with an eligible ticket, an approved UAE sponsor or resident, an authorised travel agency, or by applying at the UAE Embassy in Islamabad/Consulate in Karachi—each route has specific document, fee and timing requirements.

This article explains each application route in detail, provides step-by-step checklists, clarifies the documents and typical fees, and highlights the cultural and entry rules you must follow on arrival. Why Pakistani Nationals Need a Prearranged Dubai Visit Visa

The visa-on-arrival rule and who it covers

The UAE offers visa-on-arrival privileges to many nationalities, but Pakistan is not on that list. Visa-on-arrival systems are reserved for passport holders of specific countries and depend on bilateral arrangements. For Pakistanis, the requirement is clear: obtain a valid UAE entry permit before boarding your flight. That permit takes the form of a visit visa issued by UAE immigration authorities or a visa facilitated by an approved airline or sponsor.

How that affects your travel planning

Because entry requires prior approval, you must factor visa processing into your timeline at the same priority as booking flights and accommodation. Missed or incomplete documentation is the most common cause of denied boarding or refused entry, not immigration scrutiny after arrival. In practice, the fastest and most reliable routes are via airlines that offer visa services if you meet their eligibility rules, or through a sponsor in the UAE (family, employer or hotel) who can submit the application on your behalf.

Who Can Sponsor a Dubai Visit Visa and When to Use a Sponsor

Types of sponsors available

A sponsor is a UAE-based person or entity that legally takes responsibility for your visit visa application. Common sponsors include a UAE resident (family member or friend), a UAE employer, a hotel/resort (for tourists), or an authorised travel agency. For some visa categories, only certain sponsors are accepted—for example, family visit visas are typically sponsored by close relatives with a valid residence visa.

When a sponsor is the best route

Using a sponsor is ideal when you:

  • Are visiting family or staying with a friend in the UAE.
  • Need a longer single- or multiple-entry visa than airlines offer.
  • Are applying for a specific-purpose short-term visa (medical, mission, or family).
  • Cannot meet airline eligibility rules for online visa services.

Sponsorship applications are submitted directly in the UAE by the sponsor to immigration or through UAE-based service centres, but Pakistani applicants will need to provide notarised and attested supporting documents as requested.

Routes To Apply From Pakistan: Step-by-Step

Route 1 — Apply Through Emirates or Etihad (Airline-Facilitated Visas)

Who can use this route

If you have a confirmed Emirates or Etihad ticket that meets their eligibility rules, you can apply for a UAE entry permit through the airline’s online visa service. These airline services are convenient because they allow you to receive the visa before departure without submitting your passport physically.

Step-by-step process

  1. Book and pay for a qualifying Emirates or Etihad ticket. The booking must show an itinerary into and out of Dubai on the same ticket (single PNR) depending on airline rules.
  2. Visit the airline’s Manage Your Booking portal and follow the visa application prompts. Provide the required documents: passport copy, passport-style photo, confirmed hotel reservation or sponsor details, and any additional documents the airline requests.
  3. Pay the visa fee and any processing charges. Visa processing typically takes 3–5 working days, though you should allow more time if travel dates are close.
  4. Receive the visa electronically—print a copy and carry it to the airport. Airline staff and UAE immigration may request a hard copy on arrival.

Practical tips

  • Airlines often require the passport to be valid for at least six months and to be machine-readable.
  • If your itinerary includes other carriers, check whether the airline visa service accepts mixed-carrier itineraries; often it does not.
  • Keep copies of your hotel booking and return ticket to show immigration, and ensure dates align.

Route 2 — Apply Through the UAE Embassy (Islamabad) or Consulate (Karachi)

Suitable applicants

This route is necessary for applicants who cannot use airline services, need specific visa categories (like certain official or diplomatic visas), or prefer handling the application through nearest diplomatic channels.

Typical embassy/consulate workflow

The UAE Embassy in Islamabad and the Consulate General in Karachi process visa requests received through the applicant or an authorised travel agent. Pakistanis applying via the embassy should expect to:

  • Submit the visa form typed and fully completed; handwritten forms are not accepted.
  • Attach a white-background passport photograph and a scanned/copy of the passport.
  • Include details of the sponsor in the UAE (name, contact, and residency details).
  • Provide any supporting documentation as requested (e.g., employment letter, hotel booking, return ticket).
  • For government or diplomatic passports, provide a Note Verbale (official note) through Pakistan’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Processing time varies but embassy guidance indicates approvals typically arrive within 14 days—plan for at least two weeks.

Key requirements and rules from diplomatic guidance

  • Ordinary passport holders applying for tourist stay of 30 days may be charged a specific fee (for example, US$60 as indicated historically; confirm current fee).
  • Minors travelling without both parents need a No Objection Certificate (NOC)/affidavit attested by Pakistani authorities and translated into Arabic if asked.
  • Visa fees are usually non-refundable even if the visa is refused.

Route 3 — Apply Through a UAE Sponsor/Resident (Family or Employer Sponsorship)

How it works

A UAE resident sponsor applies on your behalf through UAE immigration channels. The sponsor will need to submit documents (their Emirates ID, passport copy, residency details) and your documents (passport copy, photos). The sponsor will receive the visa decision and send you the entry permit.

Advantages and pitfalls

A sponsor-based visa can be faster and allows for longer or multiple-entry visas. However, the sponsor must be reliable and aware of responsibilities: sponsors can be held accountable for overstays or violations, so choose a trusted sponsor and confirm they will apply correctly.

Route 4 — Apply Through an Authorised Pakistani Travel Agency or Visa Service

Why use an agency

A licensed travel agency in Pakistan that handles UAE visas removes the administrative burden—agencies know the forms, attestation steps, and embassy hours. This is a good choice for first-time applicants or those with complex documentation.

How to pick a reputable agency

Check the following: business licensing, published client feedback, clear fee structure, and whether they submit applications directly to the embassy/airline or act as intermediaries. Reputable agents will provide a timeline and checklist and will not promise guaranteed results.

Documents You Must Prepare (Essential Checklist)

Below is the single list you will want to print and tick off before submitting any application. This is the authoritative, minimal paperwork set for most Dubai visit visa applications from Pakistan.

  • Valid passport with at least six months’ validity and machine-readable page.
  • Clear colour copy of passport bio-data page.
  • One recent passport-size photograph with a white background.
  • Confirmed return air ticket showing travel dates.
  • Hotel reservation confirmation or sponsor’s tenancy/employment details.
  • Copy of Pakistani CNIC (National Identity Card).
  • Proof of funds if requested (e.g., credit card copy or bank statement) or a minimum cash guideline (UAE entry guidance may ask for evidence of funds around AED 3,000 per person).
  • NOC/attested affidavit for minors travelling without parents (if applicable).
  • Any embassy-required attested documents (e.g., Note Verbale for government officials, or sponsor documents attested in the UAE).

Keep both physical and digital copies of all documents. UAE immigration may request to see originals at arrival or during screening.

Visa Types, Validity and Typical Costs

Common visit visa types relevant to Pakistanis

  • Transit visas (48-hour and 96-hour) — if you have a short stopover and meet airline rules.
  • Short-term tourism visa — single-entry, 30 days after arrival (typical).
  • Long-term tourism visa — single-entry or multiple-entry, 60 days after arrival (where available).
  • Multiple-entry tourist visas — for frequent short visits, available under specific categories.

Airlines and the UAE government publish standard fees that vary by duration and single vs multiple entry. To give a practical range, airline facilitated tourist visa fees often fall roughly between USD 50–230 depending on length and extension options, but embassy-charged fees for Pakistani ordinary passports have historically been recorded around USD 60 for a 30-day single-entry visa. Fees can change—always confirm the current fee before applying.

Extensions and overstay penalties

Most short-term visit visas can be extended once in the UAE by visiting the immigration office before your visa expires. Extensions attract fixed fees; an extension example seen in public tariffs showed extension fees around USD 230 for tourism visas. Overstaying a visa carries fines calculated per day and can lead to administrative detention, deportation, or future entry bans. Treat visa expiry dates as sacrosanct.

Processing Times and a Recommended Timeline

Typical processing windows

  • Airline online visa: 3–5 working days (expedited options may be available).
  • Embassy/Consulate channel: up to 14 working days—apply at least two weeks before travel.
  • Sponsor applications: variable—often 3–10 working days depending on sponsor’s efficiency and workload.
  • Agency-assisted applications: typically match the embassy or airline timelines plus the agency’s processing time.

Recommended timeline (one practical list)

  1. 6–8 weeks before travel: Decide travel dates, book refundable flights if visa timing is uncertain.
  2. 3–4 weeks before travel: Prepare all documents, confirm sponsor details, consult an authorised agency if needed.
  3. 14–21 days before travel: Submit visa application through your chosen route.
  4. 3–5 days before travel: Verify visa issuance, print copies, and confirm flight check-in requirements.

Follow the timeline and allow buffer time for document attestation or embassy queries.

Common Reasons for Visa Delay or Rejection — And How to Avoid Them

Incomplete or incorrect documents

Incomplete forms, missing sponsor details, or poor-quality passport copies lead to delays. Use the embassy’s checklist and let the sponsoring party review your submission. Double check spelling, passport numbers, and contact details.

Passport validity and machine-readable passports

Passports must be valid for at least six months and be machine-readable. Handwritten or old-format passports are often rejected.

Name mismatches and ticket mismatch

Ensure the name used for the visa application exactly matches the passport and airline ticket. If multiple carriers are in the itinerary, verify airline-specific rules for their visa service.

Inadequate supporting evidence

Not providing a clear hotel booking, return ticket, or sponsor contact can cause rejection. For family visits, proof of relationship (birth certificates, marriage certificates) helps.

Suspicion of intent to overstay

If your profile or travel pattern suggests high risk of overstay (e.g., no return ticket, no job or family ties), provide stronger evidence of ties to Pakistan: employment letter, property documents, or family commitments.

NOC and minor travel documentation

Minors travelling without both parents need correctly attested NOCs. The attestation and translation process can add time, so begin early.

Practical Arrival Tips and Cultural Rules at UAE Entry

What to carry on arrival

Always carry the printed visa copy, passport, return ticket, hotel booking, sponsor contact, and proof of sufficient funds. Immigration officers may ask for any or all of these.

Customs and restricted items

Declare cash amounts above the UAE threshold and check the UAE customs list for restricted items. Prescription medication should be accompanied by a doctor’s prescription; certain controlled medicines are banned without prior permission.

Behaviour, dress, and cultural expectations

The UAE is a conservative Muslim country but cosmopolitan. During public interactions observe modest dress, avoid public displays of affection, and follow local laws about alcohol consumption (only in licensed venues). During Ramadan, avoid eating or drinking in public during daylight hours.

Photography and security

Avoid photographing people without permission, government buildings, ports, airports, and military installations. Security checks at Dubai International are routine; carry printed documents if requested.

Transport and timing considerations

When flying between Gulf cities, allow at least a two-hour buffer for connections if you must clear immigration. If you are using separate tickets for different legs, add an extra cushion as checked baggage transfers may not be automatic.

Financial Planning: Fees, Bank Statements, and Proof of Funds

Typical fee components

Visa application fees are paid to the issuing authority or airline; agencies add service charges. Expect a paid visa fee plus an agency handling charge when applicable. Embassy fees are non-refundable in most cases.

Proof of funds

Immigration sometimes asks for proof of sufficient funds. While there is no fixed global rule, a practical guideline is to show capability to cover accommodation and daily expenses—evidence can include recent bank statements, a credit card photocopy, or a sponsor letter confirming coverage.

Special Situations and How to Handle Them

Travellers with prior immigration issues or criminal records

Full disclosure during application is essential. Concealing past immigration violations or criminal history risks visa refusal or deportation. Consult the embassy or a legal adviser experienced in UAE immigration for case-specific guidance.

Medical travel and emergencies

If you are visiting for medical treatment, you will need a medical letter from a UAE healthcare provider or hospital appointment confirmation; some visa categories accommodate medical treatment visitors with supporting documentation.

Business visits and conferences

Business visitors traveling on a tourist visa must not engage in paid employment. For conferences or exhibitions, secure the appropriate visa type or ensure the event organiser issues an invitation or sponsor letter.

Attestation and Legal Documents: When They Matter

Certain documents (marriage certificates, educational degrees, commercial documents) require attestation by Pakistani authorities, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and sometimes attestation in the UAE. For short tourist visits, most of these attestations are unnecessary; they become important when applying for family, work, or residency visas. If you anticipate converting a visit visa into another visa type later, consult the sponsor and prepare attestations early.

How to Avoid Scams and Unauthorized Services

Red flags

  • Agents promising guaranteed visas or quick approvals in exchange for extra cash.
  • Requests to submit original passports without clear reason.
  • No physical address or license number for the agency.
  • Pressure to pay in cash without a receipt.

Always ask for written invoices, agency licensing details, and submit documents only to verified, accredited agents. When in doubt, apply through the airline or the UAE Embassy/Consulate directly.

After Visa Approval: Final Steps Before Travel

Once the visa is issued:

  • Print at least two clear copies of the entry permit.
  • Reconfirm flight times and check-in requirements—some carriers require visa confirmation at check-in for certain nationalities.
  • Pack supporting documents (hotel booking, return ticket, sponsor contact, CNIC) in your hand luggage.
  • Verify baggage rules, customs allowances, and any baggage restrictions on medicines.

What to Do If Your Visa Is Delayed or Refused

If the visa is delayed beyond the expected window, contact the issuing authority immediately: the airline visa helpdesk, your sponsor, or the embassy. For refusals, request the refusal reason in writing via the channel you used to apply. Some refusals are administrative and can be re-applied for after correction; others are final. Agencies can sometimes provide remediation guidance, but do not pay extra until you have a clear explanation.

FAQ

1) How long does it normally take to get a Dubai visit visa from Pakistan?

Processing times vary by route: airline-facilitated visas often take 3–5 working days, sponsor or embassy applications can take up to 14 working days. Apply at least two weeks in advance and earlier if you need document attestation.

2) Can I extend a Dubai visit visa after arrival?

Most short-term tourist visas can be extended once through UAE immigration for a fee. Extension rules and fees change occasionally, so check with the issuing channel or immigration authorities well before your visa expires.

3) What should a minor travelling alone from Pakistan to Dubai carry?

Minors must carry a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the absent parent/guardian attested by Pakistani authorities and, if requested, translated into Arabic. The embassy and airlines may have additional requirements—obtain and attestation early.

4) If my visa is refused, can I appeal?

You should request the refusal reason from the issuing authority. Some refusals can be corrected and re-applied for; others are final. Re-application may require new or additional documentation. If unsure, consult a licensed visa agent or legal adviser for next steps.
