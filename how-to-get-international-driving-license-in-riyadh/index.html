What Is an International Driving Permit (IDP) — and Why It Matters

Definition and Legal Basis

An International Driving Permit (IDP) is a multilingual translation of your national driving license that helps authorities and car rental companies in other countries interpret your driving entitlements. The IDP itself is not a standalone license; it is valid only when carried together with your national driving license. The legal framework underpinning the IDP is the United Nations conventions on road traffic (notably the 1949 and 1968 Conventions). Saudi Arabia is a contracting party, which means IDPs issued in Saudi are recognized in many member countries.

What the IDP Actually Does

The permit serves three practical functions for travelers:

  • It translates the categories and permissions on your domestic license into several languages, reducing confusion with local law enforcement.
  • It is often a rental requirement for international car hire companies and border authorities.
  • It provides a standardized document that clarifies your legal right to drive abroad for temporary visits.

Common Misconceptions

Many travelers assume their Saudi license alone will be enough overseas. While some countries accept a foreign license for short stays, many require or strongly recommend an IDP—especially for car rental and border checks. Additionally, the IDP is not a substitute for local laws: taking up residence, working, or staying beyond a tourist period usually obliges drivers to secure a local license.

Who Can Apply in Riyadh: Residents, Citizens, Tourists

Saudi Citizens and Residents

If you hold a Saudi-issued driving license, you are eligible to apply for an IDP issued by the accredited national bodies. This includes Saudi citizens and expatriate residents who have a valid Saudi license. The IDP will reflect the categories of vehicle your Saudi license permits and is intended for temporary use while traveling abroad.

Short-Term Visitors and Tourists

Visitors to Saudi Arabia who hold foreign driving licenses should obtain an IDP in the country where their license was issued before arriving in Saudi Arabia if they plan to drive within the Kingdom. For tourists departing Saudi Arabia and driving abroad, a Saudi-issued IDP is only available to those who hold a valid Saudi license—this is typically residents and citizens.

Minimum Age and Restrictions

Standard eligibility requires you to be at least 18 years old and to possess a valid domestic driving license. Temporary or provisional licenses may not always be acceptable; authorities usually require the definitive license issued by the licensing authority.

Which Organizations Issue IDPs in Saudi Arabia

Saudi Arabia recognizes two principal national issuing bodies for IDPs: the Saudi Automobile & Touring Association (SATA) and the Saudi Automobile Federation (SAF). These are the authorized entities that implement the conventions and provide officially recognized IDPs.

Saudi Automobile & Touring Association (SATA)

SATA has longstanding experience issuing IDPs and maintains physical offices where applicants can apply in person. They can advise on the correct model of IDP (1949 or 1968) depending on the destination countries you plan to visit.

Saudi Automobile Federation (SAF)

SAF also issues IDPs and maintains regional offices. For travelers near the Eastern Province or other regions, SAF branches may be more convenient. Both organizations follow international templates and requirements.

Commercial Providers and Online Options

There are private services that advertise fast international driving documents. Some operate legitimately and provide expedited physical IDPs by coordinating with official clubs, while others are not officially recognized. When in doubt, prioritize issuing bodies recognized by FIA-affiliated national associations (like SATA and SAF) to avoid invalid or non-recognized documents abroad.

1968 vs 1949 IDP Models — Which Do You Need?

The two most common IDP models are the 1949 and the 1968 versions. Which one you need depends on the country or countries you plan to visit.

  • The 1968 IDP covers more signatory countries and can be valid for up to three years (subject to the expiry of your domestic license).
  • The 1949 IDP remains valid in other jurisdictions and is sometimes required for travel to countries that adhere only to that earlier convention.

In practice, ask the issuing office which model is appropriate for your destinations; many applicants elect the 1968 model where possible because it offers broader recognition. Keep in mind that the validity of the IDP cannot extend beyond your domestic license’s validity.

Step-by-Step: How to Get an International Driving Permit in Riyadh

Below is a single, focused list outlining the practical steps to obtain your IDP in Riyadh. Follow them in order to minimize delays.

  1. Confirm eligibility and determine the correct IDP model
    • Verify that you hold a valid Saudi driving license and meet the age requirement.
    • List the countries you plan to visit and ask the issuing office which IDP model (1949 or 1968) they recommend.
  2. Prepare required documents
    • Valid Saudi driving license (original + a clear photocopy).
    • Passport (original + copy of the photo/info page).
    • Two passport-size photographs meeting standard photo requirements.
    • Completed IDP application form (available at SATA/SAF offices or online).
    • Payment for administrative fee (cash, card or bank transfer—confirm accepted methods).
  3. Choose your application route
    • Apply in person at a SATA or SAF branch in Riyadh for immediate processing where available.
    • Use an accredited online service that coordinates with the national association if you need remote processing or courier delivery.
  4. Submit your application and pay the fee
    • Present original documents and submit copies.
    • Complete the form with accurate address details as they appear on your Saudi license.
  5. Collect your IDP or request delivery
    • In-person applicants can sometimes receive the IDP same-day.
    • Courier delivery typically takes 3–7 business days depending on the option selected.

This condensed process is what most applicants in Riyadh will follow; subsequent sections unpack each step in detail so you can make the most practical choices.

Documents, Fees, and Photo Requirements — The Details

Required Documents (Bring Originals and Copies)

Prepare the following items to speed the application:

  • Original Saudi driving license plus a clear photocopy of the front and back.
  • Original passport and a copy of the bio-data page.
  • Two recent passport-size photographs; many offices require plain background dimensions consistent with passport photos. Do not use scanned or photocopied photos.
  • Completed application form (obtainable at the issuing office or online if available).
  • Payment method for fees and any courier charges if delivery is requested.

Most issuing offices expect the name and address on the IDP to match your domestic license, so ensure the address you provide corresponds to what appears on your Saudi license.

Typical Fees and Payment Methods

Fees vary by issuing body and whether you choose express service or courier delivery. Expect a modest administrative fee for the IDP itself and additional charges for same-day processing or shipping. Payment methods differ by office; SATA and SAF often accept card and bank transfer in addition to cash—check local branch details before applying.

Photograph Standards

Photographs must be recent, in color or black-and-white, on a plain background with a neutral expression. Polaroids are usually acceptable, but scanned or photocopied images are not. Place your signature on the back of the photographs if requested by the issuing office.

Where to Apply in Riyadh: Offices and Practical Tips

Main Offices and Locations

The primary in-person route in Riyadh is through the SATA office. These offices handle IDP issuance and can advise on the correct model. When visiting, bring all original documents and copies organized in a clear folder to make processing smooth.

Practical tips for in-person visits:

  • Visit early in the morning to avoid queues.
  • Bring cash and a card, just in case a card reader is unavailable.
  • Ask about same-day processing if you are on a tight deadline.
  • Confirm opening hours and whether an appointment is required—many offices support walk-ins, but some now use scheduled slots.

Using Regional Branches or SAF Offices

If you are closer to a SAF office or other regional issuing center, they can often process IDPs similarly. SAF may be more convenient for applicants in the Eastern Province and other regions; confirm services and required documentation before traveling.

Online Applications and Courier Options

Some accredited providers and national clubs offer online applications with courier delivery within a few days. If you prefer not to visit a branch, verify that the online service partners directly with the national association (SATA or SAF) so that the IDP issued will be recognized internationally. Online processing is helpful for expatriates with tight schedules, but expect courier fees and slightly longer wait times compared to immediate in-person issuance.

Timelines: How Long Will It Take?

Processing times depend on the issuing channel you choose:

  • In-person at official offices: Same-day issuance is often possible if you present complete documentation and visit early; otherwise processing may take a few business days.
  • Online application with courier delivery: Typically 3–7 business days, depending on the provider and delivery method.
  • Remote processing via third-party expedited services: Some services claim 24–48 hour turnaround, but confirm they are coordinating with the recognized national bodies to avoid invalid permits.

To avoid stress, apply at least one to two weeks before your travel date if you plan to use courier services; apply several days in advance if visiting in person.

Using Your IDP: Practical Rules and Best Practices

Carry Both Documents at All Times

The IDP is valid only alongside your domestic license. Always carry the original Saudi driving license and the IDP together, along with your passport, when driving abroad.

Insurance and Rental Car Requirements

Car rental companies often require both an IDP and your domestic license as part of their verification. Purchase adequate insurance—third-party and collision coverage—as the IDP does not alter insurance rules. Verify whether your existing Saudi car insurance has any international reciprocity or whether you need to buy local or travel-specific coverage.

Local Laws and Residency Rules

The IDP does not override local licensing laws. If you plan to work, take up residence for an extended period, or become a long-term resident of another country, you will likely need to apply for a local driving license in that jurisdiction.

Validity Periods

  • 1949 IDP: Typically valid for 1 year from the date of issuance.
  • 1968 IDP: Often valid for up to 3 years, but cannot exceed the validity of your domestic license.

Some countries only accept one model or limit the period foreigners may drive there on an IDP. Confirm requirements for each country on your itinerary.

Converting a Foreign License to a Saudi License (and Vice Versa)

For New Residents Coming to Saudi Arabia

If you have a foreign license and move to Saudi Arabia, you may be eligible to convert your license to a Saudi driving license without retaking tests if your home country has reciprocal agreements. The conversion process varies by nationality and requires coordination with local traffic authorities.

For Saudi License Holders Going Abroad Long-Term

Holding an IDP provides temporary legal translation for driving abroad. If you plan to reside abroad beyond the period permitted for tourists, contact local licensing authorities in the destination country to understand whether you must convert your Saudi license to a local one.

Women Driving in Saudi Arabia — The Current Context

Since the nationwide lifting of the driving ban, women with valid driving licenses (Saudi or internationally recognized) are permitted to drive within Saudi Arabia. Female travelers who obtain a Saudi driving license or a valid IDP paired with a foreign license can legally drive in countries that recognize the IDP. Women applying for an IDP in Riyadh follow the same documentation requirements as men.

Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Mistake: Bringing Incomplete Documentation

Many applicants lose time because they bring only photocopies or mismatched address details. Always bring original documents and ensure the address on your application matches your Saudi license.

Mistake: Choosing the Wrong IDP Model

Selecting the incorrect IDP model for your destination can complicate travel. Verify the countries you will visit and confirm whether they require the 1949 or 1968 IDP.

Mistake: Relying on Unrecognized Providers

Some third-party services sell invalid documents. Confirm that the issuing body is affiliated with the national automobile association and that the IDP will be recognized under UN conventions.

Mistake: Not Checking Rental Company Policies

Car rental companies sometimes require an IDP even if local law doesn't. Pre-check policy and provide your documents in advance if the rental company requests verification.

How an IDP Relates to Car Rental, Insurance, and Border Crossings

Renting a Car Abroad

Most international rental agencies require presentation of an IDP plus your domestic license, passport, and a credit card. Booking in advance and emailing scanned documents to the rental company can speed pickup.

Insurance Considerations

An IDP does not confer insurance coverage. Before driving, confirm that your auto insurance or the rental company’s policy covers the countries you plan to visit and consider supplemental travel insurance for comprehensive protection.

Border Crossings and Cross-Border Driving

If you plan to drive across borders (for example in the Gulf region or Europe), check whether additional permits or vehicle documentation (vehicle registration, green card insurance) are necessary. Some borders require temporary vehicle import permits and additional insurance.

Practical Scenarios and Recommended Approaches

Short Leisure Trip from Riyadh to Another Country

If you're flying from Riyadh to another country for a short tour and plan to drive while there, obtain your IDP in Riyadh before departure. Choose the IDP model that matches your destination and ensure you have adequate insurance.

Business Travel with Tight Timelines

If you need an IDP on short notice, visit a SATA or SAF office in Riyadh early in the day for potential same-day issuance. If you cannot visit in person, use an accredited online service but allow several days for courier delivery.

Long Multi-Country Road Trips

For extensive itineraries across multiple countries, choose the 1968 IDP when possible and verify country-by-country rules. Maintain clear records of your domestic license validity, and check whether temporary vehicle import permits or visas are needed for specific borders.

Country-Specific Nuances: Examples and Things to Check

Different countries impose different rules on foreign drivers with IDPs. Always check each country’s entry and driving rules before travel. Examples worth confirming include whether the destination:

  • Recognizes the 1968 IDP or requires the 1949 model.
  • Imposes limits on how long a foreign driver may use an IDP.
  • Requires certification or stamping of the IDP upon arrival.
  • Has specific insurance or vehicle importation requirements.

If you plan to drive from Saudi Arabia into neighboring states, consult local embassy guidance and rental companies for cross-border policies. For travelers returning to Saudi Arabia after driving abroad, ensure your vehicle documents comply with Saudi customs if you import or register a vehicle.

Safety, Etiquette, and Local Road Culture

Driving behavior and enforcement vary by country. When driving abroad, respect speed limits, seat-belt laws, and local customs. Be prepared for different road signage, right-hand vs left-hand driving, and varied enforcement practices. Regardless of location, carry your IDP, domestic license, passport and insurance documents at all times.

If you’re returning to Saudi driving after time abroad, refresh yourself on local traffic laws, which include strict rules on distracted driving and seat-belt use, and know that enforcement has grown more consistent across major cities.

Avoid these mistakes by preparing a clear folder of documents, emailing confirmations to rental and insurance providers ahead of arrival, and giving yourself extra time for border procedures.

FAQ

Q: How long is an International Driving Permit issued in Riyadh valid? A: Validity depends on the IDP model: the 1949 IDP is generally valid for one year from issuance, while the 1968 IDP can be valid for up to three years but cannot exceed the validity of your Saudi driving license.

Q: Can I get an IDP with a temporary Saudi license? A: Temporary or provisional licenses are often not accepted. Authorities typically require a full, definitive domestic driving license. Confirm with the issuing office before applying.

Q: Do I need an IDP to rent a car abroad? A: Many car rental companies require an IDP even if local law accepts your domestic license. Check rental policies in advance and carry both your IDP and domestic license.

Q: Can I apply online if I’m based in Riyadh? A: Yes. Accredited online services and some issuing bodies offer applications with courier delivery. Ensure the service is officially coordinated with SATA or SAF to guarantee recognition abroad.
