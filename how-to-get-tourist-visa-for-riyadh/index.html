How to Get Tourist Visa for Riyadh

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Introduction

Saudi Arabia has deliberately opened its doors to travelers over the past few years, and Riyadh—an energetic capital that blends modern growth with deep cultural roots—has become a must-see destination for first-time visitors. Whether you’re planning a short city break to explore museums and contemporary neighborhoods or using Riyadh as the launching point for wider travel across the Kingdom, understanding the tourist visa process is the practical first step that makes an enjoyable trip possible.

Short answer: You can apply for a Saudi tourist eVisa online if your nationality is eligible. The eVisa is typically issued as a one-year, multiple-entry visa that allows stays of up to 90 days per visit for leisure, family visits and Umrah (Hajj is excluded). To travel to Riyadh, prepare the required documents, complete the online application, pay the fee, and carry an approved eVisa (digital or printed) when you arrive.

This article explains exactly how the eVisa works, who qualifies, the precise documents and steps you’ll need, common mistakes to avoid, how to handle arrival in Riyadh, and practical planning advice for turning a visa into a confident, culturally respectful visit. Understanding the Saudi Tourist Visa

What the Tourist eVisa Is and What It Allows

The tourist eVisa was introduced to simplify international travel to Saudi Arabia. Under its common terms, the eVisa is a digital visa applied for online and approved electronically, allowing eligible international visitors to enter the Kingdom for tourism-related purposes. Typical permissions include sightseeing, visiting family and friends, attending cultural events, and performing Umrah (Hajj pilgrimage is not permitted on a tourist visa). The eVisa is usually valid for multiple entries over a one-year period, with a maximum length of stay per visit commonly set at 90 days.

The important practical point: the eVisa facilitates leisure travel to Riyadh and other Saudi destinations without requiring a local sponsor or a traditional embassy appointment in most cases. That simplicity has opened opportunities to explore Riyadh’s museums, heritage sites, dining scene and growing calendar of public events.

Who Is Eligible

Eligibility depends on nationality. Saudi authorities publish lists of eligible countries and periodically update them. Citizens of many Western countries, parts of Asia and several other regions are commonly eligible for the online eVisa, but the safest approach is to verify your status on the official portal or with your local Saudi embassy or consulate before applying.

Visa rules sometimes vary for holders of diplomatic or official passports, for residents of certain countries, and for travelers coming from countries with travel restrictions. If you hold dual nationality or reside in a third country, check which passport you intend to use and whether that passport qualifies.

Limitations and Exclusions

A tourist visa is designed for leisure and short visits. It does not authorize employment, long-term study, or participation in paid work. Hajj is explicitly excluded; if you plan to attend Hajj, you must obtain the specific pilgrimage visa issued during Hajj season under separate rules. Additionally, travelers must meet standard entry requirements (valid passport, return ticket, and no legal barriers to entry).

Step-By-Step Application Process

Below is a practical, step-by-step flow you can follow from preparation to arrival in Riyadh. Follow each step to minimize delays.

  1. Prepare documents and digital materials you’ll need.
  2. Access the official Saudi eVisa application portal and create an account.
  3. Complete the online form with accurate personal details and passport information.
  4. Upload required documents including a passport-style photo and passport scan.
  5. Pay the visa fee online using the accepted payment methods.
  6. Wait for processing and notification by email; download and save the approved eVisa.
  7. Travel to Riyadh, present your passport and eVisa at immigration, and follow arrival procedures.

This sequence keeps the process clear and orderly. Exact field names and required uploads can change, so always use the current online form and review any guidance on the official portal before submitting.

Essential Documents You’ll Need

  • A passport valid for at least six months from the planned date of entry (digital scan of bio page required).
  • A recent passport-style photograph that meets the portal’s dimensions and background requirements.
  • Proof of onward or return travel (round-trip ticket or itinerary).
  • Accommodation details: hotel booking confirmations or address of where you will stay.
  • A valid payment method for the online fee (credit/debit card usually accepted).
  • Travel insurance is strongly recommended and sometimes required for certain nationalities.

Carry both digital copies and printed copies of all documents. At the border, immigration officers expect to see your passport and the eVisa; having supporting documents accessible on a mobile device and as hard copies makes entry smoother.

Visa Timeline, Fees and Processing

Processing times vary with nationality, application volume, and seasonal demand. Many applicants receive a decision in a few days; some nationalities experience faster approvals. Processing time can extend during peak travel periods or if additional checks are needed. Visa fees differ by nationality and are collected online at the time of application.

Key practical advice: apply at least two to four weeks before your planned travel date to allow time for processing and any follow-up requests for documents. If your trip is time-sensitive, some nationalities and providers offer expedited services, but guaranteed timelines are not universal—apply early.

Common Reasons Applications Are Denied — And How to Avoid Them

Applications are commonly rejected for preventable reasons. Prevent these by reviewing your submission carefully.

  • Passport errors: The passport image or passport details on the application don’t match the travel document. Always double-check passport numbers, full name spelling, birthdate, and expiry date.
  • Photo problems: Photos that don’t meet the required size, lighting, or background standards can trigger rejection. Use a recent, high-quality image that follows the portal’s specifications.
  • Incorrect or inconsistent information: Name discrepancies (nicknames, omitted middle names), mismatched details across documents, or conflicting travel dates raise red flags.
  • Incomplete documentation: Missing round-trip tickets or accommodation evidence can cause delays or denials. Have confirmations ready before applying.
  • Previous immigration or legal issues: Past visa overstays, deportations, or criminal records may complicate approval. Be transparent and check with the embassy if you have concerns.

If your application is refused, the portal or consular office typically provides a reason. Use that guidance to correct issues or reapply.

Arrival in Riyadh: What to Expect at Immigration and Customs

Immigration Procedures

When you arrive at King Khalid International Airport (RUH) in Riyadh, present your passport and eVisa at the immigration counter. The officer will verify your identity, scan your passport, and stamp your entry. Electronic gates and biometric screening are in use in many airports, so be ready for fingerprints or a photograph. Keep printed and digital copies of your visa and confirmation emails accessible.

Baggage, Customs and Restricted Items

Saudi customs rules apply to all arrivals. Declare items that are restricted or subject to duty. Prohibited items include certain types of religious materials intended for proselytizing, and there are strict controls for alcohol and pork products. Electronic devices and cameras are generally allowed, but exercise caution when photographing sensitive infrastructure or people in private settings—ask permission before taking pictures.

Transportation from the Airport

Riyadh has taxis, ride-hailing apps, and rental car services at the airport. If you prefer private transfer, book in advance through reputable providers. Public transport options are growing, but for first-time visitors, a taxi or ride-hailing service is usually the simplest way to reach city neighborhoods.

Practical Travel Planning for Riyadh

Where to Stay and Neighborhoods to Consider

Riyadh’s accommodation landscape ranges from global luxury hotels to business-class and mid-range options. Choose a base depending on your priorities:

  • Central business districts give easy access to museums, commercial centers, and major restaurants.
  • Diplomatic and newer neighborhoods often offer modern hotels with international amenities.
  • For cultural exploration, staying near historic districts offers proximity to museums and traditional markets.

Book refundable rates where possible for flexibility. If you intend to visit other Saudi regions, confirm intercity travel times and internal flights ahead of time.

SIM Cards, Connectivity and Payments

You can buy local SIM cards at the airport or city shops. Prepaid plans are affordable and cover data and calls. Credit cards are accepted at most hotels, restaurants and shops in Riyadh, but carry some cash (Saudi riyal) for smaller purchases and markets.

Dress Code and Daily Practicalities

Riyadh is a conservative city. Travelers should dress modestly—long trousers for men and clothing that covers shoulders and knees for women. Women are not universally required to wear an abaya, but many visitors choose a modest outer layer to blend into the local environment and show respect for conservative settings. Situational sensitivity—especially at religious sites and official buildings—is important; follow local norms and posted guidance.

Safety, Health and Emergency Services

Riyadh is safe for travelers who respect local laws and cultural expectations. Familiarize yourself with requirements related to medication import (some medicines need prescriptions or prior declaration). Routine vaccinations should be up to date. For health concerns, Riyadh has internationally accredited hospitals and clinics; find out where your nearest medical facility is and ensure your travel insurance covers medical evacuation if needed.

Cultural Etiquette and Legal Considerations

Saudi Arabia’s culture emphasizes hospitality and respect. A few cultural and legal practices to keep in mind will help you travel respectfully and avoid misunderstandings.

  • Public behavior: Avoid loud public displays of affection. Public displays are generally frowned upon.
  • Photography: Be cautious photographing people, government buildings, or military sites. Always ask permission before photographing local residents, especially women.
  • Alcohol and controlled substances: Alcohol is prohibited; possession or use can lead to arrest. Likewise, drug laws are strict and carry severe penalties.
  • Religious observance: Be mindful during prayer times and the month of Ramadan. Some businesses adjust opening hours, and public eating during daylight in Ramadan is discouraged.
  • LGBTQ+ travelers: Saudi law does not recognize same-sex relationships and conducts related to sexuality can have legal consequences. Research risks and take precautions to protect personal safety.

Understanding and following local laws and customs is essential for a trouble-free stay in Riyadh.

Planning Side Trips from Riyadh

Riyadh serves as an effective hub for exploring other parts of the Kingdom. Popular extensions include cultural and natural destinations such as AlUla’s archaeological sites, historic Jeddah and its coastline, and the holy cities for those eligible. Each destination carries its own planning considerations—internal flights can be short and efficient, but remote areas may require additional time for transfers and lodging.

If you’re planning to combine Riyadh with trips to archaeological sites like those in AlUla, consider booking internal flights or guided transfers in advance to optimize your time. Bringing Family, Children and Special Cases

Traveling with children or as part of a family group requires a few additional considerations. For minors, ensure passports are valid and that any children traveling with only one parent carry consent letters or custody documentation as required by their home country and Saudi authorities. If you’re traveling with elderly relatives or travelers with mobility needs, check hotel accessibility and medical services in Riyadh before booking. If you require specialized medical support, bring sufficient medication and carry supporting medical documentation to present at immigration if necessary.

If you plan on a family visit, the tourist visa covers family leisure travel but does not automatically permit them to work or study. Make sure everyone has their own valid travel documents and individual eVisas as required.

Extending Your Stay or Changing Visa Status

Extending a tourist eVisa or changing visa status can be complex. The tourist eVisa is intended primarily for short-term visits and extensions may not be easily granted. If you need to stay longer for family reasons, unexpected circumstances, or health care, contact the Saudi immigration authorities or your nearest Saudi consulate for official guidance. If you plan to switch from tourism to work or residency, in most cases you will need a sponsor and formal processing that typically takes place from outside the Kingdom or through official local processes guided by an employer.

For reliable updates and further details about visa rules and options, consult official government channels and your nearest Saudi diplomatic mission before assuming extension options are available.

Mistakes to Avoid When Applying

Careful preparation prevents problems. Avoid these common mistakes:

  • Applying with nearly expired passports. Make sure your passport has at least six months validity from your planned arrival date.
  • Uploading low-resolution or outdated photos. Use a recent, clear image following the portal’s specifications.
  • Waiting until the last minute to apply. Processing times vary and last-minute applications increase stress.
  • Assuming all travel reasons are covered. The tourist visa excludes employment and Hajj; choose the correct visa category for your purpose.

If you’re uncertain about any step, reach out to official sources or a trusted travel advisor before submitting your application.

Costing Your Trip: Visa Fees and Related Expenses

Visa fees vary by nationality and are payable during the online application. Beyond the visa fee, budget for the following: international flights to Riyadh, domestic transfers, accommodation, travel insurance, daily expenses like food and transportation, and any guided tours or entrance fees to attractions. If you plan to perform Umrah, factor in logistical arrangements and any additional permits or bookings required.

How to Combine Riyadh with Other Destinations

Riyadh is centrally located and connected by frequent domestic flights. Practical multi-city plans include combining Riyadh with coastal Jeddah for Red Sea experiences, an archaeological and desert adventure in AlUla, or a cooler mountain break in Taif during the hotter months. When combining destinations, allow buffer days for internal transfers, especially if you’re traveling from Riyadh to remote regions where road travel is longer.

If your interest is archaeological sites and outdoor landscapes, plan AlUla as an independent stop and book guided services well in advance to ensure access to protected sites and guided trails. For people interested in urban culture and shopping, adding Jeddah enriches your itinerary with coastal and historic contrasts.

Using Official Resources and When to Seek Assistance

Always start with official information for the most current visa rules and procedures. Embassies and consulates provide authoritative guidance tailored to your nationality and situation. If you encounter technical issues during an online application or receive unexpected requests from the portal, contact official channels or your nearest Saudi diplomatic mission.

Sample Itineraries Centered on Riyadh

Short Stay: 3 Days in Riyadh

Spend your first day exploring historic quarters and a major museum, the second day visiting contemporary landmarks and food districts, and the third day taking a short excursion to nearby cultural sites outside the city. This tight schedule prioritizes the core urban experience while keeping travel time minimal.

Week Long: Riyadh Plus Cultural Extension

Use three days to take a deeper look at Riyadh’s museums, markets, and cultural events. Then take a short flight to AlUla or Jeddah for a three-night extension to enjoy landscape and heritage sites. Book internal flights early and plan hotels near the sites you value most.

Two-Week Exploratory Trip

Start in Riyadh and plan internal flights to AlUla and Jeddah, allowing time for guided tours and possible excursions to Taif or other mountain regions. This itinerary lets you combine urban cultural experiences with dramatic archaeology and coastal landscapes.

For each itinerary type, factor visa validity and length of stay to ensure your plans fit the permitted duration.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I perform Umrah on a tourist visa?

Yes. Performing Umrah is generally permitted on a tourist eVisa, but Hajj is excluded. If you plan to perform Umrah, arrange mosque access and local logistics in advance and check any season-specific rules that may apply.

How long does it typically take to get the eVisa?

Processing times vary by nationality and application conditions. Many applicants receive approval within days, but allow at least two to four weeks for planning. Apply early, especially during high-travel seasons.

Do I need travel insurance for my visa?

Travel insurance is strongly recommended and sometimes required for certain nationalities or specific circumstances. Insurance offers protection for medical emergencies and trip disruptions; confirm requirements for your nationality before applying.

What should I do if my application is rejected?

If your application is refused, review the reason provided and correct any errors if you choose to reapply. Common fixes include ensuring passport validity, improving photo quality, and providing clear proof of accommodation and return travel. For complex cases, contact the nearest Saudi diplomatic mission for advice.
