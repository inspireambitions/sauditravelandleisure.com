Understanding UAE Visit Visas for Nepali Citizens

The Policy Basics

Nepali nationals are not eligible for visa-on-arrival in the UAE and must obtain a pre-approved visit visa prior to departure. The UAE issues several short-stay visa categories suitable for Nepali applicants: transit visas (48 and 96 hours), short tourist visas (14, 30 days), longer tourist visas (60 days), and family visit visas. Each category has clear duration, entry, and extension rules.

Visas are typically issued as electronic entry permits (eVisas). Once approved, the eVisa is emailed to you and must be printed and carried to present to immigration on arrival. Approval is subject to UAE immigration approvalâmeeting requirements raises the probability of issuance but does not guarantee entry.

How Visas Are Issued (Channels and Sponsors)

There are four practical channels Nepali applicants use to obtain a UAE visit visa:

  • Airlines and approved carriers: Some carriers offer an online or managed booking service that includes visa application support when you book your flight with them.
  • UAE-based sponsors: A UAE resident (family member or employer), or a hotel can sponsor your application and submit it through UAE channels.
  • Registered travel agencies and visa service providers: Licensed operators in Nepal can prepare and submit applications on your behalf.
  • UAE diplomatic missions: You can submit an application through the UAE embassy or consulate in Kathmandu or the nearest accredited mission, where available.

Each route has trade-offs in cost, speed, and documentation requirements. Later sections compare these options in detail.

Which Visa Should You Choose?

Common Visa Options for Nepali Citizens

  • 48-hour Transit Visa: Ideal if Dubai is a short stopover on the way to another country. Valid for 48 hours from arrival.
  • 96-hour Transit Visa: For longer stopovers under four days.
  • 14-day Tourist/Visit Visa: Short vacations or events where you do not need a full month.
