Who Is Eligible To Apply Through Emirates?

Passport holders who need prearranged visas

Many nationalities can obtain visas on arrival and do not need to apply in advance. For those who do require prearranged visas, Emirates allows online application only if the itinerary is on Emirates flights and the ticket has been issued. The key eligibility checkpoints are:

  • You hold a passport valid for at least six months from your travel date (machine-readable).
  • You have a confirmed Emirates ticket issued and your itinerary into and out of Dubai is contained in the same booking.
  • The ticket number typically starts with 176 or the itinerary is an Emirates-operated sequence.
  • You apply at least four working days before travel; Emirates recommends applying as early as possible.

Some nationalities face additional documentation requirements (national ID copies, additional proof of relationship for family members, etc.). When in doubt, use the Emirates visa search tool or check with Emirates staff to confirm specifics before submitting.

Special cases: transit passengers and Dubai Connect

Emirates provides short-stay transit visas (48-hour and 96-hour) for passengers who need to leave the airport during a long layover. These are available online or at Emiratesâ Dubai Commercial Visa office in Terminal 3 and are only valid for travel on Emirates tickets outbound from Dubai.

Which Visa Types Can Emirates Arrange?

Step-By-Step: Applying For A Dubai Visit Visa Through Emirates (Practical Process)

1. Confirm your Emirates travel booking

Before any visa application, ensure your itinerary is confirmed on Emirates and that the ticket is issued. Your ticket number should begin with 176. Applications through Emirates are only accepted for passengers with such tickets and itineraries fully on Emirates flights.

2. Verify your visa requirement and the correct visa type

Use Emiratesâ visa eligibility tool or your local embassy site to check whether you are eligible for visa on arrival or need a prearranged visa. If your nationality requires a prearranged tourist visa, decide which duration and entry type you need based on trip length and re-entry plans.

3. Prepare documents

Collect and scan the documents listed in the previous section. Ensure passport images are clear, photographs meet specifications, and hotel or residency documents match travel dates on the ticket.

4. Apply via Manage Your Booking (recommended online route)

  • Log in to Manage Your Booking on emirates.com using your booking reference and last name.
  • Navigate to the visa section (if eligible) and follow the application prompts. Upload the required documents.
  • Pay the visa fee online (fees vary by visa type and nationality; VFS processing fees may apply).
  • Keep confirmation emails and reference numbers; Emirates will update you by email on visa status.

Applying online is convenient because you can track the application and avoid traveling to a ticketing office.

5. Alternative: Apply at Emirates ticketing offices (in the UAE or local offices)

If you are applying from within the UAE through a family sponsor or need in-person assistance, visit a local Emirates ticketing office with original documents. Residents can sponsor family members and submit hard copies as required.

Common Problems And How To Avoid Them

Incorrect ticket or codeshare issues

A frequent problem is applying when part of the itinerary is not on Emirates or the ticket number does not start with 176. Applications are rejected in such cases. Always confirm that your inbound and outbound flights to/from Dubai are Emirates-operated and on the same PNR.

Low-quality document uploads

Blurry passport images, mismatched dates, or incomplete hotel confirmations will delay processing or trigger rejection. Scan documents at high resolution, ensure dates align, and match names exactly as on the passport.

Last-minute applications

Emirates requires a minimum processing window (four working days recommended). Applying very close to your departure increases the risk of denial. Plan your visa application as soon as your ticket is issued.

Unclear sponsor documentation

If staying with relatives, provide clear proof of relationship (birth certificates, marriage certificates). For friend sponsors, a refundable deposit may be required by UAE immigrationâensure the sponsor understands these rules.

Overstays and fines

UAE immigration enforces strict penalties for overstay. If your travel plans change, apply for an extension before your visa expires or exit the UAE before the deadline. The sponsor or applicant remains responsible for overstay fines.

Fees, Timing And Refunds

Fee structure

Visa fees vary by type (48-hour transit, 96-hour, single 30-day, multiple-entry 30-day, single/multi 60-day visas). Emirates publishes indicative fees and these often include VFS processing charges. Fees are non-refundable once the visa has been issued; partial refunds (minus processing fees) apply if the visa is canceled before processing.

Processing times

Refund rules and deposits

If you apply but later cancel before processing, Emirates may refund the visa fee minus a small administration deduction. For certain sponsorship arrangements a refundable deposit may be held by immigration; the sponsor must claim it within a specified period.

What To Expect At Dubai Immigration

Visual screening and printed documents

Dubai immigration now performs random visual screening. If selected, you may be asked at the screening counter to present a hard copy of your visa or printout with the visa number. If you donât have a copy, a small fee in AED is charged for printing.

Passport validity and stamping

Immigration will check passport validity (minimum six months recommended) and stamp entry. The stamp duration and visa validity will correspond to the permit you applied for. Keep a copy of the stamped passport page and the electronic visa as proof during your stay.

Behavior at immigration

Dubai immigration officers are strict about accurate information and document integrity. Answer questions clearly and present documents proactively. If your visa was prearranged through Emirates, show your printed permit and the booking reference when requested.

Extending A Visit Visa And Overstay Rules

How to extend

Some tourist visas are extendable once; the extension process requires visiting a UAE immigration office and may require the original passport and ticket copy. Fees for extension are set by immigration and can change. If you anticipate needing more time, begin the extension process well before your visa expires.

Alternatives to extension

Alternatives To Emirates For Obtaining A UAE Visit Visa

Through hotels and licensed travel agencies

Hotels and licensed UAE travel agents can sponsor tourist visas if you have a confirmed reservation. This route is often used by travelers who do not fly Emirates or who book package deals. Always verify the agentâs license and check that the reservation will be accepted as valid proof.

Through UAE residents (sponsored visas)

If you have family or close relatives residing in the UAE, they can sponsor your visa via immigration channels. Sponsors must submit required documents and may need to provide proof of their residency and relationship.

Direct embassy channels

Embassies no longer issue tourist visas for most visitors; the UAE relies on carriers, agents, hotels, and residency sponsors for tourist permit applications. However, for special diplomatic or long-stay requests, embassies can provide guidance and assist with authentication where necessary.

Safety, Insurance, and Financial Requirements

Medical insurance expectations

Some UAE visa categories, especially long-term or multi-entry visas, require a valid health insurance policy applicable in the UAE. Even if not mandatory, travel health insurance is strongly advised.

Financial proof for long-term options

Long-term multi-entry tourist visas (such as the five-year option) have financial requirements like a bank balance threshold. If you intend to apply for these programs, prepare bank statements or proof of funds and confirm the specific requirements before applying.

Protecting yourself from fraud

Only use official Emirates channels, licensed travel agents, or reputable hotels to arrange visas. Do not pay or share sensitive documents with unknown third parties. If you need help, contact Emirates customer service directly or visit an official ticketing office.

Troubleshooting Rejections And What To Do Next

Common reasons for visa rejection

  • Incorrect or incomplete document submission.
  • Mismatch between ticket dates and hotel bookings.
  • Passport with insufficient validity.
  • Prior immigration violations or unresolved overstays.

Steps to take after a rejection

If Emirates informs you of a rejection, request a clear reason and document list. Often, providing the missing information or correcting an error and reapplying resolves the issue. If a rejection is due to immigration discretion (e.g., past overstays), seek advice from legal or immigration specialists.
