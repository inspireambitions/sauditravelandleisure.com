How to Get Visit Visa to Dubai From Sri Lanka

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Dubai remains one of the world’s most visited cities for business, shopping, and leisure. For many travelers from Sri Lanka, obtaining the correct visit visa is the essential first step toward a smooth, worry-free trip to the UAE. This article lays out the exact pathways, documents, timelines, and practical strategies Sri Lankan passport holders need to secure a visit visa to Dubai with confidence.

Short answer: Sri Lankan citizens must obtain a pre-arranged visit visa before traveling to Dubai. You can apply through the UAE embassy channels, accredited visa centers, major airlines that offer visa services, or licensed UAE-based sponsors. The fastest, most dependable routes are applying via an airline (if eligible) or a registered visa service provider, ensuring you meet document requirements and passport validity rules.

This post explains visa categories that apply to Sri Lankans, step-by-step application routes, required documentation, realistic processing times and fees, common reasons for refusal and how to avoid them, arrival and immigration procedures in Dubai, options for extending your stay, and practical planning frameworks—so you can move from curiosity to a well-planned trip. Throughout, I deliver the practical details and local insights that distinguish Saudi Travel & Leisure’s travel planning approach: inspirational, yet operational—helping you plan with certainty and experience Dubai at its best.

Visa Types That Apply to Sri Lankan Citizens

Short-Term Tourist Visas (Single-Entry and Multiple-Entry)

For ordinary Sri Lankan passport holders, the primary options are short-term tourist visit visas: single-entry 30-day visas and longer 60-day tourist visas. These permits allow stays for leisure, visiting family, or short business-related meetings that do not require a work permit.

Transit Visas

If your trip to Dubai is a short stopover en route to another destination, a transit visa (48-hour or 96-hour) may apply. These are usually tied to specific airline ticketing conditions and typically require proof of onward travel.

Sponsor-Based Visas

If you have a UAE-based sponsor—such as a family member, an employer, or a hotel/tour operator—they can arrange a visit visa on your behalf. Sponsor-based visas are common for family visits and are processed by UAE-based immigration authorities.

eVisa vs Prearranged Entry Permit

The term eVisa or electronic entry permit is commonly used for applications submitted online and approved without submitting the passport physically. Approved entry permits are electronic and must be printed or stored on a mobile device for arrival. For Sri Lankan nationals, nearly all tourist visas must be prearranged and approved before boarding.

Eligibility and Passport Requirements

Passport Validity

Your passport must be valid for at least six months from the date of intended entry into Dubai. Machine-readable passports are strongly preferred; handwritten passports may be rejected by some processing systems.

Passport Type and Restrictions

Diplomatic or official passports sometimes have separate processes. Ordinary passport holders from Sri Lanka should follow the regular tourist visa channels. If you hold a non-standard travel document, check with the UAE visa center for specific guidance.

Eligibility Checks

Before you begin, confirm that you are not subject to a travel ban or an unresolved overstay record in the UAE. Any prior overstays or unresolved fines can complicate approval.

Where You Can Apply

UAE Embassy and Visa Centre in Sri Lanka

The UAE Embassy in Colombo primarily handles diplomatic matters and may not issue routine tourist visas directly. For routine applications, licensed visa centers partnered with the UAE embassy or accredited visa application centers in Colombo will be the right contact points.

Airline Visa Services

Major carriers that fly direct to Dubai often provide visa application services for eligible passengers. These services require you to have completed a paid booking with the airline and place the visa application through their “Manage Booking” or visa desk channels. These airline services can be among the most straightforward options because they guide you through document requirements and submission.

Licensed Travel Agents and Visa Service Providers

Accredited travel agencies and visa service providers in Sri Lanka can submit visa applications on your behalf. Choose providers with a proven track record and transparent fee structures; they will manage document collation and liaison with UAE immigration authorities.

UAE-Based Sponsors

If a UAE resident sponsor (family, friend, company or hotel) is arranging your visa, the sponsor submits the application through the UAE immigration portals. This route is common for family visits or business invitations.

Online eVisa Platforms

There are legitimate online platforms that help submit applications to UAE immigration authorities. Use reputable services and allow for government processing times, and expect to provide scanned passport photos and supporting documents.

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Confirm the visa type needed for your travel purpose and duration.
  2. Gather the required documents (passport, photos, bookings, accommodation proof).
  3. Choose the application channel—airline service, visa center, sponsor, or accredited agency.
  4. Submit documents and pay fees; ensure the passport details match all booking references.
  5. Monitor the application status and receive the electronic entry permit when approved.
  6. Print or save the approved visa and present it at check-in and on arrival.

(Note: This numbered list is the first of two allowed lists and provides the clear procedural flow you should follow when applying from Sri Lanka.)

Required Documents

  • Valid Sri Lankan passport with at least six months validity.
  • Recent passport-sized color photograph (white background, full-face).
  • Completed visa application form (online or paper, depending on channel).
  • Copy of confirmed return or onward flight itinerary.
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or sponsor’s tenancy/letter).
  • Proof of sufficient funds if requested (bank statements).
  • If applying through sponsor: copy of sponsor’s Emirates ID/residence permit and proof of relationship where applicable.

(This is the second and final list permitted in the article. Use it as your master checklist before submission.)

Processing Times and Typical Fees

Processing time varies significantly by application channel and visa type. Typical timeframes you should expect:

  • Airline-managed visa: Often 3–7 working days if all documents are in order.
  • Visa center or embassy-facilitated applications: 5–15 working days.
  • Online eVisa via accredited services: 5–20 business days depending on verification steps.

Fees also vary by type (30-day vs 60-day, single vs multiple entry) and by who handles the application. As a planning reference, short-term 30-day tourist permits processed through airline/official channels can be in the range of USD 90–200 inclusive of service charges, while longer or multiple-entry visas cost more. Always confirm total fees with the service provider before paying.

Fees, Refunds, and Deposit Rules to Watch

Be aware that visa fees are usually non-refundable once processed. In some sponsor-based applications, sponsors are asked to provide refundable deposits for certain visitor categories; these deposits have specific claim and refund conditions. Avoid paying large upfront fees to unverified providers—use accredited offices and insist on a receipt and clear refund policy.

Practical Options: Apply Through Airline, Agency, or Sponsor — Pros and Cons

Applying Through an Airline (Pros)

Applying via an airline often offers the cleanest experience: you book your ticket, the airline’s visa service handles submission, and you receive the entry permit electronically. Airlines usually provide clear instructions and have faster liaison channels with UAE authorities.

Applying Through a Licensed Travel Agent (Pros)

An accredited Sri Lankan travel agent can manage document assembly and follow up for you, which is useful if you need help with translations or bank statements. Reputable agents also provide customer support if issues arise.

Sponsor-Based Applications (Pros)

If you have a family member or company in the UAE acting as sponsor, that route can be direct and sometimes quicker, especially if the sponsor has established credentials with immigration authorities.

Drawbacks to Consider

The main downsides across channels are variable processing times and the risk of working with unaccredited intermediaries. Avoid unauthorized agents and always demand official receipts and a copy of the application submission. If your visa is rejected, refund policies vary and sometimes the visa fee is non-refundable.

Common Reasons Applications Are Delayed or Rejected — And How to Prevent Them

Many delays and refusals stem from preventable errors. Anticipate these and remove them from your application process.

  • Passport validity less than six months. Solution: renew your passport before applying.
  • Mismatched information across passport, flight ticket, and application form. Solution: cross-check every field.
  • Low-quality or non-compliant photographs. Solution: use a professional passport photo service following UAE photo guidelines.
  • Incomplete supporting documents (missing return ticket or accommodation proof). Solution: prepare a folder of all required documents and verify with your application channel.
  • Unclear sponsor documentation or expired sponsor residence permits. Solution: verify sponsor status and ask your sponsor to provide updated copies.
  • Previous overstays or unresolved fines in the UAE. Solution: resolve fines via the UAE’s official channels or seek legal advice before applying.

Proactively addressing these common issues will reduce the likelihood of delays or refusal.

Arrival Procedures in Dubai: What to Expect at Immigration

When you arrive at Dubai International or Al Maktoum, immigration will examine your passport and your electronic entry permit. Here are reliable tips based on the arrival experience:

  • Carry a printed copy of your approval email and e-permit. While many approvals are digital, having a hard copy expedites processing when asked.
  • Immigration may request hotel reservation confirmation or proof of funds. Keep a PDF of your booking and a recent bank statement (digital copy).
  • Random visual screening can occur; Dubai immigration may ask travelers to proceed to an additional screening counter. Officers may request a printed copy of the visa or proof of onward travel—be prepared.
  • Biometric checks are usually completed at arrival for certain categories; follow officer instructions politely and promptly.
  • Ensure your passport name exactly matches the ticket and visa. Any variation may cause delays or refusal to board.

Extending Your Visa and Overstay Penalties

If you wish to lengthen your stay, tourist visas may be extendable through the official visa extension process at immigration offices or sometimes online. Extension fees apply and often require presenting the original passport. Overstaying without applying for an extension will result in fines and could affect future visa approvals; the UAE enforces overstay penalties strictly. If a medical emergency prevents timely departure, contact UAE immigration or your embassy for guidance.

What to Do If Your Visa Application Is Refused

A refusal letter should state a reason. Typical corrective actions include:

  • Reapply after rectifying documentation errors.
  • Seek clarification via the visa center or airline that submitted the application.
  • If refusal is due to prior immigration records, contact UAE immigration or pursue legal advice.
  • Avoid multiple consecutive reapplications without addressing the refusal reason, as this can complicate future attempts.

Keep copies of all communications and payment receipts. If you applied through an agent, insist on written confirmation of the refusal and recommended next steps.

Cost-Saving and Timing Strategies

  • Book non-refundable airline fares only after you have received visa approval; some flight types are required by certain application channels.
  • Apply early—ideally at least three to four weeks before departure to buffer unexpected delays.
  • Compare official government channels, airline services, and reputable agents on total fees (government fees plus service charges).
  • If your plans are flexible, choose 30-day single-entry visas for shorter stays; for back-and-forth regional itineraries, consider multiple-entry visas if available.

Practical Packing and Travel Prep for Sri Lankan Travelers

Plan your pre-departure checklist pragmatically: verify passport validity, print your visa approval, prepare hotel and return ticket confirmations, secure travel insurance, and carry essential medications with prescriptions. Learn basic UAE entry etiquette: immigration officers appreciate clear, concise answers. Dress respectfully when interacting with officials.

A Traveler’s Blueprint — How to Plan Your Visa Timeline

Begin planning at least six weeks before travel. Weeks breakdown (prose):

Start by checking passport validity and renewing if needed. Two to four weeks before travel, decide on the application channel and gather documentation—photos, ticket bookings, hotel reservations, sponsor documents if applicable, and proof of funds. Submit the application no later than 14 days before travel, but allow more time for embassy processed or complex sponsor-based applications. As approvals can vary, aim to lock in your booking only after receiving the entry permit. Keep digital and printed copies of all approvals and confirmations.

Common Mistakes Sri Lankan Applicants Make (And How to Avoid Them)

Many applicants underestimate the importance of document consistency. The passport name must match the ticket and visa application exactly; even spacing or hyphen differences can cause problems. Another frequent oversight is insufficient passport validity—renew in advance. Use the checklist above and, if you use an agent, insist on a pre-submission review to catch missing or mismatched items.

On-the-Ground Tips for a Smooth Arrival in Dubai

At the airport, keep documents in an accessible folder. Expect occasional secondary screening; remain calm, provide requested documents, and avoid giving unnecessary explanations. Use official airport transport options or reputable ride-share apps to reach your accommodation. If you are traveling during peak seasons, book arrivals in daylight hours when immigration counters are fully staffed.

What to Expect After Landing — Practical Steps in the First 24 Hours

Once you have cleared immigration and reached your accommodation, confirm your itinerary details: ensure you have transport arrangements to any early-morning appointments and locate nearby embassies or consulates in case of urgent issues. If you need to extend your visa, research the nearest immigration office and required documents immediately—processing windows and hours can vary.

Safety, Conduct, and Cultural Considerations

Respect local laws and customs. Public behavior norms differ from Sri Lanka, so be mindful of dress codes in public spaces, alcohol regulations, and local rules regarding public displays of affection. Troubleshooting: If You Miss a Flight or Need an Emergency Visa Amendment

If you miss a flight and your visa has strict date conditions, contact your airline and visa provider immediately. For emergency changes (medical or family), your sponsor or visa agent may request amendments with evidence. Keep embassy and visa center contact details handy, and remember that fees and processing times for amendments vary.

FAQ

Do Sri Lankan citizens get visa on arrival in Dubai?

No. Sri Lankan citizens typically need a pre-arranged visit visa before boarding a flight to Dubai. Visa-on-arrival privileges do not generally apply to Sri Lankan passport holders, so secure your permit in advance through an airline, accredited visa center, sponsor, or licensed agency.

How long does it take to process a Dubai visit visa from Sri Lanka?

Processing can range from as little as 3–4 working days with airline-managed or priority services to 10–20 business days for embassy-processed or complex sponsor applications. Apply at least two to four weeks ahead of travel to allow buffer time for verification or unexpected requests.

Can I extend my tourist visa while in Dubai?

Many tourist visas can be extended once through UAE immigration, subject to fees and availability. Extensions usually require presenting your passport to immigration offices. Plan ahead and enquire about extension rules with your visa provider.

What should I do if my application is refused?

First, obtain the refusal reason from the processing body. Address the specific cause—such as renewing an insufficient passport, correcting mismatched details, or providing missing documentation—and reapply. If the refusal involves past immigration records, seek guidance from UAE immigration or legal counsel before reapplying.
