How to Renew Driving License in Riyadh

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Introduction

Saudi Arabia is welcoming more visitors and residents than ever, and for anyone planning to drive in the Kingdom—whether for daily commutes, business trips, or exploring the wider region—keeping your driving license current is essential. Riyadh, as the capital and the primary hub for government services, offers straightforward digital pathways for renewals, but the process still requires careful preparation to avoid delays or unexpected fines.

Short answer: You can renew your Saudi driving license in Riyadh primarily through the Absher platform after passing a required medical (Efada) check and settling any traffic violations; fees depend on the chosen validity period (commonly calculated at SAR 40 per year for private licences). The entire workflow can be completed online if your Absher profile is up to date, but there are practical exceptions and in-person options for special cases.

This article walks you, step-by-step, from preparing documents and clearing fines to scheduling the Efada test and choosing delivery. It explains special situations (expired licences, lost cards, suspended licences, expatriate considerations), highlights common mistakes that cause delays, and provides regional context that helps Riyadh-based drivers coordinate renewals alongside travel plans within the Kingdom. The goal is to give you the exact blueprint to renew confidently—fast and without surprises.

Why Renewing On Time Matters in Riyadh

Driving with an expired licence in Riyadh carries administrative fines and can lead to complications with traffic stops, vehicle registration, insurance claims, and employer compliance. Riyadh’s traffic enforcement is consistent, and many checkpoints verify licence validity electronically. Beyond legal risk, a valid licence is often required by employers, rental agencies, and when crossing provincial borders within Saudi Arabia.

Renewing proactively also helps you lock in longer validity (up to 10 years in many cases), which saves time and administrative hassle. For frequent travelers or business professionals, staying ahead of renewal dates is part of smooth life logistics in the city.

The Core Requirements: What You Must Have Ready

Below is a focused checklist of the documents and account conditions the authorities expect when you attempt a renewal in Riyadh.

  • Valid Iqama (residency permit) or Saudi national ID
  • Passport copy (if applicable)
  • Current driving licence (original or clear electronic copy)
  • A completed Efada medical report (vision and basic health checks)
  • An Absher account with up-to-date photo and fingerprint status
  • Proof of payment for the renewal fee
  • All traffic fines cleared before renewal

These elements form the technical baseline. The next sections explain how to obtain each item and how to avoid common pitfalls that cause renewals to be rejected.

Understanding Validity Periods and Fees

Renewal durations typically allow you to choose the licence term that best suits your plans. For private vehicle licences the cost is often calculated at SAR 40 per year, which translates into widely used packages such as SAR 80 for two years, SAR 200 for five years, or SAR 400 for ten years. Motorcycle licences and other categories can have different fee structures; always confirm the exact fee when you begin the transaction in Absher.

Choosing a longer validity is usually the best value for residents who plan to remain several years, while short renewals may suit temporary residents. You can also weigh renewal length against potential changes in residency status, job transfers, or planned moves out of the Kingdom.

Where to Start: Absher and Why It’s Central

Absher is the government’s digital gateway for civil and traffic services. For most drivers in Riyadh, Absher is the fastest and cleanest path for licence renewal. A properly configured Absher account saves trips to government offices and lets you track the status of the Efada medical report, confirm fines are cleared, and select delivery options for the physical card.

If your Absher profile lacks a current photo or fingerprint status, your renewal will be delayed. Make sure Absher displays a recent passport-style photo and that biometrics have been captured—both are often required to validate identity before the renewal is issued.

Step-by-Step Renewal Process (Detailed)

The following sequence provides a practical narrative of the renewal process, with conditional branches for the various situations residents encounter. After the checklist that follows, each step is explained in depth.

  1. Prepare documents and confirm Absher status.
  2. Check and clear traffic violations.
  3. Schedule and complete the Efada medical test.
  4. Process renewal through Absher and pay fees.
  5. Choose delivery method and monitor status.
  6. Handle special cases (lost card, suspended licence, expired beyond grace).

1. Prepare Documents and Confirm Absher Status

Begin by checking that your Iqama and passport details are current on Absher. The platform reads these details to validate identity during renewal. If your Absher account lacks a clear photograph or has incomplete biometric data, visit a designated service center in Riyadh to update them. This is frequently the single most common reason renewals are rejected.

Because many employers manage employee passports and permits centrally, coordinate with HR early if you require documents they hold. For the most effective flow, prepare digital scans of your Iqama and passport and ensure the current driving licence is visible in Absher under your profile.

2. Clear Traffic Violations

Absher will typically block licence renewal if outstanding fines exist. Use the traffic services section to list and pay fines electronically; payment options include SADAD, bank gateways, and in many cases, direct debit from local banking apps. Clearing fines before you proceed saves time and prevents a rejected application.

If you believe a fine is incorrect or under dispute, start an inquiry in parallel, but clear uncontested fines before initiating the renewal. For drivers who travel across provinces, check fines logged by traffic authorities in other regions; Absher aggregates these centrally.

3. Schedule and Complete the Efada Medical Test

The Efada medical is a standard vision and health screening required for licence issuance. In Riyadh there are multiple authorized medical centers where tests are performed and results uploaded electronically to the system your renewal will reference.

Book Efada early: appointment slots can fill during morning peaks or around public holidays. The test includes basic vision checks and a review of fitness to drive; some categories (heavy vehicles, professional licences) require additional examinations. After the test, confirm the medical report is linked to your Absher profile before starting the renewal; this avoids rejection.

For families or employees arranging group renewals, several clinics offer coordinated slots for multiple people, which speeds the process and reduces waiting times.

4. Process Renewal Through Absher and Pay Fees

With documents ready, fines cleared, and the Efada linked, log into Absher and navigate to My Services â E-Services â Traffic Services â Renew Driving License. Select the relevant licence, pick the duration you prefer, and complete payment via the platform.

If you are renewing an expatriate licence and your original licence was exchanged from a foreign permit, Absher will verify the existing record and may skip the driving test. If a test is required, Absher will direct you to the appropriate booking channel for theory or practical exams.

After payment, Absher will confirm the request and provide a reference number. Monitor the status in the service history area and ensure the physical card delivery address is correct if you requested postal delivery.

5. Choose Delivery Method and Monitor Status

Two common delivery methods exist: pickup at an authorized traffic office (Muroor) or postal delivery via Saudi Post. Postal delivery is convenient for those with stable addresses in Riyadh; select it in Absher and provide a complete address. If you prefer to collect the card in person, ensure you bring original ID and the Absher confirmation code.

If the delivery is delayed, track the status through Absher and Saudi Post. In some high-demand periods the card issuance and delivery can take several business days; plan renewals well before travel dates or vehicle registration renewals.

6. Special Cases and Conditional Paths

This is where many drivers get tripped up. Manage these scenarios proactively:

  • Expired Licence Beyond Grace: If your licence has been expired beyond a long grace period, there may be additional fines and requirements; contact the traffic department or an authorized service center to confirm the exact steps.
  • Lost or Damaged Card: Use Absher’s “Report Lost Licence” function if available, and apply for a replacement. A new Efada is not always required unless the previous medical has lapsed.
  • Suspended Licence: If your licence is suspended due to violations, fulfill the suspension conditions and any required training before attempting renewal. Absher will reflect suspension status, and the renewal will not proceed until conditions are met.
  • Renewing While Abroad: Some residents temporarily outside Saudi Arabia can renew through authorized service centers or designate someone with Power of Attorney to complete the process locally. Procedures vary by case.

Common Roadblocks and How to Fix Them

Many residents understand the basic renewal steps but still encounter delays. Below are frequent obstacles with precise fixes.

Absher Profile Missing Photo or Biometrics: Visit a certified service center in Riyadh to update your photo and fingerprint status. Some large hospitals and Muroor branches offer this service.

Efada Not Linked to Absher: Confirm the medical center uploads results electronically. If not, request a manual upload or a digital receipt you can present at a Muroor office.

Disputed Fines Block Renewal: Open a dispute in Absher immediately and, if required, pay contested fines temporarily to proceed with renewal. If a refund is justified after dispute resolution, the system or traffic department will process it.

Licence Issued But Not Delivered: Verify the delivery address in Absher and track the Saudi Post reference. If the card is delayed beyond the typical timeframe, contact your nearest Muroor branch.

Renewal Denied Due to Suspension or Violations: Obtain a written statement from the traffic department outlining remedial steps. Completing any required courses, paying compounded fines, or meeting prohibition periods will clear the hold.

Expat-Specific Considerations

Expatriates often face unique scenarios: foreign licence exchange, renewals tied to Iqama validity, and employer-managed documentation.

Foreign Licence Holders: If your home licence was exchanged for a Saudi licence, the renewal will follow Saudi procedures. Some nationalities benefit from licence exchange without testing, but renewal still requires Efada and Absher processing.

Iqama Expiry: Your driving licence cannot be renewed if your Iqama is expired. Ensure your residency documentation is current first. Employers who manage sponsorship may need to coordinate renewals alongside work permit renewals.

Employer-Assisted Renewals: Many companies in Riyadh handle renewals centrally, especially for fleets or staff who travel frequently. If your organization provides this, confirm they register the renewal in Absher and verify the delivery arrangement.

Power of Attorney and Delegated Renewals: You can authorize a representative to renew on your behalf; follow the official power of attorney protocols and ensure the representative’s Absher profile is configured to act on your behalf.

Renewing a Lost, Damaged, or Stolen Licence

When a physical card is lost or damaged, renew or replace it promptly to avoid inconvenient stops and potential fines.

Report and Replace: Use Absher to declare the licence lost or damaged if that function is available, then initiate the replacement. Bring an Iqama and a formal police report if required by the Muroor office for theft cases.

Replace via Absher or In-Person: Most replacements can be started on Absher and finished at a Muroor office or by choosing postal delivery. If your Iqama has been updated since the original issue, match the new details carefully.

Fees and Timing: Replacement fees are similar to standard renewal costs for the remaining validity period. If you replace because the licence is near expiry, consider renewing for a longer term to reduce repeated renewals.

Renewal When Your Licence Is Linked to a Foreign Exchange

If your Saudi licence was originally issued via exchange from a foreign licence, the renewal mechanics are usually identical to locally issued licences. The key difference is that Absher will have a record referencing the original exchange; no new foreign documentation is required unless the record is incomplete.

If the original exchange was never recorded or there is a gap, bring the original foreign licence’s certified Arabic translation (if available) and coordinate with Muroor staff to reconcile records before initiating renewal.

Useful Timing and Practical Planning Tips

  • Renew Early: Start renewal tasks up to 180 days before expiry. This avoids last-minute issues and gives time to clear fines or resolve medical problems.
  • Afternoons Are Quieter: For in-person visits in Riyadh—if required—late afternoons often have shorter queues outside of Friday prayer times and public holidays.
  • Employer Calendar: Align renewals with employer HR processes if your company manages official documents.
  • Plan for Postal Waits: If you choose postal delivery, allow extra days around public holidays or busy travel seasons.
  • Combine Renewals: If you manage a fleet or multiple family licences, coordinate Efada appointments in group slots to save time.

What To Do After Renewal

Once you receive the renewed card, confirm all information (name, expiry date, licence category) and store the physical card safely. Update any digital copies used for travel or employer records and keep a photo of the new licence in a secure digital location for emergencies.

If the licence will be used for rental car pickups or vehicle registrations, some agencies require presenting the physical card; plan accordingly before any planned trips.

What If You Need to Change Licence Details (Name, Address, etc.)?

Name or Address Changes: These often link to national civil affairs and your Absher profile. Update your personal details in Absher and ensure the change is reflected in Iqama records before applying for a renewal that includes updated details.

Court-Ordered Name Changes: Provide the official documentation to a Muroor branch and request that records be updated. Renewals may be denied if identity documents do not match.

International Moves: If you plan to leave Saudi Arabia for a prolonged period, evaluate renewing for shorter periods or arranging for a representative to handle renewals while you are abroad.

Cost-Saving and Time-Saving Strategies

Choose the longest renewal period you reasonably expect to need; it usually minimizes administrative repeat work. If you seldom drive and expect to leave the Kingdom, a short renewal may make sense, but remember the administrative overhead of frequent renewals.

Group bookings for Efada and centralized employer processing save time and reduce missed workdays. Some medical centers offer expedited digital uploads to Absher for a small service charge—worth it if you’re on a tight schedule.

If you run into a complicated case—suspension, legal dispute, or cross-border documentation—use a trusted agent or consult the Muroor office directly in Riyadh. How Renewals Interact with Vehicle Registration and Insurance

Many vehicle-related services require a valid driving licence. Renewing before your vehicle’s registration renewal avoids cascading delays. Some insurers will not process claims for drivers without a valid licence, so confirm your renewal before any long trips.

If you are moving vehicles between regions, confirm registration requirements with the local traffic office; some administrative steps depend on the driver’s licence validity and category.

Regional Context: Renewals Outside Riyadh

If you plan to renew while traveling outside Riyadh—whether for business to Jeddah or a weekend to Taif—local Muroor offices and medical centers implement the same national procedures. However, local appointment availability varies. For eastern travel or visits to Dammam and Al Khobar, consult regional guidance before booking an Efada test to ensure digital linkage to Absher is immediate.

When to Seek Professional Help

Complex cases—licence suspensions, legal disputes, or incomplete foreign-exchange records—benefit from professional assistance. Authorized traffic consultants, legal advisors, or employer HR teams can liaise with Muroor offices on your behalf. For businesses and recruitment firms managing large volumes of renewals, formal service channels can apply to streamline renewals and prevent service suspension for licensed activities.

Final Checks Before You Start

Before you begin a renewal today, confirm:

  • Your Absher account is active with up-to-date photo and biometric status.
  • You have cleared and paid any outstanding fines.
  • Efada appointment is scheduled and linked to Absher.
  • The Iqama and passport information matches what Absher displays.
  • You have selected a delivery option and verified the address or planned pickup.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long before my licence expires should I start the renewal process? A: Begin up to 180 days before expiry. Early renewal avoids last-minute problems and gives time to resolve fines or medical issues.

Q: Can someone else renew my licence for me in Riyadh? A: Yes. A legally authorized representative with the correct power of attorney and their own Absher access can renew on your behalf, provided all documents and payments are in order.

Q: What happens if I have unpaid fines when I try to renew? A: Absher will block renewal until fines are cleared. Pay fines through Absher to proceed; if a fine is disputed, open a dispute but consider paying temporarily to avoid renewal delays.

Q: Can I renew my Saudi driving licence while travelling outside the Kingdom? A: In many cases, you can arrange renewal through an authorized representative or specific service centers, but requirements vary. Coordinate with your employer or a trusted representative before you leave.
