How to Travel to Dubai From Abu Dhabi

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

The Gulf is moving at speed: Dubai and Abu Dhabi together draw millions of visitors each year, and travel between the two cities is one of the region’s busiest short-distance corridors. Whether you’re landing in Abu Dhabi and want to spend a day in Dubai, planning an inter-city transfer during a business trip, or arranging a relaxed road trip along the coast, understanding the practical steps, costs, and local rules will save time and money.

Short answer: You can travel from Abu Dhabi to Dubai by public bus, taxi or ride-hail, private transfer, or by renting and driving a car. Buses (E100/E101/E102 and the Abu Dhabi Airport Express) are the cheapest reliable option and take roughly 1 hour 15 minutes to 2 hours depending on route and traffic. Taxis and private transfers are faster and more flexible but cost significantly more; renting a car gives you independence for stops and detours.

This article answers the question “how to travel to Dubai from Abu Dhabi” in practical detail. You’ll find step-by-step instructions for each transport mode, realistic cost and time expectations, simple safety and etiquette rules for the UAE, sample day-trip and overnight itineraries, and common mistakes to avoid. The goal is to give you a clear, confident blueprint so you can choose the right option and execute it smoothly.

Overview: Distance, Time and the Corridor You’ll Use

Geography and the Main Highway

Abu Dhabi and Dubai sit on the southern and northern stretches of the UAE coastline, linked by the E11 highway — the country’s primary arterial road. The straight-line distance between both city centers is about 120 kilometers (75 miles); driving distance on the E11 is roughly 140–160 kilometers depending on start and end points. Travel time typically ranges from 1 hour 15 minutes to 2 hours.

Typical Travel Times by Mode

Peak-hour traffic (weekday mornings 7–9am and evenings 4–7pm) can add 30–60 minutes to the journey. Expect:

  • Bus (E101/E100/E102 or Airport Express): 1 hour 15 minutes to 2 hours depending on stops and boarding location.
  • Taxi/Private Transfer: 1 hour 10 minutes to 1 hour 45 minutes depending on pick-up/drop-off locations and traffic.
  • Rental Car / Self-Drive: 1 hour 15 minutes to 1 hour 45 minutes with quick toll-free progress on the E11.
  • Organized Day Tour or Shuttle: 1 hour 30 minutes to 2 hours (often timed with schedule and stops).

Practical Points About Timing

Plan extra time if you must make timed reservations (dinner, tours, flights). If you’re connecting from Abu Dhabi Airport to Dubai for a same-day onward flight, give yourself a minimum 4–5 hours cushion to account for customs, baggage, and road time.

Transport Options: Practical, Step-By-Step

I’ll walk you through each main option, explain costs and timing, give step-by-step actions, and list the pros and cons so you can choose the right approach for your trip.

Public Bus — The Budget, Reliable Option

Public buses are the most cost-effective way to travel between Abu Dhabi and Dubai. They are comfortable, air-conditioned, and run on fixed schedules.

What you need to know

  • Main routes: E100 (Abu Dhabi Central Bus Station â Al Ghubaiba in Old Dubai), E101 (Abu Dhabi Central Bus Station â Ibn Battuta Bus Station), E102 (Abu Dhabi Airport â Ibn Battuta). There’s also the Abu Dhabi Airport Express with a slightly different fare structure and frequency.
  • Cost: Expect AED 25–35 one-way (prices can change). Payment is by NOL card on Dubai’s intercity services.
  • Duration: Roughly 1 hour 15 minutes to 2 hours depending on route and stops.

Step-by-step: Taking the bus from Abu Dhabi to Dubai

  1. Buy or top up a NOL card. If you’re leaving from Abu Dhabi Airport, there are kiosks near the bus stop. If you’re in the city, buy or top up at the Abu Dhabi Central Bus Station before departure. A minimum balance is required to board.
  2. Go to the appropriate boarding area (Abu Dhabi Central Bus Station or the airport bus stop). Look for E100/E101/E102 markings and the digital displays.
  3. Board when called and tap your NOL card on the reader. Store luggage under the bus if required; small carry-on bags go in the cabin.
  4. Enjoy the ride; there are monitors announcing stops and some buses offer Wi-Fi and USB chargers.
  5. Disembark at the designated Dubai station (Ibn Battuta, Al Ghubaiba, or downtown depending on the route) and continue by metro, tram, or taxi.

Why choose the bus

  • Economy: Lowest out-of-pocket price.
  • Predictability: Fixed schedule and reliable service on main corridors.
  • Comfort: Modern coaches with air-conditioning and reclining seats.

Drawbacks

  • Time: Longer than a direct taxi in light traffic.
  • Flexibility: Fixed stops and schedules; not door-to-door.

Taxi and Ride-Hail — Faster Door-to-Door

Taxis and ride-hailing apps (Careem and Uber) provide door-to-door transfers and are the fastest non-private-driving option. Fares vary with vehicle type and demand.

What to expect

  • Cost: Typically AED 200–400 one-way between central Abu Dhabi and destinations across Dubai. Airport pickup / drop-off and traffic can push fares higher.
  • Time: 1 hour 10 minutes to 1 hour 45 minutes, depending on traffic and precise pickup/drop-off points.
  • Payment: Card and cash generally accepted; through the app is often easiest.

How to book and ride

  1. Use a ride-hail app or pick up an official Abu Dhabi taxi. For app bookings, input your Dubai destination and request an intercity trip.
  2. Confirm the route with the driver (E11 via the coastal highway is normal) and estimate arrival time.
  3. Pay through the app or in cash/card at the end of the trip.

Pros

  • Convenience and speed.
  • Door-to-door service.
  • Good option when traveling in a small group splitting cost.

Cons

  • Costly compared with the bus.
  • Surge pricing can make a significant difference during peak hours or events.

If you care about a smooth luxury transfer or concierge-level pick-ups, private transfer companies specialize in airport-to-hotel or hotel-to-hotel service with meet-and-greet options.

Private Transfers and Chauffeur Services

Private transfers are the most comfortable and highest-end option without self-driving: think poised vehicles, meet-and-greet, fixed schedules.

Why travelers choose private transfers

  • Reliability for timed arrivals (conferences, weddings, flights).
  • Privacy and space for luggage.
  • Fixed pricing and professional drivers.

Booking and cost

  • Book through travel platforms or local transfer companies; expect AED 350–600 for a premium sedan or larger vehicle.
  • Confirm cancellation terms, waiting-time rules, and luggage allowances before booking.

When to pick this option

  • Arrival with a group or family and lots of luggage.
  • Business travelers with tight timelines.
  • Travelers who prefer no driving responsibility and guaranteed pickup.

Rental Car / Self-Drive — Freedom With Responsibility

Renting a car gives you the freedom to stop along the way, explore suburbs, or drive at your own schedule.

Key considerations

  • Road quality: Excellent. The E11 is a modern highway with good signage.
  • Speed limits: Typically up to 120 km/h on highways; adhere to posted signs. Speed cameras are common.
  • Toll system: Dubai uses Salik toll gates. Your rental agreement should clarify how tolls are charged and whether a Salik tag is active.
  • Insurance: Check collision damage waiver, third-party liability, and cross-emirate coverage.
  • Driving license: International visitors can usually drive with their home country license; verify at the rental counter.

Step-by-step rental process

  1. Book online in advance. Choose an airport or city pick-up location depending on your schedule.
  2. At pickup, present your passport, driving license, and credit card for the deposit.
  3. Inspect the car for existing damage and confirm fuel policy and toll arrangements.
  4. Program your route (E11) and ensure your phone has hands-free setup for navigation.

Pros

  • Full control of schedule and route.
  • Ability to visit attractions en route (miracle gardens, coastal viewpoints).
  • Often cost-effective for groups.

Cons

  • Parking and city driving in Dubai can be expensive and require planning.
  • You are responsible for obeying local traffic laws; fines can be expensive.
  • Salik tolls and parking fees add to the total cost.

Organized Tours and Shuttles

If you want to combine transport with sightseeing, day tours and shuttle services provide curated experiences plus logistics. These can be a very efficient way to see highlights without arranging multiple tickets.

Advantages

  • Door-to-door pickup in many cases.
  • Onboard commentary and curated stops.
  • Single price covering transport and some attractions.

Limitations

  • Less time for independent exploration.
  • Fixed schedules and group-based pacing.

If you prefer a packaged experience, search for “Day Tour of Dubai from Abu Dhabi” or a similar offering; these tours optimize travel time and help you focus on top attractions.

Cost Comparison and Quick Decision Framework

Making the right choice is about balancing time, budget, and comfort. The simple decision framework below helps you pick a transport mode based on priorities.

  • Lowest cost, no rush: Bus (E101/E102/E100).
  • Fastest door-to-door for small groups: Taxi / Ride-hail.
  • Maximum privacy and reliability: Private transfer / chauffeur.
  • Flexibility and exploration: Rental car.
  • Guided sightseeing included: Organized tour or shuttle.

Practical Booking and On-The-Day Checklist

You’ll be tempted to improvise on the day — but a bit of preparation removes stress. Follow this compact checklist before departure.

  • If using the bus, purchase or top up a NOL card and verify the bus code (E100/E101/E102).
  • If taking a taxi or ride-hail, book in the app in advance if you have a tight schedule.
  • For rentals, confirm toll and insurance policies and take photos of the vehicle at pickup.
  • For private transfers, confirm pickup time, contact details for the driver, and luggage allowances.
  • Allow buffer time for security checks if you start at Abu Dhabi Airport, and factor in traffic peaks.

A soft rule of thumb is to add 30–45 minutes extra to any estimated travel time when planning connections.

Step-By-Step: How To Take the Bus From Abu Dhabi Airport To Dubai

Use this practical micro-guide when arriving at Abu Dhabi’s terminal and wanting to reach Dubai by public transport.

  1. After immigration and luggage, follow signs to the ground transportation area where the intercity buses board.
  2. Locate the bus counters and NOL vending kiosks. If there isn’t a NOL machine, the Abu Dhabi Airport Express counter sells tickets in cash or card for the express service.
  3. Choose between E102 and the Abu Dhabi Airport Express. E102 accepts NOL cards and is cheaper; Airport Express accepts cash/card and often runs more frequently.
  4. Head to the boarding area and stow large luggage under the coach. Keep travel documents and valuables with you.
  5. Tap your NOL card on entry (if required) and take a seat. Expect a stop at Ibn Battuta in Dubai or direct service to central Abu Dhabi depending on the route selected.
  6. On arrival at Dubai, switch to metro or tram from Ibn Battuta station or hail a taxi to your final destination.

This exact sequence removes uncertainty and helps you reach Dubai without surprises.

Safety, Rules and Local Etiquette

Traveling between emirates is straightforward, but being aware of a few legal and cultural rules ensures a stress-free transfer.

  • Seatbelts: Mandatory for all passengers.
  • Driving laws: Strict penalties for using mobile phones without hands-free devices and for unsafe lane changes. Speed cameras and traffic enforcement are widespread.
  • Alcohol: Only consume in licensed venues. Drinking and driving is a serious offense.
  • Dress and behavior: Public spaces in both cities are tolerant but conservative in etiquette. Dress modestly in government or religious areas.
  • Documentation: Carry your passport when traveling between emirates as ID checks can happen, especially at airports or for hotel check-ins.

Sample Itineraries: Day Trip and Extended Visit

Here are realistic sample itineraries that match common travel goals.

1-Day Dubai Visit (Ideal If You’re Based in Abu Dhabi)

Morning

  • Depart Abu Dhabi by early bus (E101 or Airport Express) leaving between 7:00–8:00 to avoid peak traffic.
  • Arrive at Ibn Battuta or Al Ghubaiba around 9:00–10:00.

Midday

  • Head to Downtown Dubai: visit the Dubai Mall, see the Dubai Fountain, and go to the Burj Khalifa observation deck (book tickets ahead for timed entries).

Afternoon

  • Quick lunch in Downtown, then take a taxi to the Marina for a walk or short cruise.

Evening

  • Return by bus or private transfer; schedule your return no earlier than 20:00 to avoid rush-hour delays.

2-Day Mini Break (Overnight in Dubai)

Day 1: Morning arrival, Downtown and Marina, evening desert safari or show. Day 2: Morning at a theme park (if visiting from Abu Dhabi consider booking a Yas Island shuttle from Dubai), afternoon shopping at Mall of the Emirates, late return to Abu Dhabi.

These itineraries optimize time while accounting for realistic transit durations.

Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

I’ve guided hundreds of travelers across this corridor; here are the mistakes I see most often and how to avoid them.

  • Mistake: Underestimating traffic. Solution: Add a built-in cushion of 45–60 minutes for timed commitments.
  • Mistake: Trying to buy bus tickets on the vehicle. Solution: Buy and top up a NOL card at the station or airport kiosk.
  • Mistake: Renting a car with unclear toll policies. Solution: Confirm Salik tag arrangements and how tolls are billed before signing.
  • Mistake: Assuming ride-hail is always cheaper. Solution: Check apps during off-peak times but compare to taxi meters and consider that long journeys can exceed budget expectations.

Anticipating these points prevents last-minute problems and keeps your trip on track.

How to Make the Transfer Comfortable and Efficient

Beyond choosing a transport mode, a few practical tips make the experience better.

  • Bring a lightweight scarf or sweater for buses and taxis: air-conditioning can be brisk.
  • Carry a small bottle of water and an adapter for your phone charger.
  • Save transit apps and local mapping tools offline in case mobile data is limited.
  • Keep a printed or screenshot copy of meeting confirmations and hotel addresses in Arabic and English for drivers.

These small adjustments increase your comfort and reduce friction along the way.

Sustainability and Making Smarter Choices

If reducing your environmental footprint is a priority, choose the bus or a shared transfer where possible. Public transport is the most efficient per passenger and the Gulf region is expanding intercity coach services to reduce emissions. When renting or taking private transfers, consider vehicles with hybrid or electric options if available.

Troubleshooting On The Road

If you encounter delays, lost luggage, or booking problems:

  • For bus issues, speak to station staff and keep ticket receipts.
  • For ride-hail disputes, use the app’s support feature and take screenshots of your trip.
  • For rental problems, call the company’s emergency number and document damage with photos and timestamps.

These steps protect you financially and legally and make it easier to resolve complications.

Practical Tips for Visiting Dubai From Abu Dhabi — Beyond the Transfer

  • Plan attractions by neighborhood to minimize internal travel time once in Dubai.
  • Book Burj Khalifa, museum entries, and desert safaris in advance, especially during peak season.
  • If visiting during summer, plan indoor activities for mid-day heat.
  • Use the metro and tram inside Dubai where possible — it’s often faster than road transport across town.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Which bus should I take from Abu Dhabi Airport to get closest to Downtown Dubai? A: The Abu Dhabi Airport Express and E102 both serve routes to Dubai; E102 typically terminates at Ibn Battuta which connects directly to the Dubai Metro Red Line. From there you can ride the metro to take you close to Downtown Dubai (Burj Khalifa/Dubai Mall). If you prefer a direct express option, confirm departure and stops at the airport counters.

Q: How much should I budget for a private transfer between Abu Dhabi and Dubai? A: Expect a private sedan transfer to cost in the range of AED 350–600 depending on vehicle class and included extras (meet-and-greet, waiting time). Book in advance for a guaranteed rate and to avoid surge pricing.

Q: Are there tolls or hidden charges when driving from Abu Dhabi to Dubai? A: Dubai uses Salik tolls — active toll gates on entry into and within parts of Dubai. Rental companies often bill tolls to your deposit or via a tag placed on the vehicle; confirm how tolls are handled before you accept the car. Parking fees in Dubai are also common and should be factored into daily costs.

Q: Can I use a taxi app from Abu Dhabi to book a ride to Dubai? A: Yes — Careem and Uber operate in both emirates and can be used for intercity trips. However, fare estimates can vary and surge pricing may apply. For airport pickups, check whether the app will allow inter-emirate trips from the airport pickup point or if airport rules require a licensed taxi. If in doubt, arrange a private transfer.
