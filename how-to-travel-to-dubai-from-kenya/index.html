Booking Flights: Airlines, Routes, and Smart Strategies

Direct vs. Connecting Flights

Direct flights from Nairobi to Dubai are the fastest and least hassle: daily nonstop options are offered by Kenya Airways and Emirates to DXB, and Air Arabia to SHJ. flydubai also operates a few weekly nonstop services. Nonstop flight time averages around 5â6 hours.

Connecting flights through hubs such as Doha, Addis Ababa, or Riyadh will lengthen travel time but can be cheaper or provide better schedule options. If you need to break up travel, a day in Doha or Abu Dhabi can be built into the itinerary.

Choosing an Airline: What to Consider

Evaluate carriers on:

  • Schedule convenience: departure and arrival times that fit your plans.

  • In-flight comfort for longer flights if cost difference is small.
  • Visa assistance or bundled ticket+visa packages, which some carriers offer.
  • Frequent flyer benefits, if you collect miles.

For many travelers, the convenience of a nonstop flight with a reputable carrier outweighs a modest cost saving on a one-stop itinerary.

  • Budget direct routes to Sharjah via Air Arabia can be the lowest-cost option, but remember ground transfer times to Dubai.

On-the-Ground Logistics in Dubai

Money, Payments, and Tipping

The UAE dirham (AED) is used throughout Dubai. Credit and debit cards are widely accepted; carry a small amount of cash for taxis in case of short trips or for small purchases. ATMs are available at the airport and around the city.

Tipping is customary for good service: 10â15% in restaurants where a service charge is not already included, and small tips for porters and taxi drivers.

Local mobile providers (Etisalat and du) sell tourist SIM cards at the airport and city kiosks. eSIMs are a convenient option for compatible phones and can be purchased pre-departure. Consider a data plan that covers maps and ride apps; many hotels now offer reliable Wi-Fi as well.

Practical Problems and How to Solve Them

What If Your Visa Is Delayed or Denied?

If processing extends beyond expected timelines, contact the service provider immediately. Many agents will escalate or advise on document gaps. If denied, secure written reasons and reapply only after addressing the documented concerns.

Missed Connection or Flight Disruption

For connecting flights, ask the airline about rebooking policies and accommodation if the delay is their responsibility. If you booked separate tickets, allow longer layovers to mitigate the risk of missed connections.

Lost Passport or Documents

Report lost passports immediately to local police in Dubai and contact the Kenyan embassy or consulate in the UAE. Keep digital copies of passport and visa to speed replacement processes.

FAQ

Q: Do Kenyan passport holders get a visa on arrival in Dubai? A: Generally, Kenyan passport holders need to obtain a UAE visa before travel. There are specific exceptions for holders of certain visas or residence permits from other countries; however, you should not assume eligibility. Apply through an airline, authorized visa provider, or the UAE e-visa channels, and confirm approval before travel.

Q: How long is the flight from Nairobi to Dubai? A: Nonstop flights typically take around 5 to 6 hours from Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (NBO) to Dubai International Airport (DXB). Flights to Sharjah have similar flight times but require additional ground transfer time to reach central Dubai.

Q: What is the best way to get from Dubai Airport to the city center? A: From DXB, the Dubai Metro is convenient and cost-effective if your accommodation is near a station. Taxis are metered and plentiful; ride-hailing apps provide another reliable option. From SHJ or AUH, plan for a longer road transfer time and use pre-arranged taxis or inter-emirate transport services.

Q: Can I use a tourist visa to work in Dubai? A: No. Tourist visas are not valid for employment. If you intend to work, secure the appropriate work permit and visa through an employer or official channels before starting any paid activity.
