Visa Options For Indian Passport Holders

Eligibility Overview and New Entry Options

Indian nationals have multiple paths to enter the UAE, depending on passport type, existing visas/residence permits with other countries, and the purpose and length of stay.

If you hold an Indian passport and also have a valid US visa, UK visa, Schengen visa, or a residence permit for certain countries (US, UK, EU residence cards from specific countries, Canada, Australia, Japan, New Zealand, Singapore, Republic of Korea, etc.), you may be eligible for a single-entry 14-day visa on arrival for a fee. This scheme simplifies short visits for many travelers but has clear eligibility criteria and a fee attached.

Otherwise, Indian travelers should generally secure a prearranged UAE tourist visa before departure. Airlines such as Emirates and certain visa providers allow online preapproval, and national visa processing centers and VFS offices in India handle many applications.

Types of Visit Visas (Practical Summary)

Tourist visas for Indian travelers come in several common forms. The most relevant for visitors from India are:

  • Longer 60- and 90-day tourist visas â suitable for extended stays, remote work stints, or multi-week itineraries.

Visa longevity, cost and extension rules differ between types. Extensions are possible in many categories but usually require a visit to immigration offices in the UAE and payment of additional fees. Keep in mind overstaying a visa results in penalties charged per day.

How to Apply: The Step-By-Step Process

  1. Decide the visa type that matches your travel dates and purpose.
  2. Confirm eligibility for visa-on-arrival (if you carry a qualifying third-country visa/residence).
  3. If applying in advance, choose a channel: airline-managed visa service (e.g., Emirates Manage Your Booking), a VFS Global center, the UAE Embassy/Consulate, or a licensed travel agency in India.
  4. Complete the form, upload required documents (passport copy, photo, confirmed return ticket, hotel booking or host invitation) and pay the visa fee.
