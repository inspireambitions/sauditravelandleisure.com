Frequently Asked Questions

Do U.S. passport holders need a visa before traveling to Dubai?

No. U.S. passport holders traveling for tourism typically receive a visit stamp on arrival valid for up to 90 days in a 180-day period. Ensure your passport is valid for six months beyond your planned entry date and carry proof of onward or return travel.

What are the best ways to travel between Dubai and Abu Dhabi?

The most common options are private car, taxi, or inter-emirate buses. Driving takes about 45â60 minutes depending on traffic. For a stress-free day trip, consider a guided transfer or pre-booked private shuttle that includes timed entry to Abu Dhabi attractions.

Is it safe to travel to Dubai as an American traveler?

Yes. Dubai is generally very safe for tourists, with low violent crime. Take normal travel precautions against petty theft, secure valuables, and respect local laws and cultural norms. Carry travel insurance and note consular contact information before departure.

What is the best time of year to visit Dubai?

The most comfortable weather is between November and March, when temperatures are mild. This is also peak season with higher prices and larger crowds. Shoulder months like October and April can be a good compromise for fewer crowds with still-pleasant weather.
