Top 14 Best Indian Restaurants in Al Khobar

2025-08-28

Looking for the best Indian restaurants in Al Khobar? You’re in for a treat! This vibrant city in Saudi Arabia is home to a wide range of Indian dining spots — from traditional North Indian curries to South Indian delicacies and modern fusion dishes. Whether you crave biryani, dosas, or tandoori grills, Al Khobar offers something for every palate.

In this guide, we’ll explore the Top 14 Indian restaurants in Al Khobar, highlighting their pros, cons, price range, and what they’re best known for.

Top 14 Indian Restaurants in Al Khobar

1. Copper Chandni

Pros: Authentic North Indian cuisine, elegant interiors
Cons: Can get busy on weekends
Price: SAR 70–120 per person
Best for: Family dinners and group gatherings

2. Mumtaz Restaurant

Pros: Famous for Hyderabadi biryani, generous portions
Cons: Limited vegetarian options
Price: SAR 50–90 per person
Best for: Biryani lovers

3. Maharaja by Vineet

Pros: Fine dining experience, celebrity chef curated menu
Cons: On the pricier side
Price: SAR 150–250 per person
Best for: Luxury dining and special occasions

4. Kerala Express

Pros: Authentic Kerala meals, great seafood options
Cons: Service can be slow at peak hours
Price: SAR 40–80 per person
Best for: South Indian cuisine fans

5. Taste of India

Pros: Cozy ambiance, good mix of veg and non-veg dishes
Cons: Small seating capacity
Price: SAR 60–100 per person
Best for: Casual dining with friends

6. Indian Summer Restaurant

Pros: Excellent curries and kebabs, stylish interiors
Cons: Parking can be an issue
Price: SAR 70–130 per person
Best for: Couples and business dinners

7. Gazebo Restaurant

Pros: Mughlai specialties, family-friendly environment
Cons: A bit crowded during weekends
Price: SAR 80–140 per person
Best for: Traditional North Indian flavors

8. Delhi Darbar

Pros: Affordable, authentic street-style food
Cons: Basic interiors
Price: SAR 30–70 per person
Best for: Quick budget-friendly meals

9. Malabar Restaurant

Pros: Specializes in Malabari and seafood dishes
Cons: Menu limited to Kerala-style cuisine
Price: SAR 40–90 per person
Best for: South Indian seafood lovers

10. Tandoori Hut

Pros: Great tandoori grills and naan bread
Cons: Not many dessert options
Price: SAR 50–100 per person
Best for: Meat and grill lovers

11. Curry Leaves

Pros: Balanced menu with veg and non-veg options
Cons: Décor is simple
Price: SAR 50–90 per person
Best for: Everyday Indian meals

12. Spicy Village

Pros: Wide variety of spicy curries, casual vibe
Cons: Not ideal for people preferring mild flavors
Price: SAR 40–80 per person
Best for: Spice lovers

13. Paradise Biryani Point

Pros: Hyderabadi biryani specialty, takeaway option
Cons: Not much variety outside biryani
Price: SAR 40–70 per person
Best for: Quick biryani fix

14. Chatori Galli

Pros: Street-food style Indian snacks, fun vibe
Cons: Limited main course options
Price: SAR 20–50 per person
Best for: Indian street food lovers
