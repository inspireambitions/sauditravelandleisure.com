Top 14 Best Indian Restaurants in Jazan (2025 Foodie Guide)

Updated August 30, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Jazan is a city where tradition meets the sea.
While Arabic cuisine dominates, Indian restaurants in Jazan bring bold spices, biryanis, and tandoori flavors that locals and expats both love.
If you are craving rich curries, fluffy naan, or a budget thali, this list will guide you to the 14 best Indian restaurants in Jazan — covering budget eats, fine dining, vegetarian-friendly spots, and family restaurants.

Where can I find the best Indian restaurants in Jazan?

1. Curry King – A Local Favorite

Curry King is the go-to for classic Indian curries.
The restaurant is popular among families for its affordable menu and authentic flavors.
Must try: Butter chicken, dal tadka, garlic naan.

  • Price: SAR 40–70
  • Best for: Families and casual dinners
  • Why visit? Consistent taste, homely vibe

2. Hyderabad House – The Biryani Specialist

If you want the most authentic Hyderabadi biryani in Jazan, this is the place.
The restaurant also offers Mughlai curries with rich spices.
Must try: Chicken biryani, mutton korma.

  • Price: SAR 60–100
  • Best for: Biryani lovers, spice enthusiasts
  • Why visit? Aromatic rice, big portions

3. Royal Masala – Elegant North Indian Dining

Royal Masala is known for North Indian gravies and fine dining.
It’s one of the few places in Jazan where you can enjoy Indian food in an elegant setting.
Must try: Paneer butter masala, naan basket.

  • Price: SAR 70–100
  • Best for: Couples, special occasions
  • Why visit? Ambience + authentic North Indian taste

4. Bombay Bites – Street Food Meets Comfort

Bombay Bites brings the taste of Indian street food to Jazan.
It’s especially loved by vegetarians for its snacks and light meals.
Must try: Samosas, chaat, pav bhaji.

  • Price: SAR 30–60
  • Best for: Vegetarians, quick bites
  • Why visit? Affordable, fun street-style food

5. Maharaja’s Table – Fine Dining With a Royal Touch

If you are looking for a luxury Indian dining experience in Jazan, Maharaja’s Table delivers.
Rich interiors, candle-lit ambiance, and premium dishes make it unique.
Must try: Tandoori lamb chops, prawn masala.

  • Price: SAR 100–150
  • Best for: Date nights, corporate dinners
  • Why visit? Premium service + authentic Indian flavors

6. Tandoori Junction – Clay Oven Goodness

Known for its freshly baked naan and smoky kebabs, Tandoori Junction is a hit for group dining.
The platters are generous, making it ideal for sharing.
Must try: Chicken tikka, seekh kebabs, butter naan.

  • Price: SAR 50–90
  • Best for: Families and groups
  • Why visit? Perfect tandoori experience

7. Zaika Restaurant – North & South Indian Mix

Zaika combines South Indian dosas with North Indian curries.
It’s a balanced spot for those who want variety.
Must try: Masala dosa, chicken curry, idli sambar.

  • Price: SAR 40–70
  • Best for: Mixed groups, South Indian food lovers
  • Why visit? Diverse menu

8. Masala Darbar – Family-Friendly Comfort

Masala Darbar is simple yet reliable, offering homestyle curries and kid-friendly dishes.
Must try: Dal fry, chicken curry, roti.

  • Price: SAR 40–80
  • Best for: Families with kids
  • Why visit? Affordable, comfortable dining

9. Indian Zaafran – Cozy & Flavorful

Indian Zaafran is a small but cozy spot known for consistent gravies.
Must try: Butter chicken, palak paneer.

  • Price: SAR 50–80
  • Best for: Couples and small groups
  • Why visit? Warm service, tasty gravies

10. Bombay Spice – Seafood & Curries

If you want Indian seafood, Bombay Spice is your place.
Goan-style curries and fresh fish make it stand out.
Must try: Fish curry, prawn masala.

  • Price: SAR 60–90
  • Best for: Seafood lovers
  • Why visit? Rare Indian seafood in Jazan

11. Rajdhani Restaurant – Unlimited Thalis

Rajdhani is famous for unlimited thali meals.
It offers a true Indian dining experience with multiple curries and sides.
Must try: Vegetarian thali.

  • Price: SAR 40–60
  • Best for: Thali lovers, large appetites
  • Why visit? Best value Indian food

12. Taste of India – Balanced Menu

Taste of India appeals to all taste buds, with both spicy and mild dishes.
Must try: Chicken korma, veg pulao.

  • Price: SAR 40–70
  • Best for: Mixed groups
  • Why visit? Good for beginners to Indian food

13. Indian Fusion – Modern & Creative

Indian Fusion blends Indian classics with modern twists.
Dishes are creative, and presentation is Instagram-worthy.
Must try: Fusion curries, Indo-Arab kebabs.

  • Price: SAR 70–100
  • Best for: Trendy diners
  • Why visit? Creative plating, unique recipes

14. Spice Villa – Live Grill Experience

Spice Villa is a lively spot with open kitchen grills.
Perfect for groups who enjoy live cooking.
Must try: Tandoori chicken, kebab platter.

  • Price: SAR 50–80
  • Best for: Friends & gatherings
  • Why visit? Grilled food + vibrant vibe

FAQ – People Also Ask

Which Indian restaurant in Jazan is best for families?

Masala Darbar and Tandoori Junction are top choices with family seating.

Where can I find the best biryani in Jazan?

Hyderabad House is the most popular for authentic Hyderabadi biryani.

What are the most affordable Indian restaurants in Jazan?

Curry King and Rajdhani Restaurant are budget-friendly picks.

Which Indian restaurants offer vegetarian meals?

Bombay Bites and Rajdhani Restaurant are great for vegetarians.

Are there fine dining Indian restaurants in Jazan?

Yes—Maharaja’s Table is perfect for luxury dining.

Final Words

From street food snacks at Bombay Bites to premium dining at Maharaja’s Table, Jazan has Indian restaurants for every mood.
Whether you want a spicy biryani, a South Indian dosa, or a family thali, this list covers it all.
Explore one today and enjoy the vibrant taste of India in Jazan.
