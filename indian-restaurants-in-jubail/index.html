Top 14 Best Indian Restaurants in Jubail (2025 Updated)

2025-08-29

Jubail, one of Saudi Arabia’s busiest industrial cities, is also a melting pot of cultures — and Indian cuisine shines brightly here. From rich curries to sizzling tandoori grills, you’ll find flavors that remind you of Delhi, Mumbai, Hyderabad, and beyond.

This guide highlights the Top 14 Best Indian Restaurants in Jubail, carefully curated based on food quality, authenticity, service, price, and overall dining experience. Whether you’re craving butter chicken, spicy biryani, or vegetarian delights, Jubail has something for every taste.

Top 14 Indian Restaurants in Jubail

1. Spice of India

Pros:

  • Authentic curries with traditional spices.
  • Elegant ambiance with family-friendly seating.
  • Excellent service and staff recommendations.

Cons:

  • Slightly expensive compared to casual restaurants.
  • Gets crowded on weekends—reservation suggested.

Price: Around 300–400 SAR per person for a full meal.

Best For: Family gatherings and those seeking authentic North Indian cuisine. Don’t miss their gulab jamun for dessert.

2. Tandoori Flame

Pros:

  • Wide variety of kebabs and grilled meats.
  • Freshly baked naan from traditional clay ovens.
  • Spacious dining with modern interiors.

Cons:

  • Spice levels can be high for some.
  • Service gets slow during peak hours.

Price: 250–350 SAR per person.

Best For: Meat lovers and groups who enjoy tandoori platters.

3. Hyderabad House

Pros:

  • Famous for Hyderabadi biryani with authentic flavors.
  • Large menu with both veg and non-veg options.
  • Generous portions.

Cons:

  • Ambiance is simple, not luxury.
  • Heavy crowd on Fridays.

Price: 200–300 SAR per person.

Best For: Biryani lovers looking for an authentic Hyderabad taste.

4. Curry Corner

Pros:

  • Budget-friendly Indian meals.
  • Simple homestyle cooking.
  • Quick service and takeout available.

Cons:

  • Limited seating area.
  • Menu is small compared to bigger restaurants.

Price: 80–120 SAR per person.

Best For: Students, workers, and those who want affordable everyday Indian food.

5. Biryani Hut

Pros:

  • Specializes in aromatic biryanis.
  • Good portion sizes.
  • Delivery service available.

Cons:

  • Not ideal for dine-in (small space).
  • Limited vegetarian options.

Price: 150–250 SAR per person.

Best For: Takeaway and delivery—perfect for a biryani night at home.

6. Urban Veg Bites

Pros:

  • 100% vegetarian menu.
  • South and North Indian classics like dosa, thali, and paneer curry.
  • Calm, family-friendly atmosphere.

Cons:

  • Not suitable for meat lovers.
  • Service speed can vary.

Price: 150–220 SAR per person.

Best For: Vegetarians and families looking for healthy Indian dining.

7. Delhi Darbar

Pros:

  • Traditional North Indian curries and bread.
  • Cozy interiors with Indian décor.
  • Good balance of price and quality.

Cons:

  • Parking can be difficult.
  • Some dishes are oily.

Price: 200–300 SAR per person.

Best For: Couples and small families looking for classic Indian dishes.

8. Green Curry Palace

Pros:

  • Mix of Indian and Indo-Chinese dishes.
  • Wide vegetarian variety.
  • Freshly prepared curries with bold flavors.

Cons:

  • Indo-Chinese may not suit everyone’s palate.
  • Waiting time during rush hours.

Price: 180–250 SAR per person.

Best For: Foodies who love trying fusion Indian dishes.

9. Masala Junction

Pros:

  • Modern presentation of Indian dishes.
  • Excellent seafood curries.
  • Comfortable interiors.

Cons:

  • Prices slightly higher than average.
  • Menu less extensive than competitors.

Price: 250–350 SAR per person.

Best For: Seafood lovers and those looking for a premium dining experience.

10. Quick Bites Dosa

Pros:

  • South Indian snacks like dosa, idli, and vada.
  • Fast service for quick meals.
  • Affordable pricing.

Cons:

  • Small restaurant with limited seating.
  • Not ideal for fine dining.

Price: 70–120 SAR per person.

Best For: South Indian food lovers and quick lunch breaks.

11. Maharaja Palace

Pros:

  • Royal-style interior with traditional touches.
  • Wide North Indian and Mughlai menu.
  • Perfect for large gatherings.

Cons:

  • Expensive compared to others.
  • Takes time to prepare food.

Price: 350–450 SAR per person.

Best For: Weddings, corporate dinners, and celebrations.

12. Zaika Express

Pros:

  • Balanced menu with both veg and non-veg options.
  • Affordable combo meals.
  • Great for takeout.

Cons:

  • Ambiance is average.
  • Some dishes are mild in flavor.

Price: 120–180 SAR per person.

Best For: Budget-conscious families and office workers.

13. Punjab Grill

Pros:

  • Rich Punjabi-style curries.
  • Grilled kebabs and chicken tikka.
  • Comfortable dining atmosphere.

Cons:

  • Portions slightly small for the price.
  • Parking challenges.

Price: 250–300 SAR per person.

Best For: Punjabi food lovers craving butter chicken and dal makhani.

14. Royal Tandoor

Pros:

  • Signature kebabs and spicy curries.
  • Elegant interiors with family sections.
  • Excellent naan and bread variety.

Cons:

  • Expensive compared to similar options.
  • Service can be inconsistent.

Price: 300–400 SAR per person.

Best For: Family dinners and business meetups.

FAQs About Indian Restaurants in Jubail

What’s the most authentic Indian restaurant in Jubail?

Spice of India and Delhi Darbar are known for authentic North Indian flavors, while Hyderabad House is best for biryani lovers.

Which Indian restaurant is best for vegetarians?

Urban Veg Bites and Green Curry Palace are top choices with extensive vegetarian menus.

Are there any affordable Indian restaurants in Jubail?

Yes, Curry Corner and Quick Bites Dosa offer budget-friendly meals under 100 SAR per person.

Which restaurant serves the best biryani in Jubail?

Hyderabad House and Biryani Hut are crowd favorites for authentic biryani.

Do these restaurants offer delivery?

Most of them provide delivery through food apps, especially Biryani Hut, Zaika Express, and Curry Corner.

Final Words

Jubail’s Indian food scene is vibrant and diverse — from street-style dosas to luxurious Mughlai feasts, you’ll find a restaurant that suits your budget and taste. Whether you’re looking for a quick lunch or a royal dinner, these Top 14 Best Indian Restaurants in Jubail are worth trying.
