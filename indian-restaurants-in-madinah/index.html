Top 14 Best Indian Restaurants in Madinah (2025 Updated)

Updated August 28, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Madinah is a city known for its spiritual significance and cultural diversity, and its food scene reflects just that.

From aromatic biryanis to sizzling kebabs, Indian restaurants in Madinah serve a taste of home for South Asian pilgrims, expats, and food lovers alike.

Whether you’re looking for budget-friendly curries near the Haram or fine-dining with family, here’s a hand-picked list of the 14 best Indian restaurants in Madinah—complete with pros, cons, price ranges, and signature dishes.

How to Choose the Right Indian Restaurant in Madinah

Authenticity & Taste

Look for restaurants known for traditional spices, tandoori items, and fresh curries.

Location & Accessibility

Many travelers prefer restaurants within walking distance of the Prophet’s Mosque.

Price Range & Value

Expect meals to range between SAR 15–40 per person depending on the style (casual vs fine dining).

Ambience & Service

Families may prefer sit-down restaurants, while solo travelers or groups may enjoy fast casual spots.

Variety of Dishes

Vegetarian options, South Indian delicacies, and late-night service can make a big difference,

Top 14 Indian Restaurants in Madinah

1. Al Baik Tandoori House

Quick Answer: Famous for its charcoal-grilled kebabs and butter chicken, this restaurant offers affordable meals close to the Haram.

  • Pros: Authentic taste, generous portions, budget-friendly
  • Cons: Often crowded during prayer times
  • Price Range: SAR 15–25 per person
  • Best For: Pilgrims looking for quick Indian meals after prayers
  • Signature Dish: Tandoori Chicken with Garlic Naan

2. Curry Garden Restaurant

Quick Answer: A favorite among expats, known for vegetarian curries and North Indian thalis.

  • Pros: Wide vegetarian options, calm ambience
  • Cons: Slightly higher prices compared to casual eateries
  • Price Range: SAR 20–35
  • Best For: Families and vegetarian diners
  • Signature Dish: Paneer Butter Masala with Jeera Rice

3. Shahzaib Biryani Center

Quick Answer: If you crave biryani, Shahzaib is the go-to spot, serving large, flavorful portions at pocket-friendly prices.

  • Pros: Authentic Hyderabadi biryani, generous servings
  • Cons: Limited menu beyond biryani
  • Price Range: SAR 12–20
  • Best For: Budget-friendly quick meals
  • Signature Dish: Hyderabadi Chicken Biryani

4. Zaika Indian Cuisine

Quick Answer: A fine-dining choice offering Mughlai curries, kebabs, and fusion dishes.

  • Pros: Elegant ambience, wide menu, good for groups
  • Cons: Higher price point
  • Price Range: SAR 30–45
  • Best For: Family dinners and celebrations
  • Signature Dish: Mutton Rogan Josh

5. Spice Hub Madinah

Quick Answer: Popular for South Indian dishes like dosa, idli, and sambar—hard to find elsewhere in Madinah.

  • Pros: Authentic South Indian flavors, affordable
  • Cons: Small dining space
  • Price Range: SAR 15–25
  • Best For: Vegetarian meals and light snacks
  • Signature Dish: Masala Dosa with Coconut Chutney

6. Tandoori Flame Restaurant

Quick Answer: Great for tandoori grills and hearty curries.

  • Pros: Fresh naan, flavorful chicken dishes
  • Cons: Service can be slow during rush hours
  • Price Range: SAR 20–35
  • Best For: Dinner with friends
  • Signature Dish: Chicken Tikka Masala

7. Taste of India

Quick Answer: Offers a balanced mix of North and South Indian cuisines.

  • Pros: Menu variety, family-friendly atmosphere
  • Cons: Moderate waiting times
  • Price Range: SAR 20–35
  • Best For: Mixed groups with varied tastes
  • Signature Dish: Butter Chicken with Garlic Naan

8. Mughal Darbar

Quick Answer: Known for royal Mughlai curries and kebabs, giving a “feast-like” experience.

  • Pros: Authentic Mughlai recipes, rich gravies
  • Cons: Prices slightly high
  • Price Range: SAR 30–40
  • Best For: Dinner parties and groups
  • Signature Dish: Chicken Korma

9. Indian Zaika Express

Quick Answer: A fast-casual restaurant serving affordable curries and chapati.

  • Pros: Quick service, budget-friendly
  • Cons: Limited seating space
  • Price Range: SAR 10–20
  • Best For: Solo diners and quick meals
  • Signature Dish: Dal Tadka with Roti

10. Swaad Restaurant

Quick Answer: Offers authentic home-style Indian food, popular among locals and expats.

  • Pros: Homely flavors, friendly staff
  • Cons: Not ideal for large groups
  • Price Range: SAR 15–25
  • Best For: Comfort food cravings
  • Signature Dish: Rajma Chawal

11. Indian Palace Restaurant

Quick Answer: A premium dining spot offering lavish thalis and multi-course meals.

  • Pros: Elegant setup, variety of dishes
  • Cons: Higher than average cost
  • Price Range: SAR 35–50
  • Best For: Special occasions
  • Signature Dish: Royal Veg Thali

12. Deccan Spice

Quick Answer: Perfect for spicy Andhra-style and Hyderabadi dishes.

  • Pros: Spicy authentic recipes, good quantity
  • Cons: Too spicy for some diners
  • Price Range: SAR 20–30
  • Best For: Spicy food lovers
  • Signature Dish: Andhra Chicken Curry

13. Royal Tandoor

Quick Answer: Blends traditional tandoor cooking with modern dining style.

  • Pros: Freshly baked naan, good meat dishes
  • Cons: Long waiting times during peak hours
  • Price Range: SAR 25–35
  • Best For: Evening meals with family
  • Signature Dish: Lamb Seekh Kebab

14. Punjab Darbar

Quick Answer: A casual eatery loved for Punjabi curries and lassi.

  • Pros: Rich flavors, generous portions, affordable
  • Cons: Simple ambience
  • Price Range: SAR 15–25
  • Best For: Quick family dinners
  • Signature Dish: Butter Paneer Curry

Frequently Asked Questions

Which Indian restaurant is closest to the Prophet’s Mosque?

Al Baik Tandoori House is located just a short walk from the Haram, making it convenient for pilgrims.

Where can I find vegetarian Indian food in Madinah?

Curry Garden and Spice Hub offer extensive vegetarian options, including paneer curries, dosas, and lentil dishes.

What’s the average cost of eating at an Indian restaurant in Madinah?

Expect to pay SAR 15–25 per person at casual eateries and SAR 30–50 at fine-dining spots.

Which restaurant serves the best biryani in Madinah?

Shahzaib Biryani Center is highly rated for its Hyderabadi-style chicken biryani at budget-friendly prices.

Final Words

Finding authentic Indian food in Madinah is easier than ever, with options for every budget and taste. Whether you’re near the Haram seeking quick meals, or looking for a family dining experience, the restaurants listed above offer the best of Indian cuisine in the holy city.
