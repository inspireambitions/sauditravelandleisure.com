Top 14 Best Indian Restaurants in Taif

Updated August 29, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Taif, known as the “City of Roses,” is not only famous for its natural beauty but also for its growing food culture. Among the many international cuisines here, Indian food holds a special place. From aromatic biryanis to spicy curries and crispy dosas, Taif has some excellent restaurants to satisfy your cravings.

In this guide, we’ll highlight the 14 best Indian restaurants in Taif, along with their pros, cons, price range, and best recommendations so you know exactly where to go.

Top 14 Indian Restaurants in Taif

1. Albaik Indian Flavors

Pros: Known for flavorful curries, family-friendly atmosphere
Cons: Gets crowded in the evenings
Price: SAR 40–90 per person
Best for: Family dinners

2. Mumtaz Mahal Restaurant

Pros: Great biryani selection, good service
Cons: Limited vegetarian options
Price: SAR 50–100 per person
Best for: Biryani and kebab lovers

3. Kerala Spice Taif

Pros: Authentic South Indian meals, tasty seafood
Cons: Décor is very simple
Price: SAR 40–80 per person
Best for: South Indian food fans

4. Curry House Restaurant

Pros: Wide variety of North Indian curries, naan bread is excellent
Cons: Parking space is limited
Price: SAR 60–110 per person
Best for: Traditional North Indian dining

5. Hyderabad House Taif

Pros: Specializes in Hyderabadi biryani, affordable
Cons: Seating area is small
Price: SAR 35–70 per person
Best for: Quick biryani fix

6. Tandoori Nights

Pros: Juicy tandoori chicken and kebabs, warm ambiance
Cons: Can be heavy on the spice
Price: SAR 60–120 per person
Best for: Grilled meat lovers

7. Indian Summer Express

Pros: Stylish interiors, wide menu variety
Cons: Prices are a bit higher
Price: SAR 80–140 per person
Best for: Couples and special dinners

8. Taj Palace Restaurant

Pros: Great mix of vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes
Cons: Service can be slow during peak hours
Price: SAR 50–100 per person
Best for: Group dining

9. Malabar Village

Pros: Kerala-style dishes, fish curry is highly recommended
Cons: Limited desserts
Price: SAR 40–90 per person
Best for: Seafood and South Indian meals

10. Chatori Zaika

Pros: Street-food style snacks, fun vibe
Cons: Small seating space
Price: SAR 20–50 per person
Best for: Street food lovers

11. Zaika Biryani Point

Pros: Authentic Hyderabadi biryani, good portion size
Cons: Focused mostly on biryani only
Price: SAR 35–70 per person
Best for: Biryani enthusiasts

12. Maharaja Restaurant

Pros: Elegant dining, excellent curries
Cons: Slightly expensive
Price: SAR 100–180 per person
Best for: Luxury dining and occasions

13. Delhi Darbar Taif

Pros: Affordable, authentic Indian street-style meals
Cons: Décor is basic
Price: SAR 30–60 per person
Best for: Budget-friendly dining

14. Spice Village Restaurant

Pros: Balanced mix of North and South Indian cuisine
Cons: Limited beverage options
Price: SAR 50–90 per person
Best for: Everyday Indian meals

Taif’s Indian dining scene is a blend of traditional flavors and modern experiences. From budget-friendly biryani spots like Hyderabad House to fine dining at Maharaja Restaurant, there’s something for every taste and occasion.
