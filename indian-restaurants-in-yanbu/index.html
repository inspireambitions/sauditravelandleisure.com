14 Top Best Indian Restaurants in Yanbu

Updated August 29, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Yanbu Al-Bahr (also known simply as Yanbu) is a vibrant Red Sea city blending rich Middle Eastern flavor with a hub of expat communities—including a passionate Indian dining scene.

Whether you're a craving for classic biryani, crispy dosa, or aromatic curries, Yanbu has an option for every palette.

This guide features the 14 best Indian restaurants in Yanbu, selected based on local popularity, guest reviews, authenticity, menu variety, and overall experience—bringing you the best of both authenticity and value

Top 14  Indian Restaurants in Yanbu

1. Sultan Grill

Pros:

  • Expertly cooked tandoori meats and biryanis.
  • Comfortable, welcoming interior suitable for families.
  • Friendly staff providing menu guidance.

Cons:

  • Slightly higher price point.
  • Crowded during weekends; reservations recommended.

Price: Approx. 300–400 SAR per person

Best For: Fans of rich, aromatic biryani and grilled Indian specialties.

2. The Ocean Restaurant

Pros:

  • Modern space with stylish décor.
  • Offers Indian and Indo-Chinese dishes.
  • Fresh ingredients and well-presented meals.

Cons:

  • Slightly expensive.
  • Menu may be overwhelming for first-time visitors.

Price: 250–350 SAR per person

Best For: Families or groups who enjoy variety in one dining experience.

3. Zafran Restaurant

Pros:

  • Multi-cuisine menu featuring Indian, Pakistani, and European dishes.
  • Delicious desserts and fresh juices.
  • Ideal for small gatherings.

Cons:

  • Not exclusively Indian.
  • Limited seating for large groups.

Price: 200–300 SAR per person

Best For: Those looking for an elegant dining experience with a variety of flavors.

4. Aryaas India Restaurant – Yanbu

Pros:

  • Authentic Indian flavors with generous portion sizes.
  • Wide selection of vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes.
  • Specialty desserts that leave a lasting impression.

Cons:

  • Interior is simple and casual.
  • Can get busy during peak hours.

Price: 200–300 SAR per person

Best For: Traditional Indian cuisine enthusiasts seeking hearty meals.

5. Cumin – Indian Cuisine Restaurant

Pros:

  • Broad selection of North and South Indian dishes.
  • Tandoori specialties and freshly baked breads.
  • Consistently flavorful curries.

Cons:

  • Limited information about ambiance.
  • Can be busy during lunch hours.

Price: 200–300 SAR per person

Best For: Lovers of tandoori and regional Indian specialties.

6. Shaheen Restaurant

Pros:

  • Homestyle Indian and Pakistani dishes.
  • Affordable prices and generous servings.
  • Family-friendly atmosphere.

Cons:

  • Simple interior décor.
  • Service can be slow at times.

Price: 100–180 SAR per person

Best For: Comfort food seekers and budget-conscious diners.

7. Indese Restaurant

Pros:

  • Authentic spices and hearty portions.
  • Warm, friendly service.
  • Popular with locals and expats alike.

Cons:

  • Limited menu for vegetarians.
  • Smaller dining space.

Price: 150–250 SAR per person

Best For: Groups and families wanting authentic Indian flavors.

8. Yanbu Restaurant

Pros:

  • Specializes in South Indian dishes such as dosa and egg curry.
  • Clean, affordable, and family-friendly.
  • Fresh ingredients with bold flavors.

Cons:

  • Vegetarian variety is limited.
  • Casual setting, not ideal for fine dining.

Price: 80–150 SAR per person

Best For: South Indian cuisine lovers and casual breakfasts.

9. Pakistani Hotel

Pros:

  • Large menu with hearty meals.
  • Budget-friendly pricing.
  • Quick service for takeout.

Cons:

  • Ambiance is very basic.
  • Some dishes may be mild for spice lovers.

Price: 80–150 SAR per person

Best For: Budget-conscious diners looking for filling meals.

10. Relax Restaurant Yanbu al Bahar

Pros:

  • Combines Gulf and Indian flavors creatively.
  • Affordable prices and friendly staff.
  • Cozy, welcoming atmosphere.

Cons:

  • Fusion dishes may not appeal to purists.
  • Limited parking space.

Price: 120–200 SAR per person

Best For: Diners seeking a mix of Indian and Gulf-inspired cuisine.

11. Sham Judodna (Cham Jedoudna)

Pros:

  • Stylish interiors and pleasant dining experience.
  • Menu includes biryanis and Indian-influenced dishes.
  • Family-friendly seating.

Cons:

  • More Levant-inspired than purely Indian.
  • Menu is limited compared to other Indian restaurants.

Price: 150–250 SAR per person

Best For: Cultural dining experiences blending Indian and Middle Eastern flavors.

12. Sultan Al Shawaya (Sultan Grill)

Already highlighted in #1; well-loved for its grill and biryani specialties.

13. Additional Local Favorites

Many locals enjoy smaller downtown takeaways serving home-style Indian dishes like samosas, masala chai, and fried rice. These places often have no formal name but are valued for taste and affordability.

14. Café & Takeaway Indian Options

Off-the-menu Indian meals in waterfront cafés, especially Indian-style wraps, kebabs, and snacks, are popular among expats.

Best For: Quick bites and casual meals on the go.

FAQs About Indian Restaurants in Yanbu

Which restaurant serves the best authentic biryani in Yanbu?

Aryaas India Restaurant and Sultan Grill are highly recommended for flavor-packed, authentic biryani.

Where can I enjoy South Indian dishes like dosa or Kerala-style curry?

Yanbu Restaurant is the go-to spot for Kerala specialties and South Indian breakfast dishes.

Are there budget-friendly Indian restaurants in Yanbu?

Yes—Shaheen Restaurant, Pakistani Hotel, and Yanbu Restaurant offer affordable meals under 150 SAR.

Do any restaurants provide homestyle Indian meals?

Shaheen and Indese Restaurant are known for hearty, home-style dishes with generous portions.

Are there vegetarian-friendly Indian options?

Cumin and Aryaas India Restaurant provide a wide range of vegetarian dishes, including paneer curries and vegetable biryanis.

Final Words

Yanbu’s Indian cuisine scene is diverse and flavorful, ranging from traditional South Indian meals to aromatic North Indian curries and biryanis. Whether you want a casual bite, a family dinner, or a refined dining experience, these 14 Indian restaurants in Yanbu offer something for everyone.

Explore, taste, and savor the variety—your tastebuds will thank you!
