Is Alcohol Available in Riyadh?

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Introduction

Riyadh is attracting more international visitors every year as Saudi Arabia opens up its tourism and cultural offerings. Curious travelers commonly ask whether alcohol is available in the Saudi capital—it's one of the questions that shapes how people plan dinners, business meetings, and longer stays. Getting a clear, practical answer is essential if you want to travel to Riyadh with confidence and respect local laws.

Short answer: Alcohol is not generally available to the public in Riyadh. Limited, tightly controlled exceptions exist for diplomatic personnel and, under newly announced policies, a small number of licensed venues in specific high-end zones will be allowed to serve or sell certain alcoholic drinks beginning under a phased program. For most visitors and residents, possession, consumption, or public intoxication remain illegal and carry serious consequences.

This article explains the current legal framework, recent policy changes, where alcohol may be available under strict conditions, how enforcement works, and—most importantly—how you should plan your visit to Riyadh to avoid legal or cultural missteps. How Saudi Law Has Historically Treated Alcohol

A short historical context

Modern legal restrictions on alcohol in the Kingdom date back to the early 1950s, when public sale and consumption were outlawed. For decades that position defined daily life: public places, streets, and hotels operated under a prohibitionist model. Diplomatic missions historically used diplomatic consignments to bring limited quantities for official use. Enforcement was strict, and penalties for unauthorized possession or consumption could include fines, imprisonment, and deportation for foreigners.

Diplomatic exceptions and controlled imports

Even under prohibition, diplomatic channels created a parallel, regulated flow of alcohol into the Kingdom for embassy staff and accredited personnel. Diplomatic pouches and official consignments were the practical means for many missions to maintain limited supplies for events and internal use. That long-standing exception is the reason diplomatic personnel have historically had different practical access to alcohol than ordinary residents or tourists.

Recent Policy Shifts: What Changed and Why

Small, targeted adjustments in 2024–2026

Over recent years Saudi authorities have introduced incremental reforms tied to economic diversification and tourism ambitions. In 2024 a controlled off-license opened in Riyadh’s Diplomatic Quarter to serve non-Muslim diplomats under a strict registration and quota system. This move was framed by officials as a practical step to cut down illicit trade entering through diplomatic channels and to bring more transparency to existing diplomatic imports.

Following that, in 2025–2026 announcements outlined a phased licensing approach that would permit the sale and consumption of beer, wine and cider (but exclude spirits and beverages above roughly 20% alcohol by volume) in a limited set of controlled environments: luxury fiveâstar hotels and resorts, designated tourist hubs such as the Red Sea projects and Neom, licensed distributors, compounds for expatriates, and certain international cultural and business events.

Why the government is making measured changes

The shift is a calibrated attempt to balance economic goals with cultural sensitivities. Saudi Arabia’s Vision 2030 calls for a significant expansion of international tourism, hospitality, and major events infrastructure. Many national planners and hospitality stakeholders see limited, well-regulated alcohol availability as a way to make high-end tourism packages, MICE (meetings, incentives, conferences and exhibitions) events, and resort experiences competitive with neighboring Gulf destinations, while still maintaining broad respect for social norms.

What “Available” Means Today: Practical Reality in Riyadh

Where alcohol may be available under current rules

Today, the practical picture in Riyadh looks like this: ordinary hotels, restaurants and shops do not sell alcohol to the general public. The diplomatic shop in the Diplomatic Quarter provides authorized diplomatic personnel with limited access. Policy announcements about expanded licensing point to future availability in specified luxury hotels and tourist resorts, but rollout is gradual and will be accompanied by detailed regulations.

The following short list summarizes the locations where alcohol is currently or soon may be available under strict controls:

  • Diplomatic facilities and the diplomatic off-licence for accredited non-Muslim diplomats.
  • Designated fiveâstar hotels and luxury resorts when they become licensed under the phased program.
  • Specific tourism projects and resorts (e.g., the Red Sea developments, Neom resorts, Sindalah) once they implement licensing and operational controls.
  • Licensed venues within private, gated expatriate residential compounds where rules permit controlled consumption.

(That list is intended as a functional outline—regulatory details will vary by license and are subject to change as the government finalizes rollout.)

What types of alcoholic beverages are expected to be allowed

The announced approach focuses on beer, wine and cider, with an explicit exclusion in initial rollout plans for highâproof spirits and beverages above certain alcohol content thresholds. Authorities have framed the limits as a way to offer choices reasonable for international visitors while controlling public order and aligning with cultural expectations.

Important caveats for travelers and residents

Even in venues that are or will be licensed, access will not be open-ended. Expect the following:

  • Age limits will be enforced.
  • Production, domestic sale outside licensed areas, home-brewing, and drinking in public spaces will remain prohibited.
  • Strong identification and registration requirements will apply in places such as the diplomatic shop.
  • Regulations on transport, disposal, and bringing alcohol into the country will be strict; unauthorized importation remains illegal.

Enforcement, Penalties, and What to Avoid

How enforcement is carried out

Saudi law enforcement traditionally treats unauthorized possession and consumption of alcohol seriously. Enforcement techniques can include routine searches, inspections in private residences when there is reasonable cause, and checks at venues. Police responses prioritize public safety and order; public intoxication is treated particularly harshly because it is viewed as a direct challenge to social norms.

For foreigners, penalties commonly include fines, detention, and possible deportation depending on the circumstances and severity. Saudi authorities also retain discretion to apply other penalties codified in the criminal justice framework.

Typical penalties you should know

Penalties vary with the facts of each case, but the practical consequences to keep in mind are:

  • Confiscation of alcohol.
  • Monetary fines and potential jail time.
  • Deportation for foreign nationals in significant violations.
  • Official disciplinary action for employees or business owners who fail to abide by licensing rules.

Because outcomes can be unpredictable and sensitive, the safest course is strict compliance with law and local guidance.

What This Means for Non-Muslim Diplomats and Expatriates

Diplomatic access and quotas

Diplomats have historically had different practical access to alcohol due to the privileges of diplomatic consignments. The Diplomatic Quarter’s new off-licence formalizes and regulates this privilege: accredited nonâMuslim diplomats may purchase limited quantities with proof of status, and strict inventories and monthly quotas may be enforced as part of the registration process. These measures aim to eliminate illicit sales and create traceable, legal access for diplomatic missions.

Residential compounds and expatriate life

Designated expatriate compounds have traditionally contained their own rules regarding alcohol. Under the phased legalization approach, a small number of compounds may receive licenses to permit consumption within private compound boundaries. However, the policy framework will likely require explicit registration, defined service protocols, and strict enforcement to ensure compliance with national law.

If you live in Riyadh as an expatriate, check compound rules carefully and consult your employer or compound management before making assumptions.

For Businesses, Hoteliers and Event Organizers

Licensing expectations and operational controls

Businesses seeking to serve alcohol under the new licensing framework will need to follow rigid operational rules: staff training in responsible service, secure procurement and disposal procedures, controlled access to licensed areas, and detailed record keeping. Hotels and event organizers must align their practices with the licensing authority’s conditions, including age verification, limits on times and spaces for service, and possible caps on quantities.

Balancing customer expectations and cultural norms

International hotel chains and event planners operating in Riyadh will need to balance guest expectations with cultural sensitivity. Successful operators will design offerings that deliver international hospitality standards—quality food, beverage, and entertainment—while respecting Saudi regulations and traditions. That will mean investing in high-quality non-alcoholic beverage programs and explicit guest communication about what is and isn’t allowed on property.

Safety, Social Etiquette, and Digital Conduct

Avoiding risky behavior

Public intoxication is both illegal and socially unacceptable. Even if alcohol is available in a venue, behaving in a way that disturbs public order or disrespects local customs can lead to serious legal consequences. Maintain decorum, avoid loud public displays, and travel with a plan to return to your accommodation safely.

Social media and photos

Pictures and posts that glamorize alcohol consumption or public intoxication can have legal and reputational consequences in Saudi Arabia. Exercise caution with what you share online while in the Kingdom. When in doubt, err on the side of privacy and discretion.

Connecting Alcohol Policy To Broader Travel Choices in Saudi Arabia

Variations by destination within the Kingdom

Different cities and regions will experience policy changes at different paces. Coastal resort projects designed for international tourism are early candidates for licensing, while conservative religious cities maintain stricter standards for public life and worship-related spaces.

If your itinerary includes iconic sites beyond Riyadh, consider the regional context:

  • AlUla’s hospitality investments are designed around high-end international tourism; resort areas may incorporate licensed options under controlled guidelines.
  • Religious cities such as Makkah and Madinah retain strict regulations tied to pilgrimage and religious observance; travelers should not expect any alcohol availability in those holy precincts.

Balanced Analysis: Pros, Cons, and What to Expect Next

Potential benefits of limited, regulated availability

Limited legalization in controlled zones can boost international tourism appeal, attract large international events, and improve hospitality revenues. It also provides a legal route to eliminate illicit markets and enhances transparency and public safety through regulated sales and responsible service training.

Risks and social considerations

The government is navigating strong cultural sensitivities. Rapid changes risk provoking backlash among conservative segments of society. Enforcement complexity, implementation of licensing controls, and mixed messaging during rollout could create confusion for visitors and businesses. Authorities will likely continue to roll out measures slowly and with strong oversight.

Likely near-term scenario

Expect gradual expansion in defined, highâend tourist zones and licensed hotels, accompanied by clear rules restricting where and how alcohol may be purchased and consumed. Enforcement in public spaces will remain strict, and special national events (for example, major tournaments and religious observances) may be treated differently with temporary prohibitions.

FAQ

1) Can I drink alcohol in my hotel room in Riyadh if the hotel is licensed?

If the hotel is licensed in a designated program, consumption may be permitted only within clearly specified private areas of the property and under the rules established by the license. Always confirm written policy with the hotel beforehand. Do not assume private hotel rooms are an exception unless explicitly authorized.

Yes. Any licensing framework will include age verification measures and minimum age requirements for service. Venues will require official identification and may deny service if a guest cannot prove eligibility.
