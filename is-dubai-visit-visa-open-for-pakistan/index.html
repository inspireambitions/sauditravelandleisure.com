Introduction

Travel between South Asia and the Gulf remains one of the busiest corridors in global aviation, and many Pakistani travelers ask the same practical question before booking: is Dubai accepting visitors from Pakistan right now? The answer determines whether you can finalize flights, apply for visas, and plan your itinerary with confidence.

Short answer: Yes — Dubai is open to visitors from Pakistan, but Pakistani passport holders generally cannot obtain a visa on arrival and must secure a prearranged visit visa through an approved channel. That means applying before travel via the UAE embassy/consulate, an approved airline visa service, a licensed travel agent, or a UAE-based sponsor. Processing times, required documents, and sponsor rules vary by application route, so build time into your plan.

This article explains what "open" means in practice for Pakistani nationals, walks you through every application option, lists exact documents and timelines, explains airport arrival checks and common pitfalls, and gives a practical blueprint for planning a smooth trip to Dubai. Along the way I’ll point you to helpful regional travel resources and explain how to combine a UAE visit with travel in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia when you want a multi-country Gulf itinerary that runs smoothly.

What “Open” Means: Visa Policy Basics for Pakistani Nationals

Visa On Arrival vs. Prearranged Visa

Some nationalities receive a visa on arrival in the UAE — a quick stamp at immigration that removes the pre-travel visa step. Pakistan is not on that visa-on-arrival list. For Pakistani passport holders, "open" means the UAE will accept visitors, but you must secure permission before arriving.

The common prearranged routes are:

  • Applying through the UAE embassy or consulate in Pakistan.
  • Using an Emirates or Etihad online visa service linked to an airline booking.
  • Applying through VFS Global or an approved visa-processing partner.
  • Arranging a visa through a licensed travel agency or a UAE-based sponsor (family, friend, hotel, or corporate sponsor).

Why the distinction matters

A visa-on-arrival lets you book flight and land without a prior application. For Pakistanis, the prearranged requirement adds steps: document collection, payment of fees before travel, and sometimes sponsor guarantees. Planning time and having documentation ready are the practical consequences.

Are Visas Currently Being Issued To Pakistani Nationals?

Current operational reality

UAE immigration and the Emirates/Etihad airline visa services continue to process visitor visas for Pakistanis. The UAE embassy in Islamabad and the Consulate General in Karachi accept applications, and licensed travel agents in Pakistan can submit applications through approved channels. Airlines like Emirates provide a preapproved visa service for passengers holding their ticket. VFS Global also operates visa submission in many markets.

Processing continues unless an applicant’s individual background or documentation is incomplete. However, policy details — required documents, fees, processing times — change periodically. Always confirm requirements close to your travel date.

Practical implications for travel planning

Start your visa application at least 10–14 working days before departure to allow for processing and any additional document requests. For government officials or diplomatic passports, additional steps such as a note verbale from Pakistan’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs might be required. If a UAE-based sponsor is applying on your behalf, their local status and documents will be part of the approval process.

Which Visa Types Are Relevant For Pakistani Visitors?

Tourism and Short-Stay Visas

The common categories used by Pakistani visitors are:

  • Single-entry short-term tourist visas (30 days).
  • Single-entry long-term tourist visas (60 days).
  • Multiple-entry tourist visas (30 or 60 days) depending on eligibility.
  • Transit visas (48-hour or 96-hour) for layovers.
  • Visit visas for family or friends sponsored by a UAE resident.

Each visa type has a validity window before entry, a duration allowed after entry, and different extension rules. Fees differ by type and by the processing method chosen (embassy vs. airline service vs. travel agent).

Special categories and exceptions

Diplomatic and official passports have different procedures. Government officials may require a note verbale. Some travellers (for example, medical patients) may follow a health-specific entry procedure and should coordinate with the sponsor or medical facility.

How To Apply: Routes, Pros and Cons, and Practical Steps

Applying Through UAE Embassy or Consulate in Pakistan

Applying directly through the UAE embassy/consulate provides the most formal channel, and it’s often required for certain passport types and official visits.

  • Where to start: Book an appointment or check the consular instructions on the embassy’s website, and prepare physical documents as required.
  • Pros: Formal, appropriate for official purposes; clear record with consulate.
  • Cons: Physical submission, possibly longer turnaround, stricter documentation.

Typical embassy steps: complete a typed application, provide passport copy and photos, submit sponsor details where needed, pay the visa fee, and collect the visa once issued.

Applying Through Approved Airlines (Emirates, Etihad)

If you are flying with Emirates or Etihad, you can often secure a preapproved entry permit through the airline’s "manage your booking" service, provided your itinerary is on a qualifying ticket.

  • Pros: Convenient online path tied to your flight; streamlined for passengers; no need to visit a consulate in many cases.
  • Cons: Service may require the ticket number to meet airline-specific rules (e.g., Emirates requires EK ticket numbers), the stand-alone airline visa is limited to passengers flying with that carrier, and additional agency processing fees may apply.

Important operational detail: if you are applying via an airline, your itinerary often must include Emirates/Etihad flights for both inbound and outbound sectors, or the application might be ineligible.

Applying Through Licensed Travel Agencies or VFS Global

Licensed travel agents or visa-processing centers like VFS Global can submit applications on your behalf and handle the document logistics.

  • Pros: Helpful if you are unfamiliar with the process; agents often provide pre-checks and faster submission; accessible in many Pakistani cities.
  • Cons: Additional service fees; quality varies — use an accredited agency.

When using an agent, insist on a clear checklist and a receipt for the visa fee; verify that the agent will refund or rectify if a processing error occurs.

Sponsor-Based Applications (UAE Resident or Company)

A UAE resident, family member, or company can sponsor your visit visa. The sponsor submits the application and provides their ID and attestation documents.

  • Pros: Simple if you have a legitimate sponsor inside the UAE; common for family visits.
  • Cons: Sponsor accountability; refundable deposits may be required in some cases; sponsor must provide accurate contact and identity details.

This route is the standard for family visits, longer stays linked to employment connections, or when a UAE host covers the formalities.

Step-by-Step Application Checklist (Numbered List)

  1. Confirm passport validity: ensure your passport has at least six months' validity from the date of planned travel.
  2. Choose the correct visa type: decide between tourist 30/60-day, multiple-entry, transit, or sponsor-based visit visa depending on your purpose.
  3. Select your application route: embassy/consulate, airline, licensed travel agent, VFS Global, or UAE-based sponsor.
  4. Gather documents: passport copy, passport-size photographs (white background), hotel reservation or tenancy contract (if staying with family, provide relationship proof), confirmed return ticket, bank statements or proof of funds, and sponsor documents where applicable.
  5. Complete application form: use the typed embassy form if applying at the consulate, or the airline/agent online form where applicable.
  6. Pay fees: visa fees are non-refundable; where required, pay a refundable sponsor deposit or processing fee in advance.
  7. Submit and wait: typical processing ranges from 3–14 working days depending on the channel and visa type; track the application and respond promptly to document requests.
  8. Receive the visa: obtain the printed visa or digital permit and carry a physical copy during travel. Make sure the visa dates match your ticket.
  9. Arrive in Dubai: present the visa and passport at immigration; be prepared for random visual screening and to show printed documents if requested.

Required Documents — Clear, Practical List

  • Valid passport with minimum six months validity and at least two blank pages.
  • Recent passport-size photo on a white background (as per specifications).
  • Color copy of the passport bio-data page.
  • Confirmed return or onward airline ticket.
  • Confirmed hotel booking for the duration of the stay or tenancy contract if staying with family.
  • Proof of funds or a valid credit card (some immigration desks request evidence of sufficient funds—having at least AED 3,000 in cash or equivalent is a commonly cited rule of thumb).
  • Sponsor details if applicable: Emirates ID copy, residency visa copy, tenancy contract, or letter from employer.
  • For minors traveling alone or with one parent: notarized no-objection certificate or guardian affidavit, attested documents as per consular guidance.
  • For government officials: a recent note verbale from Pakistan’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs where required.

Many applicants will also be asked to provide additional documents on a case-by-case basis. If you’re applying through an airline or agent, they will advise on any extra paperwork necessary for your particular profile.

Processing Times, Fees, and Refunds

Typical processing windows

  • Airline-linked preapproved permits: often 3–4 working days but can be faster depending on ticketing and the airline’s internal queue.
  • Embassy/consulate applications: generally allow 7–14 working days depending on caseload and document checks.
  • Travel agents and VFS Global: variable, often similar to airlines or embassies but may add a day for submission handling.

Always allow buffer time. If the visa is tied to an Emirates/Etihad ticket, the ticket number may determine eligibility and timing.

Fees and deposits

Visa charges vary by type (48-hour transit, 30-day tourist, 60-day tourist, single or multiple entry) and by processing route. In some sponsorship cases, a refundable deposit (for example, AED 1,000) may be required; sponsors must claim refunds within the stipulated timeframe. Visa fees are typically non-refundable if the visa is rejected.

Confirm the exact fee with the embassy, airline, or agent before payment and keep your payment receipts.

Arrival Procedures at Dubai Airports

Immigration checks and random screening

Dubai Immigration maintains routine security checks and has introduced random visual screening for some arrivals. If selected, you may be asked to present a printed copy of your visa or visa number confirmation page. Carrying a hard copy of your visa is wise; if you lack one, a small fee may be charged for a printed copy at the airport counter.

What immigration officers may ask

Officers commonly ask for proof of accommodation, return ticket, purpose of visit, and proof of funds. Keep hotel confirmations and return flight e-tickets easily accessible.

Practical arrival tips

Carry hard copies of your visa, hotel booking, and return ticket. Have contact details for any UAE sponsor or local hotel. Be prepared to present additional documents on request. Remain calm and courteous — clear, polite answers will help pass checks quickly.

Transit Visas and Stopovers for Pakistani Travelers

Short transit options

If your itinerary includes a long layover in Dubai, you can apply for a 48-hour or 96-hour transit visa, subject to airline and ticket eligibility. These are typically intended for passengers who have an onward connection on the same ticket.

Longer stopovers and multi-city itineraries

If you plan a longer stopover to sightsee in the UAE, opt for a 30- or 60-day tourist visa obtained in advance. Make sure your inbound and outbound flights are coordinated with the visa validity.

Combining UAE stopovers with travel in Saudi Arabia

If your trip includes both the UAE and the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, coordinate visas and timelines early. Saudi Arabia has its own entry requirements and e-visa/residency rules. If you plan an overland or multi-airline connection, confirm that each leg matches the relevant visa dates and entry conditions. Special Cases: Minors, Students, and Medical Travellers

Children and minors

Minors traveling alone or with one parent often need an attested affidavit or no-objection certificate from the non-traveling parent(s). The UAE embassy/consulate has strict requirements about attestation and translations. Ensure paperwork is ready and attested according to consular instructions.

Students and medical travellers

Students moving for short-term courses or travelers seeking medical care should follow the visa path tailored to their purpose; medical institutions and educational bodies often assist with the sponsor paperwork required for visa processing.

Sponsors, Guarantees, and Why Accuracy Matters

When a UAE resident sponsors a visitor, that sponsor assumes responsibility for the visitor during their stay. The sponsor’s identity, residency status, and documentation will be scrutinized. Incomplete sponsor information, incorrect phone numbers, or expired residency documents can delay or deny a visa.

If a sponsor is a company, the company’s authorization letter and business documents may be required. When using a family sponsor, relationship proof (birth certificate, marriage certificate) may be necessary.

Common Reasons Visas Are Delayed Or Denied

  • Passport validity under six months or damaged passport.
  • Incomplete or incorrect application forms (handwritten forms are sometimes rejected; typed form required for some consulates).
  • Absence of required sponsor paperwork or hotel reservation.
  • Mismatch between ticket dates and visa dates.
  • Previous immigration violations or criminal records.
  • Failure to provide attested affidavits for minors.
  • Inconsistent or insufficient proof of funds when requested.

Avoid these issues by double-checking all entries, using typed forms where required, and pre-verifying sponsor and hotel details. If applying through an agent, ask for a pre-submission checklist and a written confirmation of document completeness.

Overstay Penalties, Cancellations, and Appeals

Overstay consequences

Overstaying a UAE visa triggers daily fines and may result in detention, deportation, and a ban on returning to the UAE until fines and legal requirements are resolved. The fines can escalate with each day of overstay, so monitor visa expiry dates closely.

Visa cancellations and appeals

Visa cancellations are handled by the issuing authority. If your visa is refused, the embassy or issuing authority will typically state the reason; however, appeals can be complex. If you believe a refusal was in error, consult the issuing embassy or use a licensed immigration lawyer or reputable agent in Pakistan or the UAE.

Practical Packing, Health, and Cultural Notes for Pakistani Travelers

Documentation and financial readiness

Bring printed copies of your visa, return ticket, hotel booking, and sponsor contact. Carry a modest amount of local currency and a card for contingencies. Immigration officers may ask for proof of funds — having readily accessible evidence reduces friction.

Health and travel insurance

Travel insurance is strongly recommended. For medical travellers, coordinate coverage with your UAE host institution. Carry a copy of any prescriptions (with generic names) and a physician’s letter for prescribed controlled medicines. Some medications require a doctor’s prescription and prior clearance.

Cultural etiquette reminders

Dubai is cosmopolitan but respects local customs. Dress modestly in public spaces, especially in conservative areas. During Ramadan, avoid eating, drinking, or smoking in public during daylight hours. Be polite in public behavior, and avoid photographing people without consent. These tips reduce misunderstandings and help your visit be respectful and smooth.

Airlines will typically specify the documents required and whether original passport submission is needed.

If Your Travel Is Time-Sensitive

When you need urgent travel, prioritize embassy channels and confirm expedited processing options with the consulate or airline service. Some visa types or emergency requests for family visits may require direct consular handling. If you’re already in the UAE and need an extension or change of status, official UAE immigration counters at airports and local immigration offices provide guidance, but rules vary and same-day changes are not guaranteed.

Final Steps Before You Go

FAQ

Q1: Can Pakistani passport holders get a visa on arrival in Dubai? A1: No. Pakistani passports are not on the UAE visa-on-arrival list. Pakistani nationals need a prearranged visa obtained before travel through the embassy/consulate, an approved airline service, a licensed travel agent, or a UAE sponsor.

Q2: How long does it take to process a Dubai visit visa for Pakistanis? A2: Processing times vary by route. Airline-linked preapproved permits often process in about 3–4 working days, embassy applications can take 7–14 working days, and travel agent submissions fall within similar windows. Always allow extra days for document requests or clarifications.

Q3: What documents do I need to enter Dubai from Pakistan? A3: At minimum: a passport with at least six months’ validity, passport-size photos, a color copy of your passport, confirmed return ticket, confirmed accommodation or sponsor tenancy, proof of funds or a credit card, and sponsor documents if relevant. Minors and officials may need additional attested paperwork.

Q4: Can I extend my tourist visa after arrival? A4: Some tourist visas can be extended once after arrival, depending on visa type. Extension rules and fees vary; extensions often require an application at an immigration office and may require the original passport and ticket copies. Check the conditions attached to your visa when it’s issued.
