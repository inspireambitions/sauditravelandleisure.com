U.S. and allied government advisories are conservative by nature. They emphasize precautions because they must account for worst-case scenarios. A practical approach is to read official notices (State Department, FAA notices) and then translate them into travel decisions you can act on: change flight timing if an airline issues restrictions, avoid large public gatherings if advised, and postpone nonessential travel during high-alert periods.

Crime, Petty Theft, and Scams

What Youâre Most Likely to Encounter

Dubaiâs crime profile for tourists is dominated by petty theft, scams, and opportunistic fraud rather than violent crime. In practical terms: crime risk is manageable if you apply basic precautions commonly used in any major global city.

Practical Anti-Theft Habits

Carry minimal cash, use a money belt or a cut-resistant crossbody bag, and split valuables between different pockets. When using ATMs, choose machines inside hotel lobbies or banks and shield your PIN. When dining or visiting nightlife venues, stay with trusted companions and never leave drinks unattended.

Recognizing and Avoiding Common Scams

Romance/dating scams often start with fast emotional engagement and requests for money. Gold and jewelry purchase scams promise off-market bargains and then present counterfeit items. Work-offer scams require upfront payments or personal documents; treat job offers that arrive without a company interview or local presence skeptically.

Trust your instincts. If something feels pressured or too good to be true, step away and consult official sources or local contacts.

Visas, Entry Rules, and Passport Issues

Visa on Arrival and Longer Stays

Most U.S. passport holders receive a tourist visa on arrival for short stays. If you plan to stay longer than the standard period or to work or study, secure the appropriate visa in advance. Be aware that any previously issued UAE visas must be properly canceled if you have multiple entries on your record; immigration can deny exit or entry if visa paperwork is irregular.

Passport Types and Restrictions

Emergency passports and certain passport markers may not be accepted by UAE authorities. Diplomatic or official passport holders have distinct rules and may need a visa before travel. If you have dual nationality, the UAE recognizes only the passport you used to enter.

Exit Controls and Legal Holds

The UAE maintains the right to place exit bans for outstanding legal or financial cases. Ensure no unresolved debts, court cases, or civil claims could impede departure. This is a real operational risk: travelers have reported being detained due to long-standing disputes or unpaid bills they were unaware of.
