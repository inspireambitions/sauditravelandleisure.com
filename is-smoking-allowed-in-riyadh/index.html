Is Smoking Allowed in Riyadh?

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Introduction

Riyadh is a city in motion: modern towers rise beside historic neighborhoods, and millions pass through its airports, malls, and cultural sites every year. As Saudi Arabia opens more widely to international visitors, questions about day-to-day rules are common—especially those that affect behavior in public. One of the most frequently asked is straightforward: can I smoke in Riyadh?

Short answer: Smoking in Riyadh is allowed only in tightly controlled settings. Public smoking is broadly restricted across the Kingdom and enforced in places such as government buildings, hospitals, universities, mosques, public transport and many tourist facilities. Designated smoking rooms still exist in some private businesses and certain venues, but the safest assumption for visitors is that smoking is prohibited indoors almost everywhere unless a clearly marked smoking area is provided.

This article explains what those restrictions mean for travelers and residents, how enforcement works in Riyadh, the rules for shisha and e-cigarettes, where you can legally buy tobacco, and practical steps to avoid fines or cultural awkwardness. How Smoking Laws Work In Saudi Arabia — The Foundation

National Framework Versus Local Practice

Saudi Arabia’s tobacco controls are governed by national legislation and executed through ministerial regulations and municipal rules. Over the last decade the country has significantly tightened restrictions: airports and workplaces were among the first to restrict smoking; later decrees extended bans to a wide range of public spaces and introduced penalties for violators.

In practice this means two layers shape your experience in Riyadh. The first is the national legal framework that lists the types of places where smoking is prohibited and regulates sales, packaging, and the supply chain. The second is local implementation—municipal inspectors, airport authorities, hotel management, and private businesses—who enforce those rules and set on-the-ground policies. Understanding both layers helps you avoid surprises.

The Key Legal Changes That Matter To Travelers

Riyadh’s regulations reflect a nationwide trajectory with several milestone changes:

  • Smoking bans expanded from airports and universities into government buildings, hospitals, educational institutions, and tourist facilities.
  • A broad 2016 decree clarified bans near places of worship, schools, sports venues, and public transport, while stiffening fines for violations.
  • Packaging and sale rules grew stricter: plain packaging, prominent health warnings, restrictions on single-stick sales, and age limits for purchasing tobacco.
  • E-cigarettes and vaping devices became regulated rather than outright prohibited; they are treated similarly to tobacco where smoking is banned.

These national rules shape what you see in Riyadh’s malls, hotels, museums, and streets. Which Places Are Categorically Smoke-Free?

Officially, a wide range of places is off-limits for smoking. Below is a concise list of the most relevant categories you will encounter in Riyadh; think of this as the default “no smoking” map when you move around the city. (If a list is present in a location, follow the posted signage—municipal fines can apply.)

  • Government buildings and ministries
  • Hospitals, clinics and all public healthcare facilities
  • Educational institutions: schools, universities, and training centers
  • Religious sites and areas around mosques
  • Public transport (buses, taxis when designated, stations) and airports (except designated zones)
  • Tourist facilities: many hotels, museums, and archaeological sites
  • Sports facilities, cultural centers, and charity institutions

Smoking In Specific Places Around Riyadh

Airports and Public Transport

Riyadh’s airports were among the first places to see formal smoking restrictions. Designated smoking rooms remain in some terminals but are limited and clearly signposted. Smoking in non-designated areas—boarding gates, lounges, restrooms—can result in fines or intervention by airport security.

Public buses and other official transport modes are smoke-free. Taxis are typically non-smoking; drivers may refuse passengers who smoke in their vehicles. If you plan to drive, remember that roadside smoking near public transport hubs and stations is discouraged and sometimes monitored.

For passengers arriving or departing through Saudi airports or traveling between cities such as Riyadh and Jeddah, always check the local airport signage and staff guidelines; airport policies can vary slightly by terminal. Hotels, Resorts, and Private Accommodations

Hotels take a practical approach: many offer a limited number of designated smoking rooms or floors, while luxurious international brands might maintain stricter internal policies. However, because national law allows municipalities and hotel management to set stricter rules, do not assume every hotel has smoking rooms.

If you need a smoking room, ask at booking and confirm in writing. Expect an additional cleaning fee if hotel staff discover smoking in non-smoking rooms. For travelers heading to high-tourism destinations like AlUla or mountain resorts in Taif, some accommodations adopt stricter smoke-free policies to protect historic sites and guest comfort—consult the hotel’s policy before booking to avoid surprises tourist-site restrictions and hotel rules and mountain resort considerations.

Restaurants, Cafés, and Shisha Lounges

The rules around restaurants and cafes are nuanced. Places that prepare or process food are generally expected to be smoke-free, but some venues provide enclosed, ventilated smoking rooms specifically for shisha (waterpipe) or cigarette smokers. Importantly, where food is served and consumed, smoking is generally limited and food service inside designated smoking rooms is often prohibited.

Shisha lounges exist in Riyadh but they are subject to municipal licensing, zoning rules, and strict safety standards. Many modern cafés that advertise shisha will operate within licensed spaces and adhere to ventilation and packaging regulations. Always verify that a shisha venue is formally licensed and respects local restrictions; when in doubt, ask staff or check for municipal approvals.

Mosques and Religious Sites

Smoking is explicitly banned in and around places of worship. This includes the immediate approaches, courtyards, and areas designated for religious study. In Riyadh, as in other Saudi cities, respect and deference around mosques is expected—avoid smoking within sight of religious buildings and during prayer times. For travelers heading to pilgrimage sites, Makkah and Madinah maintain the strictest enforcement of these rules; if your itinerary includes stops in those cities, consult travel resources tailored to their specific expectations rules around holy sites and local customs in religious destinations.

Open Public Spaces and Streets

Outdoor spaces can be less strictly enforced than indoor public places, but Riyadh’s urban spaces are changing. Municipalities have increased their focus on public health and cleanliness, so areas near playgrounds, schools, and mosques may be monitored. If you must smoke outdoors, step away from crowds, public entrances, and families; look for designated outdoor smoking zones when provided.

E-cigarettes, Vaping, and Nicotine Devices

E-cigarettes and vaping devices occupy a complex regulatory niche in Saudi law. As of recent regulations, the sale and import of e-cigarettes are permitted under specific SFDA controls, and e-cigarette products are subject to packaging and ingredient rules. However, the use of e-cigarettes is prohibited anywhere smoking is banned.

From a traveler’s standpoint, treat e-cigarettes like conventional smoking: don’t use them indoors unless a sign explicitly allows vaping, and avoid carrying unlabelled or unconventional refill liquids. For purchasing e-cigarettes locally, licensed shops and pharmacies will be your safest options; keep all receipts and paperwork in case of routine checks.

Buying Tobacco Products: What Travelers Need To Know

Age Limits, Packaging, and Where To Buy

Tobacco sales are limited to individuals 18 and older. Retailers must comply with SFDA packaging rules—plain packaging and large health warnings are standard. Single-stick sales are banned; you will typically find only sealed packs, approved sizes, and licensed products.

Recent municipal measures have also tightened the locations and licensing for tobacco retailers. For instance, shops cannot be within a set distance from mosques and schools; retail outlets must meet safety, architectural, and transparency standards. Because these rules evolve, purchase tobacco only from authorized retail outlets that display valid licensing and comply with visible safety standards.

Shisha Tobacco and Cafés

Shisha tobacco sale is regulated similarly to cigarette sales. Public shisha service is allowed only in licensed venues that meet ventilation and safety standards. If you buy packaged waterpipe tobacco, ensure it is sealed and labeled per SFDA requirements.

Customs and Bringing Tobacco In

If you plan to bring tobacco into Saudi Arabia, be mindful that packaged tobacco is subject to customs rules and must conform to health warning and packaging standards. Avoid bringing unlabelled or homemade tobacco products. If you anticipate needing supplies during travel, your safest path is to purchase sealed, licensed products from a local vendor after arrival.

Enforcement and Penalties

Who Enforces the Rules?

Enforcement falls to a combination of authorities: municipal inspectors, public health officers, airport security, and law enforcement. Businesses may also enforce rules internally and can levy fines or refuse service if guests violate non-smoking policies.

Municipalities are increasingly using administrative fines, closure orders for violating businesses, and public education campaigns to reduce smoking in prohibited spaces. Retailers can face licensing revocations and significant penalties for selling to minors or selling non-compliant products.

Typical Fines and Consequences

Penalties vary by location and the severity of the violation. Individuals caught smoking in prohibited places may receive fines ranging from moderate to substantial, depending on recent decrees. Vendors face heavier penalties if they sell to minors, fail to display required warnings, or operate too close to prohibited areas like mosques or schools. Repeat or egregious violations by businesses can trigger closure or license revocation.

Because laws update and municipalities set enforcement priorities, the prudent traveler behaves conservatively: assume indoor smoking is forbidden unless a clearly marked, ventilated smoking room is present.

Cultural Etiquette: Beyond the Letter of the Law

Respecting Local Norms

Riyadh is culturally conservative. Even in places where smoking is technically allowed, exercising discretion matters. Avoid smoking near families, children, or at events where the majority may view it as inappropriate. During Ramadan, public behavior around food and drink is more visible and sensitive; smoking in public during daylight hours can draw disapproving attention even if not legally penalized in a particular outdoor spot.

Gender and Public Behavior

Gender dynamics influence social expectations. While female smoking exists in Saudi society, public behavior involving smoking can carry different social consequences depending on location and audience. As a visitor, avoid provocative or conspicuous behavior, and follow the lead of venue staff and signage.

Private Spaces

Smoking in private homes is a matter of household rules. If you are invited to a private home, ask first. Hosts often appreciate the courtesy. In shared accommodation, such as apartments or Airbnbs, smoking policies will vary—confirm before lighting up.

Practical Steps For Travelers: How To Plan Around Riyadh’s Rules

Below is a straightforward traveler checklist to convert the legal framework into practical actions. Use these steps as a pre-travel blueprint to avoid fines and ensure respectful behavior while in Riyadh.

  • Confirm hotel smoking policy at booking and request a designated smoking room if available.
  • Carry a photo ID proving you are over 18 before purchasing tobacco.
  • Buy sealed, licensed tobacco products from authorized retailers; avoid single-stick or unpackaged sales.
  • Treat e-cigarettes like traditional smoking—don’t vape indoors unless explicitly allowed.
  • Avoid smoking near mosques, schools, hospitals, or transport hubs; look for posted signs.
  • If attending events or dining in mixed company, assume smoking is unwelcome unless a smoking area is indicated.
  • When in doubt in a public place, ask staff: they will tell you where smoking is permitted and where it is not.

How To Find Designated Smoking Areas And Licensed Retailers

Spotting Official Signage and Permits

Licensed venues display permits, safety certificates, and municipal approval notices. In hotels and airports, designated smoking rooms are clearly marked and equipped with ventilation systems. When in public spaces, look for municipal signs indicating "No Smoking" or dedicated smoking zones. Retail outlets will usually display a license number and sometimes a QR code linking to licensing details.

Using Apps and Local Staff

Local ride-hailing apps, hotel concierges, and venue staff are usually well-versed in the nearest legal smoking options. Ask politely and they can direct you to a licensed shisha lounge, an authorized retailer, or the nearest approved smoking area.

Shisha Safety And Rules

Shisha remains a popular social activity, but regulation and safety standards have tightened. Licensed shisha lounges must follow ventilation, packaging, and fire safety requirements. In licensed venues, disposable mouth tips and properly sealed tobacco containers are best practice. Avoid street or makeshift setups where safety and compliance are uncertain.

If you have respiratory conditions or are sensitive to smoke, avoid shisha venues; smoke concentration in enclosed shisha rooms can be high.

Business Travelers And Corporate Policies

Companies in Riyadh often enforce strict non-smoking policies across offices and campuses. Meetings in government buildings and corporate offices will generally be smoke-free. If you represent a company, check corporate facilities policies in advance and brief visiting colleagues accordingly. Employers may provide designated outdoor smoking areas or partner with local clinics for smoking cessation as part of employee health programs.

Health And Cessation Resources

Saudi Arabia has stepped up public health campaigns on smoking cessation. If you want to quit or reduce use while visiting, look for clinics and services that provide nicotine replacement therapy and counseling. The market for NRT has grown, and pharmacies can advise on approved nicotine products. For longer stays, municipal health centers may offer consultations and community programs.

Regulatory Trends To Watch

Saudi tobacco policy continues to evolve. Recent measures targeted retail licensing, store distance from mosques and schools, and tightened packaging and labeling rules. Municipalities have also increased technical requirements for tobacco retailers—CCTV, fire safety, and storefront restrictions are now common. Expect more rigorous inspections and technology-driven enforcement (QR codes, digital licensing checks) as the regulatory system modernizes.

If your travel plans include historic or sensitive tourist sites, monitor site-specific rules because some destinations apply extra protections that restrict smoking even in nearby outdoor spaces. Common Traveler Questions Answered In Practical Terms

Can I smoke in hotel balconies or terraces?

Not always. Some hotels prohibit smoking anywhere on balconies to prevent smoke entering neighboring rooms or common areas. Confirm the specific property’s outdoor smoking policy before assuming balconies are allowed.

Are shisha pipes provided in all cafés?

No. Licensed shisha venues provide compliant equipment and sealed tobacco. Many mainstream cafés do not offer shisha; those that do will display proper licensing and safety measures.

Is vaping considered less restricted?

No. Vaping is regulated but treated like smoking regarding where it can be used: if smoking is banned in a space, vaping is generally banned too.

What happens if I’m fined?

Fines are typically monetary and issued by municipal authorities or security personnel. Businesses can refuse service and report violations. In serious or repeated cases, more significant legal consequences may follow.

Practical Scenarios And How To Handle Them

Scenario: You’re at a busy mall and want to smoke

Malls in Riyadh are largely smoke-free indoors. Look for clearly marked outdoor smoking zones or ask mall security for guidance. Smoking near children’s play areas or food courts is not acceptable.

Scenario: A café allows shisha but you smell smoke outside in seating

Check if the shisha area is in a separate, ventilated enclosure. If it isn’t, request seating away from the source or choose a different venue. Hospitality staff generally accommodate reasonable requests.

Scenario: You need cigarettes late at night

Most supermarkets and licensed convenience retailers sell sealed packs during shop hours. Avoid purchasing from informal street vendors or unlicensed sellers—products may be non-compliant and sales could be illegal.

Conclusion

Riyadh’s approach to smoking is deliberate: the city prioritizes public health, respect for religious spaces, and clean public environments. For travelers that translates into a clear behavioral rule of thumb—assume indoor smoking is prohibited unless a properly ventilated, officially marked smoking area exists. E-cigarettes follow the same restrictions, shisha is permitted only in licensed venues, and retailers operate under strict licensing and packaging rules.

Following a few practical steps—check hotel policies, buy only sealed licensed tobacco, avoid smoking near mosques and schools, and ask staff when unsure—keeps you within the law and aligned with local expectations. FAQ

Is smoking permitted in Riyadh’s public parks?

Smoking policies in parks vary by municipality. Many parks near playgrounds and family areas discourage or prohibit smoking; others may have designated outdoor smoking zones. Look for signage and favor areas away from children and public facilities.

Can I bring vaping liquids into Saudi Arabia?

You can bring commercially sealed vaping liquids, but they must comply with SFDA regulations. Avoid unlabelled or homemade liquids and carry proof of purchase when possible.

What should I do if I’m asked to stop smoking in a venue?

Comply immediately, apologize, and move to a designated area if one is provided. Refusing can lead to fines or involvement by venue security.

Are there support services for quitting smoking while visiting?

Yes. Larger cities like Riyadh provide clinics, pharmacies with nicotine replacement products, and public health programs. If you plan a longer stay and want support, ask your hotel concierge or check municipal health service listings for referrals.
