Is There a Train From Jeddah to Riyadh?

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This page remains available for reference, but we consolidated this search intent under Is There Train From Jeddah To Riyadh.

Introduction

Saudi Arabia is investing heavily in transport as part of its Vision 2030 transformation, and travelers frequently ask whether they can ride between the Kingdom’s two largest cities by rail. Short answer: There is currently no direct passenger train that runs from Jeddah to Riyadh. The western high-speed Haramain line links Jeddah with Makkah and Madinah, while Riyadh’s rail connectivity focuses on regional and urban networks; a dedicated Jeddah–Riyadh passenger link has not entered regular service.

This article explains why there isn’t a direct train service today, how the national rail picture is evolving, and—most importantly—practical, expert alternatives and step-by-step blueprints for moving between Jeddah and Riyadh with confidence. You’ll find travel-time comparisons, booking strategies, routings that mix rail and air where sensible, recommended stopovers if you drive, cultural logistics, safety advice, and sample itineraries you can implement immediately. Our main message: while a single-seat train ride from Jeddah to Riyadh isn’t an option today, the Kingdom’s modern transport network and short domestic flights make the route easy to plan. With the right framework you can choose the fastest, most scenic, or most budget-friendly option and turn travel time into productive or restorative time on the road.

The Current Rail Landscape in Saudi Arabia

The Rail Services That Exist Today

Saudi Arabia’s rail network is already modern in sections. The two most prominent passenger services you’ll encounter as a traveler are the Haramain high-speed rail line and urban rail projects like the Riyadh Metro. The Haramain high-speed service connects the western corridor—Makkah, Jeddah, King Abdullah Economic City and Madinah—at speeds up to 300 km/h, providing fast, air-conditioned, Wi-Fi-enabled travel between those cities.

Riyadh’s passenger rail presence is primarily urban and regional: the Riyadh Metro serves intra-city travel, and regional rail projects focus on extending connectivity inside the central and eastern provinces. Because existing passenger routes emphasize different corridors, a continuous passenger rail link between Jeddah and Riyadh is not part of the operational network at this time. Why Haramain Doesn’t Solve Jeddah–Riyadh Travel

Haramain is a game-changer for pilgrims and travelers moving along the western coastal corridor. It is optimized for the Makkah–Madinah axis and serves Jeddah as a major node, but it does not reach Riyadh. Because Haramain runs north–south along the Red Sea side of the country while Riyadh sits inland to the east, passengers cannot use Haramain alone to reach Riyadh.

Why There Is No Direct Jeddah–Riyadh Passenger Train Today

Geography and Historic Investment Patterns

The Kingdom’s earliest large-scale rail investments focused on freight corridors and pilgrimage flows. Freight corridors such as the North–South and the port-to-inland rail projects prioritized moving goods and minerals. Passenger-focused investment concentrated where demand was immediate—pilgrimage routes between Makkah and Madinah and urban mobility in Riyadh.

Designing a high-speed passenger line between Jeddah and Riyadh requires routing through challenging terrain and across hundreds of kilometers of desert, careful station placement, and integration with both cities’ transport hubs. Large-capacity passenger demand between these two cities is currently served more efficiently by short flights and long-distance buses, which influenced investment decisions to date.

Commercial Viability and Alternatives

A high-speed line becomes commercially attractive when projected passenger volumes justify the capital outlay. Domestic airlines already serve the Jeddah–Riyadh corridor with frequent daily flights at competitive prices; buses and private cars provide low-cost alternatives. Until rail can offer a clear time or cost advantage at scale, large rail-capex projects face slower development. That said, there are ongoing discussions and planning studies for expanding high-speed and intercity passenger rail across the Kingdom, which could include an eventual Jeddah–Riyadh corridor.

Proposed Projects and the Future of Intercity Rail

National Rail Aspirations

Saudi planners have publicly discussed an expansive national rail strategy that envisions faster, more passenger-focused corridors in addition to freight links. Long-term development frameworks reference improved east–west and north–south connectivity, and some studies explicitly consider links that would connect Riyadh to coastal cities, including Jeddah. These projects tend to be phased across decades and require coordination among multiple agencies, financing partners, and environmental assessments.

What Travelers Should Watch For

If you prefer rail travel, keep an eye on official announcements and transport ministry updates because large infrastructure projects often move from feasibility studies to construction over several years. When a Jeddah–Riyadh passenger corridor is confirmed, it will affect travel pricing, corridor travel times, and regional stop patterns. Until then, plan your trip using current, reliable modes.

Practical Alternatives: How to Travel Between Jeddah and Riyadh Today

When the direct train isn’t available, there are three practical categories of alternatives: fly, drive, and bus—plus hybrid rail+air options for passengers who want to use existing trains for part of the journey.

Quick Option Comparison

  1. Flight: Fastest—flights between Jeddah and Riyadh take roughly 1 hour 20 minutes airborne; add check-in and transfers and total door-to-door time is often 3–4 hours. Airlines include Saudia, flynas, and flyadeal with frequent daily departures. Flights are the top choice for business travelers and time-sensitive travelers.
  2. Drive: Scenic and flexible—the drive is about 950–1,000 km depending on route and takes approximately 9–10 hours of driving time. A timed, well-planned road trip with rest stops can be rewarding for those wanting control over pace and stopovers.
  3. Bus: Cost-effective—SAPTCO and other long-distance bus operators run scheduled services between Jeddah and Riyadh; travel time typically ranges from 10–14 hours depending on service and route. Buses offer comfortable coaches with onboard amenities and are a popular budget option.
  4. Rail + Air: Practical hybrid—use Haramain to cover western corridor legs (for example, Jeddah to Madinah) and then fly from Madinah or Jeddah to Riyadh if timing and pricing work. This is rarely faster than direct flights from Jeddah but can be used as part of a multi-city itinerary.

Flight: Step-by-Step for a Smooth Air Transfer

For most travelers the fastest, most efficient option is a direct flight. Follow this framework:

  • Book early when possible to secure the best fares on legacy and low-cost carriers.
  • Choose airports: in Jeddah use King Abdulaziz International Airport (JED). In Riyadh fly into King Khalid International Airport (RUH) for the most flight options.
  • Allow time: early check-in for domestic flights is often recommended 90 minutes before departure at peak times, though many carriers allow online check-in and mobile boarding passes.
  • Luggage and prayer needs: domestic baggage allowances vary by carrier; check the airline website before packing. Airports and many airlines provide prayer rooms—confirm availability on airport maps.
  • Transfers and local transit: both JED and RUH have ground transport options—taxis, ride-hailing apps, and car rental desks. For help smoothing the last-mile logistics, visit our portal for booking tips and tools.

Driving: Practical Road-Trip Blueprint

Driving gives you flexibility and the chance to visit regional highlights. If you choose to drive, use this travel blueprint.

Pre-trip preparation: ensure your driver's license is valid in Saudi Arabia, secure a reliable vehicle or rental with GPS and air-conditioning, and confirm that insurance includes long-distance coverage. Plan fuel stops and driver swaps if possible.

Routing: The most straightforward routes are eastbound across central Saudi Arabia. Typical road time is 9–10 hours depending on stops. Road conditions on main highways are generally good, with rest stations and facilities at service areas on major routes.

Safety and comfort: schedule driver rest breaks every 2–3 hours, keep water and basic first-aid supplies onboard, and avoid driving after midnight on rural stretches where livestock or low-visibility hazards may appear. Consider overnighting in a midpoint city like Taif if you want a more relaxed pace and to see mountain scenery.

Stops and experience: plan prayer breaks and meals in major towns. If you have time and inclination, route detours to historical or scenic spots—our section on sample itineraries describes practical one- and two-day stopovers.

Bus: How to Use Intercity Coaches Confidently

Long-distance coach services are a practical low-cost choice. SAPTCO operates modern long-haul coaches with AC, seat reservations, and onboard restroom facilities.

Booking and schedules: purchase tickets online through the operator’s website or at bus stations. Popular departure times are early morning and late evening to accommodate sleeping or daytime travel preferences.

Comfort tips: bring an overnight kit (neck pillow, earplugs), snacks, and a power bank. Buses typically stop for breaks at service stations where you can pray and eat.

Transit hubs and tickets: bus terminals in both Jeddah and Riyadh are well-connected to local taxis and ride-hailing services. For questions about regional bus connections that complement city travel, consult our overview of travel across the Kingdom.

Mixed Rail + Air: When It Makes Sense

If your itinerary includes stops in Madinah or Makkah, you can combine Haramain travel with a flight to Riyadh from either Jeddah or Madinah. This works best if you’re visiting pilgrimage sites or want the rail experience for part of the trip.

Booking, Tickets and Price Benchmarks

How to Compare Prices Effectively

Start with a simple comparison matrix: airlines vs. bus vs. car rental. For flights, use an aggregator for initial price discovery and then cross-check the carrier’s website for final fares. Domestic airfares in Saudi Arabia can be highly competitive; with low-cost carriers you’ll often find sales and promos.

For long-distance buses, check official operator schedules and look for premium or sleeper coach options if you prefer more comfort. For car rentals, compare daily rates, fuel policies, and insurance inclusions; weekly rates will often be more economical for multi-day trips.

Typical Price Ranges (Indicative)

  • Flights: one-way domestic fares vary widely—on sale you may find fares comparable to low-cost carriers’ promotions; regular fares are generally affordable relative to the convenience offered.
  • Buses: long-distance bus tickets are usually an economical option, often substantially cheaper than flights.
  • Car rental: depends on vehicle class and season; expect to pay more during pilgrimage seasons or national holidays.

Onboard Experience, Luggage Rules and Amenities

Flights

Domestic flights offer seat classes from economy to business on select carriers. Baggage allowances differ by airline and fare type. Airports commonly feature prayer rooms, dining options, and lounges. If you need assistance or have accessibility requirements, contact the airline in advance.

Buses

Modern long-distance coaches include reclining seats, individual lights, onboard toilets, and sometimes WiâFi. Baggage is stored underneath; confirm luggage limits at booking.

Trains

If you use Haramain as part of a mixed journey, expect a comfortable high-speed experience: air conditioning, dedicated prayer areas at major stations, onboard catering options at times, power outlets, and fast travel between stops. Haramain strongly emphasizes punctuality and safety.

Cultural and Practical Etiquette for Intercity Travel

Dress, Behavior and Prayer Times

Saudi Arabia now welcomes international tourists and travelers with clear expectations. Dress modestly in public spaces—men should avoid tank tops in cities, women should wear modest clothing; local norms can vary by region. Public prayer times are observed nationwide; stations, airports and many rest stops offer prayer spaces and will often stop operations for major prayer times.

Gender and Family Travel

Travel policies have evolved; families and women traveling independently can access public transport services. If traveling in mixed groups or families, choose seating that best suits comfort preferences and check operator policies for any reserved seating categories.

Food and Consumption

Alcohol remains prohibited; food and beverage services are widely available in stations, airports and rest stops. Pack snacks for long road trips, and allow extra time to stop for meals in major towns.

Safety and Emergency Preparedness

Road Safety

  • Fatigue is a major risk on long drives. Schedule driver swaps or overnight stops.
  • Keep a first-aid kit, spare tyre and basic tools in the vehicle.
  • Use main highways where possible; they are better maintained and have service stations.

Health and Weather

  • Carry water and sun protection for daytime travel in summer months — temperatures can be extreme.
  • Use air-conditioned transport where possible; in rural areas, plan for limited services.

Local Contacts and Insurance

  • Purchase travel or car insurance with emergency assistance coverage.
  • Store relevant local contact numbers and embassy details in your phone and printed copy.

Packing and Pre-Trip Checklist

  • Valid ID and printed/digital tickets.
  • Power bank, adapters and chargers.
  • Comfortable clothing appropriate to local norms.
  • Refillable water bottle and snacks.
  • Travel insurance documentation.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1: Will I be able to take a direct train from Jeddah to Riyadh in the next year? A1: No confirmed passenger service for a direct Jeddah–Riyadh rail line is operational today. Large national rail projects take time; watch official transport announcements for updates, but plan travel now around flights, buses, or driving.

Q2: How long does the flight take versus driving? A2: Flight time is about 1 hour 20 minutes airborne; door-to-door you should budget roughly 3–4 hours including transfers. Driving is typically 9–10 hours of road time depending on route and stops.

Q3: Can I combine Haramain train travel with a journey to Riyadh? A3: Yes. Haramain serves Jeddah–Makkah–Madina. Travelers can use Haramain for western corridor legs and then fly from Madinah or Jeddah to Riyadh as part of a multi-city itinerary.

Q4: Are there comfortable overnight buses between the two cities? A4: Yes—national long-distance coach operators offer modern, air-conditioned overnight and daytime services with scheduled rest stops. Book in advance for preferred seats and times.

Conclusion

While a single-seat intercity train from Jeddah to Riyadh does not exist today, the Kingdom’s transport options—frequent domestic flights, reliable intercity bus services, and well-maintained highways—make the route straightforward to plan. Use the decision framework presented here: decide whether speed, cost, or experience is your priority; choose the right combination of flight, drive or bus; book early and plan for prayer and rest stops; and rely on local transport hubs and station amenities to keep the trip smooth.
