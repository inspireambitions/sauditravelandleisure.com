Types of Visit Visas Available and Which One You Need

Short transit permits (48-hour and 96-hour)

Transit or stopover permits are low-cost, limited-length visas intended for short layovers or brief visits when your itinerary passes through Dubai. These are typically issued through airlines (for passengers on a single ticket with an eligible carrier) and are non-extendable. Use this option if your plan is a short stopover, a brief business handoff, or a quick hotel overnight.

Tourism visas (30-day single or multiple entry, extendable)

The 30-day tourist visa is a common choice for leisure travelers. It can be single-entry or multiple-entry and, in many cases, is extendable once (subject to rules and fees). Pakistanis usually obtain these visas through certified travel agents, airlines (when booking an Emirates or Etihad itinerary), or via a sponsor in the UAE. The 30-day visa is the right choice for typical holiday visits.

Long-term tourist visas (60-day stays)

If you plan a longer stay for family visits or extended holiday, the 60-day visa offers more flexibility. It is processed similarly to the 30-day visa but features a longer validity after entry. These visas are slightly more expensive and may require stronger supporting documents like confirmed return tickets and proof of accommodation.

Sponsored visit visas (family and friend sponsors)

A UAE resident or company can sponsor your visit visa. Sponsors submit the application and provide required identity and residency documentation. Sponsorship is a common route when visiting relatives or when an employer or host in the UAE is handling the travel logistics. For Pakistani applicants, specific sponsor documentation and sometimes a refundable deposit may be requested.

Embassy-issued visas and diplomatic categories

Embassy-issued visas are generally used for governmental, official, or diplomatic travel and have their own particular procedural rules. Documents You Will Be Asked For (And How To Prepare Them)

Standard documents required across most application routes

The core package you should assemble in advance includes:

Common Pitfalls and How To Avoid Them

Pitfall: Relying on verbal promises or unverified agents

Always confirm visa issuance through official channels and get written confirmation. Avoid informal promises from unknown agents; only use certified travel agencies or airline visa services.

Pitfall: Missing document attestations

Document attestation requirements vary. Failing to get the right certificates attested (for example, school or degree documents for some visa categories) can lead to rejection or delays. Use official embassy attestation checklists when preparing sensitive documents.

Pitfall: Insufficient passport validity

Your passport must be valid for at least six months from the date of entry. Renew passports early; embassies and airlines will reject applications for passports with short validity.

Pitfall: Misunderstanding sponsor obligations

If you travel on a sponsored visa, understand whether your sponsor must claim a deposit or provide local contact details. Lack of clarity on this can lead to processing delays or complications during entry.
