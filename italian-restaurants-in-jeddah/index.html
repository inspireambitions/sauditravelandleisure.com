12 Top Best Italian Restaurants in Jeddah (2025 Foodie Guide)

Updated August 30, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Jeddah is known for its vibrant food culture, and Italian cuisine holds a special place in the city’s heart.
From wood-fired pizzas to creamy pastas and fine-dining trattorias, Italian restaurants in Jeddah offer something for every taste and budget.
This guide highlights the 12 best Italian restaurants in Jeddah — covering ambiance, menu, price, and what each spot is best known for.

Where can I find the best Italian restaurants in Jeddah?

1. Piatto – Classic Italian Dining

Piatto is one of the most popular Italian chains in Saudi Arabia.
Known for its handmade pasta and wood-fired pizza, it offers both family-friendly dining and romantic vibes.
Must try: Fettuccine Alfredo, Margherita pizza.

  • Price: SAR 70–120
  • Best for: Families & casual date nights
  • Why visit? Consistent quality, cozy interiors

2. Il Gabbiano – Fine Dining by the Sea

Il Gabbiano is a premium Italian restaurant overlooking the Red Sea.
It blends authentic flavors with an elegant dining experience.
Must try: Risotto alla Milanese, seafood pasta.

  • Price: SAR 120–200
  • Best for: Business dinners & luxury dining
  • Why visit? Scenic sea view + upscale menu

3. Casa Mia – Authentic Italian Flavors

Located inside Hilton Jeddah, Casa Mia is loved for its authentic recipes straight from Italy.
The menu includes classics like risotto, gnocchi, and wood-fired pizzas.
Must try: Veal scaloppini, tiramisu.

  • Price: SAR 100–180
  • Best for: Romantic dinners & celebrations
  • Why visit? Authentic chef-crafted meals

4. San Carlo Cicchetti – Trendy & Modern

This London-born brand has made a name in Jeddah for modern Italian dining with small sharing plates.
The décor is elegant, and the food presentation is stylish.
Must try: Lobster ravioli, truffle pizza.

  • Price: SAR 120–200
  • Best for: Trendy foodies & groups
  • Why visit? Chic interiors + creative menu

5. Aromi – Venetian Inspired Dining

Aromi, at the Waldorf Astoria, offers a Venetian-inspired dining experience.
It’s a top pick for those seeking luxury with authentic Italian flavors.
Must try: Duck ravioli, tiramisu.

  • Price: SAR 150–250
  • Best for: Luxury dining & special events
  • Why visit? Exclusive fine dining with elegance

6. Bella Vista – Italian with a View

As the name suggests, Bella Vista offers a beautiful view of the Red Sea.
It combines a relaxed atmosphere with traditional Italian food.
Must try: Spaghetti carbonara, seafood pizza.

  • Price: SAR 80–130
  • Best for: Families & casual gatherings
  • Why visit? Seaside ambiance + relaxed vibe

7. Brio – Casual & Stylish

Brio is a modern casual dining spot offering pizzas, pastas, and light bites.
It’s a favorite for quick Italian meals without the fine-dining price tag.
Must try: Penne arrabbiata, four-cheese pizza.

  • Price: SAR 60–100
  • Best for: Quick lunches & casual outings
  • Why visit? Affordable, chic interiors

8. Tiamo – Rustic Italian Atmosphere

Tiamo brings rustic Italian vibes with brick walls and a warm interior.
It’s a mix of casual dining and semi-fine dining.
Must try: Lasagna, tiramisu.

  • Price: SAR 80–130
  • Best for: Friends & family dinners
  • Why visit? Cozy interiors, homestyle pasta

9. Rossopomodoro – Naples Pizza Tradition

Rossopomodoro is famous for its authentic Neapolitan pizzas.
The dough is soft, airy, and baked in a traditional oven.
Must try: Pizza margherita, caprese salad.

  • Price: SAR 70–120
  • Best for: Pizza lovers
  • Why visit? Authentic Naples-style pizza

10. Cova Milano – Elegant Italian Café

Cova Milano offers a mix of fine dining and café vibes, originally from Milan.
Its pastries and light Italian dishes make it unique.
Must try: Ravioli, Italian desserts, espresso.

  • Price: SAR 100–150
  • Best for: Business meetings & coffee lovers
  • Why visit? Stylish Italian café atmosphere

11. Andiamo – Modern Italian Eatery

Andiamo serves Italian classics with a modern Saudi twist.
The menu is diverse, making it popular with locals.
Must try: Pizza Bianca, pesto pasta.

  • Price: SAR 70–110
  • Best for: Families & groups
  • Why visit? Fusion-style Italian flavors

12. La Cucina – Contemporary Italian Dining

La Cucina is a stylish restaurant known for its contemporary take on Italian dishes.
It’s great for casual dining and group outings.
Must try: Spaghetti bolognese, tiramisu.

  • Price: SAR 70–120
  • Best for: Groups & casual dinners
  • Why visit? Simple, fresh flavors

FAQ – People Also Ask

Which is the best Italian restaurant in Jeddah for fine dining?

Il Gabbiano, Aromi, and Casa Mia are the top luxury Italian spots.

Where can I get the best pizza in Jeddah?

Rossopomodoro and Piatto serve the most authentic pizzas.

Which Italian restaurant is affordable in Jeddah?

Brio and La Cucina are budget-friendly options.

Which Italian restaurant has the best sea view?

Il Gabbiano and Bella Vista are perfect for dining with a sea view.

Are there vegetarian options in Jeddah’s Italian restaurants?

Yes, most restaurants like Piatto and San Carlo Cicchetti offer vegetarian pizzas, pastas, and salads.

Final Words

From fine dining by the sea at Il Gabbiano to casual pizza nights at Brio, Jeddah has Italian restaurants for every mood.
Whether you’re craving wood-fired pizza, creamy pasta, or an elegant Venetian-inspired meal, this list will guide you to the best Italian dining spots in Jeddah.
