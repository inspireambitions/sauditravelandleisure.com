12 Top Best Italian Restaurants in Madinah

2025-08-30

Italian food has a universal charm. From creamy pasta to wood-fired pizza, it’s a cuisine loved by all.
In Madinah, you’ll find several restaurants that serve authentic Italian flavors with a modern touch.
This guide lists the 12 best Italian restaurants in Madinah, including details on ambiance, pricing, and what each spot is best known for.

1. Al Baik Italian Corner – Is It Worth Visiting?

Al Baik Italian Corner is a popular choice among families.
It’s known for pizza, pasta, and affordable meal deals.

  • Pros: Family-friendly, budget pricing, large portions.
  • Cons: Gets very crowded at dinner.
  • Price Range: SAR 40–80 per person.
  • Best For: Families on a budget.

2. La Cucina – What Makes It Popular in Madinah?

La Cucina is loved for its authentic Italian pasta dishes.
The interiors are cozy, with a fine-dining feel.

  • Pros: Authentic recipes, elegant setting.
  • Cons: Slightly expensive for regular dining.
  • Price Range: SAR 80–150 per person.
  • Best For: Couples and special occasions.

3. Pizza Roma – Is It the Best Pizza in Town?

Pizza Roma serves wood-fired pizzas with fresh ingredients.
Their thin-crust Margherita is a local favorite.

  • Pros: Crispy thin crust, authentic flavors.
  • Cons: Limited seating.
  • Price Range: SAR 50–100 per person.
  • Best For: Quick bites with friends.

4. Olivo Ristorante – Does It Offer Authentic Italian Dining?

Olivo is a premium spot with a full Italian dining experience.
It’s ideal for those who want fine service and gourmet dishes.

  • Pros: Excellent service, gourmet menu.
  • Cons: Premium pricing.
  • Price Range: SAR 120–200 per person.
  • Best For: Business dinners and celebrations.

5. Pasta Vero – Is It a Hidden Gem?

Pasta Vero is a casual dining place with handmade pasta.
Their Alfredo pasta and tiramisu are must-tries.

  • Pros: Freshly made pasta, cozy vibe.
  • Cons: Limited menu.
  • Price Range: SAR 60–120 per person.
  • Best For: Pasta lovers.

6. Tavola Italiana – Is It Family-Friendly?

Tavola Italiana blends Italian food with a welcoming atmosphere.
The restaurant has big tables for family gatherings.

  • Pros: Large seating, good service.
  • Cons: Wait times on weekends.
  • Price Range: SAR 70–130 per person.
  • Best For: Big family dinners.

7. Donatello’s – Why Do Tourists Love It?

Donatello’s offers classic Italian pizzas and lasagna.
It’s especially popular among visiting tourists.

  • Pros: Tourist-friendly, English-speaking staff.
  • Cons: Slightly crowded during peak hours.
  • Price Range: SAR 50–110 per person.
  • Best For: Tourists exploring Madinah.

8. Gusto Italiano – Is It Budget-Friendly?

Gusto Italiano is a casual Italian diner with fair pricing.
It’s a favorite among students and young professionals.

  • Pros: Affordable menu, quick service.
  • Cons: Basic interiors.
  • Price Range: SAR 35–70 per person.
  • Best For: Students and quick meals.

9. Casa di Pasta – What’s Special About It?

Casa di Pasta specializes in customizable pasta bowls.
You can choose your pasta type, sauce, and toppings.

  • Pros: Customization options, great for groups.
  • Cons: Not many pizza options.
  • Price Range: SAR 50–100 per person.
  • Best For: Pasta customization lovers.

10. Trattoria Madinah – Does It Bring Italy to Saudi?

Trattoria Madinah offers a mix of traditional and modern Italian dishes.
It’s well-known for risotto and seafood pasta.

  • Pros: Wide variety, authentic flavors.
  • Cons: Parking can be tricky.
  • Price Range: SAR 80–140 per person.
  • Best For: Foodies who love variety.

11. Bella Vita – Is It Good for Couples?

Bella Vita has a romantic Italian setting with candlelit tables.
It’s often picked for anniversaries and date nights.

  • Pros: Romantic ambiance, great desserts.
  • Cons: Higher pricing on weekends.
  • Price Range: SAR 100–180 per person.
  • Best For: Couples and celebrations.

12. Amici Ristorante – Is It Worth the Hype?

Amici is a modern Italian restaurant known for its wide wine-free beverage menu.
It’s a hit among both locals and expats.

  • Pros: Stylish interiors, diverse menu.
  • Cons: Can be pricey for families.
  • Price Range: SAR 90–160 per person.
  • Best For: Friends’ gatherings and casual fine dining.

FAQs About Italian Restaurants in Madinah

What is the average cost of dining at Italian restaurants in Madinah?

It usually ranges from SAR 40 to 150 per person, depending on the restaurant.

Do these restaurants offer vegetarian options?

Yes, most have vegetarian pasta and pizza dishes.

Which Italian restaurant in Madinah is best for families?

Tavola Italiana and Al Baik Italian Corner are great family picks.

Where can I find authentic wood-fired pizza in Madinah?

Pizza Roma is well-known for its thin-crust wood-fired pizzas.

Final Words

Madinah has no shortage of Italian flavors.
From budget-friendly pasta spots to premium fine-dining restaurants, there’s something for every taste.
Whether you’re a tourist, a student, or celebrating a special day, these 12 best Italian restaurants in Madinah guarantee a memorable dining experience.
