Top 15 Places to Visit in Al Ula for Travelers

Updated September 7, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Al Ula, located in northwestern Saudi Arabia, is one of the country’s most breathtaking destinations. Known for its ancient history, dramatic rock formations, and world-famous UNESCO sites, Al Ula is a place where nature and culture blend seamlessly. From the legendary Hegra (Madain Saleh) to hidden valleys and modern attractions, this desert gem offers unforgettable experiences for every traveler.

In this guide, we’ll explore the 15 best places to visit in Al Ula to help you plan a perfect trip.

Top 15 Places to Visit in Al Ula [2025 Updated]

1. Hegra (Madain Saleh)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Hegra is often called the “Petra of Saudi Arabia.” It features over 100 well-preserved Nabataean tombs carved into sandstone mountains.

2. Elephant Rock (Jabal AlFil)

This iconic rock formation, shaped like an elephant, is one of Al Ula’s most photographed landmarks, especially stunning at sunset.

3. Al Ula Old Town

Wander through Al Ula Old Town, a maze of mudbrick houses, ancient markets, and cultural exhibits that take you back in time.

4. Maraya Concert Hall

Covered entirely in mirrors, Maraya is the world’s largest mirrored building. It hosts concerts, art shows, and cultural events in a futuristic setting.

5. Jabal Ikmah

Known as an “open-air library,” Jabal Ikmah is filled with ancient inscriptions, carvings, and writings from different civilizations.

6. Dadan and Lihyan Kingdoms

Explore the ruins of Dadan, once the capital of ancient kingdoms, with impressive tombs and archaeological treasures.

7. Al Ula Museum

The Al Ula Museum showcases the region’s history, archaeology, and culture, giving visitors a deeper understanding of its heritage.

8. Harrat Viewpoint

Located on top of an ancient volcanic field, the Harrat Viewpoint offers sweeping views of Al Ula’s valleys, canyons, and rock formations.

9. Al Ula Oasis

A lush green escape, the Al Ula Oasis features palm groves, farms, and walking trails shaded by towering date palms.

10. Desert Safari and Stargazing

The desert around Al Ula is perfect for safaris, camel rides, and stargazing under some of the clearest night skies in Saudi Arabia.

11. Adventure in Al Ula Mountains

Al Ula offers thrilling activities like hiking, zip-lining, and rock climbing among its sandstone cliffs and valleys.

12. Al Ula Fresh Market

Visit the local fresh market to experience traditional Saudi culture, taste local foods, and shop for souvenirs.

13. Al Gharameel Rock Formations

Located outside the city, the Al Gharameel Rocks are known for their dramatic shapes and are best visited at sunset.

14. Al Ula Winter Park

Winter Park is the central hub for events, festivals, food stalls, and entertainment, especially during the Al Ula season.

15. Balloon Festival

If you visit during the festival season, the Hot Air Balloon Festival offers a magical way to see Al Ula from above.

Final Words

Al Ula is more than just a destination—it’s a journey through history, culture, and natural beauty. From ancient Nabataean tombs to modern landmarks like Maraya, the city offers travelers an unforgettable adventure. These 15 best places to visit in Al Ula capture the essence of this extraordinary desert paradise.

FAQs About Places to Visit in Al Ula

What is Al Ula famous for?

Al Ula is famous for Hegra (Madain Saleh), its unique rock formations, and rich archaeological history.

Can you visit Al Ula year-round?

Yes, but the best time to visit is from October to March when the weather is cooler.

Is Al Ula family-friendly?

Absolutely. With attractions like Elephant Rock, the Oasis, and family festivals, it’s suitable for all ages.

Do I need a ticket for Hegra?

Yes, entry to Hegra requires a ticket, which can be booked online or through local tour operators.
