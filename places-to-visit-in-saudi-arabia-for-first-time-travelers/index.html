Places to Visit in Saudi Arabia for First Time Travelers

Updated January 20, 2021 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

If you're visiting Saudi Arabia for the first time, you might be surprised by how diverse the country is. From ancient ruins in the desert to modern city skylines and green mountain towns, there’s a lot to seeand each region has its own character. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or just experiencing a new culture, here are a few places that make a great starting point.

AlUla is one of the country’s most impressive and historic areas. Located in the northwest, it’s home to Hegra, Saudi Arabia’s first UNESCO World Heritage Site, with rock-carved tombs that date back over 2,000 years. The desert landscapes around AlUla are dramatic and beautiful, with massive sandstone formations and open skies. It’s a peaceful place where you can explore at your own pace, far from the noise of big cities.

If you want something more lively, head to Jeddah, especially the Corniche, a long stretch of seaside walkway along the Red Sea. It’s popular with locals and tourists alike. You’ll find restaurants, cafés, public art, and a relaxed atmosphere—perfect for an evening stroll. Jeddah is also known for its historic old town, Al-Balad, which has traditional buildings made of coral stone and narrow alleyways full of shops and markets.

In the capital, Riyadh, one of the best places to start is the National Museum.

It gives a solid introduction to Saudi history, culture, and the changes the country has gone through in recent decades. The museum is modern and well-designed, and it’s a good way to get context before exploring other parts of the country. Riyadh also has busy shopping areas, large parks, and modern architecture, offering a different side of Saudi life.

For something more natural, the Edge of the World is a must-see. It’s a massive cliff on the edge of the Tuwaiq Escarpment, just outside Riyadh. Standing at the top, you get sweeping views of the empty desert stretching out below. It’s especially striking at sunset. The drive there is part of the experience too, taking you through wide, open landscapes that feel almost untouched.

If you're looking for cooler weather and green scenery, head south to Abha. It sits high in the mountains and has a completely different feel from the rest of the country. Temperatures are mild, even in summer, and the area has parks, markets, and cable cars that run over the valleys. It’s a great place to relax and take in the views, especially if you want a break from the heat.

Each of these places shows a different side of Saudi Arabia. From old villages to seaside views, mountain escapes to modern cities, the country offers more variety than many people expect. If possible, try to visit more than one region. The distances can be long, but domestic flights are easy to book and well-connected. No matter where you go, you’ll find a mix of hospitality, culture, and places you won’t find anywhere else.
