Top 20 Restaurants in Al Ula 2025 Updated

Updated August 27, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Planning a trip to Al Ula in 2025? This breathtaking city, known for its UNESCO heritage sites and desert landscapes, also offers an impressive dining scene.

From authentic Saudi cuisine to international fine dining under the stars, the restaurants in Al Ula deliver unforgettable flavors.

In this guide, we’ve handpicked the Top 20 restaurants in Al Ula, highlighting their pros, cons, prices, and what they’re best known for—so you can enjoy a perfect meal during your visit.

Top 20 Restaurants in Al Ula

1. Suhail Restaurant

  • Pros: Fine dining with modern Saudi cuisine, cultural experience
  • Cons: Expensive compared to local options
  • Price: $$$
  • Best For: Foodies, cultural dining, couples

2. Al Nakheel Restaurant Al Ula

  • Pros: Authentic Middle Eastern dishes, traditional vibes
  • Cons: Gets crowded during peak hours
  • Price: $$
  • Best For: Families, group dining

3. Daimumah Restaurant (Farm-to-Table)

  • Pros: Fresh organic ingredients, outdoor dining experience
  • Cons: Limited seating, must book in advance
  • Price: $$$
  • Best For: Health-conscious diners, unique experiences

4. Harrat Restaurant (Habitas Al Ula)

  • Pros: Stunning desert views, international menu
  • Cons: Pricey for budget travelers
  • Price: $$$
  • Best For: Luxury dining, tourists

5. Al Khayal Restaurant

  • Pros: Grilled meats & kebabs, affordable
  • Cons: Simple interior
  • Price: $-$$
  • Best For: Casual meals, quick bites

6. Somewhere Restaurant

  • Pros: Fusion cuisine, trendy interior design
  • Cons: Portions may feel small
  • Price: $$-$$$
  • Best For: Young travelers, couples

7. Madrasat Addeera Café

  • Pros: Artisan coffee, light meals, cultural setting
  • Cons: Limited full-meal options
  • Price: $-$$
  • Best For: Coffee lovers, light snacks

8. Pink Camel Pastry Boutique

  • Pros: Famous for pastries & desserts, Instagram-worthy café
  • Cons: Not ideal for full meals
  • Price: $$
  • Best For: Desserts, social media photos

9. Al Gharameel Desert Dining

  • Pros: Outdoor dining under stars, traditional Saudi experience
  • Cons: Only available via booking, weather dependent
  • Price: $$$
  • Best For: Tourists, unique dining experience

10. Al Ula Grill House

  • Pros: Steaks & BBQ, family-friendly
  • Cons: Limited vegetarian options
  • Price: $$
  • Best For: Meat lovers, families

11. Shaden Resort Restaurant

  • Pros: Fine dining with luxury resort atmosphere
  • Cons: Expensive, limited local dishes
  • Price: $$$
  • Best For: Honeymoons, luxury stays

12. Al Talah Traditional Kitchen

  • Pros: Authentic Saudi home-style food, cultural vibes
  • Cons: Limited menu variety
  • Price: $-$$
  • Best For: Traditional food lovers, cultural tourists

13. The Heart of Oasis Café

  • Pros: Surrounded by palm trees, organic dishes
  • Cons: Slow service sometimes
  • Price: $$
  • Best For: Relaxed meals, nature lovers

14. Circolo Italian Restaurant

  • Pros: Authentic Italian pizzas & pastas, cozy interior
  • Cons: Higher prices than local eateries
  • Price: $$-$$$
  • Best For: Italian cuisine fans, couples

15. Al Manahil Family Restaurant

  • Pros: Affordable, wide menu selection
  • Cons: Basic interiors
  • Price: $
  • Best For: Families, budget dining

16. Desert Lotus Restaurant

  • Pros: Asian fusion flavors, stylish décor
  • Cons: Pricey compared to portion sizes
  • Price: $$-$$$
  • Best For: Food explorers, modern dining

17. Roadside BBQ Al Ula

  • Pros: Local-style grilled meats, affordable
  • Cons: Outdoor only, seasonal
  • Price: $-$$
  • Best For: Street-food lovers, casual eaters

18. Al Malaz Restaurant

  • Pros: Wide Middle Eastern menu, family seating
  • Cons: Not luxury-level dining
  • Price: $$
  • Best For: Families, group dining

19. Banyan Tree Resort Restaurant

  • Pros: World-class fine dining, scenic desert backdrop
  • Cons: Extremely pricey
  • Price: $$$$
  • Best For: Ultra-luxury travelers, special occasions

20. Heritage Oasis Café

  • Pros: Traditional drinks, light snacks in heritage setting
  • Cons: Not for full meals
  • Price: $-$$
  • Best For: Cultural experience, tea & coffee lovers

Final Words

Al Ula is not only a land of history and desert beauty but also a rising hub for food lovers. From traditional Saudi kitchens to luxury desert resorts, the variety of restaurants in Al Ula ensures every visitor finds a perfect dining experience. Whether you’re on a budget or seeking fine dining under the stars, this updated 2025 list will help you decide where to eat next.

FAQs About Restaurants in Al Ula

Q1: What is the best restaurant in Al Ula for fine dining?

Suhail Restaurant and Harrat at Habitas Al Ula are top fine dining choices.

Q2: Are there budget-friendly restaurants in Al Ula?

Yes, Al Khayal, Al Manahil, and roadside BBQ stalls are great affordable options.

Q3: Which restaurants in Al Ula are vegetarian-friendly?

Somewhere Restaurant, Daimumah, and Circolo Italian offer vegetarian choices.

Q4: Are there unique dining experiences in Al Ula?

Yes, Al Gharameel Desert Dining and Daimumah farm-to-table offer unforgettable setups.

Q5: Do Al Ula resorts have restaurants?

Yes, Shaden Resort and Banyan Tree Resort feature luxury dining for their guests.
