Top 20 Must-Try Restaurants in Makkah for Every Food Lover

Updated August 26, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Makkah, the holiest city in Islam, welcomes millions of visitors every year. Along with its spiritual importance, the city also offers a diverse food scene that blends traditional Arabian flavors with international cuisines.

Whether you’re looking for authentic Saudi dishes, Middle Eastern delights, or international dining options, Makkah has something for everyone.

In this updated 2025 guide, we explore the Top 20 Restaurants in Makkah that provide unforgettable dining experiences for both locals and visitors.

Best Restaurants in Makkah

1. Al Baik

Al Baik is a household name in Saudi Arabia and a must-visit restaurant in Makkah. Famous for its fried chicken, sandwiches, and seafood, Al Baik offers affordable meals with consistent quality. The quick service and family-friendly environment make it a favorite among pilgrims and locals alike.

2. Al Tazaj

Al Tazaj is well-known for its grilled chicken and freshly prepared meals. With a focus on healthy and flavorful food, it’s a perfect stop for families seeking delicious yet wholesome dining. Their traditional Middle Eastern rice dishes are also highly recommended.

3. Al Deyafa Restaurant

Located near the Haram, Al Deyafa is known for its buffet-style dining experience. Guests can enjoy a wide variety of international cuisines, including Arabian, Indian, and Western options, making it ideal for large groups and families.

4. Al Rehab Restaurant

Al Rehab offers authentic Middle Eastern cuisine with a focus on Saudi specialties. From kebabs to rice dishes, the restaurant provides a traditional dining experience with generous portions and a welcoming atmosphere.

5. Albaik Express (Haram Branch)

This branch of Al Baik is especially convenient for pilgrims due to its close location to Masjid Al-Haram. It’s perfect for those who want quick yet satisfying meals after long hours of worship.

6. Foul & Tamees Traditional Eateries

Makkah has many small local eateries serving foul (fava beans) and tamees (fresh flatbread). These spots are perfect for a traditional breakfast and give visitors an authentic taste of Saudi street food culture.

7. Al Shorfa Restaurant

With a view overlooking the Haram, Al Shorfa offers both international and local dishes in a fine-dining setting. It’s popular among travelers who want a more luxurious dining experience while staying close to the holy sites.

8. Hardee’s

For visitors who crave fast food, Hardee’s in Makkah provides burgers, fries, and quick meals. It’s widely accessible and suitable for pilgrims looking for familiar international tastes.

9. Zamzam Lobby Restaurant

Located in the Zamzam Pullman Hotel, this restaurant offers a wide buffet with Middle Eastern and Asian dishes. The elegant setting and diverse menu make it a popular choice for hotel guests.

10. Shobra Restaurant

Shobra is famous for traditional Hijazi dishes. Their stews, rice dishes, and grilled meats are flavorful and authentic, giving travelers a true taste of local cuisine.

11. Al Diyafa Food Court

Inside Abraj Al Bait Mall, Al Diyafa Food Court offers a variety of dining options, from international chains to local favorites. It’s convenient for those staying near the Haram and looking for quick choices.

13. Al Khairat Restaurant

This restaurant is loved for its affordability and wide menu selection. It offers Middle Eastern staples like shawarma, kebabs, and rice platters, making it popular with budget travelers.

14. Al Khair Restaurant (South Asian Specialties)

This spot is known for serving flavorful Indian and Pakistani dishes. Their biryani, curries, and freshly baked bread are especially popular among South Asian pilgrims.

15. Starbucks (Abraj Al Bait)

For coffee lovers, Starbucks provides a familiar taste with a variety of drinks and light snacks. Located inside Abraj Al Bait, it’s a convenient stop for travelers looking for a quick break.

16. Five Guys

Five Guys brings the taste of American-style burgers and fries to Makkah. Its fresh ingredients and customizable menu make it a favorite for those seeking a casual dining experience.

17. Arabesque Restaurant

Arabesque, located in Al Safwah Towers, offers Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisine in a refined setting. It’s ideal for those looking for fine dining close to Masjid Al-Haram.

18. Broast House

Broast House is another local favorite for fried chicken. Known for its crispy broast and quick service, it’s a reliable spot for comfort food at affordable prices.

19. Hyderabad House

For those craving authentic Indian and Hyderabadi dishes, Hyderabad House is a great choice. Their biryanis and curries are well-loved by both locals and South Asian pilgrims.

20. Afandim Turkish Restaurant

Afandim brings the rich flavors of Turkey to Makkah with dishes like kebabs, pide, and baklava. It’s a must-visit for anyone wanting to explore Turkish cuisine in the holy city.

Final Words

Makkah offers much more than its spiritual significance—it’s also home to a vibrant and diverse food culture. From local Saudi favorites to international chains and fine-dining restaurants, the city caters to every palate and budget. Whether you’re in search of a quick meal near the Haram or a lavish dining experience, this list of the Top 20 Restaurants in Makkah 2025 Updated will help you discover the best places to eat during your stay.

FAQ About Restaurants in Makkah

What food is Makkah famous for?

Makkah is famous for traditional Saudi dishes such as kabsa, foul & tamees, and various grilled meats.

Are there vegetarian options in Makkah restaurants?

Yes, many restaurants offer vegetarian dishes, including Middle Eastern mezze, salads, and international options.

Is dining near the Haram expensive?

Prices vary—food courts and fast-food chains are affordable, while hotel restaurants and fine-dining spots are more expensive.

Can I find international restaurants in Makkah?

Yes, Makkah has international chains like Starbucks, Hardee’s, Five Guys, and Fogo de ChÃ£o, alongside local favorites.

What is the best time to eat out in Makkah?

The best time is after prayer times when restaurants are less crowded, especially after Maghrib and Isha prayers.
