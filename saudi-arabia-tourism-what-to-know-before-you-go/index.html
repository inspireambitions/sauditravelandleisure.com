Saudi Arabia Tourism: What to Know Before You Go

2021-01-21

Saudi Arabia has recently stepped into the spotlight as a major travel destination.

Known for its religious significance, the country is now promoting its cultural, natural, and historical sites to international tourists. With a mix of ancient ruins, modern cities, and vast deserts, Saudi Arabia offers something unique in the region.

A New Era for Tourism

Until a few years ago, Saudi Arabia was mostly closed to leisure tourism. That changed in 2019, when the country introduced a new tourist visa system. Citizens from over 50 countries can now apply online for an eVisa, making travel much easier and faster. The goal is clear: to welcome more visitors and diversify the economy beyond oil.

What to See and Do

Tourism in Saudi Arabia offers more variety than many expect:

History and heritage: Visit ancient sites like AlUla (home to Hegra, a UNESCO World Heritage Site) and Diriyah, the original home of the Saudi royal family.

City life: Explore Riyadh, the capital, with its museums, skyscrapers, and growing entertainment zones. In Jeddah, walk the Corniche, visit traditional souks, and enjoy Red Sea views.

Nature and adventure: Hike the Asir Mountains near Abha, go diving in the Red Sea, or visit dramatic landscapes like the Edge of the World, just outside Riyadh.

Best Time to Visit

The best months to visit Saudi Arabia are from October to March, when temperatures are cooler and more comfortable for outdoor activities. Summer can be very hot, especially in central and southern regions.

What to Know Before You Go

Saudi Arabia is safe for travelers, but it's important to follow local customs:

Dress code: Modest clothing is required in public. Women are not required to wear an abaya, but shoulders and knees should be covered.

Behavior: Public displays of affection are discouraged. Alcohol is not allowed. Respect for local traditions goes a long way.

Religion: Non-Muslims cannot visit Mecca or Medina, but can travel freely in other parts of the country.

You should also check up-to-date travel guidelines, including visa requirements and COVID-19 rules, before your trip.

Why Visit Now?

Saudi Arabia is still a fresh destination for many tourists. That means fewer crowds, new experiences, and a sense of discovery. The tourism infrastructure is growing fast, with new hotels, museums, and transportation options opening every year. If you’re looking for something different from the usual tourist trail, this is a great time to go.

Popular cities to include in your itinerary:

Riyadh: Best for modern culture and history

Jeddah: Best for seaside vibes and art

AlUla: Best for ancient history and stunning desert views

Abha: Best for cool weather and green landscapes

Respect local laws, plan your itinerary ahead, and try local food like kabsa (rice with meat and spices) and mutabbaq (stuffed pastry). Many Saudis speak English, especially in cities, and are happy to help tourists.
