Saudi Arabia’s Most Famous Places

Updated January 20, 2021 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide


Saudi Arabia is home to some of the most well known and meaningful places in the region. These sites reflect the country’s religious roots, deep history, and modern growth.

At the top of the list is Mecca, the holiest city in Islam. Every year, millions of Muslims from around the world travel there for the Hajj and Umrah pilgrimages. Entry is only allowed for Muslims, but even from afar, Mecca holds major spiritual importance.

Medina is another major religious center. It’s where the Prophet Muhammad is buried, and the Prophet’s Mosque is one of the largest and most visited in the world. Like Mecca, Medina is open only to Muslims in its central areas, but its influence is felt far beyond its borders.

For history and natural beauty, AlUla stands out. This area in the northwest is home to Hegra, Saudi Arabia’s first UNESCO World Heritage Site. With massive stone tombs carved into cliffs, AlUla is often compared to Petra in Jordan. It’s also becoming a hub for tourism, with cultural festivals, outdoor art, and eco-friendly resorts.

In Riyadh, the Kingdom Tower is a modern icon. Its sleek shape and sky bridge draw visitors looking for views of the capital city from above. It's one of the tallest buildings in the country and a symbol of Saudi Arabia’s urban growth.

Down by the Red Sea, Jeddah’s Floating Mosque is a favorite spot for visitors. Officially known as the Al Rahma Mosque, it sits right at the water’s edge and appears to float when the tide is high. It's a peaceful place to watch the sunset or take photos of the sea meeting the city.

These famous places show how Saudi Arabia blends religion, culture, and modern life. Whether you're visiting for spiritual reasons or just to explore, each site tells part of the country’s story.
