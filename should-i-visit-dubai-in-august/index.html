Health & Safety: How To Stay Well in August

Hydration is the single most important discipline. Drink small amounts frequently rather than large quantities sporadically. Avoid heavy activity between 11:00 and 16:00. Choose light, loose clothing and stay in shaded or air-conditioned environments as much as possible.

Watch for signs of heat exhaustion: dizziness, excessive sweating followed by reduced sweating, rapid pulse, headache, nausea. For children and older adults, shorten outdoor exposure and plan medical contingencies. If you have respiratory conditions, check local air reports during dust events and keep inhalers or medications handy.

Sunburn prevention is critical. Even short exposures can cause substantial sun damage. Apply broad-spectrum sunscreen before leaving shade and reapply after swimming.
