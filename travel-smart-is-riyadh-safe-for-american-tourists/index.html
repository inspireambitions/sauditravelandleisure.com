Required Travel Documents and Visas

The first thing you need to do before going to Saudi Arabia is to get the right travel papers. For American tourists, it is a must to have a valid tourist visa. You can apply for this visa online by using the official Visit Saudi website. Make sure your passport will not expire for at least six months after your planned leaving date.

Tourist visas let you enter and leave the country more than once. You can stay up to 90 days in Saudi Arabia within one year. You cannot work in Saudi Arabia with a tourist visa. If you stay longer than allowed, the fines are heavy, and you could also get sent back home.

It is smart to check the most recent Saudi Arabia country information. Entry rules can change at any time, so look up the latest updates before your trip. You should always keep digital and paper copies of your visa and passport with you. Saudi authorities can ask to see them during any security check.

Health Insurance and Medical Requirements

Health insurance is required for everyone who wants to visit Saudi Arabia, even for people from the US. With tourist visas, you get coverage for COVID-19. Still, it is a good idea to look at your insurance policy and make sure it also covers bigger medical problems, cancellation of plans, and a way to get you out of the country if you need it.

The Ministry of Health in Saudi Arabia says that you should find out what vaccines you might need before you go. This is very important for people who plan to visit religious sites or are going to the country from other places that have some infectious diseases, like yellow fever or polio. There have been cases of Middle East respiratory syndrome in recent years, so the right health steps are important.

There are good hospitals and clinics in the major cities of Saudi Arabia, like Riyadh. You might need to pay first if you need to get help at a private place. Saudi Arabia’s official currency is the Saudi Arabian Riyal (SAR). In Riyadh, most people use cash or credit cards for shopping and other buys. You will find ATMs in many places across the city. It is a good idea to ask your bank about any fees for using your card in another country.

For communication, buy a local SIM card when you get to Saudi Arabia. This helps you get good rates for calls and data. Companies like STC, Mobily, and Zain have prepaid SIM cards. You will need to show your passport to get one.

Most hotels and shopping malls give free Wi-Fi. Make sure you use secure networks to protect yourself from online threats. When you have steady ways to talk to people, it’s easy to get around the city, talk with friends and family, and reach help if you need it.

Food and Water Safety Precautions

Making sure food and water are safe is very important in Saudi Arabia. You can follow these steps to help keep yourself safe:

  • Do not drink tap water. It is better to drink sealed bottled water.
  • Do not eat food that is raw or not cooked all the way, especially meat and seafood.
  • Drink and eat only dairy products that are pasteurized. Stay away from any food that is not.
  • Be careful about eating at street vendors or in crowded public places. Choose restaurants that are well known.

Always keep your hands clean. Try not to use ice cubes in drinks. These habits can help lower your chances of getting stomach problems or infectious diseases while you are in Saudi Arabia.
