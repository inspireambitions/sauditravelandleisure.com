Where Muscat Is â Geography and Regional Context

Muscat on the Map

Muscat occupies a strategic coastal position on the Gulf of Oman, roughly halfway along the northeastern edge of the Arabian Peninsula. It is cradled by the Hajar Mountains to the west and north and opens to the Arabian Sea to the east. This seafront location historically made it a natural port and trading hub between Arabia, East Africa, and South Asia.

Geopolitically, Muscat is the capital and largest city of the Sultanate of Oman. The metropolitan area includes several wilayat (provinces) and spans thousands of square kilometers, combining seaside districts, mountain foothills, and irrigated valleys called wadi.
