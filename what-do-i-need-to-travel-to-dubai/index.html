Understanding Entry Basics

Immigration officers commonly expect to see a return or onward ticket and a confirmed place to stay for the duration of your visit. Health, Vaccinations, and Medical Requirements

If you are traveling from a country with yellow fever risk, you will need proof of yellow fever vaccination. Other standard advice applies: bring basic travel medicine (rehydration salts, analgesics, antiseptics) and a small first-aid kit.

Customs, Prohibited Items, and Cultural Restrictions

UAE customs is strict about items that contradict public morality, religious sensitivities, and national security. Drones and Photography Equipment

Drones are regulated in the UAE and need permits for recreational or commercial use. If you plan to bring a drone, obtain permission in advance and register it where required. Photography in public places is generally fine, but exercise discretion around government buildings, military installations, and private property. Money, Currency, and Payments

The UAE dirham (AED) is used everywhere. Major credit cards are widely accepted, but small vendors, taxis, and markets may prefer cash. Notify your bank of travel dates and locations to reduce the risk of transaction holds. Carry a small amount of AED on arrival for visas, taxi fares, and incidentals; airport exchange counters or official ATMs are convenient.
