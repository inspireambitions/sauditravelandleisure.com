Introduction

Dubai draws millions of visitors every year with its skyline, shopping, and rapidly evolving cultural scene. If you're planning a trip from Saudi Arabia or elsewhere in the Gulf, clarity about entry rules, paperwork, and practical on-the-ground arrangements makes the difference between a smooth arrival and travel stress.

Short answer: You need a valid passport (at least six months’ validity), the correct visa or entry permit for your nationality, proof of onward or return travel, and basic health and travel documents. Beyond paperwork, plan logistics — flights or land crossings, accommodation confirmation, travel insurance, and an understanding of local laws and customs — to ensure a seamless visit.

This article explains, step by step, what documents and preparations are required, how to secure the right visa, the rules for different nationalities, practical arrival and departure procedures, and the cultural and legal behaviours that travellers must observe. It also provides regional travel planning strategies and tools from Saudi Travel & Leisure so you can move confidently between the Kingdom and the UAE.

Our main message is clear: with the right preparation you can move beyond uncertainty to travel to Dubai with confidence, focus on experiences that matter, and combine your trip with travel across the Gulf using the planning frameworks we recommend.

Who Needs a Visa — The Basics

Passport Validity and Passport Types

All travellers must hold a passport that is valid for at least six months beyond the date of entry. Machine-readable regular passports are required; many airlines will refuse boarding if your passport does not meet this requirement. Diplomatic and official passports have different rules — consult your embassy before travel.

If you hold multiple passports or travel documents, always enter and exit the UAE on the same passport to avoid exit control complications. This rule is strictly enforced.

Who Can Get a Visa on Arrival

A large number of nationalities are eligible for a visa on arrival at Dubai’s international airports. Eligible passports are stamped on arrival with a visit permit valid for a specified period (commonly 30, 90, or longer depending on nationality). This convenient system eliminates the need for advance paperwork for many travellers, but it is crucial to confirm your eligibility before travel.

Who Needs a Prearranged Visa

Some nationalities must obtain a visa before departure. Whether you apply through an airline, a hotel, a travel agency, or a sponsor in the UAE, make sure you receive an electronic copy or reference number that you can present if required during arrival checks.

Special Cases: GCC Nationals, Residents, and Transit Visitors

Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) citizens typically do not need a visa and may travel using national ID cards in many cases, though a passport is recommended. Residency permits issued by GCC countries, the U.S., the U.K., and EU states can affect visa-on-arrival eligibility for some nationalities — check the latest rules for your combination of passport and residency.

If you are transiting through Dubai for a short stay, there are dedicated transit visa options (48-hour and 96-hour) that you can request in advance or, in some cases, obtain on arrival, depending on your itinerary.

Detailed Visa Pathways and How to Apply

Visa on Arrival — What To Expect

If your nationality qualifies for a visa on arrival, officials will stamp your passport at immigration. Requirements typical at arrival include a passport with minimum validity of six months, and occasionally proof of a confirmed onward or return ticket. Some travellers are asked for a hotel reservation confirmation. Immigration officers may conduct random checks or request printed copies of visa confirmation pages, so it’s wise to carry a printed copy of any preapproved visa or hotel booking.

When arriving on an airline that offers online visa services, you may be able to obtain your permit electronically before landing. Emirates and other carriers facilitate this for passengers whose itineraries qualify; make sure to check the airline’s visa services when booking.

Prearranged Tourist Visas — Options and Documents

If your nationality requires a prearranged visa, you typically apply through a UAE embassy, a visa application service, your airline, or a UAE-based sponsor (hotel, tour operator, or private sponsor). Types include short-stay single-entry, multiple-entry, and long-stay visitor visas. Standard documents asked for are:

  • Passport copy with at least six months’ validity
  • Passport-sized photograph
  • Confirmed travel itinerary and airline ticket
  • Proof of accommodation for your stay
  • Payment for visa fees

Processing times vary by method and nationality. If a hotel or agent sponsors your visa, get an electronic copy or visa number to present on arrival.

Transit Visas and Short Layovers

For short stopovers where you want to leave the airport, 48-hour and 96-hour transit visas are available for eligible travellers. Airlines often offer to facilitate these visas for passengers with a qualifying itinerary. If you plan a short city visit during a layover, apply for the transit visa ahead of time where possible to avoid airport queues.

Work, Study, and Long-Term Stays

Tourist entry permits do not allow work or study. If you plan to work, join a program, or stay long-term, arrange the correct residency visa before undertaking any formal or paid work. That process involves a sponsor, medical tests, and residence permit formalities once you are in the UAE.

Common Nationality Rules and Notable Exceptions

U.S. and European Passport Holders

United States and many European passport holders are typically issued a visit visa on arrival; the period and conditions can vary, so ensure passport validity and carry proof of onward travel. Some nationalities receive a 90-day stamp while others receive 30 days — check before travel.

Indian Nationals and Recent Policy Updates

Indian passport holders may be eligible for special visa-on-arrival schemes if they hold valid U.S., U.K., EU, Canadian, or other qualifying residence documents or visas. Recent changes have introduced short-term arrival permits for certain cases. Because rules change periodically, confirm current eligibility and fees before departure.

Non-Standard or Travel Document Holders

If you hold a travel document rather than a full national passport, visa rules differ significantly. Many travel documents are not accepted for entry; check with the UAE embassy well in advance.

Diplomatic, Official, and UN Travel

If traveling on diplomatic or official passports, or for the United Nations, separate visa procedures and document requirements apply and generally require formal letters and additional documentation from sponsoring authorities.

Practical Pre-Departure Checklist

  1. Ensure your passport is valid for at least six months from your entry date. Apply for renewal early if necessary.
  2. Confirm whether you’re eligible for visa on arrival or need a prearranged visa. If required, secure the visa and carry a printed copy of the visa number and hotel confirmation.
  3. Book and confirm return or onward tickets—immigration may request proof.
  4. Purchase travel insurance that covers medical evacuation and unforeseen events.
  5. Check medication rules and secure permits for controlled prescriptions if needed.
  6. Prepare printed copies of key documents: passport, visa, hotel booking, travel insurance, and emergency contacts.

(That checklist is a practical, single-at-a-glance list to use during preparation.)

Arrival Procedures: At the Airport and Beyond

Navigating Dubai Airports

Dubai has two major airports: Dubai International (DXB) and Dubai World Central (DWC). Both offer robust public transport, taxis, and rent-a-car services. Immigration lanes separate e-gates, visa-on-arrival, and visa-checked travellers; expect lines during peak hours. If you’re selected for additional screening, remain calm and provide the documentation requested.

Immigration Screening and Common Pitfalls

Immigration officers may ask for a return ticket, proof of accommodation, or financial means. Avoid common pitfalls by carrying printed confirmations and knowing the exact dates of your stay. Travelers have been denied boarding or entry with insufficient passport validity, missing visas, or previous un-cancelled UAE visas; verify cancellations on prior visas if applicable.

Baggage, Customs, and Declared Items

Declare large sums of cash and certain restricted items at customs. Prohibited imports include some medications, religious materials intended for proselytizing, and items that contradict public morals. Firearms, even inert parts and shells, are strictly banned without express permission. When in doubt, check with the UAE embassy before travel.

Getting a Local SIM, Currency, and Immediate Needs

At the airport you can purchase local SIM cards (Du, Etisalat, Virgin Mobile) and currency. If your trip involves business or extended stays, consider buying a tourist SIM with generous data or an eSIM in advance. Cash is useful for taxis and small purchases; the dirham (AED) is pegged to the US dollar, and major cards are widely accepted.

Accommodation, Sponsorship, and Hotel-Linked Visas

Hotels as Visa Sponsors

Many hotels will sponsor a tourist visa for guests. If your visa is processed by a hotel, ensure you receive the electronic visa confirmation and check that your travel dates align with the visa validity stated. Keep a copy of the sponsorship confirmation accessible during transit and at immigration.

Short-Term Rentals and Family Stays

If you stay with family or in private accommodation, a host may act as sponsor in certain visa categories. For private stays, carry proof of relationship (where required) and a tenancy contract or invitation letter that contains full host contact details.

Check-In Requirements and Identification

Hotels require passports at check-in and may keep copies for registration. Some properties ask for a deposit or ID card during your stay. Respect local policies and carry a digital copy of your passport and visa as a backup.

Health, Medication, and Safety

Vaccination and COVID-19

As of the latest guidance, routine travel rarely requires COVID-specific entry requirements, but rules can shift. Carry up-to-date vaccination records where applicable and check airline and UAE government portals before travel.

Medication Rules and Controlled Substances

Some medications available elsewhere are restricted or require a health permit in the UAE. If you rely on prescription medicine, obtain an official prescription in English and, where needed, apply for an import permit from UAE health authorities prior to departure. Carry medication in original packaging with a clear prescription label.

Medical Care and Travel Insurance

Dubai offers world-class medical facilities. Ensure your travel insurance covers treatment and medical evacuation if needed. Keep the number for your insurer and the local emergency numbers handy.

Laws, Customs, and What Not To Do

Public Behaviour and Cultural Norms

Public displays of affection beyond holding hands are discouraged and, in some situations, can attract legal consequences. Dress modestly in conservative areas, and follow mosque-specific rules when visiting places of worship: women should carry a shawl to cover hair when required, and both sexes should dress modestly.

Alcohol, Nightlife, and Legal Limits

Alcohol is available in licensed hotels and venues for those over 21; public drunkenness is unlawful. If you consume alcohol, you must do so responsibly and only in licensed locations. Driving under the influence is a serious offense with severe penalties.

Photography and Respecting Privacy

Do not photograph individuals, especially women, without permission. Avoid photographing sensitive government or military installations. When in commercial or tourist areas, follow signage and staff instructions regarding photography.

Customs and Prohibited Items

The UAE has strict rules on items such as e-cigarettes, certain medications, religious materials for proselytizing, pornography, and items that conflict with moral codes. Possession of some items can lead to severe penalties, so verify before packing.

Money, Costs, and Tipping

Currency and Payment Methods

The dirham (AED) is the local currency. Credit and debit cards are universally accepted in Dubai, but always carry some cash for taxis, small vendors, and markets. ATMs are widely available; notify your bank about travel to avoid card blocks.

Typical Costs and Budgeting

Dubai ranges from budget-friendly street food to ultra-luxury experiences. Plan your budget according to the activities you value: shopping and dining can vary widely in cost. The metro, buses, and taxis provide affordable city travel; ride-hailing apps are popular and often offer transparent pricing.

Tipping

Tipping is appreciated but not obligatory. A small amount for drivers, hotel staff, and restaurant servers is customary when service is good.

Getting Around: Transport and Regional Connections

Public Transport in Dubai

Dubai’s metro system connects key districts and is efficient for many tourist routes. Buses, trams, ferries, and taxis fill gaps. Purchase a Nol card for seamless fare payment across public transport modes.

Taxis and Ride-Hailing

Taxis are metered and regulated; women-only cars are available for female passengers. Ride-hailing services like Careem operate across the city as convenient alternatives.

Driving, Car Rentals, and Road Rules

An international driving permit plus your home license may be required depending on your nationality. Road rules are strictly enforced; speeding and distracted driving carry heavy fines. If renting a car, inspect the vehicle carefully and understand insurance coverage.

Flights and Overland Travel From Saudi Arabia

Frequent flights connect Saudi cities such as Riyadh and Jeddah to Dubai; these are short, reliable options that many travellers prefer for speed. Seasonal Considerations and What to Pack

Best Time to Visit

Dubai’s peak season runs from late October through March when temperatures are comfortable for outdoor activities. Summers are extremely hot, with daytime temperatures often exceeding 40°C (104°F), making indoor attractions and waterparks more appealing.

Packing Tips by Season

Travel in cooler months: include layers for cooler evenings, smart-casual outfits for dining and urban exploring, and comfortable shoes for walking.

Travel in summer: pack breathable clothing, sun protection, and plan activities in air-conditioned venues. Always carry a refillable water bottle and sun protection.

For both seasons, pack modest clothing options for mosque visits and conservative districts, along with a lightweight scarf for women to use when necessary.

Practical Tips for Smooth Entry and Exit

Keep Your Travel and Visa Records Handy

Carry printed and digital copies of your visa, passport photo page, travel insurance, hotel confirmations, and return ticket. Immigration can request these at any time.

Check for Exit Bans and Outstanding Legal Issues

Be aware that the UAE enforces strict exit controls for individuals with outstanding legal or financial issues. If you have legal matters in the UAE or are unsure, verify your status with the appropriate UAE portals or your embassy before travel.

Cancellation and Overstay Fees

If you overstay your visa, you will be charged fines per day. Extensions may be available for some visa types, but they require action at immigration offices and original documents. Budget for potential extension fees if your plans are fluid.

Cultural Experiences and Practical Recommendations

Combining Dubai With Nearby Destinations

If you’re planning a broader Gulf itinerary, Dubai pairs naturally with Abu Dhabi for cultural institutions and national museums, and with Saudi destinations for longer cultural itineraries. How to Choose Accommodation Based on Your Trip Style

Luxury shopping and beachfront resorts cluster in areas like Jumeirah and Downtown. Budget travellers will find good-value options in older districts and near transport hubs. For families, look for hotel facilities geared toward children and convenient access to family-oriented attractions.

Local Experiences Worth Planning

Plan for an evening dhow dinner cruise, a sunrise desert experience, and a visit to at least one cultural district and market. For shopping, time your visit around the Dubai Shopping Festival or seasonal sales if budgets and calendars allow.

Dealing With Problems: Lost Documents, Arrest, or Medical Emergency

Losing Your Passport or Visa

Contact your embassy immediately for assistance and to obtain temporary travel documents if necessary. Simultaneously, notify local police and your airline. For Saudi travellers, consular contact information and procedural steps should be obtained before travel and saved with other documents.

Arrest or Legal Issues

If you are detained or face legal proceedings, contact your embassy immediately. Follow official procedures and obtain legal representation. Avoid public disputes and always comply politely with law enforcement to prevent escalation.

Medical Emergencies

If you require urgent care, Dubai hospitals provide emergency services. Call the local emergency number and your travel insurer for guidance on hospital selection and payment procedures.

Smart Rules of Thumb Before Booking

Verify Entry Rules Twice

Visa and entry rules change frequently. Even if you travelled recently with no issues, confirm requirements again within two weeks of departure.

Print Important Documents

Electronic copies are essential, but printed backups can save time during immigration checks and when random screenings occur.

Use Reputable Visa Channels

When a prearranged visa is required, use official embassy channels, recognized visa agencies, or your airline’s visa service. If a hotel is sponsoring your visa, confirm the sponsor details and secure a printed copy of the visa confirmation.

Budget for Additional Fees

Visa processing fees, exit fees for land departures, and potential medical costs can add up. Factor these into your travel budget and maintain an emergency cash buffer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need to show at Dubai immigration?

You should be ready to present your passport (valid for at least six months), your visa or entry permit (if prearranged), and proof of onward or return travel. Carry hotel confirmations and travel insurance details as supporting documents.

Can I get a visa on arrival for Dubai?

Whether you can get a visa on arrival depends on your nationality and residency documents. Many passport holders are eligible for visas on arrival; others must apply in advance. Always check your specific eligibility before departure.

Are there items I absolutely must not bring into the UAE?

Yes. Items such as certain medications without permits, materials deemed offensive to public morals, and certain communication or military equipment are restricted or prohibited. Verify your items against the UAE’s customs list before packing.

What happens if I overstay my visa in Dubai?

Overstaying incurs fines per day and can create complications for future travel to the UAE. Extensions may be possible for some visa types but require timely action at immigration and payment of fees. If your plans change, address extensions before your visa expires.
