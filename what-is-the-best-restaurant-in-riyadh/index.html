What Is the Best Restaurant in Riyadh

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Introduction

Riyadh’s dining scene has evolved faster than many travelers expect. As the city embraces global hospitality and a renewed national focus on leisure travel, Riyadh now hosts a clutch of restaurants that stand shoulder to shoulder with the world’s best—each offering a distinct reason to visit. That abundance makes answering "what is the best restaurant in Riyadh" both a practical question and a question of taste.

Short answer: The single best restaurant depends on what you value most—view and prestige, culinary artistry, or cultural authenticity. For first-time visitors seeking a quintessential Riyadh fine-dining moment—one that combines dramatic setting, polished service, and consistent culinary excellence—The Globe at Al Faisaliah remains the most iconic pick. It delivers a dining experience that is unmistakably Riyadh: elevated, ceremonious, and memorably theatrical.

This article explains why The Globe earns that recommendation, provides a clear framework to evaluate the city's top restaurants, profiles the leading contenders across cuisines and budgets, and gives practical instructions for reservations, dress code, transport, and local etiquette. By the end you'll have a confident, implementable plan for choosing and booking the right restaurant—whether you want a landmark dining night, a hidden neighborhood favorite, or a culinary tour across the Riyadh food map.

Why Choosing a Single "Best" Restaurant Needs a Framework

What "best" actually means in Riyadh right now

"Best" can describe highest culinary mastery, most beautiful setting, best value, or most culturally meaningful experience. Riyadh's restaurants vary dramatically across those axes. A minimalist omakase might be the best for technique; a rooftop lounge with sweeping skyline views is the best for ambience; a classic Saudi home-cooking spot is the best for cultural depth.

To make a useful recommendation you need explicit criteria: what are you optimizing for? Time of visit, companions, budget, dietary restrictions, and desire for spectacle all matter.

The evaluation framework I use

To make confident recommendations, I evaluate restaurants against six practical criteria that matter to travelers and locals alike:

  • Culinary quality: ingredient sourcing, technique, and consistency.
  • Atmosphere and setting: architecture, view, and how the space supports the meal.
  • Service and hospitality: professionalism, cultural sensitivity, and timing.
  • Value: portion sizes, pricing relative to experience, and transparency.
  • Accessibility: reservations, location, and transport logistics.
  • Cultural fit: how the meal matches the visitor’s expectations of Riyadh—whether traditional or cosmopolitan.

This framework lets you decide whether you want "the best steak in Riyadh," "the most Instagrammable dinner," or "the most authentic local meal." The rest of this article applies this grid to the top contenders, giving you the tools to pick—and to plan—your perfect dining night.

A Close Look at Riyadh’s Contenders

Why The Globe Is the Most Iconic Choice

The Globe sits inside the glass orb at the top of Al Faisaliah Tower. The setting is cinematic: a private dining capsule with panoramic city views that mean the architecture and the meal are inseparable. Service at The Globe is formal and polished, which matches the theatrical setting. For travelers who want a single dining experience that reads as definitively “Riyadh” and leaves a lasting impression, The Globe checks the high-ceremony box in ways few other venues do.

Culinary style here leans international with an emphasis on refined presentation and premium ingredients. Portions and prices reflect that positioning, so the experience is best reserved for celebrations, special nights, or a first high-impact impression of the city.

Nozomi and High-Concept Asian Fine Dining

Riyadh’s contemporary scene includes excellence in pan-Asian and modern Japanese restaurants. These venues deliver tight, elegantly executed plates, strong beverage programs (sake and cocktail pairings), and design-forward interiors. They score high on culinary precision and service, and are popular with Riyadh’s dining-focused crowd.

If Japanese technique, attention to plating, and a lively, upscale soundtrack matter to you, these restaurants are outstanding alternatives to Western-style fine dining.

Traditional and Modern Saudi Cuisine

As Saudi Arabia opens up to international tourism, a parallel movement is the elevation of native culinary traditions. Restaurants and private kitchens are now re-interpreting Najdi and Hejazi dishes with contemporary technique—bringing cultural context to the plate without losing soul. For visitors seeking authenticity, these restaurants provide not just food but storytelling: how recipes, spices, and hospitality traditions fit into local life.

Steakhouse and Meat-Focused Excellence

Riyadh has strong steakhouses that cater to meat-focused palates with aged cuts, theatrical service, and well-curated wine and cocktail programs. These are ideal for business meals and celebrations where a robust protein and a polished, masculine ambience are the priority.

Casual Neighborhood Dining and Cafés

For daily meals, Riyadh’s neighborhoods host vibrant cafés and casual restaurants that serve everything from Lebanese mezze and Syrian grills to premium coffee and bakery items. These spots are where locals dine during the week and where you can get a real feel for local routines without formalities.

Detailed Profiles: Where to Go Depending on What You Want

Best For the Ultimate Landmark Experience: The Globe at Al Faisaliah

The Globe’s advantage is its architecture-meets-dining concept. It’s designed as a landmark experience: you’re not just eating; you’re occupying an engineered moment. This matters for travelers who value impression and ceremony.

What to expect: elevated multi-course menu, formal service, dress-forward clientele, and a view that frames Riyadh’s skyline. Timing matters—book for sunset to watch the city shift from day to night.

Reservation tip: ask for a table in the orb with a view and arrive early to experience the public spaces of Al Faisaliah Tower. Consider informing staff if celebrating—small gestures are handled meticulously here.

Best For Modern Asian: Nozomi and Counterparts

These restaurants excel with high-precision sushi, creative small plates, and a social late-night energy. They’re excellent for date nights and groups who want a lively, design-led experience.

What to expect: sharing plates, strong cocktails, an emphasis on presentation, and contemporary playlists. Make reservations for weekend evenings; mention dietary restrictions for bespoke sushi preparations.

Best For Cultural Dining: Modern Najdi and Hejazi Experiences

These restaurants reinterpret Saudi flavors with contemporary technique. Expect slow-cooked meats, fragrant rice, spice balances, and a menu that explains provenance.

What to expect: multi-course tasting menus or sharable plates that mimic the home-chef experience but with restaurant polish. These venues are great for travelers who want cultural insight through food.

Best For Steak and Grills: Classic Steakhouses

High-end steakhouses in Riyadh offer dry-aged cuts, open grills, and an emphasis on precise but bold flavors. They are also often the best places to find curated wine lists.

What to expect: hearty plates, formality, and predictable quality—great for business dinners and celebrations.

Best For Casual, Everyday Dining: Neighborhood Cafés and Bakeries

From specialty coffee shops to regional bakeries, these places are where the city breathes. They’re ideal for working breakfasts, relaxed lunches, and people-watching.

What to expect: friendly service, lower price points, and menus that rotate seasonally. These spots provide insight into local day-to-day life.

Practical Logistics: Reservations, Dress Code, and Timing

How to Reserve with Confidence

High-end restaurants in Riyadh commonly require reservations—some weeks in advance for weekend nights. Book through the restaurant’s online system, by phone, or through a trusted hotel concierge. When you reserve, state any dietary restrictions, the occasion, and your preferred seating (window, private room, late seating).

If your first choice is unavailable, pick a comparable option from the contenders list and ask the host for timing that avoids peak table turnover. For popular venues, plan 2–3 weeks ahead if visiting during high season or during festivals.

Dress Code and Cultural Considerations

Riyadh’s dining culture is conservative compared with regional capitals. Smart-casual to formal attire is standard in top-tier restaurants. Men should avoid athletic wear; women should dress modestly—long sleeves and skirts or trousers are a safe, respectful choice. Abayas are not mandatory for most upscale restaurants frequented by international guests, but leaning conservative will make the experience smoother.

Respect local customs: phone calls at the table are acceptable but keep volumes low; photographers should ask before photographing staff or other diners.

Best Times To Dine

For sweeping views and sunset light, book a table that spans late afternoon into evening. For quieter dining and faster service, choose a weekday or an early seating. Many restaurants have late-night bars or lounges that stay lively on weekends.

Transport and Access: Getting to Riyadh’s Top Tables

Driving, Taxis, and Ride-Hailing

Riyadh is primarily a car city. Taxis and app-based services are reliable routes to restaurants. If you rent a car, ensure your route accounts for traffic peaks—weekday morning and evening rush hours can add significant time.

Valet parking is common at major hotels and fine-dining restaurants. For neighborhood cafés in dense areas, street parking and drop-offs are common.

Hotels and Concierge Services

If you’re staying at a major international hotel, use the concierge to secure reservations, arrange pick-ups, and request special arrangements like high chairs or private dining rooms. Concierge teams in Riyadh are generally proactive and can help expedite hard-to-get reservations.

How Much You Should Expect to Spend

Riyadh’s top-tier restaurants are priced comparably to international capitals. For an iconic fine-dining experience like The Globe, expect premium pricing reflective of the setting and service. Pan-Asian fine dining and high-end steakhouses also sit at higher price points.

For casual dining, expect significantly lower costs—cafés, bakeries, and small neighborhood restaurants offer excellent value and locally-flavored meals at affordable prices.

The Complete Comparison: How The Globe Stacks Up Against Alternatives

Strengths of The Globe

The Globe wins when you value spectacle, location, and service—attributes that are rare to combine so cleanly. The design and the setting create an indelible memory, which makes it particularly suited for milestone celebrations and first-time visitors who want a definitive "Riyadh night out."

When Other Restaurants Might Be Better

If your priority is culinary innovation or a specific cuisine—such as omakase precision, an avant-garde tasting menu, or a deeply local Najdi meal—other restaurants will outperform The Globe in their respective specialties. For a raw expression of culinary technique, look to high-concept Asian restaurants. For cultural authenticity, choose a Najdi dining space.

Quick Decision Guide (one list)

  • If you want iconic and ceremonial: choose The Globe.
  • If you want Japanese technique and modern plating: choose contemporary Asian fine dining.
  • If you want cultural narrative and local flavors: choose modern Najdi/Hejazi restaurants.
  • If you want a robust steak and wine program: choose a top steakhouse.
  • If you want an affordable local experience: choose neighborhood cafés and bakeries.

Sample Itineraries: Eat Like a Local or Celebrate in Style

Three-Day Food-Focused Visit to Riyadh (numbered list)

  1. Day 1 — Landmark Welcome: Afternoon visit to a major site, then sunset dinner at The Globe. Dress formal and book a window table; use this night as your first impression of Riyadh’s skyline and hospitality.
  2. Day 2 — Modern and Cosmopolitan: Lunch at a contemporary Asian restaurant with sharing plates; afternoon at a museum or art gallery; evening at a steakhouse with a curated wine pairing.
  3. Day 3 — Cultural Exploration: Morning coffee and pastries at a local café; a guided Najdi culinary experience for lunch that explains spice blends and traditional dishes; dinner at a modern Saudi restaurant that interprets regional recipes.

This three-day plan balances spectacle, culinary technique, and cultural immersion. Use your hotel concierge to arrange reservations and transport for the top-tier evenings.

Mistakes Travelers Make—and How to Avoid Them

Mistake: Booking Too Late or Showing Up Unannounced

High-end venues often sell out weekend seats early. Book ahead and confirm the reservation by phone or email a day prior. For iconic venues, failing to reserve can mean missing the entire experience.

Mistake: Ignoring Local Dress and Preconceptions

Although Riyadh is modernizing quickly, conservative dress remains prudent. Check the restaurant’s dress code and choose smart attire. This avoids awkward refusals at the door and shows respect for local customs.

Mistake: Treating Riyadh Only as a "Luxury" Destination

Riyadh’s culinary richness is not only about fine dining. Neighborhood eateries and family-run places offer deep, affordable, and meaningful culinary experiences. Mix high-end nights with local meals to get a full picture.

Mistake: Not Factoring Traffic and Prayer Times

Plan your travel time carefully—traffic and prayer times can affect service hours. Call ahead if you’re unsure about opening times during holidays or religious observances.

How to Get the Most Out of Your Reservation

Communicate Early and Clearly

When you book, make your priorities known: window seat, dietary restrictions, celebration details. Good restaurants appreciate the heads-up and will often tailor the experience.

Use Hotel Concierges and Local Guides

Concierges and local dining guides can secure preferred seating and recommend less-obvious options suited to your tastes. Pairing and Tasting Strategies

For multi-course menus, ask the sommelier for pairing suggestions. If you prefer lighter fare, request portions to be scaled or focus on tasting menus. In many top venues, staff will accommodate special requests to improve your experience.

Beyond Riyadh: Day Trips and Regional Dining Context

Complementing a Riyadh Trip with Regional Flavors

Riyadh makes an excellent base to experience Saudi Arabia’s broader culinary landscape. For coastal seafood, a trip to the seafood scene in Jeddah or a longer coastal voyage is enlightening. For heritage and archaeological context that informs local cuisine, visiting AlUla enriches your understanding of regional ingredients and historical foodways. Pilgrimage cities like Makkah and Madinah each have unique food traditions worth exploring if your itinerary allows.

These side trips place Riyadh's top restaurants into a national culinary context and bring fresh comparisons that help you refine what "best" means for your tastes.

Safety, Dietary Needs, and Accessibility

Food Allergies and Dietary Restrictions

Top restaurants in Riyadh are accustomed to international dietary needs. When you reserve, clearly specify allergies or restrictions. Many kitchens will prepare special plates when given advance notice.

Accessibility

Major hotels and landmark restaurants have accessibility features, but the level varies by venue. Confirm direct access routes and elevator availability in advance if mobility is a concern.

Health and Hygiene

Riyadh maintains high standards for food safety and hygiene, particularly in top-tier restaurants. Standard travel vaccinations are sufficient for most visitors.

A Practical Checklist for Your Night Out

Before you head to a top Riyadh restaurant, check these items: reservation confirmation, transport arrangement (driver or ride-hail), dress code, payment method accepted (major cards are widely used), and any required identification for hotel or private club access.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which restaurant should I book first for a short stay in Riyadh?

Book a night at The Globe if you want the most iconic dinner experience. For a balance of technique and atmosphere, reserve a modern Asian restaurant for another night, and leave time for at least one local neighborhood meal to taste Saudi hospitality.

Are reservations required at Riyadh’s top restaurants?

Yes—especially for weekend dining and for signature venues. Book at least 1–2 weeks in advance during high season, and use hotel concierge services if you need help.

What is appropriate attire for dining in Riyadh?

Smart-casual to formal is safe for most top restaurants. Men should avoid athletic wear; women should choose modest outfits such as long sleeves and trousers or skirts. When in doubt, ask the restaurant.

Can I find vegetarian or vegan options in Riyadh’s fine-dining scene?

Yes. Many high-end restaurants will accommodate vegetarian and vegan preferences if you provide advance notice. Casual cafés also provide plant-forward options.
