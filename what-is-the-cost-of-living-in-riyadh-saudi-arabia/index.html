Most residents find owning a car convenient in Riyadhâs urban layout. Fuel is inexpensive relative to global markets, reducing ongoing running costs, but initial purchase prices, insurance, and maintenance should be included in your budget.

Healthcare And Insurance

Public vs Private Care

Saudi nationals have access to public healthcare at no or low cost; expatriates must rely primarily on private healthcare, and most employers include medical insurance in employment packages. Insurance coverage varies widely; review policy inclusions for outpatient visits, maternity care, and emergency evacuations.

Typical private medical costs:

  • GP consultation: a moderate fee in private clinics.
  • Specialist visits and diagnostic tests: higher, especially without insurance.
  • Hospitalization: can be expensive in private hospitals.

When you negotiate employment, ensure the health insurance covers dependents and includes a reasonable network of local private hospitals and clinics. For short-term visitors and tourists, the visa-insurance cover works for usual travel medical needs but may exclude adventure sports and certain elective procedures.

Common Budgeting Pitfalls And How To Avoid Them

Many newcomers underestimate one or more of these items:

  • Summer utility spikes: Air-conditioning can drastically increase electricity bills. Budget for triple the base rate during extreme months.
  • School fees and admissions timing: Popular international schools have waiting lists and high deposits; plan and budget early.
  • Underinsuring healthcare: A minimal policy can lead to large out-of-pocket costs. Verify emergency evacuation and specialist coverage.
  • Lifestyle inflation: Easy access to high-quality entertainment and dining can push costs up quickly. Set discretionary limits early.
  • Misunderstanding lease agreements: Check for service charges, maintenance responsibilities, and renewal clauses that can change annual cost.

Avoid these pitfalls through early planning, requesting written breakdowns from landlords and schools, and using employer benefits where available.

Moving To Riyadh: Timeline And Practical Checklist

Start planning at least three months before your move. Key milestones:

  • Two to three months out: research neighborhoods, request salary breakdown, and decide on schooling options.
  • Six to eight weeks: secure temporary accommodation for arrival, begin visa and documentation processes, and obtain initial health insurance confirmation.
  • Two to four weeks: finalize permanent housing options, set up banking, order home internet, and register with local clinic and school.
  • Arrival month: obtain local ID (Iqama for residents), finalize utility and internet setup, and register children with schools.

Early planning ensures cost surprises are minimized and gives time to negotiate benefits that will shape your monthly budget.
