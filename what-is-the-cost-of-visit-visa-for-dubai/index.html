What Is the Cost of Visit Visa for Dubai

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Dubai attracts millions of visitors each year with its skyline, beaches, and fast-moving tourism sector. For travelers from Saudi Arabia and the wider Gulf, understanding visa costs is one of the first practical steps in planning a smooth trip. Short answer: the cost of a Dubai visit visa depends on your nationality, the type and duration of the visa, and the application channel you choose. Eligible nationalities receive a visa on arrival free of charge in many cases, while prearranged tourist visas typically range from about USD 10 for short transit permits to between USD 90 and USD 231 for standard 30- and 60-day tourism visas; additional processing fees, VAT, sponsor charges, and agency fees can raise the total.

This article explains, with practical certainty, how Dubai visit visa pricing works, the full set of options you’ll encounter, and the decision framework to choose the right visa at the lowest true cost for your trip. You’ll learn how visa-on-arrival works and who qualifies, how airline and GDRFA visa pricing compares, what hidden costs to expect, and the step-by-step process to apply, extend, or change a visa once you’re in the UAE. Along the way I’ll connect these details to travel-planning frameworks we teach at Saudi Travel & Leisure so you can plan your Dubai trip with cultural confidence and logistical clarity. Why Visa Costs Vary: The Simple Economics Behind Pricing

Different Providers, Different Fees

Dubai visit visa costs are not fixed by a single rate sheet visible to the traveler. Multiple channels issue visas: immigration authorities (GDRFA in Dubai), airlines (Emirates, Etihad for Abu Dhabi connection), hotels and travel agencies, foreign embassies, and private sponsors inside the UAE. Each channel has its own fee structure and processes; some add convenience or eligibility benefits but also charge processing fees. Airline-provided visas are convenient for ticket-holders and often transparent in USD; GDRFA fees may be published in AED; embassies and consular services may quote different rates including service and mail fees.

Nationality And Residency Status Affect Cost And Access

Some nationalities are eligible for visa-free entry or visa on arrival for fixed durations (30 days, 90 days, or 180 days) at no charge. Other nationalities must obtain a prearranged visa, and fees vary depending on whether the visa is single-entry or multiple-entry, and whether it’s 30 or 60 days. Residency in another country (for example, a US Green Card or UK residence permit) can also change eligibility and fee levels for certain nationalities.

Add-Ons, Taxes, And Agency Margins

Beyond the base visa price are VAT (5% on many services in the UAE), VFS or agency processing fees, refundable security deposits sometimes requested for private sponsorships, hotel “sponsorship” service charges, and fees for printing or producing hard-copy confirmations at the airport. These add-ons are the reason an advertised fee can balloon by USD 30–150 in practice.

Cost Range By Visa Type: What To Expect

Visa On Arrival — Free Or Minimal Cost (If Eligible)

For many passport holders, the simplest and cheapest option is to receive a visa on arrival. Eligible nationalities include a long list of European countries, the United States, Canada, Australia, Japan, and more. These visas can be:

  • 30-day single-entry stamps given free on arrival for many nationalities.
  • 90-day multiple-entry visas for a set list of countries (often valid for six months from issue).
  • Special 180-day arrangements for certain passport holders (e.g., Mexico).

If you qualify for visa on arrival, the cost is effectively zero. Always check your specific passport’s eligibility before travel. If immigration requires a printed copy of a preapproved visa or confirmation, there can be a small charge (for example, AED 30) for a printed copy at the screening counter.

Transit Visas — Very Low Cost for Short Stops

Transit visas (48-hour or 96-hour) are available to travelers stopping over in Dubai. These are inexpensive: airlines publish prices such as USD 10 for 48-hour transit permits and around USD 49 for 96-hour transit permits. They are typically non-extendable and require an onward ticket. If you’re using Emirates’ online service or applying through an airline booking, the transit visa is often the fastest and cheapest option for a short stop.

Standard Tourism Visas — The Most Common Paid Options

Prearranged tourism visas are the majority of paid options travelers purchase. Industry-standard price brackets (inclusive of VAT depending on the channel) you can expect are:

  • Short-term single-entry (30 days): About USD 90.
  • Short-term multiple-entry (30 days): About USD 136.
  • Long-term single-entry (60 days): About USD 177.
  • Long-term multiple-entry (60 days): About USD 231.

These tickets are usually extendable once while in the UAE, and the extension fee (when available) is commonly around USD 230. Prices above are typical of airline and travel agency packages; government portals may present slightly different AED figures.

Special-Case Fees And Embassy Rates

For some nationalities, embassies process visas with different fixed fees. For instance, consular processing fees for long-stay or multiple-entry visas may be higher and include service charges (e.g., US consular numbers historically show different pricing brackets). Additionally, nationals from certain countries may face higher fees or be required to provide additional identity documentation or security deposits, which change the effective cost.

Preapproved Single-Use Entry For Indians (Example Of A Targeted Program)

There are targeted programs for specific passport holders: for example, eligible Indian nationals holding certain residence permits can obtain a single-entry 14-day visa on arrival for a fee around USD 63. This is an example of how nationality plus residency status can create distinct, lower-cost options.

Step-By-Step: How To Choose The Right Visa (and Minimize Cost)

Choose between three broad strategies based on your profile and travel plan: (A) use visa-on-arrival where eligible; (B) secure a prearranged airline or GDRFA visa for predictable short/long stays; or (C) have a sponsor apply if you’re visiting friends, family, or on official business that requires a local host.

  1. Confirm passport eligibility for visa-on-arrival or a 90-day multiple entry stamp by checking official UAE visa lists or airline preapprovals.
  2. If visa-on-arrival is available, verify passport validity (six months) and confirm you have return/outbound tickets and accommodation proof, as immigration can ask for these.
  3. If prearranged is required, compare direct GDRFA fees and airline-processing fees for the same visa length (convert AED to USD and add VAT and agency charges). Airlines sometimes combine the visa fee with the ticket for convenience.
  4. If traveling with a packaged tour or staying in a hotel, ask whether the hotel will sponsor and what the hotel’s service fee is.
  5. If visiting family or using a private sponsor, confirm whether a refundable security deposit or additional fees apply and request a written invoice.
  6. When in doubt, apply through the most transparent channel (airline or GDRFA website), ask for written confirmation, and print a copy for airport screening.

How To Apply: A Practical Workflow

Digital Applications Through Airlines Or GDRFA

Airlines like Emirates allow you to apply for a visa during or after booking through the “Manage Your Booking” service. The application will list the visa types and fees in USD and require passport scans, a photo, and tickets. Processing time is commonly 3–4 working days for prearranged visas, though transit and short visas can be quicker.

Dubai’s immigration authority (GDRFA) offers services for tourist visas through their online portal and smart application. GDRFA fees tend to be listed in AED and require passport validity of six months, a passport copy, a passport photograph, and hotel or return ticket confirmations. Some tourism visas processed through GDRFA are refundable under limited circumstances and are extendable once.

Applying Via A Hotel Or Tour Operator

If a hotel sponsors your stay, they will ask for your passport copy and ticket details and apply on your behalf. Hotels often charge a service fee for this convenience. This route is reliable for short stays but may be costlier than applying directly via an airline or GDRFA.

Sponsor Or Private Application Inside UAE

If you have a UAE sponsor (company, family member or friend who is a UAE resident), the sponsor can apply through local immigration channels. Sponsors may need to submit identity copies, proof of relationship for family visas, or a refundable security deposit in specific circumstances. Private sponsorships are useful for longer or multiple-entry visas but watch for deposit amounts and sponsor service fees.

Embassy Or Consulate Applications (When Outside The UAE)

For travelers who prefer dealing with their local embassy, a visa can be obtained from the UAE embassy or consulate. Embassy processing typically requires application forms, passport copies, passport photos, a letter of purpose, and payment by money order or specified payment methods. Embassy fees vary; consular rates can be higher than airline or GDRFA channels.

Documents, Validity And Other Entry Requirements

Standard Documents You Will Be Asked For

  • Passport valid for at least six months from date of travel.
  • Color copy of passport information page.
  • Recent passport-sized color photograph.
  • Confirmed return or onward ticket (for transit visas).
  • Hotel booking or tenancy contract if staying with a family member.
  • For certain nationalities, additional identity proofs (national ID cards) may be required.

Validity Periods And Stay Limit

Visa validity and permitted stay are distinct. For example, a 30-day tourist visa might be valid to enter within 60 days of issuance but allows a 30-day stay from entry. Transit visas are valid for a short window pre- and post-arrival. Always check the specific visa’s validity to ensure entry and travel schedule alignment.

Special Notes On Children And Family Travel

Children must have their own passports and may require additional supporting documents, such as birth certificates for proof of relation when family sponsorship is used. If sponsoring or traveling with family members, keep clear digital and printed copies of relationship documents to avoid delays.

Extensions, Overstays, And Fines — The Hidden Costs

Extending Your Visa While In The UAE

Many tourist visas are extendable once. Extension fees are usually significant — often in the USD 200–250 range — and require a visit to an immigration office. Extensions may require original passport presentation and updated ticket information. If you think you may stay longer than planned, budget for the possibility of paying an extension fee.

Overstay Penalties

Overstaying can become expensive quickly. UAE overstay fines accumulate per day and additional administrative charges apply. Overstays can also lead to deportation orders with associated fines or bans on re-entry. Always plan for contingencies and, if your flight schedule changes, act promptly to request an extension before your visa expires.

Refunds And Cancellations

Visa fees are typically non-refundable once issued and used. Some channels may refund fees if an application is canceled before processing begins, often with administrative deductions. Always confirm the refund and cancellation policy before paying.

Practical Cost Examples: How Numbers Add Up

Below are frameworks—models you can plug your own numbers into—so you can calculate your actual expected cost based on the visa channel you choose. These are not “case stories” but templates you can reproduce for your trip.

  • Model A — Eligible for Visa On Arrival: Base visa fee: USD 0 Airport copy charge (if needed): AED 30 (~USD 8) Contingency (printing, travel to immigration): USD 10 Total expected cost: USD 18
  • Model B — Prearranged 30-Day Tourist Visa via Airline: Base visa fee (airline rate): USD 90 Airline processing fee: USD 10–20 VAT (5% if applicable): USD 4.50 Agency or hotel service fee (if used): USD 15–50 Total expected cost: USD 120–175
  • Model C — 60-Day Multiple-Entry Tourist Visa: Base visa fee: USD 231 VAT and processing: USD 20–30 Extension contingency reserve: USD 230 (if you might extend) Total expected cost (without extension): USD 260–300 Total expected cost (with one extension): USD 490–560

These models demonstrate why comparing channels and asking for a full invoiced total before payment matters.

Common Mistakes Travelers Make (And How To Avoid Them)

Travelers often assume the visa fee is the whole cost. Hidden fees for processing, printing, refundable deposits, passport handling, and hotel service charges are common. Another frequent error is misunderstanding visa validity windows—arriving after the visa’s “valid before” date can result in denial of boarding.

To avoid these issues, always request a full written invoice that lists VAT and any service or processing charges, confirm passport validity for six months, and print both your visa confirmation and flight/hotel documents. If you’re traveling from Saudi Arabia, factor in transit rules and check flight schedules from hubs like Riyadh when planning a same-day arrival.

Special Cases And Country-Specific Notes

GCC Nationals

Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) citizens typically enjoy freer movement and specific entry arrangements; many GCC residents do not need prearranged visas to enter the UAE. If you hold a GCC passport, check entry protocol before you travel.

Indian Nationals With Residence Permits

Eligible Indian passport holders with valid US visas, green cards, or EU/UK residence permits may qualify for specific preapproved single-entry 14-day visas at modest fees (approximately USD 63 in some airline programs). This targeted policy is an important cost-reduction route for a large traveling population.

US, UK, European Citizens

Many Western passport holders receive a complimentary 30-day or 90-day visa on arrival; that makes travel from markets like the UK and US potentially visa-cost-free, placing the emphasis on travel insurance and accommodations as your main expenses.

Travelers From Higher-Fee Countries

Some nationalities face higher fees, security deposit requirements, or additional documentation. If your nationality appears on an additional-document list, budget for longer processing and potential sponsor deposit requirements.

Comparing Visa Channels: Airlines vs GDRFA vs Hotels vs Embassy

  • Airlines: Fast for ticket-holders; fees are transparent and usually quoted in USD. Good for short-notice trips booked together with flights.
  • GDRFA (Dubai Immigration): The government route can be cost-effective and direct, but published rates in AED require conversion and you may face extra admin fees when applying through third parties.
  • Hotels/Tour Operators: Convenient if you value one-stop service; usually more expensive due to service fees.
  • Embassies/Consulates: Best for travelers who prefer official processing at home; often the slowest and sometimes the most expensive.

If you’re comparing costs, request a total-including-VAT quote and a timeline for processing before committing.

Airport Procedures, Screening, And Small Fees To Watch

Dubai immigration has introduced random visual screening in some instances. If selected, you may be asked to present a hard copy of your visa; they may charge a nominal fee (for example, AED 30) for printing. Keep a printed copy of your visa confirmation and hotel reservation to avoid this. Also be ready with passport photos and documents if applying at immigration counters for extensions or changes.

Two Checklists To Keep Costs Predictable

  • Essential cost checklist before you pay for a visa:
    • Confirm base visa fee and currency (USD vs AED).
    • Ask for VAT and processing/agency fees to be listed.
    • Confirm whether a refundable security deposit is required for your nationality or sponsor type.
    • Verify that the visa is valid for your intended travel dates.
    • Confirm extension policy and fees in case plans change.
  • Practical document checklist (bring printed copies on arrival):
    • Passport with at least six months validity.
    • Printed visa confirmation or airline preapproval email.
    • Confirmed return/outbound ticket.
    • Hotel reservation or letter of invitation (if staying with family).
    • Passport-size photo and, if applicable, documents proving relationship for family sponsorship.

Timing: How Long Will It Take To Get The Visa?

Transit visas and short transit permits can be processed in as little as 24–72 hours via airline portals. Standard tourist visas via airline or GDRFA often take 3–4 working days. Embassy processing can take longer depending on local operations; plan for at least a week when in doubt. For last-minute travel, verify same-day or 24-hour express processing availability and the associated premium.

Avoiding Visa Scams And Unnecessary Fees

Always apply through official channels or reputable agencies. If an agent requests upfront cash without issuing a documented invoice or demands unusually high deposits, stop and seek a second opinion. Scammers target rushed travelers; asking for proof of a registered tourism or travel license and an invoice that itemizes VAT and service fees is a sensible protective step.

What If You’re Changing Plans: Converting A Visa Or Reapplying

If you change dates or need a different visa type, it’s usually necessary to cancel the existing visa (if possible) and apply anew. Cancellations may not be refundable once processed. If you need to change your visa while in the UAE (for example, converting a visit visa to a residency permit), consult immigration early—these processes are complex and often require a local sponsor and documentation.

Final Planning Checklist For Travelers From Saudi Arabia

Before you board the plane:

  • Confirm if you require a prearranged visa or can get a visa on arrival.
  • Verify passport validity (six months minimum).
  • Print your visa confirmation and flight/hotel documents.
  • Budget for the visa base fee plus VAT, processing, and any sponsor service charges.
  • If you need airline assistance, check the airline’s Manage Your Booking visa services for transparent quotes.

Conclusion

Understanding the cost of a visit visa for Dubai is straightforward once you break the decision into eligibility, base fee, channel, and add-ons. Many travelers pay nothing if they are eligible for visa-on-arrival; others will pay between roughly USD 10 for a short transit permit and USD 231 (or higher when adding fees) for longer multiple-entry tourism visas. Your total cost depends on the channel (airline, GDRFA, hotel, or embassy), VAT and processing fees, and any sponsor deposits or extension fees. Use the decision framework of Eligibility + Cost + Convenience + Risk to select the best option for your trip, and always ask for a full, itemized invoice before paying.

FAQ

What is the cheapest way to get a Dubai visit visa?

The cheapest path is visa on arrival when your passport qualifies; that is free in many cases. If you require a prearranged visa, short transit permits purchased through airlines are the lowest-cost paid option (around USD 10–49). Always confirm eligibility before travel.

How much does a 30-day Dubai tourist visa cost?

A 30-day tourist visa purchased through common airline channels often costs about USD 90 for a single-entry option and around USD 136 for a multiple-entry option. Additional VAT, processing, and agency fees can increase the total.

Can I extend a Dubai visit visa and how much does that cost?

Many tourist visas can be extended once; extension fees are not trivial—expect a fee in the region of USD 230 for an extension depending on the channel and current regulation. Extensions require visiting immigration or using approved digital channels and may require presenting your original passport.

Do I need travel insurance to get a Dubai visa?

While travel insurance is not always a strict visa requirement for standard tourist visas, having health/travel insurance is strongly recommended and may be required for certain visa categories or during periods of changing health regulations. Always check requirements for your specific visa before applying.
