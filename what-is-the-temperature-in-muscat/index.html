What Is The Temperature In Muscat

Updated October 24, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Muscat has become a city travelers plan around not just for its culture and coastline but for its reliable warm climate that shapes every outdoor plan. Whether you're timing a beach day, packing for a desert drive, or arranging a hike into the Hajar foothills, understanding Muscat's temperature patterns will make your trip smoother and more enjoyable.

Short answer: Muscat is hot for most of the year. Average daytime highs range from the mid-20s °C (mid-70s °F) in winter to the mid-30s–low 40s °C (mid-90s–100+ °F) in summer, with nights staying relatively warm. Coastal humidity raises the heat index in summer; winter offers the most comfortable conditions for outdoor sightseeing.

This article explains exactly what those numbers mean, how Muscat’s geography creates its weather, how temperatures change hour-to-hour and month-to-month, and—most importantly—how to plan practical, comfortable travel around the climate. As the voice of Saudi Travel & Leisure, I’ll give you field-tested blueprints for timing activities, choosing clothing and accommodation, and using regional comparisons to set realistic expectations for your trip.

Understanding Muscat’s Climate and Temperature Drivers

Geography Shapes the Thermometer

Muscat sits along the northeastern Omani coast at the foot of the Al Hajar Mountains. That coastal position moderates daytime extremes compared with inland deserts but also brings high humidity, especially from late spring through early autumn. The nearby sea keeps night temperatures from dropping sharply, so day-to-night temperature swings are smaller than in interior desert areas.

The city’s layout and local topography produce microclimates. Narrow streets and older neighborhoods like Muttrah can feel warmer in the day due to heat retained by buildings, while seaside areas such as Qurum and the Corniche benefit from sea breezes. Elevated areas in the Hajar foothills are noticeably cooler, and even a short drive inland can lower temperatures by several degrees.

Regional Winds and Their Effects

Muscat’s weather is influenced by seasonal wind patterns. During summer, prevailing winds along the Gulf coast can be humid and warm, increasing the apparent temperature. In winter, northerly or northwesterly winds tend to be drier and cooler, producing more comfortable conditions for outdoor activities. Strong, gusty winds occasionally pick up dust and reduce visibility, but they also provide welcome relief on sweltering days.

The Monsoon Misconception

A common misunderstanding is that the khareef (southern monsoon) affects Muscat. It does not. The khareef characterizes the Dhofar region in southern Oman and creates cool, misty conditions in Salalah. Muscat’s climate follows a more typical hot-arid to hot-semiarid pattern, with the sea moderating temperatures but not producing monsoon-like cooling.

Seasonal Temperature Overview

Understanding Muscat’s year broken into practical seasons helps you plan activities and packing. Below is a concise seasonal summary to set expectations before we dive into month-by-month detail.

  • Winter (December–February): Mild and very pleasant. Daytime highs usually mid-20s °C (mid-70s °F), nights often low 20s °C (low 70s °F) or cooler. Best weather for sightseeing, hiking, and festivals.
  • Spring (March–May): Warm and progressively hotter. Daytime temperatures climb from high 20s to low 30s °C (upper 70s–mid 80s °F). Drying conditions; early spring is still comfortable.
  • Summer (June–September): Hot and humid. Daytime highs commonly 35–42 °C (95–108 °F) with high humidity near the coast making it feel hotter. Outdoor activity recommended for early morning and late evening only.
  • Autumn (October–November): Rapidly more pleasant. Temperatures drop into the high 20s–low 30s °C (80s–mid 80s °F) with lower humidity and clearer skies—great for beaches and evening strolls.

Month-By-Month Temperature Patterns

To plan precisely, a month-by-month look tells you what to expect for highs, lows, humidity trends, and sea temperatures.

December–February (Peak Comfort Season)

December through February is the most comfortable stretch. Average highs are about 24–27 °C (75–81 °F); nights cool to around 16–21 °C (61–70 °F). Humidity is lower than in summer, and sea temperatures are still pleasant for swimming in many cases by mid-February.

Travel implications: These months are ideal for long walks along the Corniche, visiting souks early in the day, and hiking in nearby wadis or mountains without thermal stress. Accommodation demand rises, so book well in advance if traveling during holidays or festival dates.

March–May (Warming Trend)

March sees a steady warm-up. Highs move from the upper 20s into the low 30s °C (80s–90s °F) by April and May. Nighttime cooling becomes less effective as the sea temperature rises and humidity begins to climb.

Travel implications: Plan outdoor activities for morning or late afternoon. If you’re sensitive to heat, target March and early April for intense outdoor pursuits; save beach days for late afternoon to sunset to avoid peak heat.

June–September (Hot Season)

Summertime is dominated by heat and humidity. Daytime temperatures often sit between 35–42 °C (95–108 °F), and high humidity can push the heat index into dangerous ranges. Nighttime temperatures frequently remain above 25 °C (77 °F), so there is little respite.

Travel implications: Focus on air-conditioned museums, indoor markets, or a private beach club with shaded areas. Avoid strenuous midday hikes. If traveling in this period, choose hotels with reliable air conditioning, plan short excursions, and remain vigilant about hydration and sun exposure.

October–November (Transition To Comfort)

October starts to cool quickly; daytime highs slide back into the low 30s °C (mid 80s–90s °F) and November becomes especially pleasant. Humidity drops and sea breezes return to being comfortable rather than oppressive.

Travel implications: Late October and November are excellent for combining beach time with cultural touring. It’s a smart shoulder-season choice—fewer crowds than winter and good weather for exploration.

Daily Temperature Patterns and Microclimates

Diurnal Range: What To Expect Each Day

Muscat’s coastal position means small diurnal ranges compared to inland deserts. During comfortable months, a clear morning might be in the low 20s °C, rising to the mid-20s or low 30s by afternoon. In summer, mornings are already warm, and evenings stay sticky.

Understanding these daily swings helps schedule activities: early mornings are typically best for energy-intensive pursuits like climbing to the old forts or visiting mountain viewpoints, while evenings are ideal for promenades, harbor cruises, or outdoor dining.

Coastal Versus Inland Differences

Coastal Muscat—Muttrah, Qurum, the Corniche—feels humid, especially in summer, but benefits from steady sea breezes. Drive 20–40 minutes inland toward the Hajar foothills and temperatures drop; higher elevation results in appreciably cooler air. If you want a near-instant cooler microclimate, head to Jabal Akhdar and other highlands (note that Jabal Akhdar is several hours’ drive and significantly higher altitude).

When planning activities, treat coastal and mountain areas as separate destinations regarding clothing and water needs.

How To Interpret Temperature Numbers: Heat Index, Dew Point, And Sea Temperature

Heat Index vs Air Temperature

The heat index combines temperature and humidity to express how hot it feels. A 38 °C (100 °F) day with high humidity can feel several degrees hotter, increasing the risk of heat-related illness. In Muscat’s summer, always consider the heat index, especially if you plan any exercise outdoors.

Dew Point and Comfort

Dew point is a reliable measure of moisture in the air. Dew points above 20 °C (68 °F) indicate oppressive humidity; in Muscat’s summer, dew points can hover in that uncomfortable range. Lower dew points in winter and early spring explain why the same temperature can feel more pleasant at different times of year.

Sea Temperature

Muscat’s sea temperatures are warm year-round, cooling slightly in winter and rising significantly by late spring and summer. Warm sea temps are great for swimming and water sports, but in very hot months the combination of hot air and warm water can make midday beach sessions feel particularly draining.

Practical Travel Blueprints Based On Temperature

Below are planning frameworks—actionable templates you can apply depending on when you visit.

Morning-Midday-Evening Blueprints

  • Morning blueprint (best for physical activity): Start before sunrise, enjoy a seaside walk or hike in the cooler hours, visit indoor museums as it warms, then rest during midday.
  • Midday blueprint (when temperatures peak): Schedule air-conditioned experiences—museums, souks with shaded inner courtyards, culinary workshops—or take a siesta.
  • Evening blueprint (best for dining and culture): After sunset, stroll the Corniche, visit night markets, book harbor or dhow cruises, and dine outdoors in seaside restaurants as temperatures become pleasant.

Apply these blueprints flexibly: in winter, morning to evening is comfortable; in summer, compress outdoor time into early morning and late evening.

Timing Activities By Season

For each major activity—beach, mountain hiking, desert exploration, cultural touring—use the following guidance: beaches and harbor cruises are enjoyable most months but are best in late autumn through spring; hiking in wadis or mountains is safest in winter and early spring; desert trips are more comfortable in winter and shoulder seasons; cultural sites are accessible year-round but plan mid-day rest in hotter periods.

Packing And Clothing: Practical Advice For Muscat’s Temperatures

The list below gives essentials tuned for Muscat’s temperature ranges so you can pack with confidence.

  • Lightweight, breathable fabrics (linen, cotton, moisture-wicking synthetics)
  • A light jacket or long-sleeve layer for cool winter nights or heavily air-conditioned interiors
  • Sun protection: wide-brim hat, sunglasses, high-SPF sunscreen
  • Comfortable closed shoes for walking and sandals for the beach
  • Swimwear and quick-dry towel
  • Reusable water bottle with insulation to keep water cool
  • Light scarf or shawl for cultural sites and to shield from sun or sudden breezes

Follow local customs for modesty—particularly in public places and religious sites—while prioritizing breathable fabrics that keep you cool. A lightweight long-sleeve shirt or scarf can protect from sun and also respect cultural norms.

Health, Hydration, and Safety in Relationship to Temperature

Recognizing Heat-Related Illness

Signs of heat exhaustion and heatstroke include excessive sweating followed by hot, dry skin; dizziness; nausea; confusion; and rapid heartbeat. In Muscat’s hot months, minimize risk by pacing activity, drinking water frequently (even before you feel thirsty), and avoiding alcohol and heavy meals during peak heat.

Special Considerations for Children, Elderly, and Sensitive Individuals

Children and older adults are more vulnerable to heat. Keep them in cool, shaded environments during hottest hours and plan medical contingencies if you travel with prescription medications that may interact with heat.

Car Safety and Temperature

Cars parked in sun can reach dangerously high internal temperatures. Never leave children or pets in a parked vehicle. Use sunshades, park in shade where possible, and run the air conditioning briefly before passengers re-enter.

Accommodation, Air Conditioning, And Amenities

Choosing Accommodation Based On Temperature

Summer visitors should prioritize modern hotels with reliable air conditioning and good insulation. Beachfront properties often have pleasant sea breezes, but humidity can still make interiors feel warm; check for efficient AC and blackout curtains. In winter, you can opt for smaller guesthouses or boutique hotels that provide heating only if needed, since nights remain mild.

Pools, Private Lounges, and Outdoor Facilities

If you travel in summer, having access to a shaded pool area and private beach facilities makes a major difference to comfort. Confirm pool opening hours (some close midday for maintenance) and whether private cabanas or shaded loungers are available.

Transportation And Weather: Practical Tips

Driving In Heat and During Dust Events

High temperatures and dusty winds occur occasionally. Check local forecasts for dust storms that can affect visibility. Keep a full-size sunshade for the windshield and extra coolant for longer drives. When planning mountain drives, recall that temperatures and road conditions can change quickly with elevation.

Flights and Airport Considerations

Muscat International is a modern airport with climate control; however, taxis and ride-hailing vehicles can be warm on arrival. Book airport transfers if you arrive in peak heat, ideally selecting an operator who confirms air-conditioned vehicles.

Using Forecasts and Planning Tools Effectively

Short-Term Forecasts Versus Climate Normals

Short-term forecasts (hourly to 10-day) matter for day-to-day decisions. Climate normals (30-year averages) show what to expect when selecting the month to travel. Both types of information are useful: use forecasts to decide what to pack for the coming week and climate normals when choosing the season for your trip.

Recommended Routine for Forecast Checking

Two weeks before departure, monitor weekly forecasts for any unusual heat spikes or dust events. One week prior, refine your schedule for outdoor activities to mornings and evenings if heat is expected. On the day, check hourly forecasts and UV index to plan sunscreen and hydration.

Activity-Specific Temperature Advice

Beach and Water Sports

Late autumn through spring offer ideal water and air temperatures. In summer, choose early morning swims or sunset sessions. High humidity increases sunburn risk—apply water-resistant sunscreen and reapply frequently after swimming.

Desert Excursions and Wadi Hikes

Desert safaris are far safer and more pleasant in winter and early spring. If you schedule a wadi hike, do it in cooler months and never enter wadis after heavy rain due to flash-flood risk. Afternoon desert temperatures can be severe in summer and are best avoided.

Mountain Trekking

For higher-altitude mountain treks, select winter and early spring for sunny yet cool conditions. Even in warmer months, mornings are the best window for movement. Pack layered clothing to accommodate cooler elevations and sun protection for exposed ridgelines.

City Sightseeing

Plan sight-seeing circuits to alternate between outdoor sites (forts, waterfronts) and air-conditioned stops (museums, cafes). If traveling during Muscat Festival season in January–February, expect pleasant days and lively evening programming.

Booking And Timing Strategies In Relation To Temperature

  • If your priority is comfortable outdoor exploration and festivals, book December–February. Reserve accommodation early due to peak season demand.
  • If you prefer fewer tourists and lower prices while accepting warmer days, choose October–November or March–April.
  • Summer travel is viable for indoor experiences, spas, and discounted resorts; book hotels with strong AC and flexible cancellation in case of heat advisories.

When building a multi-destination Gulf itinerary, coordinate dates so that you don’t traverse from a mild coastal climate into an inland city with much higher summer daytime heat without an intermediate rest day.

Practical Checklist For Managing Temperature While Traveling

Below is a compact checklist to translate temperature knowledge into immediate actions:

  • Before you go: check average monthly temperatures and forecast for your dates.
  • Daily: schedule outdoor activities for mornings/evenings during warm months.
  • Packing: prioritize breathable layers, sun protection, refillable water bottle.
  • Accommodation: confirm air conditioning reliability and pool/shade availability.
  • Health: know heat illness symptoms and keep electrolyte tablets readily available.

Why Temperature Matters To Cultural And Seasonal Experiences

Temperature shapes not only comfort but how you experience culture. Festivals, evening markets, and coastal dining thrive when temperatures are mild. Ramadan practices, prayer times, and the rhythm of daily life also shift somewhat with season; for example, late-summer fasts can be more physically demanding during unusually hot periods, and scheduling tours respectfully during Ramadan requires awareness of local routines.

Understanding the temperature gives you the power to choose the kind of trip you want—active exploration, relaxed seaside leisure, cultural immersion, or a mix—while staying safe and comfortable.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What is the average daytime temperature in Muscat during summer? A: Daytime highs usually range between 35–42 °C (95–108 °F), with high humidity near the coast making it feel hotter. Plan activities for morning and evening.

Q: Is winter in Muscat cool enough for outdoor hiking? A: Yes. December through February are comfortable for hiking, sightseeing, and outdoor festivals, with daytime highs often in the mid-20s °C (mid-70s °F).

Q: How much does humidity affect comfort in Muscat? A: Humidity significantly affects perceived temperature. High humidity in summer raises the heat index, increasing the risk of heat-related illness, so prioritize hydration and shade.

Q: Can I swim in Muscat year-round? A: Sea temperatures are generally warm enough for swimming year-round, though winter water is cooler. Swimmer comfort depends on personal preference and air temperature.
