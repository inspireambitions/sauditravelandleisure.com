Packaging, Shipping, and Customs

Fragile Items

Wrap pottery and lamps in bubble wrap, then clothes; place in the center of a suitcase surrounded by soft items. Consider shipping through a gallery that offers international shipping and insurance.

Food and Plant Products

Dates and packaged sweets travel well. For frankincense and spices, use sealed commercial packaging. Check your home-country customs for plant resins and food import rules.

Weapons and Bladed Items

Khanjars and daggers can be restricted. Always declare such items to customs at departure and arrival, and keep purchase receipts. Better yet, arrange export documentation or ship with a reputable freight forwarder.

Shipping Services

Many galleries and higher-end shops provide shipping; they can advise on customs paperwork and handling fragile crates. If you plan to ship, allow timeâinternational shipping can take weeks.
