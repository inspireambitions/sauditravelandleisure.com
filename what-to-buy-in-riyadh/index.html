Riyadh Guides

What to Buy in Riyadh

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Introduction

Riyadh is no longer just the business and political heart of Saudi Arabia — it has also become one of the region’s most exciting shopping destinations. From towering luxury malls where international designers sit beside local ateliers, to age-old souks that still trade in spices, incense and intricately crafted metalwork, the city offers a shopping spectrum that satisfies collectors, first-time visitors, and anyone who wants a meaningful souvenir to carry home.

Short answer: What to buy in Riyadh depends on whether you want authentic cultural keepsakes (oud, dates, dallah coffee sets, embroidered textiles), functional local products (saffron, spices, camel-milk skincare), or investment items (gold jewelry). For practical gifts that travel well, choose vacuum-sealed dates, pure oud oils, small brass dallahs, and handcrafted scarves. For special purchases, plan to buy gold, carpets, or large handmade pieces in person and inspect them carefully.

This article will outline the best items to buy in Riyadh, why each matters culturally, how to evaluate authenticity and quality, where to find them neighborhood-by-neighborhood, and the logistical framework to shop with confidence (payment, bargaining, customs, and packing). My mission with Saudi Travel & Leisure is to give you the blueprint to convert curiosity into a smoothly executed shopping plan so you leave Riyadh with objects that tell the story of your trip — not regrets.

Why Riyadh Is a Top Shopping Destination

A city that blends modern retail with living heritage

Riyadh’s retail landscape is striking because it’s layered. Malls like Kingdom Centre and Riyadh Park deliver international brands, comfortable air-conditioned browsing, and duty-free-style boutiques. Within the very same city, souks such as Al-Zal and Dirah preserve centuries-old trades — incense, brassware, goldsmithing and textiles — often from family-run stalls operating on traditional knowledge. That range makes Riyadh uniquely satisfying: you can comparison-shop luxury fragrances by the dozen, then walk ten minutes to haggle for a copper dallah or a hand-stitched thobe.

Economic and cultural reasons to buy locally

Several things make buying in Riyadh attractive beyond novelty. First, many products are priced more competitively than in Western markets because of local production and tax structure. Second, cultural items — from oud blends to gahwa sets — carry context here: you can ask the maker about the story behind a pattern or scent. Third, buying local supports craftspeople who are keeping traditional techniques alive. For travelers who want more than a trinket, Riyadh’s markets allow you to connect the object to a living tradition.

How to Shop in Riyadh: Practical Frameworks

Timing, opening hours and prayer timings

Riyadh’s retail hours vary by neighborhood and type of store. Malls typically open from late morning through late evening, with weekend peaks on Thursday–Saturday. Traditional souks often operate early in the morning and again after Asr prayer; many smaller shops close briefly for prayer times midday and at sunset. Build your shopping schedule around morning visits to souks (cooler and quieter) and evening visits to malls when more shops are open.

Plan buffer time for prayer closures and always ask shop staff for local market hours — they will be precise.

Payment methods and currency considerations

Saudi Riyals (SAR) are the currency you will use. Credit and debit cards are widely accepted in malls and many larger souk shops, but cash is still king for bargaining in small stalls and traditional markets. Keep a supply of smaller denomination notes (20s and 50s) for quick purchases and tips. ATMs are plentiful in malls and along major streets; many accept international cards, but notify your bank before travel to avoid blocks.

When buying high-value items (gold, carpets, antiques), use cards where possible for traceability and request official receipts. For very large purchases, it’s acceptable to pay a deposit in cash and the remainder by bank transfer — ensure you receive documentation.

Authenticity checks and what to ask

When buying perfumes, dates, gold and other high-value items, ask straightforward questions: origin, batch, weight, purity stamp, and if applicable, producer certificates. For oud and pure oils, request a small skin test or sample on blotter paper and observe how the scent evolves. For gold, insist on a stamped karat and a weight receipt. For carpets, inspect knots per square inch, fringe finishing and dye consistency.

Don’t be shy to request time to compare—reputable merchants will respect an informed buyer.

Haggling: where it’s expected and where it’s not

Bargaining is part of the culture in souks and smaller markets. In fancy malls and brand boutiques, sticker prices are generally fixed. Approach haggling politely: start lower than the asking price but not insultingly so, and be prepared to walk away. A good local rule is to aim for 20–30% off the initial price in traditional markets, less for specialty handcrafted items where margins are thinner. Know your target price by doing a quick compare between 2–3 stalls.

Shipping large items and customs advice

If you purchase bulky items like carpets or large metalwork, arrange shipping from the vendor; many reputable shops will help with packing and documentation. Keep all receipts and ask vendors for an export invoice. Check your home country’s customs rules before buying swords, large daggers, or certain animal-derived products. For many countries, items like frankincense, oud, and textiles are fine; firearms, certain weapons and protected wildlife products are restricted.

Language and etiquette

Basic Arabic phrases will get you further in a souk, and respectful etiquette grows trust. Greet first, share a cup of gahwa if offered, and never rush the interaction. Women shoppers should be aware that while shopping is perfectly normal for female visitors, conservative dress and respectful behavior pay dividends in terms of service and price.

Signature Items to Buy in Riyadh — Deep Dive

Below I break down categories by cultural value, how to judge quality, recommended purchase locations in Riyadh, and practical buying tips.

Oud and Luxury Fragrances

Why oud matters here

Oud is much more than a scent in Saudi culture — it is used in homes, ceremonies, and public spaces as an olfactory signature of hospitality. Riyadh is a major retail hub for oud blends: pure dehn al-oud oils, mixed oils (misk-oud-blends), bakhoor (incense chips), and luxurious perfume concentrates.

How to evaluate oud

Authentic oud is expensive and complex. Pure oud oil will evolve on the skin over hours: the bright top notes will give way to deeper wood and resinous tones. Beware of synthetics marketed as “oud” — reputable vendors will label “synthetic” or “mixed.” For pure oud, ask about the grade and region of agarwood. Sample on skin, not just on paper, and give the scent 10–20 minutes to develop.

Where to buy in Riyadh

High-end, trusted perfume houses are numerous in malls, but the most interesting selections are in dedicated parfumers and specialized souk stalls where you can ask for custom blends. Bring small vials or buy decanted samples for travel ease.

Dates and Date Products

Why dates are essential

Dates are central to Saudi hospitality and dietary tradition. Varieties such as Ajwa (associated with Madinah), Sukari, and Safawi differ in texture, sweetness and cultural significance. Riyadh offers everything from raw farm dates to luxury gift boxes and date-based products like jams, syrups and confectionery.

Quality indicators

Look for uniform color, no crystallized sugar on the surface (unless specified), and vacuum-sealed packaging for travel. Premium brands package dates in ornate boxes that preserve freshness; smaller markets may sell loose dates — buy from sellers with turnover so you know they rotate stock.

Where to get the best dates

Specialty date shops, luxury boutique outlets, and airport duty-free are reliable sources. Because Ajwa is specifically linked to Madinah in provenance, you may compare supply from regional vendors. Gold and Jewelry

What to expect

Gold jewelry in Riyadh tends toward high purity (21k–22k) and bold traditional designs. Jewelry is sold by weight and often crafted in family workshops. Pieces range from delicate pendants to heavy Bedouin-inspired necklaces and bracelets.

How to buy with confidence

Check the karat stamp (21k or 22k), ask for a detailed weight receipt, and compare gold rate on the day of purchase. Shop in licensed souks where certified scales are used. If you plan to export gold, retain all documentation and receipts for customs and possible resale. Avoid deals that sound too good; if the price is below market significantly, confirm the alloy and return policy.

Where to shop

Traditional gold souks and the Dirah market are classic locations to browse. For modern designs, malls host certified jewelers with international standards.

Traditional Clothing and Accessories

Abayas, thobes, shemaghs, and agals

Traditional garments have both cultural meaning and modern fashion iterations. Abayas and thobes are now offered in designer cuts and premium fabrics, while shemaghs (red-and-white headscarves) and ghutras (white headscarves) are staples for men.

Buying tips

For authentic textiles and embroidered pieces, touch the fabric and inspect stitching. For abayas, check lining and seam finishes; for thobes, feel the weave. Small embroidered details and hand-stitched trims increase value. If buying as a gift, ask about size flexibility and alteration options — many shops offer tailoring.

Coffee Culture Items: Dallahs, Finjan, and Coffee Beans

Cultural significance

Gahwa (Saudi coffee) is served in dallah kettles and small cups called finjan. The dallah is an emblem of hospitality — both practical and decorative.

What to buy and how to judge

Choose brass or copper dallahs for authenticity; make sure the handle and spout are sturdy and the lid fits. For a functional set, confirm the inner finish is safe for heat contact. Pair a dallah with local coffee blends (Khawlani beans) and a small packet of cardamom for an authentic kit.

Where to purchase

Souks and specialty household stores offer both souvenir-size dallahs and full, serviceable sets. For museum-quality or collectible pieces, heritage shops and curated boutiques around cultural centers are the right places.

Incense, Bukhoor and Mabkhara

The fragrance economy

Bukhoor and frankincense are central to Saudi home rituals. Bukhoor is often sold as wood chips perfumed with oils; mabkhara burners come in many materials, from ceramic to brass.

How to choose

Quality bukhoor is slightly oily and grainy rather than powdered. Frankincense should be resinous, amber-colored and fragrant when heated. Invest in a metal or ceramic mabkhara rather than plastic for durability and better smoke diffusion.

Where to buy

Perfume houses and traditional souks are prime spots. Ask for a small sample to test on coal to see how the scent performs.

Camel Milk Products

Why camel milk is unique

Camel milk is used in chocolates, soaps, and skincare and is prized locally for perceived health benefits and novelty abroad. Brands specializing in camel milk combine modern packaging with desert-rooted narratives.

Practical purchase advice

Camel-milk chocolates and soaps travel well if sealed. For skincare, check ingredients and preservative information; buy from reputable boutiques and ask for usage guidance.

Carpets, Textiles and Handicrafts

What to look for

Handwoven rugs and textiles range from small coasters to full-size carpets. Quality indicators include knot density, natural dyes, and even fringes. Textile motifs often reference regional identity or Bedouin patterns.

Negotiation and shipping

Carpets are negotiable; arrange professional packing for shipping if you cannot carry them. Get the vendor to provide proper documentation and a shipping quotation.

Where to go

Dirah souk and specialty carpet dealers provide options across price ranges.

Local Foodstuffs and Spices

Must-buy pantry items

Saffron, cardamom, sumac, and kabsa spice blends make practical, compact gifts. Local honey (sidr or samar) and date syrup are also high-value pantry items with long shelf life.

Buying tips

Buy sealed jars or vacuum-packed saffron in labeled packaging. For honey, look for thick, unprocessed jars; natural crystallization indicates raw product.

Modern Saudi Brands and Design

The new wave

Contemporary Saudi designers produce fashion, jewelry, homeware, and crafts that reinterpret tradition for a global audience. Buying from local designers supports the Kingdom’s creative economy and gives you something truly contemporary and made-in-Saudi.

Where to find them

Boutiques in lifestyle districts, curated concept stores and seasonal design markets showcase these brands. Check store tags for designer origin and small-batch production notes.

Collectible and Decorative Swords, Daggers and Brassware

Cultural context

Decorative swords and daggers are symbols of cultural heritage and ceremony. When buying, understand local customs and legalities in your home country regarding weapon imports.

Practical warnings

Check legal import restrictions, and ensure any weapon-like object is firmly packed and declared when required. Many shops will craft display pieces without sharp edges specifically for tourists.

Neighborhoods and Where to Buy What

Central and Historic Riyadh: Souq Al-Zal and Dirah

These neighborhoods are ideal for brassware, dallahs, traditional garments, and carpets. Small stalls allow bargaining and provide an old-market atmosphere where craftsmanship is visible.

High-End Malls: Kingdom Centre, Riyadh Park, Al Nakheel

Malls focus on luxury perfumes, designer abayas, brand jewelry and packaged dates in gift boxes. For comfortable shopping with fixed prices and secure payment methods, malls are best.

Specialty Districts and Concept Stores

Areas near cultural centers and museums host boutiques selling modern Saudi design, artisanal food products, and curated homeware. If you want contemporary Saudi-made pieces, this is where you find them.

Where to compare prices

For gold, perfume and carpets, compare 2–3 vendors across souks and malls before settling on a purchase. Use the mall for brand-guaranteed products and souks for custom or made-to-order items.

Packing and Travel Logistics for Shoppers

Essential packing checklist for shoppers

  • A small soft suitcase or duffel for delicate purchases, with padding between fragile items.
  • Vacuum-seal bags for textiles and garments to save space (buy at the market if needed).
  • Document folder for receipts, certificates of authenticity and shipping invoices.
  • A few resealable plastic bags for food items like dates and spices to avoid spills.
  • Travel insurance details and contact numbers for vendor shipping services.

This short checklist helps you manage space and protect purchases on the flight home.

Declaring purchases and customs

Keep receipts and declare items that exceed duty-free allowances in your home country. High-value items like gold jewelry or large antiques often require documentation when flying. Consult your embassy or customs site before travel for exact monetary thresholds and documentation formats.

Carrying fragile items

Wrap fragile ceramics, dallahs and glass perfume bottles in soft garments, then place them in carry-on if possible. Many shops will professionally pack delicate objects for air travel; accept this service if offered.

Decisions: When to Buy in Riyadh vs. Online

There are clear trade-offs between buying in-person and online. Buy in Riyadh when you want authenticity, tactile inspection, or local storytelling (carpets, gold, oud blends). Buy online for convenience and to compare warranty terms with established brands. If a vendor offers secure online ordering with international shipping and a verified return policy, that can be a safe option for heavy or expensive items you are unwilling to carry home.

Special Considerations and Potential Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake: Buying cheapest without verification

Low price can indicate imitation or poor quality. For high-value categories (oud, gold, carpets), verify provenance and get receipts.

Mistake: Forgetting to compare

Never buy the first piece you see for a major purchase without comparing similar items in nearby shops. Differences in workmanship and price are real.

Mistake: Not checking customs ahead of time

Some countries restrict the import of certain items (e.g., swords, animal-derived materials). Always verify before purchase to avoid fines or confiscation.

Mistake: Poor packing for flights

Fragile goods must be packed properly. Request vendor packing or use sturdy carry-on space where impact is minimized.

How to avoid scams

Shop at reputable stores for high-value items, insist on documentation for purity and weight, and prefer certified vendors in malls or established souks when possible. If a deal seems unusually good, politely decline and move on.

Cultural Notes: Buying Respectfully

Purchasing in Riyadh is not just a transaction; it is often a social exchange. Accepting a small cup of gahwa, listening to the maker’s story, and asking about care instructions for a rug are ways to show respect that often lead to better deals and a more memorable purchase. Dress modestly in markets, ask before photographing, and be patient — the pace of traditional shopping values conversation.

Seasonality and When to Buy

Certain goods have seasonal peaks. Dates are freshest in the autumn harvest; perfumes and bakhoor sell year-round but special boutique releases appear around holidays and festivals. If you want festival-themed packaging (National Day or religious holidays), shop close to those dates for limited editions. For general product availability and holiday closures that affect shopping, cross-reference national holiday schedules and local event calendars in advance to avoid closed shops or inflated festival pricing. Responsible and Sustainable Shopping

When possible, prioritize locally-made goods, small artisans and sustainable product lines. Ask vendors about materials, production methods, and whether dyes or finishes are natural. Many contemporary Saudi designers are producing items with sustainability in mind; supporting them helps local craft economies and reduces the carbon footprint of goods.

Final Buying Framework: How to Make a Smart Purchase Decision in Riyadh

  1. Identify the category and set a realistic budget for it.
  2. Do quick market reconnaissance: visit a mall for brand prices and a souk for artisan quotes.
  3. Inspect the item, ask for provenance documents where relevant, and compare 2–3 vendors.
  4. Negotiate politely in souks; accept fixed pricing in brand stores.
  5. Get a written receipt and any authenticity certificates.
  6. Pack or arrange shipping, and make sure you understand customs implications.

This step-by-step approach reduces buyer’s remorse and ensures you return with objects that both please and retain value.

Conclusion

Riyadh offers an exceptional shopping experience that balances modern retail ease and deep-rooted craft traditions. Whether you’re after pure oud oils, vacuum-sealed premium dates, a handcrafted dallah, or contemporary Saudi design, the city supplies both the product and the cultural story behind it. Use neighborhood strategies to save time, insist on documentation for high-value purchases, and plan logistics for carrying fragile or bulky items home. FAQ

What are the easiest items to carry home from Riyadh?

Compact, high-value items like vacuum-sealed dates, small perfume vials (oud), camel-milk chocolate, packaged spices (saffron, cardamom), and small brass dallahs travel easily and are often allowed by customs. Always keep receipts and ensure food items are sealed.

Is bargaining acceptable everywhere in Riyadh?

Bargaining is expected in souks and with independent stallholders. In malls and brand boutiques, prices are typically fixed. Approach haggling with respect and aim for a reasonable middle-ground rather than an aggressive discount.

Can tourists buy gold and take it home without problems?

Yes, but buy from licensed vendors, get a detailed weight receipt, and check your home country’s customs allowances. Keep documentation to declare the item if necessary when re-entering your country.

Where should I go for authentic oud and high-quality perfumes?

Specialized perfume shops and established parfumers in Riyadh’s malls and dedicated fragrance streets provide both luxury brands and bespoke blends. Test scents on skin, ask about purity, and compare a few houses before purchasing a high-value bottle.
