Getting Around Alone: Transport Deep Dive

Metro, Tram, and Nol Card

Dubai Metro is efficient, clean and connects major tourist hubs: the Red Line hits Dubai International Airport, Burj Khalifa/Downtown, and Dubai Marina via a short transfer. The Nol card is the most convenient way to use public transport; you can buy and top-up at stations. Be mindful of peak commuting hours when trains are crowded.

Women travellers often appreciate women-only carriages during busy times; these are clearly marked.

Taxis, Ride-Hailing and Pink Taxis

Taxis are plentiful and metered芒聙聰the safest option for late-night travel or destinations not covered by the metro. Uber and Careem operate widely; compare prices at the time of booking. In some areas, women-only taxis (芒聙聹pink taxis芒聙聺) are available and can be booked through certain apps or hotel concierge.

Water Transport and Abras

For heritage-area crossings along Dubai Creek, traditional abras (small wooden boats) are both cheap and atmospheric. They芒聙聶re a practical way to get between Deira and Bur Dubai and an elegant solo experience.

Car Rentals: Pros and Cons For Solo Travellers

Renting a car gives you freedom, especially for desert gateaways or visiting Hatta, but factor in parking fees, tolls (Salik), and Dubai traffic. If you芒聙聶re comfortable driving in new cities and plan multiple day trips, renting can be efficient; otherwise, rely on taxis and organized tours.

Culture, Etiquette and Legal Considerations

Dubai is cosmopolitan but rooted in local customs and laws that affect behaviour and expectations.

  • Dress: Dress modestly in public spaces芒聙聰shoulders and knees covered in mosques and conservative neighbourhoods. Beaches and hotel pools allow swimwear.
  • Alcohol: Legal in licensed venues only. Public intoxication is a criminal offence.
  • Public Displays of Affection: Avoid kissing and intimate displays in public spaces; penalties exist.
  • Photography: Be cautious photographing people, especially Emiratis and women. Always ask before close-up photos.
  • Drugs and Social Media: Extremely strict laws govern drugs and some online behaviour芒聙聰avoid anything that could be misconstrued.
  • Ramadan: During Ramadan, public eating, drinking or smoking during daylight hours is inappropriate in many places芒聙聰plan accordingly.

Respectful behaviour protects you and helps maintain the warm welcome Dubai offers to visitors.

FAQ

Is Dubai safe for solo female travellers?

Yes. Dubai is widely regarded as safe for solo female travellers. Downtown Dubai or Dubai Marina are the most convenient bases. Downtown places you within walking distance of Burj Khalifa and Dubai Mall, while Marina/JBR is ideal for beach time and evening promenades. Choose a neighbourhood with easy metro or taxi access to reduce daily travel time.

Budget travellers can manage with AED 150芒聙聯300 per day. Mid-range travellers should expect AED 400芒聙聯900 daily. Luxury travellers will spend AED 1,000 and above. Key variables include accommodation standard, number of paid attractions and whether you dine at high-end restaurants.

Cafes, beaches and evening markets are also natural places to strike up conversations, and hotel events or local meetups are good structured ways to meet fellow travellers.
