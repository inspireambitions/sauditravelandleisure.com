Cultural Etiquette and Practical Behavior

Dress and conduct

Modest dress is expected in public: men should avoid shorts in certain religious or formal contexts; women should avoid revealing clothing and carry a scarf for mosque visits. Public displays of affection are discouraged. Always ask before photographing people, especially women.

Prayer times and closures

Friday midday is the main prayer time; some attractions may be closed or operate on reduced hours. During Ramadan, food service patterns and public behaviors change ГўВҖВ” respect local practice by avoiding eating and drinking publicly during fasting hours.

Tipping and bargaining

Tipping is customary in restaurants and for guides; 10%ГўВҖВ“15% is standard if a service charge is not included. In souqs, polite haggling is common for souvenirs ГўВҖВ” keep it friendly and smile.

Safety, Health, and Accessibility

Safety

Muscat is very safe by global standards. Take normal urban precautions: watch belongings in crowded souqs and be cautious on narrow coastal roads at night.

Health

Drink bottled water if your system is sensitive, and use sun protection diligently. If you plan to swim or snorkel, choose reputable operators who follow safety standards and provide life vests.

Accessibility

Modern public sites such as the Sultan Qaboos Grand Mosque, Royal Opera House, and Al Mouj area have reasonable accessibility, but older souqs and forts have uneven surfaces and steps. If mobility is a concern, plan the itinerary around ground-level attractions and request assistance from hotels or guides.

Photography and Timing Tips

  • The mosque interior is best photographed before 11:00 AM when the light through windows is softer and crowds are lighter.
