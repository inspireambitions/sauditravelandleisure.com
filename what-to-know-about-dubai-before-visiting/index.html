Culture, Laws, and Etiquette: What You Must Know

Dress and Public Behavior

Dubai is tolerant and cosmopolitan, but public modesty is expected. Dress comfortably but respectfully: shoulders and knees covered in conservative neighborhoods and places of worship. Swimwear is acceptable at beaches, pools and waterparks but not in shopping malls or public streets.

Public displays of affection beyond holding hands are frowned upon and may lead to fines or detention. Be discreet: keeping behavior low-key avoids misunderstandings.

Alcohol, Drugs, and Legal Risks

Alcohol is legal only in licensed venues (hotels, authorized bars and certain clubs). Public intoxication and drunk driving carry severe penalties. Possession, use, or trafficking of illegal drugs is punishable with long prison sentences and heavy fines. Carry valid prescriptions for any controlled medicines and check UAE regulations before traveling.

Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a visa to visit Dubai?

Visa rules depend on your nationality. Many nationalities receive a visa on arrival, while others must apply beforehand. Check your passportâs entry requirements well in advance and ensure at least six monthsâ validity from the travel date.

Is it safe to drink alcohol in Dubai?

Alcohol is legal in licensed venues such as hotels, bars and restaurants for people aged 21 and above. Drinking in public and public intoxication are illegal and punishable. Always consume responsibly and use taxis or ride-hailing services for transit after drinking.

How should I plan my neighborhood choices for a short stay?

Cluster your activities: stay in Downtown if Burj Khalifa and Dubai Mall are priorities; choose Dubai Marina or Palm Jumeirah for beaches and waterfront dining; pick Old Dubai for cultural immersion. If time allows, split your stay across two neighborhoods to reduce transit time.

What should I do during Ramadan as a visitor?

During Ramadan refrain from eating, drinking, smoking or chewing gum in public during daylight hours. Many restaurants are closed or offer screened dining; Iftar meals after sunset are widely available. Dress conservatively and be extra mindful of local customs during this period.
