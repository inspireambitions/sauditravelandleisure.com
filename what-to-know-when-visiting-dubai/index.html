Law, Customs and Cultural Etiquette

Whatâs Allowed and Whatâs Not

Dubai is a tolerant city but governed by UAE laws. Respect for local customs and awareness of local laws is essential. Key rules to know:

  • Photography: Avoid photographing government buildings, military zones, and people (especially women and children) without consent. When in doubt, ask.

Respectful conduct makes travel simple: dress conservatively in cultural sites, follow signs, and be mindful during religious periods such as Ramadan.

Dubai is cosmopolitan and accepts many styles, yet modest dressing is appreciated in public and required in places of worship. Swimwear is fine at beaches, pools and waterparks but not appropriate outside those settings. Carry a shawl or light cover-up for when you move from beach to public areas.
