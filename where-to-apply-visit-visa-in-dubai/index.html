Where To Apply Visit Visa In Dubai

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Noindex, follow
This guide is still available for reference, but it is no longer part of the site's primary indexed Saudi focus.

Introduction

Dubai remains one of the world’s most visited cities, and travel to the UAE continues to grow as air connectivity and visa services expand. Whether you’re flying from Riyadh, Jeddah, or further afield, understanding exactly where to apply for a Dubai visit visa — and which route best fits your timing, budget, and travel profile — is the single most useful step you can take before booking.

Short answer: You can apply for a Dubai visit visa through several official channels: the Federal Authority for Identity (ICA/ICP) and General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs in Dubai (GDRFA) online portals and apps, through major airlines (like Emirates), via approved typing centres and visa service providers, or by asking a UAE-based sponsor (hotel or resident) to apply on your behalf. Each pathway has distinct requirements, processing times, and fees; choose the one that matches your passport nationality, travel dates, and the documentation you can supply.

This post explains each option in clear, practical detail. You will learn step-by-step how to apply using each route, what documents to prepare, typical timelines and fees to expect, common pitfalls and how to avoid them, and how to plan a seamless trip from Saudi Arabia or elsewhere in the Gulf. Along the way I’ll connect this visa process to travel planning logistics — accommodation bookings, travel insurance, and timing — so you arrive in Dubai ready to explore with confidence.

My main message: knowing exactly where to apply and how to prepare will save time, money, and stress, and will let you focus on the parts of the trip that matter most — the sights, the food, and the local experiences.

How UAE Visit Visas Work: The Basics You Must Know

Who Issues Visit Visas?

Two federal-level authorities and Dubai’s local immigration body manage the majority of visitor entry permits:

  • The Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Ports Security (commonly ICA or ICP) issues entry permits and provides the national digital gateway, used for many visa services nationwide.
  • The General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs — Dubai (GDRFA Dubai) handles visas specific to Dubai and provides the Dubai Now app and online services for residents and visitors.
  • Major UAE carriers (notably Emirates and to a lesser extent other regional airlines) offer visa processing for passengers when you book flights with them; they act as facilitators using government channels.
  • Authorized typing centres, visa service providers, and select travel agencies can submit applications on behalf of applicants.

Each of these channels funnels applications into the same formal immigration system, but the support you receive, the documents you must supply, and turnaround times differ.

Who Needs a Prearranged Visa vs. Visa On Arrival

Visa policies depend on your nationality and passport type. There are three common scenarios:

  • Nationals eligible for visa-free entry or visa on arrival: their passport will be stamped at the airport for 30, 90, or 180 days depending on nationality and bilateral agreements.
  • Nationals who must obtain a prearranged visit visa before arrival: these travellers must apply via the channels above and receive an entry permit before boarding.
  • Transit travellers: short-term transit visas (48-hour or 96-hour) are available in specific conditions, usually tied to having an airline ticket through Dubai.

Always verify your specific case on official government portals or with your airline before travel — policies do change and passport validity rules (usually six months) are strict.

Types of Visit Visas You’ll Encounter

Visit visas for Dubai typically fall into these categories:

  • Short transit permits (48-hour, 96-hour) — for brief stopovers.
  • Short-term tourist visas (30-day single or multiple entry).
  • Longer tourist visas (60-day single or multiple entry).
  • Special pre-approved entry permits for selected nationalities or residents of certain countries.

Some visas are extendable once in the UAE; others (e.g., many transit visas) are non-extendable. Understanding the type you need prevents surprises at immigration and avoids overstay fines.

Where To Apply: Full Breakdown of Every Route

Apply Online Through ICA/ICP (Federal Gateway)

The ICA/ICP portal is the national digital channel used by many applicants and companies. It’s designed for secure submission and is widely used for different visa categories.

How it works in practice: Create an account on the ICA portal or use UAE PASS for authentication. Choose the visa type, upload your documents (passport copy, photo, flight booking, hotel confirmation), pay the fee, and await approval. A digital permit will be issued that you can print or keep on your device.

Why choose ICA/ICP: Direct submission, fewer middlemen, and a straightforward digital trail. This is often the best route if you’re outside UAE and want a clear official receipt of application.

Timing and fees: Processing can range from same day to several working days depending on nationality and the service level. Fees vary by visa type — short-term tourism visas are typically in a moderate fee band, while longer or multiple-entry visas cost more. Always check current fees on the portal.

Apply via GDRFA Dubai (Dubai’s Local Channel)

GDRFA Dubai controls entry permits specific to Dubai visitors and residents. Their site and the Dubai Now app provide a route for visitors to apply for Dubai visit visas and for residents to sponsor guests.

How it works: Use GDRFA’s website or Dubai Now app to submit an application, often via a sponsor (hotel or resident). The app supports document upload and payment. Once approved, you will receive an electronic entry permit.

Best fit: If your trip centers on Dubai and you have a Dubai-based sponsor (hotel or family/friend), GDRFA is frequently the smoothest channel. Dubai Now also integrates local services (parking, permits), so it’s convenient for people already in the UAE.

Processing and documentation: GDRFA processing times mirror federal channels for many visa types. Expect similar requirements: passport scan, photo, flight itinerary, hotel reservation or tenancy contract, and sponsor ID for UAE residents.

Apply Through Your Airline (Emirates and Other Approved Carriers)

If you booked with certain carriers, they can apply for your UAE entry permit on your behalf.

How it works: With an eligible booking (often Emirates), use the "Manage Your Booking" feature on the airline’s website after purchase to apply for a visa. Airlines often require that your entire trip originates and returns on their flights and that tickets are confirmed before they process a visa.

Advantages: Convenience and speed—airlines can issue a visa without you traveling to an embassy or typing centre. For many nationalities, Emirates offers a quick route to a 30- or 60-day tourist visa.

Limitations: This service is usually limited to passengers holding tickets with the facilitating airline, and additional carrier-specific conditions (ticket number format, minimum lead time) apply. Fees and refund rules are controlled by the airline and can be nonrefundable once processed.

Apply Through a Hotel or UAE-Based Sponsor

Hotels, tour operators, and UAE residents can sponsor visit visas in many cases.

How it works: The hotel or sponsor submits the visa application via official channels using their resident or company ID. You provide the passport copy, photo, and travel details. The sponsor handles the rest and receives the e-permit.

When to use: When booking a package with a hotel, or if you have a friend, family member, or employer in the UAE willing to sponsor your visit.

Risks and responsibilities: Sponsors are accountable for the visitor’s conduct in the UAE and may need to guarantee return or provide a security deposit in some cases. Ensure the sponsor is legitimate and uses official processes; you should still receive the official electronic entry permit directly.

Apply At a UAE Embassy or Consulate (Where Applicable)

In some countries, especially where online options aren’t practical, visa applications are handled at UAE embassies or consulates or through contracted visa centres (e.g., VFS Global). This route is common for nationals requiring more complex vetting or additional paperwork.

Practical note: Embassies usually process visas when applicants require additional verification. If your country’s UAE embassy uses a visa centre, you’ll submit biometrics or hard copy documents there. Turnaround times in this route can be longer than online methods.

Use Approved Typing Centres and Visa Service Providers

Authorized typing centres and visa service agencies operate in many countries to prepare and submit your application on your behalf.

How they help: They fill forms, scan and upload documents, and act as intermediaries with the government portal. They are useful where applicants are not confident with online submission or where translation and notarization are needed.

Cost and caution: These services charge extra for convenience. Only use licensed providers; avoid individuals offering cash-only deals that bypass official channels — these are common sources of scams.

Transit Visas and On-Arrival Visas

Transit visas (48-hour and 96-hour) are usually available for passengers connecting through Dubai when certain conditions are met, typically requiring a valid international ticket with a confirmed onward journey. Visa on arrival is available to passport holders from specific countries and generally grants a 30- or 90-day entry stamp at the airport without prearrangement.

If you qualify for visa on arrival, no pre-application is necessary. If you need a transit visa, check the airline’s rules — some carriers will arrange it for you if your itinerary meets the criteria.

Step-By-Step: How To Apply For A Dubai Visit Visa (Practical Blueprint)

Below I lay out the practical steps you should follow, regardless of the route you choose. The process varies slightly by channel, but these stages cover the essentials and the decisions you’ll face.

  1. Confirm your visa requirement and eligibility: Check whether your passport offers visa-free entry or visa on arrival and whether you are required to obtain a prearranged visa. For most travellers, the authoritative check is through official government services or your airline.
  2. Choose the right channel: Decide between ICA/ICP, GDRFA Dubai, airline facilitation, hotel sponsorship, embassy, or a licensed service provider. Base your choice on convenience, speed, and whether you have a UAE-based sponsor.
  3. Gather documents: Passport copy (minimum six months validity usually), passport-style photo, confirmed return ticket, hotel reservation or sponsor tenancy contract, and any additional documents your nationality may require (e.g., national ID, visa for other countries, or proof of relationship for sponsored family members).
  4. Submit the application and pay fees: Use the chosen portal or provider. For airline facilitation, make sure your ticket details meet the airline’s rules. Keep payment receipts and the application reference number.
  5. Receive the electronic permit: After approval, you’ll receive a permit/reference number or an e-visa PDF. Print a copy and save a digital version to show at immigration if asked.
  6. Travel with the required documentation: Present passport, printed e-permit if available, hotel booking and onward ticket at immigration. Be prepared for random visual screenings and document checks.
  7. If necessary, extend or change visa status within the UAE: If your visa is extendable, do this through GDRFA or at airport immigration offices before the initial period expires. For longer stays, consider converting to a residency permit if eligible.

The single most common mistake: assuming an airline will process a visa for you without confirming the particular airline rules for ticket format and lead time. Always verify before relying on this convenience.

Documents Checklist (One Quick List)

  • Valid passport copy (machine-readable, at least six months validity)
  • Passport-sized color photograph
  • Confirmed return or onward flight tickets
  • Confirmed hotel booking or tenancy contract / sponsor ID
  • Proof of relationship for family-sponsored applications (if required)
  • Additional nationality-specific documents as requested (national ID, residency proofs, etc.)

Which Channel Is Best For You? A Practical Comparison

If You Need Speed and Direct Official Confirmation: ICA/ICP or GDRFA

Use ICA/ICP for a federal submission and GDRFA if your trip centers on Dubai with a local sponsor. Both routes offer official receipts and digital permits that immigration recognizes with minimal friction.

If You Prefer Convenience with Your Flight Booking: Airline Facilitation

Airline facilitation (Emirates, etc.) is ideal if you booked eligible flights and want a nearly hands-off process. It’s convenient but relies on carrier-specific ticket formats and timelines.

If You Have A Sponsor or Hotel Package

Let the sponsor apply. This is convenient and common for tourists booking hotels and packages. Ensure the permit is issued in your name — do not rely solely on verbal confirmation from the hotel.

If You Are In A Country Without Solid Online Infrastructure: Embassy Or Visa Centres

For more complicated cases, embassy submission or visa centres (VFS where available) is sensible. Expect slightly longer processing times and sometimes more documentation.

If You Lack Tech Confidence Or Need Local Support: Approved Typing Centres

Use licensed typing centres for document handling, translations, and secure submission. Expect added fees but benefit from in-person guidance.

Fees, Processing Times, and Refund Rules — What To Expect

Fees vary by route, visa type, and applicant nationality. As a reference point, short-term tourist visas offered by airlines and travel service providers commonly appear in a low-to-moderate price band, while longer or multiple-entry visas cost more. Processing time ranges from a few hours (with premium services or same-day airline facilitation) to several working days for embassy or VFS processing.

Refund rules: Visa fees are usually nonrefundable once the visa has been processed. Airlines and service providers often have specific refund and cancellation policies. If a visa application is refused, the processing fee might still be nonrefundable — check terms before payment.

Common Pitfalls and How To Avoid Them

1. Not Matching Flight and Accommodation Dates

If you apply for a visa through an airline or a portal that requires ticket and hotel dates to align, mismatched dates may cause rejection. Double-check all dates before submission.

2. Using Unlicensed Intermediaries

Avoid individuals or unverified agencies offering “quick visas” outside recognized channels. Use official portals, airline services, embassy channels, or licensed typing centres only.

3. Ignoring Passport Validity

Most UAE visa routes require at least six months of passport validity. Renew passports early if needed.

4. Missing Additional Country-Specific Requirements

Some nationalities require extra documents (national ID, additional proof of residence, etc.). Verify early and include these documents to prevent delays.

5. Relying On Verbal Confirmation From A Sponsor

Always obtain the e-permit in writing and save the official document. Do not travel without a printed or digital copy of the permit if you were requested to present it at immigration.

Extending, Changing, or Canceling A Visit Visa

Extending Your Stay

If your visa is extendable, extensions are usually processed through GDRFA Dubai offices or airport immigration. Extensions require a valid passport and sometimes the original permit; there are fees and processing times. Non-extendable visas must be respected to avoid fines.

Changing Visa Type After Arrival

Converting a short-term visit visa into a residency visa typically requires meeting new eligibility criteria (work permit, family sponsorship, property-based residency, etc.) and is handled by immigration authorities. Plan ahead — conversion can be administratively heavy.

Canceling Or Withdrawing An Application

If you need to cancel an application before processing, check the issuing channel’s terms. Some fees may be nonrefundable. Once approved and unused, a permit may still be nonrefundable depending on the provider.

Overstays, Fines, and Exit Procedures

Overstaying a visit visa in the UAE results in fines that accrue per day and can escalate to significant amounts. In many cases the sponsor or the visitor will be responsible for settling fines before departure. Repeated or long overstays can affect future visa eligibility.

If you are late leaving due to emergency circumstances, contact your sponsor, local immigration authorities, or your embassy as soon as possible for guidance. And always plan departure early to avoid last-minute overstays.

Special Considerations For Travellers From Saudi Arabia And The Gulf

Travelling between Saudi Arabia and the UAE is frequent for business, family, and tourism. Here is practical advice tailored to travellers departing from major Saudi hubs:

  • Flights from Riyadh and Jeddah: Book early and verify airline visa facilitation policies if you intend to use airline services. If you plan multi-city travel, coordinate ticketing so any airline-provided visa covers your full itinerary.
  • GCC citizens and residents: Check your specific passport and residency rules. While GCC nationals often have simplified entry, certain GCC residence permit holders may need to apply for a UAE visa before arrival. Verify with official sources.
  • Land border crossings: If you cross by road from neighbouring Gulf states, confirm the visa entry rules for both the UAE and the country you depart from. Procedures differ for land entry versus air arrival.

How To Verify Official Information Quickly

Use the following authoritative channels for verification before applying:

  • ICA/ICP official portal for federal visa services.
  • GDRFA Dubai website and Dubai Now app for Dubai-specific visa and resident sponsorship processes.
  • Your airline’s “Manage Your Booking” visa section for carrier-facilitated applications.
  • UAE embassy or consulate websites or local visa centres (e.g., VFS Global) in your country.

When in doubt, call the airline or the official authority’s helpline. Practical Scenarios And Decision Frameworks

Here are common traveller profiles and the recommended visa channels for each. These scenarios are advisory frameworks you can apply to your own circumstances.

Scenario A — Short Business Trip or Quick Stopover (1–3 Days)

Best channel: Airline transit visa (if applicable) or visa on arrival (for eligible passports). If you have a confirmed short stay with a Dubai hotel, hotel-sponsorship through GDRFA is straightforward. Book flights that meet the carrier’s requirements if using airline facilitation.

Scenario B — Tourist Staying 7–30 Days

Best channel: Apply for a tourist visit visa through ICA/ICP, GDRFA, or via airline facilitation if you booked eligible tickets. For the cleanest digital record, applying directly via federal or GDRFA channels yields an official e-permit quickly.

Scenario C — Visiting Family or Being Sponsored by a UAE Resident

Best channel: Have your UAE-based sponsor apply through GDRFA to tie your permit to a residence or tenancy agreement. Ensure the sponsor provides official confirmation and the e-permit.

Scenario D — Multi-Destination Trip Starting in Dubai (e.g., Dubai + Abu Dhabi)

Best channel: Use GDRFA or airline facilitation for Dubai entry. For side trips to Abu Dhabi, check each emirate’s local requirements only if you plan to change residence status. Practical Tips For a Smooth Immigration Experience on Arrival

Arrive at the airport with these essentials easily accessible: passport, printed or digital copy of your e-permit, hotel confirmation, and onward ticket. Immigration officers may request additional evidence or perform visual screening, so have duplicates of the key documents ready. Keep contact details of your sponsor or hotel in your phone and printed form.

If you anticipate language needs, carry translation of critical documents or ensure your sponsor uses official English forms when applying. For visitors who plan to convert to a residency permit after arrival, consult with immigration or licensed typing centres early to map the required steps.

How To Avoid Visa-Related Scams

Scams often promise faster visas for cash or ask you to hand over passports to third parties in exchange for a quick permit. Protect yourself by:

  • Using only official government portals, known airlines, UAE embassies/consulates, licensed typing centres, or verified hotels.
  • Confirming payment receipts and tracking numbers from official systems.
  • Verifying the legitimacy of a sponsor by requesting their Emirates ID number or company details and checking submission through official channels.

If something feels off, stop and verify through official helplines before proceeding.

For example, if you are flying from Riyadh or Jeddah and plan a short city-focused trip, apply for your visa early through the airline or ICA to avoid dependencies on hotel confirmation timing. If you plan to visit multiple Saudi and UAE cities, coordinate entry permit dates with flight segments to ensure coverage for the full travel period.

When Things Go Wrong: Refusals, Delays, Or Entry Denials

Visa refusals or delays are rare when documentation is complete and accurate, but they do happen. If denied:

  • Request the refusal reason in writing (where possible).
  • Contact your sponsor, the issuing authority, or your airline to clarify next steps.
  • If the issue stems from an incomplete application, you may reapply after correcting the documents.
  • If denied at the border, cooperate with immigration officers and follow their guidance; you may be returned on the next available flight.

Always keep a backup plan and extra time in your itinerary to resolve administrative delays.

FAQ

Do I need to apply for a Dubai visit visa if I hold a Saudi passport?

Most Saudi citizens benefit from easy entry to the UAE, but rules can change. Confirm passport validity and current entry policies before departure using official UAE portals or your airline. If you hold a non-Saudi residency in the Gulf, verify whether you need a prearranged visa.

Can I apply for a Dubai visit visa after I arrive in the UAE?

If you qualify for visa on arrival, you can receive entry without prearrangement at immigration. For those who require a prearranged visa, you should obtain it before travel. Some visas can be arranged through an airline upon check-in if their facility supports your ticket type.

How long does it take to get an electronic entry permit?

Processing can vary from a few hours (with airline facilitation or premium services) up to several working days via embassies or visa centres. Apply well in advance of travel, especially during busy seasons.

What should I do if my visa application is refused?

Request the reason for refusal and correct any documentation errors. Depending on the reason, you may reapply or seek clarification from the issuing authority, your sponsor, or an embassy. If refused at the border, comply with immigration procedures and coordinate with your airline for return travel if required.
