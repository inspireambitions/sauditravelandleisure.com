How to Find Reliable Sellers and Vet Quality

Buying a healthy plant starts with inspection and smart questions. Sellers vary, but the evaluation criteria are universal. Before you commit, inspect the plant and ask targeted questions.

Visual Inspection: What Healthy Looks Like

Examine leaves, stems, soil, pot condition, and root health where visible. Leaves should be vibrant, not discolored, and free of sticky residue (a sign of pests). Soil should be moist but not waterlogged; overly compacted or bone-dry soil indicates poor recent care. Inspect the plant for pests like mealybugs (white cottony clusters), aphids, or webbing from spider mites. For potted plants, gently lift the pot where possible â a healthy root system fills the container but should not be rootbound to the point of circling or strangling.

Ask the Seller: Targeted Questions

Ask where the plant was propagated, how long the seller has had it, and whether it has been treated for pests or disease. For outdoor plants, ask about frost tolerance and sun exposure. For indoor plants, ask about light and humidity needs and whether the seller can pot the plant into a decorative container. A knowledgeable seller will ask about your site conditionsâthis shows professional care.

Evaluate Seller Services and Guarantees

For expensive or large specimens, ask for a post-sale checklist or aftercare support. Nurseries and established online sellers that provide warranties or clear return policies reduce your risk.

Legitimate sellers should accept common payment methods and provide receipts. For very large orders or imported plants, ask for documentation that confirms the plantâs health and origin. If you are sourcing a protected or regulated species, verify that all legal requirements are met.

Watering, Soil and Microclimate: Practical Care for Riyadh Homes

Watering Philosophy

In Riyadh the main risk for most plants is overwatering combined with high evaporative stress. Use well-draining mixes for container plants. Heavy garden soil should be amended with coarse sand, perlite, or pumice for succulents, while richer mixes with compost suit vegetables and tropical houseplants. When repotting, move up one pot size at a time and avoid sudden shifts in sun exposure.

Afternoon sun in Riyadh is intense; provide filtered light or shade cloth for sensitive species. Use movable pots and shading solutions on terraces to protect plants during heatwaves.
