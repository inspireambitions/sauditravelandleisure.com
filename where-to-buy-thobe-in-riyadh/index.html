Where to Buy Thobe in Riyadh

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Introduction

Riyadh is changing fast: recent years have seen a dramatic rise in international visitors looking to experience Saudi culture, and shopping for a thobe—the classic long garment worn by men across the Kingdom—has become a common part of the travel plan. Whether you want an off-the-rack garment that fits comfortably for daily wear, a lightweight summer thobe for desert excursions, or a bespoke piece to wear at a formal event, Riyadh offers every option: traditional souks, modern boutiques inside luxury malls, skilled tailors, and robust online sellers.

Short answer: You can buy thobes in Riyadh across three main channels—traditional markets and fabric souks for value and variety, branded boutiques and department stores in major malls for ready-made quality, and bespoke tailors for custom fits and premium fabrics. Each route has clear advantages depending on budget, fabric preference, and how much time you want to invest in fittings.

This article will explain exactly where to shop for a thobe in Riyadh, how to choose fabrics and fits, what price ranges to expect, step-by-step measuring and ordering advice, bargaining and quality-check tips, and a practical shopping itinerary you can use whether you have a few hours or a couple of days. You’ll also find wardrobe-care guidance and cultural notes so you leave with a thobe that looks, feels, and fits like a local’s.

My aim as the KSA Travel Insider is to give you both the evocative reasons to wear a thobe—and the precise, practical blueprint to buy one with confidence.

Why Buying a Thobe in Riyadh is Worth It

Cultural authenticity and fitting experience

Riyadh is one of the best places to buy a thobe because you’ll encounter the full range: lightweight summer materials engineered for desert heat; thicker blends and wool options for winter; embroidered and tailored designs that reflect local taste. Shopping in person lets you feel fabrics, try on styles, and get instant tailoring adjustments—advantages you won’t get from a quick online purchase.

Quality and craftmanship

The Kingdom has a long textile tradition. Riyadh’s tailors and specialty stores are experienced in garment construction suited to the local climate and lifestyle. Many tailors offer hand-finished seams and locally preferred collar styles, while high-end boutiques import premium fabrics for a more luxurious finish.

Value and variety

Riyadh’s market structure delivers competitive options. If you value a ready-made thobe with a high-quality finish, malls and branded stores will meet that need. If you want good value or to customize details like cuff style or embroidery, souks and tailors offer better flexibility on price and design.

Understanding Thobe Types and Fabrics

Styles of Thobe

The thobe is a deceptively simple garment, but variations in cut and detail make a major difference to comfort and formality.

  • Classic straight-cut thobe: Traditional fit with a roomy chest and straight silhouette—very common for everyday wear.
  • Slim-fit or tailored thobe: Narrower cut through the torso, giving a more modern silhouette favored in business and formal settings.
  • Embroidered thobe: Decorative stitching along the chest, collar, or cuffs. Often used for weddings or special occasions.
  • Summer/winter variants: Lighter weaves and linen blends for hot months; heavier gabardine or wool blends for cooler seasons.

Fabric choices and how they perform

  • Cotton: Breathable and common. Pure cotton is comfortable in heat but wrinkles easily.
  • Cotton blends (poly-cotton): More wrinkle-resistant and budget-friendly; a practical everyday choice.
  • Linen: Extremely breathable but tends to wrinkle; great for casual summer wear.
  • Gabardine and wool blends: Warmer and smoother—appropriate for winter or formal thobes.
  • Luxury weaves (Egyptian cotton, fine poplins, silk blends): Used in high-end pieces for a refined drape and feel.

Collar and cuff options

Collar types and cuff styling affect formality. The mandarin collar is common; stand collars and small laps are also used. Cuff designs range from plain to buttoned or embroidered cuffs—choose based on how formal you want the garment to be.

Where to Buy: Neighborhoods, Malls, Souks, and Tailors

Best neighborhoods for thobe shopping

Riyadh’s shopping scene clusters into a few practical zones. Start by deciding the style channel you prefer, then target these areas.

  • Olaya and Al Olaya Road (modern boutiques and mall access): This central business and shopping district houses major malls and fashion retailers—ideal if you want branded or ready-made thobes with straightforward returns and quality assurance. For an overview of what Riyadh offers visitors, check the Riyadh shopping scene.
  • Al Batha and market districts (value shopping and tailors): If you want bargaining power, fabric choice, and local tailors, head to the older market districts where small shops and tailors cluster.
  • Northern and eastern suburbs with larger retail centers: Malls such as Riyadh Park and Panorama Center host both department stores and specialty men’s wear boutiques.

Malls and department stores (ready-made, brand-focused)

  • Kingdom Centre and Al Faisaliyah region: These landmarks are where you’ll find department stores and boutique chains that stock high-quality ready-made thobes. You’ll pay a premium for convenience and consistent sizing.
  • Riyadh Park and Riyadh Gallery Mall: These shopping centers balance mid-range and premium options and are good places to compare ready-made fits and fabrics.

Shopping in malls is the fastest option for travelers with limited time: sizes are standardized, and stores often accept card payments and offer exchange or return policies.

Traditional markets and souks (fabric and bargain options)

Traditional markets remain an excellent place to buy fabric by the meter or find locally sewn thobes at lower prices. Souk areas let you inspect a fabric’s weave, test colors under natural light, and negotiate on price.

When visiting markets, have an image of the style and an idea of fabric preference. This reduces time spent searching through stalls.

Tailors and bespoke shops (custom fits, premium finishes)

For the best fit—or a thobe tailored for a special event—a skilled tailor is the way to go. Many tailors in Riyadh will:

  • Take precise measurements and advise on cut and collar style.
  • Allow fabric selection from swatches.
  • Complete fittings and adjustments in 1–2 days for local customers, sometimes faster for urgent requests.

Expect to pay more than a ready-made piece, but the final product will be tailored to your body and preferences. If you plan to commission a bespoke thobe, allocate time for an initial measurement, one fitting (sometimes two), and final pickup.

Online stores and local e-commerce

Many Riyadh-based boutiques have online storefronts. Online shopping is convenient and useful for pre-research—look for seller return policies, sizing guides, and fabric descriptions. Note that color and drape can look different online than in person.

How Much Will a Thobe Cost in Riyadh?

Pricing varies widely depending on fabric, tailoring, and brand.

  • Budget options: 100–250 SAR — mass-produced thobes in poly-cotton blends found in markets or discount shops.
  • Mid-range: 250–700 SAR — better fabrics, ready-made branded pieces, or a decent tailored thobe in cotton blends.
  • Premium and bespoke: 700 SAR and up — high-quality fabrics, luxury brands, or fully bespoke garments with fine details and embroidery. Special occasion or designer thobes can reach several thousand riyals.

Tip: If you’re buying multiple thobes (for family or as gifts), ask shops about bulk discounts.

How to Choose the Right Thobe: Practical Buying Criteria

Fit and silhouette

A proper fit is the single biggest factor in how a thobe looks. The chest should have a modest drape—too tight and the garment looks strained; too loose and the proportions are off. The shoulder seam should rest near your natural shoulder point. Sleeve length will typically reach the wrist bone.

Fabric performance

Choose fabric based on the season and activity. For daytime outings in Riyadh’s heat, prefer lightweight cotton or linen blends. For cooler months or evening events, opt for thicker weaves.

Color and finish

Classic white remains standard for formal and everyday wear. Off-white, cream, and light beige are common casual choices. For events, deeper shades or embroidered accents can be appropriate. Pay attention to the fabric’s sheen: matte finishes read more traditional; subtle sheen suggests a dressier option.

Construction quality

Look at seams, stitching density, and collar reinforcement. A well-made thobe will have even stitching, secure buttons, and a well-constructed collar that holds its shape.

Practical features

Consider pockets (location and depth), closure types (buttons, hidden plackets), and ease of movement—especially if you plan to drive or take part in active excursions.

Step-by-Step: Measuring and Ordering a Bespoke Thobe

  1. Stand straight in a light shirt without bulky layers.
  2. Use a flexible cloth tape measure.
  3. Key measurements to record:
    • Neck circumference at the base.
    • Shoulder width across the back from seam to seam.
    • Chest circumference at the widest point.
    • Waist circumference at the natural waist.
    • Hip circumference (for longer cuts).
    • Sleeve length from shoulder point to wrist.
    • Thobe length from the highest shoulder point to the desired hem (mid-calf or ankle).
  4. Double-check measurements and ask the tailor to mark allowances for ease.

If you prefer, ask the tailor to take measurements to ensure precision. Many tailors are experienced with customers unfamiliar with local sizing and can advise the precise amount of ease to include.

Two Practical Lists

  1. How to Measure for a Thobe (numbered list for clarity)
  • Remove bulky outer layers and stand naturally.
  • Measure neck, shoulder width, chest, waist, hips, sleeve length, and overall length.
  • Round measurements to the nearest half-centimeter.
  • Communicate intended use (daily wear vs. formal) so the tailor knows how much ease to add.
  1. Quick Bargaining and Quality Checklist (bullet list)
  • Inspect fabric under natural light; check weave tightness.
  • Ask about fabric composition and care instructions.
  • Compare price with at least two vendors or tailors before committing.
  • For market purchases, propose a lower initial offer and negotiate calmly.
  • For bespoke work, confirm turnaround time and number of fittings included.

Sample Shopping Strategies: Half-Day and Full-Day Plans

Half-Day: Efficient Ready-Made Shopping (3–4 hours)

Begin at a central mall like Kingdom Centre or Riyadh Park to sample ready-made fits and brands. Try three to four different styles to compare silhouettes. If you find a good fit, check return policies and ask about quick tailoring services offered by the mall’s alteration counters. If time allows, pop into a nearby boutique for a second opinion on fabric quality.

If you prefer local markets, spend the morning in a market district comparing fabric options, then select a shop that will sew a simple ready-made thobe within a day.

Full-Day: Bespoke Thobe with Tailor (6–8 hours, including fittings)

Start early by visiting a reputable tailor or boutique offering bespoke services. Choose your fabric from swatches and have the tailor take measurements. Use the rest of the day to explore nearby markets or cafes; return for a first fitting later the same day if the tailor offers rapid service. Expect to schedule a final fitting the next day or within 48 hours for perfected finishing—plan accordingly in your itinerary.

How to Avoid Common Mistakes When Buying a Thobe

Mistake: Buying solely on price

Very cheap thobes are tempting, but low-cost fabrics and poor stitching will show quickly. If you want a garment that looks polished, invest at least mid-range or choose a tailor who uses better fabrics.

Mistake: Neglecting how the thobe behaves when you move

Try walking, sitting, and raising your arms while trying on a thobe. Check for tightness across the shoulders and chest and ensure sleeve length and hem are comfortable.

Mistake: Assuming one size fits all

Sizes vary by manufacturer and cut. Try multiple sizes and cuts; use tailoring to fine-tune length and sleeve fit.

Mistake: Missing tailoring timelines

If you need the thobe for a specific event, confirm the tailor’s turnaround time and include buffer days for additional adjustments.

Care, Maintenance and Travel-Friendly Tips

Washing and fabric care

  • Pure cotton: Machine wash on a gentle cycle; air-dry to prevent shrinkage.
  • Blends: Follow label instructions—many blends tolerate machine washing at low temperatures.
  • Linen: Hand-wash or use a gentle cycle; iron while slightly damp to reduce wrinkles.
  • Luxury fabrics: Consider professional dry-cleaning.

Bring a portable steamer or use hotel ironing services to keep the thobe crisp. When packing, roll the thobe rather than folding sharply to minimize creases; place it in a garment bag for long-distance travel.

Storage and longevity

Store in a cool, dry place and avoid overcrowding. Mothballs are not necessary for cotton; use cedar blocks if storing long-term.

Payment, Returns, and Language Tips

Payment methods

Most malls and formal boutiques accept cards. Smaller market stalls and some tailors prefer cash (Saudi riyals). For bargaining in markets, cash is an advantage.

Returns and alterations

Clarify return and alteration policies before purchase. Malls often have standard return windows; bespoke tailors typically do not accept returns but will perform adjustments.

Language and communication

English is commonly spoken in malls and by many tailors, but having a phrasebook or translation app helps when negotiating in traditional markets. Simple phrases about fabric, fit, and turnaround time facilitate clearer communication.

Riyadh provides a spectrum of options—from high-end boutiques and mall convenience to traditional markets and skilled tailors. Coastal cities like Jeddah often emphasize lighter fabrics and different dressing customs, while Eastern Province cities such as Dammam and Al Khobar have their own retail scenes and preferences for certain fabric blends. When to Visit to Find the Best Selection

Riyadh’s retail year is steady year-round, but certain periods can affect availability and selection:

  • Summer: Lightweight fabrics are plentiful.
  • Winter: Heavier fabrics and thicker blends become available for formal and evening wear.
  • Festival and holiday seasons: Stores may display special embroidered or ceremonial thobes and limited designs.
  • Weekdays vs. weekends: Malls and boutiques are less crowded on weekdays; souks may be livelier and have more small-shop options on weekends.

Plan your shopping day to match the variety you want to see—mall-focused expeditions are fast during weekdays; market visits are better when vendors are fully stocked on busier shopping days.

How to Verify Quality and Authenticity

  • Ask for the fabric composition and feel the weave density.
  • Examine button stitching and seam finishing on the inside.
  • Check collar interfacing—good collars have reinforced bands that hold shape.
  • For branded thobes, check labels and look for consistent logos or brand marks.
  • For embroidery, confirm the stitch density and thread quality.

If you are buying a higher-ticket item, consider photo documentation of the fabric and label, receipt retention, and confirmation of after-sales support.

Practical Tips for Non-Residents and Tourists

  • Bring a smartphone to photograph swatches and comparison pieces.
  • Carry a small amount of cash for market purchases and tipping tailors for extra service.
  • Leave a buffer in your itinerary for fittings and tailoring—same-day bespoke work is sometimes possible but often requires planning.
  • If purchasing multiple thobes to ship home, ask about international shipping and packaging options; many boutiques can arrange secure shipping for an extra fee.

Responsible Shopping and Cultural Considerations

While buying a thobe can be a cultural experience, be mindful of local etiquette. Dress respectfully while shopping, and be open to asking about appropriate styles for different occasions. If you plan to wear the thobe in religious settings or formal events, confirm that the style is appropriate—many vendors and tailors will advise you on local expectations.

Realistic Timeline for Buying and Wearing a Thobe in Riyadh

  • Quick purchase (ready-made): 30 minutes to 2 hours (fitting and immediate purchase).
  • Tailor-made, expedited: 1–2 days with rapid-turntailors.
  • Tailor-made, standard: 3–7 days depending on fabric availability and number of fittings.
  • Special-order fabrics or bespoke luxury items: up to 2–3 weeks.

Plan accordingly if you have a fixed departure date or event.

How to Tell a Good Tailor from an Average One

A skilled tailor will:

  • Ask detailed questions about intended use and preference for fit.
  • Take precise measurements and explain how much ease will be added.
  • Show fabric swatches and explain differences in weave and care.
  • Offer at least one fitting and clear turnaround timelines.
  • Retain measurements for future orders.

If a tailor rushes you or is vague about timelines, consider seeking another shop—your satisfaction depends on communication and craft.

When to Choose Ready-Made vs. Tailored

  • Choose ready-made if you have limited time, want standard sizing, or prefer easier returns.
  • Choose tailored if precise fit and fabric choice matter, or if you plan to wear the thobe often and want it to last.

A hybrid approach works well for many travelers: buy an affordable ready-made thobe for immediate needs and commission a bespoke piece as a souvenir or for a special event.

Final Practical Checklist Before You Buy

  • Know the occasion and season for the thobe you want.
  • Have measurements or be ready for the tailor to measure you.
  • Decide on a budget range and be ready to compare options.
  • Inspect fabric, seams, and collar construction.
  • Confirm tailoring turnaround and care instructions.
  • Keep receipts and photos for potential exchanges or international shipping.

Conclusion

Buying a thobe in Riyadh is both practical and culturally rewarding—whether you opt for a ready-made garment from a mall boutique, a well-priced piece from a market, or a tailored thobe crafted to your measurements. Use a structured approach: define your priorities (fit, fabric, budget), choose the shopping channel that matches those priorities, and follow the step-by-step measuring and quality checks to ensure you come away with a garment that suits your needs.

FAQ

Where are the best areas in Riyadh to try multiple thobe shops in one visit?

Head to centralized districts with both malls and markets—Olaya for malls and established boutiques, and market districts for a concentrated group of fabric and tailoring shops. These areas let you compare ready-made and bespoke options without long transfers.

How much should I expect a good bespoke thobe to cost?

For a well-constructed bespoke thobe with mid-to-high quality fabric, expect to pay from roughly 700 SAR upward, depending on fabric, lining, and embroidery. Simpler bespoke options in local fabric can be more affordable, while designer or imported fabrics increase cost.

Can I have a thobe tailored and ready the same day?

Yes, but it depends on the tailor and complexity of the garment. Many skilled tailors can offer expedited service for a surcharge; confirm turnaround time before committing and allow for at least one fitting.

Are there good online thobe options if I prefer not to visit stores?

Yes—many reputable Riyadh boutiques operate online storefronts. Use online shopping for pre-research, but if fit is important, plan an in-person fitting or choose sellers with generous return policies.
