Where to Get Alcohol in Riyadh

Updated October 13, 2025 By Saudi Travel and Leisure Editorial Team Indexable Saudi guide

Introduction

Riyadh is changing fast. Tourism numbers and international business ties have risen sharply as Saudi Arabia pursues economic diversification, and questions about practicalities—like where to get alcohol in Riyadh—are common among visitors and long-term residents alike. Understanding what is legal, what remains off-limits, and how the landscape is evolving is essential to enjoying the city responsibly and confidently.

Short answer: For most people in Riyadh today, there is no legal way to buy alcohol. The only long-standing legal exception has been tightly controlled supplies for diplomatic missions, accessed through a regulated app and quota system. Recent government announcements (aimed at select licensed locations from 2026 in restricted zones such as five-star hotels and tourist resorts) suggest limited expansion, but rules remain strict and access tightly controlled.

This article explains the current rules, the diplomatic exception, practical risks, and the likely changes ahead. I’ll also give you step-by-step planning tools, alternatives that preserve the social experience without alcohol, and a clear blueprint you can use to travel, work, or live in Riyadh while staying within the law and enjoying the city fully.

Legal Foundation: Why Saudi Arabia’s Rules Matter

The Legal and Cultural Context

Saudi Arabia’s prohibition on public sale and consumption of alcohol is rooted in religious law and decades of national policy. That framework is non-negotiable for citizens and for public life across the kingdom. Enforcement varies by context but has historically included severe penalties for possession, consumption, importation, and sale outside officially sanctioned channels.

This legal context shapes everyday life: public events, family gatherings, and hospitality sectors have developed around zero-alcohol norms, and hospitality professionals are trained to deliver high-quality experiences without relying on alcoholic beverages. Appreciating this context is the first step to navigating Riyadh respectfully and safely.

How Enforcement Works

Enforcement combines border controls, customs inspection, municipal enforcement, and police action. Penalties for breaking alcohol laws can include fines, imprisonment, public corporal punishment in extreme cases, and deportation for foreign residents and visitors. For practical travel planning, assume strict enforcement and high risk for any attempt to bring in, purchase, or consume alcohol outside authorized channels.

The Current Legal Options

Diplomatic Access: The Established Exception

For decades, diplomatic missions have been able to import limited quantities of alcohol under international agreements using diplomatic pouches. To regulate leakage into illicit markets, Saudi authorities introduced a formal system in recent years: a dedicated store in the Diplomatic Quarter and a “Diplo” registration process that enforces monthly quotas, identity checks, and purchase tracking. Only registered non-Muslim diplomatic personnel and their authorized visitors can use this channel.

How it works in practice:

  • Registration and clearance are required through official channels.
  • Purchases are subject to a monthly quota system and careful monitoring.
  • Entry and purchase may require identity verification and a digital code from the Foreign Ministry.

If you do not represent a diplomatic mission, this channel is not available.

Emerging Licensed Venues: The 2026 Licensing Plan

In May 2025 the government announced plans to license alcohol sales in roughly 600 designated locations from 2026. The program, still in its early operational stages when this article goes to press, intends to allow wine, beer, and cider (not spirits above 20% alcohol) in highly controlled contexts such as five-star hotels, luxury resorts, certain tourist hubs, designated residential compounds for expatriates, and international cultural or business events.

What to expect from this rollout:

  • Licenses limited to specific, high-end venues and tourist zones (e.g., Red Sea resort areas, NEOM, Sindalah).
  • Strict rules on sales, service, and the separation of spaces where alcohol is available.
  • Mandatory training for staff and tight record-keeping obligations for licensees.
  • Continued prohibition in public spaces, retail stores, private homes, and for home production.

This development represents a controlled opening intended to balance tourism growth with cultural norms. Until licensing is fully rolled out and operational checks are visible on the ground, travelers should assume most venues in Riyadh will not legally sell alcohol.

Realities on the Ground: What Travelers and Expats Report

Black Market and Risks

Because demand existed despite prohibition, an illicit market for alcohol arose. Reports have documented dramatic markups—one-liter bottles of vodka sold on the black market have been reported in the hundreds of dollars, and rare whiskies have fetched thousands. Quality, safety, and legal risk are major concerns: illicit alcohol may be counterfeit or unsafe, and purchasing it exposes buyers to severe penalties.

Social Reality: How Saudis and Long-Term Residents Adapt

Many Saudis and observant residents prefer the status quo, while other segments of society—especially in business or entertainment sectors—seek ways to welcome international guests without undermining cultural norms. Hospitality professionals in Riyadh increasingly offer elaborate non-alcoholic beverage programs, premium mocktails, and crafted experiences that replicate the convivial social atmosphere traditionally associated with alcohol.

Practical Blueprints: How to Plan If You Care About Alcohol Access

Before You Go: Questions to Ask

When planning travel or relocation, a clear checklist of questions will save time and avoid missteps. Ask potential hotels and event organizers:

  • Do you hold an alcohol license under the new regulations, and if so, for which dates and spaces?
  • Where and how is alcohol served? Is it limited to hotel restaurants, private event spaces, or licensed bars?
  • What identity checks or residency status are required to purchase or consume?
  • Do staff receive responsible service training and how are purchases tracked?

These questions will reveal whether a venue genuinely offers legal alcohol options or is relying on informal practices.

Step-by-Step: How to Verify Legality and Avoid Risk

  1. Confirm licensing in writing or on the hotel’s official website—don’t rely on word-of-mouth.
  2. Ask to see the specific policy that governs alcohol sales for your reservation or event.
  3. Carry printed confirmation and ID that match the venue’s requirements.
  4. Avoid any offers to purchase alcohol off-site or from private sellers.
  5. If in doubt, seek alternatives: designated licensed resorts or embassy events.

Alternatives That Preserve the Experience

World-Class Non-Alcoholic Drinking Culture

Riyadh’s hospitality industry has responded to restrictions with creativity. Top hotels, boutique bars, and restaurants now offer sophisticated non-alcoholic programs that include house-made shrubs, artisanal sodas, and complex mocktails using fresh juices, herbs, and specialty bitters (non-alcoholic). Coffee culture and ceremonial Arabic coffee service—where cardamom and saffron often feature—offer a deeply social substitute for alcoholic rituals.

Non-Alcoholic Beer and Sparkling Alternatives

International and regional brands of non-alcoholic beer and sparkling beverages are widely available and provide a sensory substitute for beer and cider. Supermarkets and hotel outlets stock premium non-alcoholic options that pair well with international menus.

Private Events Within Licensed Spaces

Some licensed hotels will allow private events where alcohol service is permitted within a sealed, supervised environment. If you are hosting a corporate event, consider contracting a licensed venue and ensuring all guest IDs and guest lists follow the hotel’s policy.

Regional Differences to Consider

Saudi Arabia is large, and pilot programs may roll out in specific regions first. Coastal resort islands and NEOM are likely to operate under different practical realities than Riyadh’s urban environment. If your itinerary includes coastal or Eastern Province destinations—where tourism-oriented developments and international compounds are more common—expect different availability rules.

Practical Scenarios and Decision Frameworks

Business Travel: How to Manage Meetings and Events

If you are traveling for business, choose venues that clearly state their policy, and include this verification in event contracts. Frame negotiations around private, licensed event spaces rather than public venues. When in doubt, host post-meeting receptions at internationally affiliated hotels that have publicly declared their licensing status.

Long-Term Residents and Expat Compounds

Expat residential compounds often operate under different rules and may fall under special licensing regimes. If you are considering long-term relocation, factor compound policy into housing decisions and consult with human resources or relocation specialists about available legal options and community practice. Responsible Behavior: Etiquette, Local Norms, and Safety

Respect and Discretion

Even where licensed sales are allowed, discretion remains critical. Public intoxication is socially unacceptable and legally risky. If you are in a licensed venue, keep behavior moderate and polite; always follow venue rules and respect staff requests. Avoid social media posts that show intoxication or illegal possession.

Medical Safety

If you have prescription needs or health-based reasons for alcohol use that require clinical oversight, discuss these discreetly with a qualified local medical professional who understands the legal environment. Never attempt to self-medicate with illicit substances.

One-Page Checklist: How to Stay Legal and Enjoy Riyadh (Quick Reference)

  1. Verify licensing: Request written confirmation from hotels or venues about their alcohol license and guest eligibility.  
  2. Follow ID rules: Bring original ID and any residency documentation required by the venue.  
  3. Avoid illicit markets: Do not buy from private sellers or smuggle alcohol into the country.  
  4. Choose licensed resorts for alcohol needs: If access is essential, plan stays at venues within the announced license program.  
  5. Embrace alternatives: Enjoy premium non-alcoholic beverages and regional cultural experiences.

This concise checklist is designed to help you make clear, low-risk choices while you explore Riyadh’s evolving hospitality scene.

Scenario Planning: Four Practical Itineraries

1) Short Business Trip (2–3 days)

Focus on central business meetings and evening receptions. Book a reputable five-star hotel that confirms its licensing status for private functions. Schedule cultural visits to balance work with local immersion—visit the National Museum or Diriyah, then unwind with a crafted non-alcoholic tasting at your hotel.

2) Leisure City Break (4–6 days)

Divide time between modern city places (Kingdom Centre, Saudi National Museum) and a desert excursion to the Edge of the World. Reserve evenings at hotels known for elevated mocktail programs or, if staying at a licensed resort, plan one dinner in the licensed area.

3) Family Visit (7+ days)

Prioritize family-friendly experiences—parks, museums, and heritage sites. Choose hotels that emphasize family amenities and non-alcoholic dining experiences. For guests who expect alcohol, discuss private function options within licensed venues well in advance.

4) Expat Move or Long-Term Stay

Research residential compounds and employer-provided relocation support. Confirm whether your compound has access to licensed communal facilities. Integrate local customs into daily life—learn coffee and tea rituals, and invest in social activities that do not center on alcohol.

Anticipating the Near Future: What to Watch

Rolling Out Licenses and What That Means Practically

Expect a phased approach: licensing will likely begin with a curated list of high-end hotels, resort islands, and select international events. Operational details—such as whether alcohol is served in open bars, private event rooms, or behind designated doors—will vary by property and region. Keep these expectations in mind:

  • Transparency from venues will be a sign of legitimate licensing.
  • New licenses will come with strict record-keeping and guest checks.
  • The expansion will not turn Saudi cities into open-drinking destinations; cultural sensitivity remains primary.

Cross-Border Strategies

Some travelers will choose cross-border weekend trips to neighboring Gulf countries with established alcohol rules. These short escapes are common for those seeking fuller beverage options while keeping Riyadh as a hub for work or cultural immersion.

Industry Adaptations

Hotels, resorts, and event planners will invest heavily in staff training, responsible service programs, and secure inventory systems. Expect to see more elevated non-alcoholic beverage development and hybrid hospitality solutions that allow international guests to feel at home without undermining local norms.

Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don’t assume a hotel’s bar is legal—always ask for licensing confirmation.  
  • Don’t accept offers from private sellers or the black market. The legal risk is high and penalties severe.  
  • Don’t post evidence of illegal possession or consumption on social media.  
  • Don’t transport alcohol through luggage or diplomatic pouches unless you are authorized to do so.

Avoiding these mistakes will protect your travel plans and your legal standing in the Kingdom.

Cultural Connections: How to Enjoy Social Life Without Alcohol

Saudi social life is rich and varied. Traditional coffee rituals, majlis hospitality, and shared meals are central. Learning the etiquette of the majlis—how to greet hosts, the order of serving coffee and dates, and how to express appreciation—will open doors to memorable cultural exchange that requires no alcohol.

Dining culture is another highlight. Saudi chefs and restaurateurs create menus that pair beautifully with non-alcoholic beverages, introducing flavors and textures unique to the Arabian Peninsula. Embrace that culinary path; it’s one of the lasting rewards of travel here.

Final Thoughts

The question of where to get alcohol in Riyadh is a practical one with social, legal, and cultural dimensions. Right now, the long-standing diplomatic exception remains the principal legal channel, while the 2026 licensing rollout signals limited, highly regulated expansion into select venues. For visitors and residents, the safest approach is to verify licensing, avoid illicit markets, and lean into the rich alternatives Riyadh offers. Thoughtful planning and cultural respect will let you experience the city’s modernity and heritage without unnecessary risk.

FAQ

Is it legal for tourists to buy alcohol in Riyadh?

No. As of now, non-diplomatic tourists cannot legally purchase alcohol in Riyadh. Limited licensure beginning in 2026 is expected to allow sales in specific licensed venues like five-star hotels and resort areas, but access will be tightly controlled.

Can expatriates living in Saudi Arabia buy alcohol?

Not generally. Expatriates may have access if they live in approved compounds or have a status tied to diplomatic or special-license rules. Always verify with your employer, compound administration, or the licensed venue.

What are safe alternatives for social drinking experiences?

High-end non-alcoholic cocktails, premium non-alcoholic beers, ceremonial Arabic coffee, and curated mocktail menus at top hotels offer safe and socially rich alternatives.

How can I confirm whether a hotel or resort is licensed?
