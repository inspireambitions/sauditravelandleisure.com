Transport: Getting Around Muscat Efficiently

Taxis, ride apps and public buses

Taxis are plentiful; insist the meter is on. App-based services make fares predictable, and hotel concierges can arrange reliable pick-ups for longer journeys. Public buses exist but are less convenient for tourists; rideshare or hire cars remain the most flexible.

Renting a car

Renting a car gives you freedom for day trips into the Hajars or longer coastal journeys. Roads are excellent, but urban parking can be limited in historic areas. If you plan multiple day trips and visits to remote beaches, renting is worth it.

Airport transfers

Most hotels can arrange pick-up and drop-off; pre-book with your hotel for a smoother arrival, especially late at night. If you prefer the metered taxis, confirm approximate fares with hotel staff so you avoid misunderstandings.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Which neighbourhood is best for first-time visitors to Muscat?

For first-timers who want a mix of convenience and local life, Azaiba or Al Ghubrah are excellent choices. They place you within easy reach of the Grand Mosque, Muttrah and the Qurum coastline while offering quieter, residential surroundings.

2. Is Muttrah a good place to stay for families?

Muttrah is great for sightseeing and cultural immersion, but families who prioritise beaches and resort amenities may prefer Qurum or a dedicated resort east of the city. Muttrah can be lively and more urban in feel.

3. Do I need a car to enjoy Muscat?

You can see many key attractions without a car using taxis and ride apps, especially if you stay in centrally located areas. Rent a car if you plan multiple day trips outside the city or want the flexibility to reach remote beaches and mountain trails.

4. Where should I stay for beachfront luxury?

Al Mouj and the resort bays near Al Jissah are the top choices for beachfront luxury. They offer premium hotels, private beaches and marinas ideal for a relaxed, high-comfort stay.
